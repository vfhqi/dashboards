[W] BGN — Peer Comparative Fundamental Analysis — Earnings & Fundamental Trends Query 4: BGN vs FBK / BMED / AZM Date: 28 April 2026 Source: [C] Claude — Public Filings, Earnings Releases, IR Presentations

1. Executive Summary

Overall Assessment: GREEN (with Yellow flags on NII delivery and BGN relative scale)

  • Peer group fundamental momentum ranking (best to weakest): BMED > AZM (recurring) > BGN > FBK (on EPS absolute growth trajectory; FBK leads on operational efficiency and medium-term momentum). There is no single dominant winner — each company leads on a different dimension.
  • BGN's position: Solid middle of the peer group. Record FY2025 net profit of €446m (+3% YoY) looks modest versus BMED's 11% growth, but the quality of the recurring component (€362m, +7% YoY) is strong. BGN is the highest-quality private banking franchise on a risk-adjusted basis; it is not the fastest grower.
  • Three most significant trend reversals or accelerations in the L12M:
    • AZM inflow acceleration — €32bn in FY2025 (record), with 66% from international — marks a structural shift away from Italian-dependent revenue; this is a peer group standout that changes the competitive framing.
    • FBK NII-to-fee rotation — FinecoBank delivered flat total revenue (€1,316m) despite NII falling 11% to €633m, offset by non-financial income rising 13% to €685m; this transition is now essentially complete and de-risks FBK's earnings quality materially.
    • BGN headline profit growth deceleration — from +32% in FY2024 to +3% in FY2025 — is the critical signal to monitor; the recurring profit sub-line (+7%) is more reassuring than the headline, but the step-change lower requires explanation.
  • Sector-wide tailwinds: Italian private banking AUM surpassed €1.37 trillion through Q3 2025 (+10.8% YoY); sector net inflows of €53bn in the first nine months of 2025; ECB deposit facility stabilised at 2.00% (consensus holds through 2026) providing NII floor; Italian household under-allocation to investments remains the structural opportunity.
  • Sector-wide headwinds: ECB rate cuts already executed (from 4.00% to 2.00% across 2024-early 2025) mean NII is structurally lower for all deposit-taking wealth managers; fee compression from passive and digital competition intensifying; market appreciation flatters AUM figures, obscuring net new money quality.
  • Stack rank by fundamental momentum: BMED (profit growth 11%, C/I improving), AZM (recurring profit +20%, inflows record), FBK (cost structure best-in-class, long-term EPS CAGR credible), BGN (high quality, moderate near-term momentum).
  • BGN's strongest card is not momentum but predictability, capital generation (CET1 well above SREP at 8.7%), and the Alleanza option value which is not in consensus numbers for 2026-2027.
  • The Alleanza partnership — 10,000 consultants, 2,700 qualified FAs, targeting €7.0-8.5bn in volumes by 2030 — is a genuine medium-term growth option not embedded in short-term guidance, making BGN's guidance trajectory deliberately conservative.
  • Q1 2026 momentum (€1.9bn YTD inflows through March, annualised >€7.5bn) already exceeds the full-year guidance of €6.5bn on an annualised run-rate basis, providing strong early validation.
  • Key risk across the group: all four companies carry NII compression exposure as European rates settle lower; differentiation increasingly rests on whether managed asset mix and fee income can absorb the NII headwind.
  • A second structural risk specific to BGN and BMED is the distribution model dependency: both rely on employed/contracted financial advisor networks where recruitment costs and retention premiums represent a rising cost line.

2. Revenue Growth & Organic Dynamics

Rating: GREEN

  • BGN FY2025 total revenue context: Revenues are not broken out as a single headline in the same way as commercial-bank peers; the relevant organic metric is net inflows. BGN delivered €6.8bn total net inflows in FY2025 (+3% vs FY2024's €6.6bn), with AUI inflows of €4.0bn. Total AUM/AUI reached €113bn, +9% YoY.
  • Peer comparison — net inflows:
    • FBK: €13.4bn net sales in FY2025 (+33% YoY), comprising AUM €5.5bn, deposits €2.0bn, AUC €6.0bn. Absolute inflow figure is almost double BGN's, though FBK's AUM base (c.€130bn) and client base (2.1m clients, adding 194,000 net in FY2025) are proportionally different.
    • BMED: €11.64bn total net inflows (+11% YoY); managed asset inflows €9.06bn (+18%); total AUM €155.8bn (+12% YoY). The 18% growth in managed asset inflows is the best quality metric in the peer group on this dimension.
    • AZM: €32bn net inflows (record); total assets €145bn (+32%). However, €32bn includes global operations and significant third-party AUM; comparability to domestic-focused BGN is limited.
  • Organic quality test — is it net new money or market appreciation? For BMED, the split is approximately 50/50 between net inflows and market effects in the L12M AUM growth. For BGN, the €6.8bn inflows against a starting AUM of ~€104bn (FY2024) represents a net new money rate of approximately 6.5% — healthy and above the sector average of c.4-5% for the Italian private banking segment.
  • Fee margin and mix: BGN's AUI (assets under investment — managed solutions) represents 59% of total assets, up from c.55% in FY2023. This mix shift is the key margin driver; managed assets generate 5-10x the revenue per euro of administered assets. AUI inflows of €4.0bn in FY2025 at 59% of total is structurally positive.
  • FBK fee mix evolution: Fineco Asset Management targets 45-50% internal AUM share by 2029 (from 39% in 2025), which will lift fee margins as proprietary products carry higher margins than third-party.
  • BMED pricing power: Net commission income rose 12% to €1.31bn in FY2025 while AUM grew 12% — suggesting fee margins were broadly stable, not compressing. This is a positive signal; the industry fear of fee compression is not yet visible in BMED's numbers.
  • BGN managed solution mix is the strongest in the domestic peer group. At 59% AUI/total assets, BGN leads FBK (lower AUM/AUC balance) and competes directly with BMED's high managed-solutions emphasis.
  • Sceptical view: The Italian sector's 10.8% AUM growth through Q3 2025 was driven approximately equally by net inflows and market appreciation. A 10-15% equity market correction would reduce AUM, depress management fees on a mark-to-market basis, and slow new client acquisition. BGN's Q1 2026 inflows of €1.9bn are strong, but the first three months benefit from year-start seasonality in Italian wealth management.
  • Forward revenue guidance: BGN guides >€6.5bn net inflows for 2026 (conservative relative to €7.5bn annualised Q1 run-rate). FBK guides for growth in all business lines with particular emphasis on investing revenues and deposit inflows in 2026 per the March CMD. BMED targets c.€9bn managed asset inflows for 2026. AZM targets €10bn inflows for 2026.
  • Market share trajectory: BGN holds approximately 8% of Italian private banking AUM (€113bn / €1.37 trillion). FBK and BMED are similar scale. None of the domestic players is materially gaining or losing market share in percentage terms — structural tailwinds are lifting all boats.
  • Revenue growth assessment: BGN's revenue dynamics (as proxied by inflow momentum and fee income) are solid but not leading. BMED is the strongest on managed-asset inflow quality. FBK's 33% net sales growth is impressive but partly reflects deposit acceleration. BGN's mix quality (AUI 59%) is a durable revenue quality advantage.

3. Margin Profile & Evolution

Rating: YELLOW (NII headwind is real; offset by fee mix holds the line)

  • Key margin metrics — FY2025:
    • BGN: recurring net profit €362m on estimated total revenue base of c.€900m+ → recurring net profit margin strong; headline net profit margin broadly flat YoY as NII compression absorbed by fee growth.
    • FBK: cost/income 26.8% (9M 2025, confirmed as full-year context), best-in-class in the peer group and arguably the best in European banking. Net profit €647m on revenue €1,316m → net profit margin ~49%.
    • BMED: cost/income improved to 37.6% (FY2025) from 39.0% (FY2024), showing operating leverage. Net profit margin: €1,240m on contribution margin of €2,110m → operating efficiency improving.
    • AZM: recurring revenues now 95% of total (up from ~70% in 2014), demonstrating substantial improvement in revenue quality and margin predictability.
  • NII structural headwind — the critical line item. ECB cut rates from 4.00% to 2.00% across 2024 to early 2025. For BGN, FY2024 NII was c.€360-380m (estimated from the 2025 guidance of €325m actual and the upward move to €330-340m guidance for 2026). FBK saw NII fall 11% to €633m in FY2025. BMED's NII was stable at €812m in FY2025 — notably resilient, implying deposit volume growth offset the rate compression.
  • BGN NII sensitivity: A €1bn increase in client deposits adds €9.5m to NII; a 10bp rate move adds only €2.5m. Volume is the lever, not rates. The 2026 NII guidance of €330-340m (vs FY2025 actual of €325m) implies a 1.5-4.6% increase, requiring deposit volume growth of c.€500m-€1.5bn — achievable given the Alleanza banking component rollout, but not risk-free.
  • Operating leverage: Both BGN and BMED demonstrate positive operating leverage — AUM growing faster than costs. BGN's operating cost growth has been contained; the headcount of financial advisors (c.2,400 PFAs at BGN) is not expanding rapidly, meaning AUM-per-advisor metrics are rising.
  • FBK margin quality: The 26.8% cost/income is structurally superior to peers (BMED 37.6%, BGN estimated 40-45%) because FBK's digital-first model requires far fewer human advisors per client. This margin gap is likely to narrow modestly over time but will not close — the business model difference is structural.
  • BMED margin trajectory: The improvement from 39.0% to 37.6% cost/income is positive and reflects NII stability + fee income growth outpacing operating costs. Management targets c.38% C/I for 2026, implying further modest improvement.
  • Sceptical view on BGN margins: BGN's headline net profit growth (+3% YoY) lagged recurring profit growth (+7% YoY), suggesting non-recurring or non-fee items dragged. If NII comes in at the low end of guidance (€330m), and fee income growth decelerates to 5% (vs c.7% in 2025), operating margin expansion could stall. Personnel costs for financial advisors — particularly at the senior/private banker level — are rising as the sector competes for talent.
  • AZM margin evolution: Recurring revenue at 95% of total is the standout peer group data point for earnings quality, but AZM's absolute margin levels are lower than FBK and broadly comparable to BGN, given the higher cost of its global distribution infrastructure.
  • Overall margin picture: FBK has the best structural margin. BMED is improving most rapidly. BGN's margin is high-quality (strong recurring mix) but the NII headwind and limited operating leverage relative to digital platforms are the constraints. The sector-level NII compression bottomed in early 2025 with rates stabilising at 2.00% — the acute headwind is past, but no tailwind returns without rate rises.

4. EPS Trajectory & Delivery

Rating: GREEN (recurring EPS quality solid; headline deceleration manageable)

  • BGN EPS history:
    • FY2022: EPS €2.86
    • FY2023: EPS €3.78 (+32% YoY)
    • FY2024: EPS €3.91 (+3.4% YoY — the first notable deceleration)
    • FY2025: Net profit €446m, EPS approximately €3.91-3.93 (flat YoY as shares outstanding stable; 2026 consensus EPS estimate €4.25)
    • Note: FY2023 was the step-change year; FY2024-FY2025 represent a consolidation phase. Recurring profit growth (+7% in FY2025) is the better quality signal.
  • Peer EPS comparison:
    • FBK: Net profit €647m in FY2025, flat YoY. EPS broadly flat as revenue composition shifted (NII -11% offset by non-financial income +13%). The CMD 2026-2029 plan targets low double-digit EPS CAGR — this is an ambitious but credible target given the operational leverage profile.
    • BMED: Net profit €1,240m (+11% YoY). EPS growth comfortably double-digit. ROE 29.1% — highest in the peer group. BMED is the EPS growth leader in the current period.
    • AZM: FY2025 net profit €526m, down from FY2024's €588m adjusted. The decline is attributable to lower performance fees and capital gains; recurring net profit grew 20% YoY, making AZM's quality-adjusted EPS trajectory the strongest, but the headline number is misleading.
  • Buyback activity: BGN has not announced a buyback programme; the payout model is pure dividend (€2.90 proposed for FY2025, payout ratio ~76%). FBK maintains a 70-80% dividend payout ratio with no buyback announced. AZM proposed a €2.00 dividend per share and also a share buyback. BMED pays a progressive dividend (€1.25 for FY2025). Across the group, buybacks are not a significant EPS-enhancement tool — the sector prefers high cash dividends.
  • BGN EPS quality: The distinction between headline EPS (flat/+3%) and recurring EPS growth (+7% on recurring profit) is important. BGN's FY2025 headline was €446m, matching FY2024's €445.8m almost exactly. Within this, recurring profit (fee-based) at €362m grew 7%. The implication: performance fees and non-recurring items contributed less in FY2025, and the base from which 2026 compounds is higher quality and more durable.
  • FBK EPS path: The 2026-2029 CMD targets are the most explicit medium-term EPS commitment in the peer group. Low double-digit CAGR on 2025 EPS base of c.€0.97 (pro-rated from 9M figures) implies EPS of approximately €1.25-1.35 by 2029. The credibility rests on: (a) pan-European expansion delivering net sales; (b) Fineco Asset Management share of AUM reaching 45-50% (higher margin); (c) cost/income staying below 30%.
  • Sceptical view on EPS: BGN's flat EPS in FY2024-FY2025 (after dramatic growth in FY2022-FY2023) could indicate a plateau rather than a consolidation before re-acceleration. If Alleanza volumes ramp slower than the 2030 €7-8.5bn target implies, and if market conditions are less benign, BGN's EPS growth could track 3-5% per annum — acceptable but not value-creating at a premium multiple. AZM's headline EPS decline in FY2025 is a caution: performance fee dependency creates earnings volatility that recurring revenue growth does not fully offset.

7. Sector & Value Chain Context

Rating: GREEN (structural tailwind intact; NII floor established)

  • Italian private banking AUM trajectory: Surpassed €1.37 trillion through Q3 2025 (+10.8% YoY). Total net inflows for the sector were €53bn in the first nine months of 2025 — roughly 4% of opening AUM, well above long-run averages of 2-3%. The sector is in an above-trend growth phase driven by Italian household savings mobilisation.
  • Italian household savings mobilisation — the structural story. Italy has one of the highest household savings rates in Europe, with a large proportion held in bank deposits, government bonds (BTP), and real estate. The financial planning penetration opportunity remains large: an estimated 60%+ of household financial wealth is in deposits and government bonds. As rates normalise downward and professional advice becomes more accessible, the long-term migration toward managed solutions is intact.
  • ECB rate trajectory: Deposit facility rate at 2.00%, held through March 2026 (eight consecutive holds). Reuters economist consensus: three-quarters expect no change before end-2026. This is the single most important variable for sector NII forecasting. A stable 2.00% rate provides a floor — not a tailwind — but removes the acute compression risk that dominated 2024.
  • Alleanza insurbanking — BGN-specific advantage: The partnership with Alleanza Assicurazioni (10,000 insurance consultants, 2,700 qualified as FAs) is not replicated at any other peer. The "Stile Unico" unit-linked product was rolled out to 2,700 private advisors in Q4 2025, with the full banking component (BG Conto Unico) in a pilot phase in early 2026 before broader rollout. Target volumes of €7.0-8.5bn by 2030 would represent 6-7% of BGN's current total assets — not transformational in year one, but cumulatively significant.
  • Competitive intensity: Three distinct competitive segments:
    • Bank-owned networks (BGN, BMED): high-touch advisory, premium pricing, employed advisor model
    • Independent digital platforms (FBK): low cost/income, scalable, digital-first
    • Global independent managers (AZM): diversified geographically, performance-fee driven
    • External competitive threats from Intesa Sanpaolo's collaboration with BlackRock and bank-fintech partnerships are real but unlikely to shift market share materially in the 2-3 year horizon.
  • Fee compression risk: The Italian sector has not yet seen material fee compression. Net commission income growth across peers (BMED +12%, BGN positive) tracks AUM growth. Passive investing penetration in Italy is lower than Northern Europe — approximately 15% of fund assets vs 35%+ in the UK. The digital threat is competitive on client acquisition but less so on AUM monetisation given the Italian cultural preference for advice.
  • Sceptical view: The sector's 10.8% AUM growth in 2025 was approximately 50% market appreciation and 50% net inflows. If equity markets retrace 15-20% (plausible given the Iran conflict risk context), AUM would fall, management fees would compress on the lower AUM base, and new inflow momentum would likely slow as clients become risk-averse. For BGN, BMED, and FBK, Q2 2026 inflows will be the first real test of whether the geopolitical environment has shifted client behaviour.
  • Structural digital disruption: Lower than peers in other European markets. Italy's private banking sector is characterised by advice-led distribution where relationships and trust matter more than price transparency. This partly explains why FBK's digital model has grown rapidly (adding 194,000 clients in FY2025) without materially cannibalising the higher-end advisory networks.
  • Value chain positioning: BGN sits in the most defensible part of the value chain — high-AUM, advice-dependent private clients where switching costs are high and fee sensitivity is lower than mass market. This is also the segment most resistant to digital disruption and passive migration.

9. Sceptical Summary

Overall confidence in BGN positive thesis: GREEN (with specific contingencies)

  • The bearish case for BGN in three components:
    • NII execution risk: 2026 NII guidance of €330-340m requires deposit volume growth of c.€500m-€1.5bn, or outperformance on reinvestment yield. If deposit volumes stagnate (clients redirect savings to AUC/AUM rather than banking products), NII could come in below €330m, closing the one remaining earnings growth lever beyond fee income. The Alleanza banking rollout in 2026 is a pilot — not yet a volume driver.
    • Alleanza conversion risk: The 10,000 Alleanza consultants are insurance advisors, not trained private bankers. Converting 2,700 registered FAs to active users of the BGN investment platform is a multi-year training and cultural challenge. The €7-8.5bn volume target by 2030 is long-dated and conditional on adoption rates that have no precedent. Initial 2025 contributions of ~€100m attributable inflows are proof-of-concept but tiny relative to the 2030 target.
    • Fee compression: Italy is behind Northern Europe in passive adoption, but the direction is clear. As ETF penetration grows and younger affluent clients become more cost-aware, BGN's 59% managed-solution mix will face gradual fee pressure. The managed solutions fee margin (estimated 60-80bp on AUM) has not yet shown compression, but the risk is 5-7 years out, not immediate.
  • FBK as the strongest technical momentum competitor: FinecoBank's CMD with explicit EPS CAGR targets, its cost/income advantage (26.8% vs BGN's estimated 40-45%), and its pan-European expansion create a medium-term story that may attract capital flows away from BGN at the margin. FBK is growing its client base at 194,000 per year (a 10% annual expansion rate on its existing base) while BGN's PFA headcount grows modestly. In scale and momentum terms, FBK is the peer most likely to gain competitive ground vs BGN over 5 years.
  • Single largest risk — NII guidance delivery: BGN's 2026 NII guidance of €330-340m is the most quantified and testable near-term commitment. The mechanics: ECB rate at 2.00% is the base; BGN's deposit sensitivity is €9.5m per €1bn volume. If deposit volumes decline by €1-2bn (as clients shift to investment products — actually part of BGN's own commercial strategy), NII could fall €10-20m below the low end of guidance. This is not catastrophic (€310-320m NII is still a sound number), but it would reset consensus expectations and likely weigh on the share price.
  • Q1 2026 early warning signals:
