Research Plan

This memo provides a comprehensive analysis of HMS Networks, Invisio, and Cicor Technologies, focusing on the transformative internal and external shifts observed over the last 12 to 18 months and projecting their trajectories through 2028-2030. The following synthesis integrates company filings, Capital Markets Day (CMD) presentations, and deep sell-side research from multiple analysts to provide a view for fundamental equity portfolio managers.

Brief Company Descriptions HMS Networks AB

HMS Networks (HardwaRE Meets Software) is a global leader in Industrial Information and Communication Technology (ICT), specializing in connectivity solutions that enable industrial hardware—such as robots, motor drives, and generators—to communicate with control systems and the Internet.

The company operates through three recently restructured divisions: Industrial Data Solutions (IDS), Industrial Network Technology (INT), and New Industries (NI).

HMS generates revenue primarily from Europe and North America, with its portfolio featuring established brands like Anybus, Ewon, Intesis, Ixxat, and the newly integrated Red Lion Controls.

Over the next three years, HMS is poised to transition from a hardware-centric provider to a more software-driven platform, aiming to increase its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) from 3% to 10% of net sales by 2030.

The business model relies on a high volume of "design wins" where HMS products are embedded into the equipment of Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs), creating long-tail revenue streams.

Invisio AB

Invisio develops and sells advanced integrated communication systems and hearing protection for professionals in defense and public safety, headquartered in Copenhagen.

The company utilizes an asset-light model, outsourcing manufacturing to European contract manufacturers while focusing internally on R&D and global sales.

Revenue is geographically diversified across Europe, North America, and Asia, serving elite military units and special forces.

Invisio’s portfolio includes headsets, control units, intercoms, and the Racal Acoustics brand, which has successfully re-established its presence in the U.S. market.

Plausibly over the next three years, Invisio will leverage a record order book and massive increases in national defense budgets to drive growth through new system-level solutions like the INVISIO Link and T30 headset.

Cicor Technologies Ltd

Cicor is a high-reliability electronics manufacturer providing Electronic Manufacturing Services (EMS) and Advanced Substrates to the aerospace, defense (A&D), medical, and industrial sectors.

The company has rapidly evolved through a "buy-and-build" strategy, completing five acquisitions in 2025 alone to become a leading pan-European player.

Cicor operates manufacturing sites across Europe (UK, Germany, France, Romania, Switzerland), North Africa (Morocco, Tunisia), and Asia (China, Vietnam, Indonesia).

Its revenue model is shifting from pure contract manufacturing toward a Contract Development and Manufacturing Organization (CDMO) model, employing over 400 engineers to support customer R&D.

Over the next three years, Cicor intends to continue its consolidation of the fragmented European EMS market, targeting revenue of over CHF 1 billion by 2028.
