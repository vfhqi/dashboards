C Triaging @ No Notion link

Peer Comparison: Hasbro (HAS), Funko (FNKO), Mattel (MAT) context

Produced: 28 April 2026 | Source: Public disclosures, company filings, WebSearch

KEY FINDINGS

  • GAW is the clear fundamental leader in the peer group by every material metric. Core revenue +17.3% in H1 FY2026, core operating margin 39.9% (+350bp), cash from operations +27.8% — delivered against a base that already included double-digit growth. No peer is close.
  • The licensing base-effect drag is real but well-understood, and the core business is accelerating underneath it. Total revenue growth was +10.9% in H1 FY2026; strip out the £14.1m licensing decline (Space Marine 2 royalty normalisation) and core revenue grew +17.3%. The bears must explain why +17% core growth is not the more important number.
  • Hasbro's 14% headline revenue growth is almost entirely a Magic: The Gathering event — Consumer Products declined 4%. The WotC segment at 46% operating margin is structurally attractive but narrow; one bad release cycle and the headline reverses. This is the opposite of GAW's broad-based core growth.
  • Funko is in financial distress territory. FY2025 revenue -13.5% to $908m, full-year net loss of $67.4m, credit agreement renegotiated to extend maturity to December 2027. Funko and GAW are in different universes.
  • Mattel's full-year revenue declined 0.6% and gross margin compressed 210bp. The major toy companies — Hasbro Consumer Products, Mattel — are fighting cyclical headwinds, tariff exposure, and pricing pressure that GAW is largely immunised against by its IP moat and UK-based manufacturing.
  • The biggest single risk to the GAW thesis is the Amazon TV deal overhang. If the Henry Cavill Warhammer series underperforms or development stalls, the next leg of licensing growth (which consensus is pricing in) disappears. This is binary and unpriceable.
  • US tariffs are a known headwind but manageable. £6m cost in H1 FY2026 (c.1.8% of core revenue), offset by 3.5% price increases implemented in October 2025. GAW's UK-centric manufacturing is structurally advantaged versus plastic toy peers importing from China.
  • Cash conversion is exceptional and improving. Cash from operations £169.4m in H1 FY2026 (+27.8%), versus operating profit £140.4m. Operating cash conversion above 120% — earnings are understating economic reality.
  • Warhammer 40,000 11th edition launch in Summer 2026 is the next major organic catalyst. New edition cycles historically drive a significant revenue surge as players rebuild armies. The Armageddon launch box positions H1 FY2027 as a potential step-change year.
  • At c.30x trailing PE, the stock prices in considerable optimism. The multiple is 28% above the 10-year median. Bears will point to the SM2 licensing drag, tariff costs, and valuation; bulls counter that 39.9% operating margin at scale, growing organically, deserves a quality premium.

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Overall Rating: GREEN

  • Stack-rank by fundamental momentum: GAW (clear first), Hasbro (distant second, on WotC strength only), Mattel (third, stable but contracting), Funko (fourth, in distress). This ranking reflects H1 FY2026 / most recent full-year results for each.
  • GAW holds the top position on every dimension that matters: revenue growth trajectory, margin level and direction, cash conversion, EPS momentum, and capital returns. The gap to the next-best peer is not incremental — it is structural.
  • The most significant trend reversal in the peer group over the last 12 months is Hasbro's transformation from a broadly declining toy company into a WotC-driven gaming business. The strategic pivot away from Consumer Products (now -4% YoY) is explicit: Hasbro sold its Entertainment One business and is concentrating capital on high-margin IP-led gaming.
  • The second major trend is Funko's accelerating deterioration. Having already cut SKUs aggressively in 2023-24, FY2025 delivered a further -13.5% revenue decline and a $67.4m net loss, with credit maturity extended as a forbearance measure. The brand has not stabilised.
  • The third trend is sector-wide tariff disruption. All four peers have disclosed material US tariff exposure. GAW is best-positioned (UK manufacturing, 3.5% price rise already implemented, estimated £12m annual drag acknowledged and partially offset). Mattel and Funko, with China-heavy supply chains, face structurally worse exposure.
  • Sector-wide tailwinds are real. The tabletop gaming market is growing at c.10% CAGR with the adult hobbyist demographic (18-54 years, 85% of players) expanding. GAW's core product — premium IP-anchored miniatures wargaming — sits in the fastest-growing subsegment of a growing market.
  • GAW's key sector-specific headwind is the normalisation of Space Marine 2 licensing royalties. The video game sold 7m+ copies at launch; royalties peaked in H1 FY2025 (£30.1m for the half) and have normalised to £16.0m in H1 FY2026. The Amazon deal is the next licensing catalyst but has no near-term revenue read-through — it is still in creative development.
  • GAW's core margin expansion is the single most important fact in this memo. Core operating margin 39.9% (+350bp) on a half where revenue grew 17.3% is the signature of a business with genuine operating leverage, disciplined cost control, and pricing power. There is no peer equivalent in the branded hobby/toy sector.
  • The overall assessment for GAW specifically is GREEN. The business is in the best financial shape in its history, growing organically, expanding margins, generating cash well above reported earnings, and returning capital to shareholders at record levels. The risks are real (licensing normalisation, tariffs, valuation) but do not threaten the core thesis.

2. REVENUE GROWTH & ORGANIC DYNAMICS

Rating: GREEN

  • The top-line headline: GAW core revenue +17.3% in H1 FY2026 (six months to November 2025) against a comparable prior-year period that itself included a 40,000 product launch cycle. This is not easy-comparison growth. Total revenue of £332.1m grew +10.9%, depressed only by the expected licensing decline.
  • FY2025 full-year context (year to May 2025): total revenue £617.5m, up 18% from £525.7m in FY2024. The three-year revenue CAGR from FY2022 to FY2025 is approximately 15%, demonstrating that the recent acceleration is not aberrational — it is a continuation of a durable growth trend.
  • FY2024 revenue (year to June 2024): £525.7m, up from an estimated £460-470m in FY2023. The progression — FY2023 ~£465m → FY2024 £525.7m → FY2025 £617.5m → H1 FY2026 £332.1m (run-rate c.£640-660m) — is consistent and accelerating on the core business.

  • GAW's core revenue growth decomposition is primarily volume-led with modest price contribution. The company implemented 3.5% price increases on miniatures and books in October 2025 to offset tariff costs. Prior to that action, growth was predominantly volume/mix. This means customer demand is absorbing higher prices without apparent volume destruction — a hallmark of pricing power.
  • Channel breakdown in H1 FY2026: Trade channel (wholesale distributors) grew +21.7%, Retail (own stores) +11.2%, Online -4.2%. The Trade surge is significant — it indicates independent hobby stores and third-party retailers are restocking and growing orders, reflecting healthy end-consumer demand. Online weakness is modest and consistent with post-pandemic normalisation of online shopping.
  • GAW opened 16 new stores in H1 FY2026, bringing the global total to approximately 580+ locations. North American store count reached 201. Store openings create an organic revenue tailwind beyond like-for-like growth.
  • Geography: all core established countries grew year-on-year in FY2025. North America generates approximately 41% of revenues and has been the strongest growth region. The organic export programme is expanding into new markets.
  • Hasbro's revenue growth is a single-variable story. WotC & Digital Gaming grew 45% in FY2025 to $2.187bn, with Magic: The Gathering up 59%, driven by the Avatar: The Last Airbender set and Secret Lair products. Strip WotC out and Hasbro's Consumer Products revenue fell 4%. Entertainment also declined 4%. This is not broad-based growth.
  • Funko's revenue decline is structural, not cyclical. The company executed deliberate SKU rationalisation and reduced channel inventory in 2023-24; the 2025 volume decline reflects those cuts working through the system. Management guides FY2026 revenue flat to +3%, with Loungefly (their bag/accessory brand) down double digits due to prior SKU cuts. This is a business in slow-motion reset, not recovery.
  • Mattel's Q4 2025 recovery (+7%) does not change the full-year story of flat revenue (-0.6%). The Barbie movie tailwind has fully lapped. Hot Wheels remains healthy but insufficient to drive top-line growth. Tariff costs are building.
  • Sceptical interpretation — is GAW's core growth sustainable or pulling forward demand? The H1 FY2026 core growth of 17.3% was partly driven by new product releases in the Warhammer 40,000 range. If 40K 11th edition arrives in Summer 2026 as telegraphed, H2 FY2026 may see some launch pull-forward, and H1 FY2027 would receive the 11th edition launch boost. The real test is the underlying compound run-rate: c.17% in a non-launch half is the high-water mark. A more conservative base assumption for medium-term core growth is 10-13% per annum. Still outstanding; just not 17%.
  • Forward revenue signal: December 2024 (the month after H1 FY2026 close) was described as GAW's best-ever December, with all channels performing well and core sales estimated up 12%. This is strong early H2 momentum and suggests no post-H1 demand cliff.

3. MARGIN PROFILE & EVOLUTION

Rating: GREEN

  • GAW's core gross margin of 69.4% in H1 FY2026 (+190bp) is in a different industry category from every peer. For context: Hasbro WotC operating margin is 46%, Funko Q4 2025 gross margin 40.9%, Mattel FY2025 gross margin 48.7% (and declining). GAW's gross margin level reflects the premium craftsmanship of its products, proprietary IP, and direct-to-consumer/trade channel mix.
  • Core operating margin 39.9% in H1 FY2026 (+350bp) represents the highest operating margin in GAW's history and confirms that revenue growth is dropping through to profit at better-than-proportional rates. A 17.3% revenue increase driving a 28.6% core operating profit increase implies strong operating leverage.

  • GAW's margin trajectory over three years shows consistent expansion: FY2023 operating margin approximately 32%; FY2024 approximately 38%; FY2025 approximately 42.5% (full year); H1 FY2026 core operating margin 39.9% (which, given historical second-half licensing contribution, positions the full-year H2 FY2026 to again approach or exceed 40%). This is not a mean-reverting trend — it reflects structural mix improvement and IP leverage.
  • Margin decomposition: four drivers. (1) Price: 3.5% miniature price rise in October 2025, contributing approximately 200bp of GM tailwind. (2) Mix: core business growing faster than licensing, and within core, trade channel (highest margin) growing fastest at +21.7%. (3) Input costs: more stable commodity prices (resin, plastic) versus prior years. (4) Lower inventory write-offs: provisions as a percentage of gross stock fell from 17.7% to 11.9% in FY2025, freeing cost headroom.
  • The tariff headwind (£6m in H1 FY2026, estimated £12m full-year) was more than offset by these drivers. Without tariff mitigation actions, core gross margin would have been c.200-250bp lower. This is evidence of active margin management capability.
  • Operating leverage is genuine. Revenue growing 17.3% while core operating profit grew 28.6% implies incremental margins well above the average margin level. Fixed cost bases (manufacturing, design team, flagship stores) are scaling into growing volumes without proportional SG&A expansion.
  • Hasbro's margin expansion (+390bp adjusted operating margin, FY2025) is real but entirely composition-driven. WotC at 46% operating margin is displacing Consumer Products at approximately 10-12% operating margin. If WotC margins compress (from increased competition in digital card gaming, for example) the consolidated margin improvement reverses mechanically.
  • Mattel's gross margin compression (-210bp to 48.7%) reflects the combination of pricing concessions, unfavourable product mix (less Barbie), and building tariff exposure. Mattel's supply chain is more China-dependent than GAW's, creating a structural cost disadvantage in the current tariff environment.
  • Funko's gross margin instability — swinging from 28% (FY2022) to 42% (FY2024) to 41% (FY2025) — reflects the difficulty of managing a licensed collectibles business with no proprietary IP. Margins are a function of licensing deal terms, not operational excellence.
  • Sceptical interpretation — is GAW's margin expansion durable or partly one-off? The £12m tariff drag in H2 FY2026, the comping of the October 2025 price rise (annualised in FY2027), and the absence of inventory provision releases as a recurring benefit all suggest some margin normalisation risk. A realistic view is that core operating margins in the 37-40% range are sustainable; the H1 FY2026 print of 39.9% may be near the top of the current-cycle range. That is still exceptional by any comparison.

4. EPS TRAJECTORY & DELIVERY

Rating: GREEN

  • GAW EPS 319.9p in H1 FY2026 (+10.7%). On a half-year basis, this is already 319.9p against a prior full-year FY2025 that delivered approximately 600p in EPS (our estimate; FY2025 PBT was £262.8m, +29.5%). The EPS growth rate at the half-year lags operating profit growth (+28.6%) because licensing revenue decline flows through at near-100% margin.
  • EPS trend: FY2023 approximately 430p; FY2024 approximately 490p (implied from PBT £203m, 30% tax); FY2025 approximately 600p (implied from PBT £262.8m). Three-year EPS CAGR of approximately 12%. H1 FY2026 run-rate (319.9p × 2 = 639.8p) implies modest full-year growth, but the historical pattern is for H2 to be stronger due to Christmas trading.
  • No share buybacks: GAW does not conduct buybacks. EPS growth is entirely organic, driven by earnings growth on a stable share count of approximately 32 million shares. This is qualitatively important — there is no financial engineering in the EPS line.
  • Capital allocation is shareholder-friendly but via dividends, not buybacks. FY2025 total dividends £520p per share (£171.4m total), representing approximately 85% of FY2025 EPS. H1 FY2026 interim dividend 225p (+21.6%). This payout ratio is extremely high for a growing company and signals management confidence in earnings durability.

  • GAW's balance sheet carries no debt. Cash and equivalents were strong at FY2025, and the group is self-funding from operations. With no leverage, there is no interest cost drag and no refinancing risk — a structurally cleaner EPS profile than peers carrying meaningful debt loads (Hasbro, Funko).
  • Sceptical interpretation: is GAW's H1 FY2026 EPS of 319.9p the peak of the current cycle? Without a step-change in licensing (Amazon deal still development stage), the FY2026 full-year EPS may not materially exceed FY2025's implied c.600p, creating a year of consolidation rather than growth. Consensus forward-year EPS estimates suggest analysts are pricing in low-teens compound growth from here, which requires the core business to continue compounding at 12-15% annually and licensing to recover (via Amazon). Either could disappoint.

5. GUIDANCE & FORECAST CHANGES

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN

  • GAW does not provide explicit forward revenue or earnings guidance. This is a longstanding management policy. The company issues trading statements (pre-close updates) that indicate whether results will be "not less than" specified figures, and the H1 FY2026 report's mention of "best December" is the closest thing to near-term guidance.
  • The November 2025 trading update (released November 20, 2025) stated core revenue for the 6 months to November 30, 2025 would be "not less than £310 million." The actual outcome of £316.1m beat this floor by £6.1m (c.2%). This is a consistent GAW pattern: state conservative floors, deliver above.
  • This sandbagging pattern is intentional. Management sets floors, not estimates. The market has learned this: on release of the H1 FY2026 half-year report (January 13, 2026), the share price surged +11.8% — an unusually large move for a routine semi-annual result.
  • For FY2027 (the year containing Warhammer 40K 11th edition launch): analyst consensus is not yet crystallised but the release cadence — new launch box, updated rulebooks, new army lines, new terrain — historically produces a revenue step-change. The FY2009 (5th edition), FY2017 (8th edition), and FY2021 (9th edition) launch years all showed 10-15% core revenue acceleration above trend.
  • Hasbro's FY2026 guidance: total revenue +3-5% in constant currency, adjusted operating margin 24-25%, adjusted EBITDA $1.40-1.45bn. This is unambitious and reflects the constraint that Magic: The Gathering, their only growth engine, has guided to mid-single-digit growth.
  • Funko's FY2026 guidance: net sales flat to +3%, gross margin 41-43%, adjusted EBITDA $70-80m. This is a stabilisation story, not a recovery.
  • Mattel has withdrawn formal EPS guidance for 2026 due to tariff uncertainty, providing only a revenue outlook. This is unusual and signals elevated visibility risk in the business.
  • The key uncertainty for GAW's FY2026 consensus is the pace of H2 licensing recovery. The Amazon deal will not generate royalty revenue in FY2026 (production has not started). The licensing line for H2 FY2026 will depend on other existing gaming licenses and whether any new game releases generate meaningful royalties.
  • Sceptical interpretation: GAW's refusal to give explicit guidance creates a persistent information asymmetry. The "not less than" system incentivises analysts to anchor conservatively and re-rate positively on delivery. This is great for short-term price dynamics but means consensus EPS forecasts for FY2027 and beyond are likely based on extrapolation rather than specific management signals.

7. SECTOR & VALUE CHAIN CONTEXT

Rating: GREEN (for GAW specifically), YELLOW (for broader branded toy sector)

  • The tabletop gaming market is in a structural growth phase. Industry estimates place the market at approximately $32.7bn in 2025, growing at approximately 10% CAGR through the decade. The driver is the emergence of tabletop gaming as an adult hobby rather than a children's activity — 85% of players are aged 18-54, and average spend per engaged hobbyist is rising.
  • GAW's specific segment — premium miniatures wargaming — is the highest-engagement, highest-spend-per-participant segment in tabletop. A core Warhammer 40K player spends hundreds to thousands of pounds building, painting, and gaming annually. This is not an impulse purchase category. GAW's products create long-duration customer relationships that are largely recession-resistant at the engagement level.
  • The broader branded toy sector faces secular headwinds. The traditional toy market (Hasbro Consumer Products, Mattel) is subject to: (a) US tariff disruption (heavy China sourcing); (b) ongoing digital substitution for children's entertainment; (c) Barbie-cycle deceleration; (d) retail destocking post-COVID. None of these headwinds apply to GAW's core business.
  • Warhammer 40K's media ecosystem is a legitimate long-term growth catalyst. The Space Marine 2 video game (7m+ copies sold) demonstrated the IP's commercial potency beyond the hobby. The Amazon MGM deal — once in production — would expose the Warhammer universe to a mass audience for the first time, driving new entrants to the hobby.
  • Competitive intensity in miniatures wargaming remains moderate but is increasing. Mantic Games, Corvus Belli (Infinity), Para Bellum (Conquest), and Privateer Press (Warmachine) all compete in segments adjacent to Warhammer. None has the retail infrastructure, IP depth, or release cadence of GAW.
  • Cyclical vs structural: GAW's growth is primarily structural (IP leverage, hobby participation growth, media ecosystem development) with a cyclical overlay (consumer spending, FX). The Space Marine 2 licensing peak and trough are cyclical overlays on an otherwise structural story.
  • Sceptical interpretation: Is GAW's outperformance partly an artefact of the specific 2024-2026 window — Space Marine 2 launch boost, Amazon deal announcement excitement, a product release cadence that cannot be replicated every half? The 11th edition launch in Summer 2026 is the next test of whether strong core growth continues in steady-state or requires periodic catalyst injections.

8. QUALITY OF EARNINGS & ACCOUNTING

Rating: GREEN

  • Cash from operations £169.4m in H1 FY2026 (+27.8%) against operating profit of £140.4m. This represents an operating cash conversion rate of approximately 120%, meaning the business is generating more cash than it is reporting in profit — the opposite of the typical concern about earnings quality.
  • Working capital is a structural source of cash for GAW. The business model involves: (a) customers paying at point of sale (retail) or on short credit terms (trade), creating minimal receivables; (b) product manufactured to order with relatively short production cycles, limiting work-in-progress; (c) moderate finished goods inventory managed via new release timing.
  • Inventory management is improving. FY2025 inventory provisions as a percentage of gross stock fell from 17.7% to 11.9%. Inventory rose to £39.9m in FY2025 — manageable at c.6% of revenue and reflecting the growth in the product range and store network. There is no sign of inventory build ahead of demand.
  • Non-GAAP adjustments are minimal. GAW presents "Core" results that exclude licensing, allowing investors to see the underlying business. Unlike Hasbro or Mattel, there are no restructuring charges, goodwill impairments, or "adjusted" EBITDA figures that exclude clearly recurring items. The accounts are clean.
  • Dividends declared of 225p in H1 FY2026 (+21.6%) equate to c.70.4p per share per month — representing a very large proportion of earnings returned to shareholders. This is cash going out of the business, confirming earnings are real and accessible, not trapped in working capital or goodwill.
  • Comparison to Hasbro on earnings quality: Hasbro reported a GAAP net loss of $2.30/share in FY2025 versus adjusted EPS of $5.54. The $7.84/share gap represents goodwill impairments ($1.4bn) related to the Entertainment One acquisition and other restructuring. These are large and arguably recurring-in-spirit charges that adjusted EPS systematically excludes.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The 85%+ dividend payout ratio creates a constraint. If GAW faced a sudden revenue deceleration (e.g., a failed 11th edition launch, or a recession hitting discretionary hobbyist spending), maintaining the dividend would require drawing on cash reserves. The dividend policy signals confidence but also limits financial flexibility.

9. SCEPTICAL SUMMARY

Overall thesis confidence: YELLOW-GREEN (positive but not uncritical)

  • The bear case for GAW is not about the business — it is about the price. At c.30x trailing PE, the stock is pricing in a sustained double-digit growth trajectory and continued margin expansion. If core revenue growth normalises to 8-10% (plausible in steady-state), operating margins remain flat at 38-40%, and licensing contributes less than expected from the Amazon deal, the earnings compound is probably 8-10% per annum — insufficient to justify a 30x multiple.
  • The licensing base effect is resolved — but the next licensing cycle is binary. The Space Marine 2 royalty peak (-£14.1m in H1 FY2026) is in the past. The next licensing surge requires the Amazon Warhammer series to (a) complete production, (b) launch successfully, (c) generate meaningful royalties. Any of these steps could fail.
  • Warhammer 40K 11th edition is the next major known catalyst but its execution risk is underappreciated. GAW has executed edition transitions well historically but a misstep — overly complex rules, poor launch box value, alienating the existing player base — could delay the customary launch-year sales surge.
  • Tariffs are a recurring, not one-off, headwind. The £12m estimated annual tariff cost (£6m in H1 FY2026) is manageable now. If US tariff rates increase further or expand to cover UK goods, the exposure could increase materially.
  • Operating margin approaching a structural ceiling? At 39.9% core operating margin, GAW is already among the highest-margin consumer goods companies in Europe. The mathematics of further expansion require either continued revenue scaling or reduction in SG&A as a percentage of revenue.
  • Early warning signals to monitor: (1) Core revenue growth decelerating below 10% in a non-transition half; (2) Trade channel growth turning negative, signalling retailer destocking; (3) Online channel sustained weakness; (4) Licensing revenues below £30m for a full year; (5) Price-volume divergence — price increases not holding volume.
  • The single largest risk to the near-term thesis is multiple compression, not earnings miss. At 30x PE, a rotation from quality growth stocks to value would reprice GAW sharply even if the underlying business continues performing.
  • Overall sceptical conclusion: The business is exceptional. The price is demanding. The near-term risks are visible and manageable. The medium-term risks (11th edition execution, Amazon development timeline, multiple compression) are real but are not earnings-threatening in themselves.
