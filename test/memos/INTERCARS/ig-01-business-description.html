Company Overview & Business Model

Core Business and Market Positioning

Inter Cars SA is the largest independent European distributor of spare parts for vehicles, ranking first in Central and Eastern Europe, second in Europe overall, and sixth globally. The company operates primarily in the Independent Aftermarket (IAM) segment, supplying automotive repair garages through a business-to-business (B2B) distribution model. To support this massive operation, Inter Cars distributes an extensive portfolio of over 5.97 million products. The core offering includes spare parts for passenger cars (PKW) and commercial vehicles (LKW), alongside specialized segments such as body parts, tires, batteries, oils, and garage equipment. Additional revenue streams are generated through specialized niche markets, including off-highway machinery, marine products, tuning accessories, and industrial components.

The 50/50 Branch Franchise Model

A defining competitive advantage for Inter Cars is its unique 50/50 branch franchise model. Unlike traditional distributors that directly own and operate all of their localized locations, Inter Cars partners with independent entrepreneurs to manage its distribution branches. Under this arrangement, Inter Cars provides the inventory, centralized logistics infrastructure, advanced IT systems, and its established brand trademark. In return, the franchise partner is responsible for local market execution, hiring local employees, and securing the physical warehouse and office space.

The goods housed in these regional branches remain on Inter Cars' balance sheet until they are officially sold to the end customer. The resulting sales margin generated by the branch is then split evenly in a 50/50 ratio between Inter Cars and the franchise operator. This decentralized structure highly incentivizes local managers to maximize sales volumes and efficiently manage their local operations. Furthermore, this model allows Inter Cars to rapidly scale its geographical footprint across new European markets with significantly lower initial capital expenditures compared to wholly-owned branch expansions.

Key Subsidiaries and Corporate Structure

Beyond its core distribution network, Inter Cars operates a diversified corporate structure with specialized subsidiaries that support its broader mobility ecosystem.

ILS Sp. z o.o. serves as the group's dedicated logistics entity, managing the complex flow of goods from global manufacturers to the vast network of distribution branches.

Motointegrator operates as an innovative s-commerce (sales with service) platform that connects retail drivers and fleet managers with professional garages recommended by Inter Cars.

Feber Sp. z o.o. broadens the company's industrial footprint by manufacturing commercial trailers and semi-trailers, while also offering post-collision repairs and car rental services.

Lauber Sp. z o.o. specializes in the remanufacturing of automotive parts, which allows customers to access high-quality components while simultaneously supporting circular economy initiatives.

Competitive Advantages and Strategic Direction

Inter Cars achieves superior market agility by organizing its massive operational scale into smaller, highly specialized business segments. The company functions as a comprehensive "one-stop shop" for independent garages, providing everything from basic replacement parts to advanced diagnostic equipment and technical training. Strategically, management strongly favors aggressive organic growth and the expansion of its franchise network over mergers and acquisitions, deliberately avoiding the complex integration risks that frequently burden industry peers. This focused strategy has allowed Inter Cars to continuously consolidate market share and consistently outpace the sales growth dynamics of rival European distributors.

Financial Performance & Metrics

Top-Line Revenue Growth

Inter Cars SA has demonstrated consistent top-line expansion despite varying macroeconomic conditions across its European markets. For the full year 2024, the company generated preliminary consolidated revenues of PLN 19.47 billion, representing an 8% year-over-year increase. This growth trajectory continued into 2025, with first-half sales reaching PLN 10.11 billion, driven largely by a 14% growth rate in foreign subsidiary sales. By the end of the first three quarters of 2025, cumulative sales revenue reached PLN 15.52 billion, marking an 8.4% increase compared to the same period in the prior year. Notably, the third quarter of 2025 alone saw an accelerated sales dynamic of 10.1% year-over-year. Recent monthly data confirms sustained momentum into 2026, with cumulative revenue for January through March 2026 reaching PLN 5.31 billion, reflecting a 12.0% year-over-year increase.

Millennium Dom Maklerski highlights that the robust performance in early 2026 was largely fueled by foreign operations, which saw a remarkable 25.4% year-over-year sales surge in March alone.

Profitability and Margin Dynamics

The company's profitability has been heavily influenced by foreign exchange fluctuations and shifting purchase prices. In the first three quarters of 2025, the consolidated gross margin on goods sold stood at 29.3%, slightly down from 29.5% in the prior year. Management has emphasized that the strengthening of the Polish Zloty (PLN) against the Euro creates natural downward pressure on prices, as the cost of goods purchased in foreign currency falls, forcing corresponding reductions in final sales prices. After eliminating the impact of these exchange rate differences, the core gross margin for the third quarter of 2025 stabilized at 29.9%, identical to the previous year and signaling a halt in the previous trend of margin degradation.

At the operating level, the consolidated operating profit for the first nine months of 2025 reached PLN 866.1 million, an increase of PLN 65.7 million over the previous year. Furthermore, the trailing twelve-month consolidated EBITDA for the period ending September 30, 2025, amounted to PLN 1.37 billion, representing a significant year-over-year improvement of PLN 193.9 million. The company ultimately achieved a net profit of PLN 582.8 million for the first nine months of 2025, up 15% year-over-year despite absorbing extraordinary impairment losses.

Working Capital and Inventory Turnover

To maintain its competitive advantage in parts availability amid supply chain uncertainties and price increases, Inter Cars has deliberately maintained elevated stock levels. Total inventory value reached PLN 5.80 billion at the end of September 2025, reflecting a 12% increase compared to the same point in 2024. This strategic stockpiling ensures the reliable supply of goods from both European and Asian manufacturers across the company's expanding logistics network, including the newly scaled warehouses in Zakroczym and Brasov. Despite the absolute increase in stock value, the inventory turnover ratio has remained highly stable, standing at 137 days at the end of the third quarter of 2025, identical to the turnover ratio recorded in the same period of 2024.

Capital Structure, Debt, and Cash Flows

Inter Cars primarily finances its extensive operational and investment activities through internally generated funds and a robust syndicated credit framework. The company utilizes a multi-bank syndicated credit facility, which provides a balanced structure of roughly 60% short-term and 40% long-term financing. This debt is heavily secured by registered pledges over the group's inventories, which were valued at over PLN 3.4 billion for collateral purposes by mid-2025.

The aggressive expansion of logistics infrastructure and intentional working capital build-up have modestly increased the group's leverage. The net debt-to-EBITDA ratio rose to 2.2 by the end of September 2025, up from 1.9 at the same point in 2024. Operating cash flows remained strong, with net cash from operating activities reaching PLN 546.7 million for the first nine months of 2025, allowing the company to comfortably cover interest expenses and fund necessary capital expenditures.

One-Off Impacts: Ukraine Warehouse Impairments

The ongoing geopolitical conflict in Ukraine has resulted in material, non-recurring financial impacts on the group's balance sheet. Following the initial Russian invasion in 2022, Inter Cars was forced to write down PLN 75 million due to the complete destruction of its central warehouse in Kyiv. More recently, in September 2025, the company suffered another major loss when its warehouse facility in Dnipro was destroyed by fire during hostilities. Consequently, the third quarter 2025 financial results include a direct revaluation write-down of PLN 20 million to account for the destroyed inventory and fixed assets at the Dnipro site. Despite these setbacks, the broader Inter Cars Ukraine subsidiary continues to operate profitably, supported by direct deliveries from the central hub in Poland.

Operational Strategy & Geographic Expansion

Geographic Footprint and Export Dynamics

Inter Cars SA has strategically shifted its center of gravity away from its native Polish market to capture higher-growth opportunities across Central, Eastern, and Southern Europe. By the end of September 2025, the company's distribution network had expanded to 680 branches, driven by the opening of 19 new locations abroad during the first nine months of the year. Consequently, domestic sales in Poland have steadily diluted as a percentage of total group revenue, falling to approximately 38% in the third quarter of 2025. This structural shift highlights the rapid growth of foreign distribution companies, which recorded a 15% year-over-year sales increase over the same period. The most dynamic geographic markets include Southern European and Balkan nations such as Serbia, Greece, Bosnia, and Slovenia. Management deliberately targets these regions utilizing its decentralized, distribution-contract model that leverages local entrepreneurs to capture market share rapidly.

Logistics Network and Supply Chain Resilience

To support its vast geographic sprawl, the group relies on its specialized logistics subsidiary, ILS Sp. z o.o., which operates a sophisticated network comprising four main logistics centers in Poland and 22 regional warehouses across Europe. Supply chain resilience is a core operational pillar, anchored by an unparalleled inventory of over 660,000 distinct stock-keeping units (SKUs). The company employs a strict multi-supplier strategy for its product groups, ensuring that if a preferred manufacturer experiences disruptions, alternative components of similar quality remain immediately available. This intentional diversification mitigates geopolitical and macroeconomic supply shocks, allowing the company to guarantee continuous parts availability and execute multiple daily deliveries to local garages.

Major CAPEX: Warehouse Automation and Skypod Integration

Inter Cars is currently executing a massive capital expenditure program aimed at robotizing its primary distribution hubs to manage rising labor costs, increase picking efficiency, and enhance overall warehouse throughput. The centerpiece of this initiative is the expansion of the European Logistics and Development Centre in Zakroczym, Poland, which entails a PLN 235 million investment to implement the advanced Skypod automated storage and retrieval system. The Skypod system utilizes autonomous robots navigating flexible, 11-meter-high racks, allowing simultaneous processing of multiple orders at a single station. The Zakroczym modernization phase is scheduled for final completion in the first quarter of 2026.

Concurrently, the group's ILS Balkan subsidiary has successfully launched a fully automated, PLN 250 million warehouse facility in Brasov, Romania. This Romanian hub mirrors the Skypod technology used in Poland and serves as a critical regional logistics node to support the surging order demand across Southern Europe and the Balkans. Management views these automated hubs as essential infrastructure to maintain the company's competitive advantage in last-mile delivery speed.

E-Commerce Rollout and Western European Expansion

Beyond its traditional brick-and-mortar footprint, Inter Cars is aggressively scaling its digital and e-commerce capabilities, particularly in Western Europe where it traditionally lacks a dense physical branch network. Through its German subsidiary, Cleverlog-Autoteile GmbH, the company is rolling out the Motointegrator platform to capture both online retail and B2B sales. Motointegrator acts as a comprehensive s-commerce (sales with service) platform, currently operating dedicated domains in markets such as Germany, France, Austria, Spain, and Italy. This digital-first strategy enables the group to penetrate highly consolidated Western markets with lower initial capital intensity, while systematically building brand awareness and customer loyalty among independent garages and retail drivers.

ESG & Corporate Governance

Sustainability Strategy and Climate Goals

In September 2023, the Management Board approved a comprehensive 2023-2030 Sustainability Strategy operating under the motto "Sustainable automotive for people and climate." The strategy is structured around five core pillars that cover the entire value chain, from spare parts manufacturers to end-customer garages. A central component of this framework is the group's decarbonization plan, which includes targets verified by the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi). Specifically, Inter Cars has committed to reducing absolute Scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions by 42% by 2030, compared to a 2021 baseline. For Scope 3 emissions, the company aims to ensure that 73% of its suppliers establish their own science-based decarbonization targets by 2028. Furthermore, these environmental commitments are integrated into the company's financing structure through a sustainability-linked loan (SLL). This syndicated credit facility includes margin adjustments based on achieving specific KPIs, such as carbon footprint reduction and increasing female participation in the Inter Cars Foundation's scholarship programs.

Circular Economy and Waste Management

Inter Cars actively promotes circular economy principles through dedicated waste management programs designed for its independent garage network. The BIO Service initiative provides comprehensive, legally compliant waste collection directly from partner garages. In 2024, the program serviced over 1,145 garages in Poland, collecting substantial volumes of hazardous and non-hazardous materials. Through this network, the company successfully recovered and recycled over 1.64 million kilograms of batteries and approximately 836,000 tires. To manage Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) obligations, the group holds a 33% stake in Partslife International Kft. The Polish subsidiary, Partslife Polska OOO S.A., acts as a packaging recovery organization that ensures the group meets statutory recycling levels for packaging, oils, and lubricants. Additionally, Inter Cars operates a dedicated Remanufacturing and Circular Economy Department to expand its portfolio of refurbished automotive parts.

Corporate Governance and Internal Audit

The corporate governance framework at Inter Cars is overseen by a Supervisory Board consisting of five members, two of whom meet the criteria for independence. The Management Board, which acts as the executive body, is currently composed of five male members appointed for a joint four-year term. To ensure operational integrity, the company formalized its internal audit function in 2018 to operate in accordance with international professional standards. This audit unit operates alongside a distributed internal control and risk management system maintained primarily by the finance and operations divisions. In 2024, Inter Cars initiated a formal process to further separate and strengthen the risk management unit within its corporate structure.

Code of Conduct and Whistleblower Policies

Ethical business practices are governed by the Code of Conduct and Good Market Practice, which strictly prohibits corruption, bribery, and anti-competitive behavior. In 2024, the Management Board further strengthened this framework by adopting the Due Diligence Policy for Responsible Business Conduct. This policy aligns with international standards such as the UN Guiding Principles and OECD Guidelines to identify and minimize human rights risks across the supply chain. To enforce these standards, Inter Cars maintains a robust internal reporting procedure that protects whistleblowers from retaliation. Employees and external partners can report abuses anonymously through an online platform or a dedicated compliance email address. All reports are managed by the General Counsel, and the effectiveness of these anti-abuse measures is reviewed annually by the Supervisory Board.

Management Board Remuneration

The remuneration policy for the Management Board is designed to align executive compensation with the group's long-term financial stability. Executive pay consists of a fixed base salary and a variable cash bonus granted under a dedicated Incentive Programme. This variable component utilizes a degressive model where the bonus pool is calculated as a specific percentage of the consolidated net profit generated by the Inter Cars Group. Variable remuneration is capped and cannot exceed 85% of the total compensation received by a board member in a given year. Currently, the company does not tie executive bonuses or share-based rights to non-financial or ESG-related performance metrics. However, management continues to annually review operational sustainability risks to optimize performance in these critical areas independently of direct financial incentives.
