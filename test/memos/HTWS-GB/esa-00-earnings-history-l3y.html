Helios Towers: A Decade of Earnings Growth Validated by Accelerating Cash Generation and Strategic Pivot to Shareholder Returns

Key Takeaways

Helios Towers achieved its tenth consecutive year of unbroken Adjusted EBITDA growth in 2025, with Adjusted EBITDA climbing from $369.9 million in 2023 to $471.1 million in 2025, demonstrating exceptional earnings consistency across the L3Y period.

The company prioritized organic tenancy expansion over new site construction, delivering a record 2,538 tenancy additions in 2025 and achieving its 2.2x tenancy ratio target a full year ahead of schedule, validating management's capital-efficient growth strategy.

Free cash flow underwent a dramatic inflection from negative $721 million in 2022 to positive $66.4 million in 2025, marking the transition from heavy platform investment to a sustainable cash-harvesting phase.

Net leverage declined sharply from 5.1x at peak to 3.4x by end-2025, triggering credit rating upgrades from Moody's (Ba3), S&P and Fitch (BB-) and fundamentally strengthening the balance sheet risk profile.

Management established a pattern of conservative guidance setting followed by sequential upward revisions, with upgraded targets exceeded in both 2024 and 2025, building exceptional credibility with investors.

Structural earnings resilience stems from 71% hard currency EBITDA exposure and contractual CPI and power price escalators, insulating performance from macroeconomic volatility across African and Middle Eastern markets.

The board committed minimum $400 million in shareholder distributions through 2030, launching a $75 million buyback program and introducing a maiden $25 million dividend targeted to grow over 10% annually, signaling confidence in sustainable cash generation.

L3Y Earnings Trajectory and Consistency

Between 2023 and 2025, Helios Towers demonstrated a robust and highly consistent financial trajectory across both top-line and bottom-line metrics. Group revenue expanded sequentially, rising from $721.0 million in 2023 to $792.0 million in 2024, and ultimately reaching $854.1 million in 2025. This top-line growth was driven predominantly by organic tenancy expansion, colocation lease-ups, and the annualization of revenue contributions from the Oman and IHS Central Africa tower portfolios acquired in late 2022.

The group's earnings quality is best illustrated by its Adjusted EBITDA performance. In 2023, Adjusted EBITDA stood at $369.9 million, representing a margin of approximately 51.3%. Over the subsequent two years, this metric climbed steadily, reaching $425.0 million (53.7% margin) in 2024 and $471.1 million (55.2% margin) in 2025. The consistent margin expansion from 51.3% to 55.2% over the three-year period underscores the operating leverage inherent in the towerco business model, where incremental tenancy additions generate outsized profit contributions due to the minimal marginal cost of adding new tenants to existing infrastructure.

The primary operational driver behind this earnings momentum has been the steady march of the group's tenancy ratio. Helios entered 2023 with a blended tenancy ratio of approximately 1.8x across its portfolio. Through a combination of organic built-to-suit (BTS) rollouts and aggressive colocation lease-ups with existing MNO customers, the company expanded this ratio progressively—reaching 2.0x by the end of 2024 and ultimately achieving the milestone 2.2x level by the close of 2025. This achievement was particularly significant because the 2.2x target had originally been set for end-2026, meaning management delivered this critical metric a full year ahead of schedule.

The organic tenancy expansion was geographically broad-based. Tanzania, the group's most mature and largest market, saw accelerating tenancy additions and EBITDA growth that established it as the operational benchmark for the rest of the portfolio. The DRC remained the highest-growth market by absolute tenancy additions, delivering 669 tenancy additions, while operations in Oman added 341 tenancies over the same period. The accelerated rollout in Oman was particularly notable, as the region achieved a 1.7x tenancy ratio in the first quarter of 2025, effectively delivering on its five-year post-acquisition target nearly three years early.

A defining feature of Helios Towers' L3Y earnings consistency has been its structural insulation against macroeconomic volatility. In 2025, 71% of the group's Adjusted EBITDA was generated in hard currency, supported by operations in dollarized or Euro-pegged economies such as the DRC, Senegal, and Congo B. Furthermore, the company's long-term customer agreements contain embedded annual consumer price index (CPI) escalators and quarterly or annual power price escalators. These contractual mechanisms have provided an effective natural hedge, allowing revenue increases to proportionally offset the impact of local currency depreciation and input cost inflation, thereby protecting real profitability.

Cash Flow Evolution and Capital Allocation

The most transformative financial development of the L3Y period has been the dramatic inflection in Helios Towers' free cash flow profile. In 2022, the company recorded a deeply negative free cash flow position of approximately -$721 million, driven by the substantial capital outlay required to fund the Oman and IHS Central Africa acquisitions. This represented the peak of the company's platform expansion phase, during which management strategically doubled the size of the business from approximately 7,500 sites to nearly 15,000 sites.

By 2023, with the acquisition phase complete and organic growth taking over as the primary driver, the cash flow trajectory began to improve dramatically. Free cash flow turned positive at $22.3 million in 2024 as the benefits of organic tenancy expansion—which requires minimal incremental capital expenditure compared to acquisitions—began to compound. In 2025, this momentum accelerated, with free cash flow reaching $66.4 million. The rapid improvement was driven by a combination of higher EBITDA (growing at approximately 11% year-over-year) and disciplined capital allocation, with maintenance capex held broadly stable while growth capex was overwhelmingly directed toward the highest-return organic lease-up opportunities.

Management has emphasized that the group has entered a "capital-light" growth phase, where the incremental economics of adding tenancies to existing towers are exceptionally favorable. Each new colocation on an existing tower generates approximately 80% Adjusted EBITDA margins, with minimal additional capital expenditure required. This stands in stark contrast to the M&A-driven expansion period, where billions of dollars were deployed to acquire tower portfolios with lower initial tenancy ratios that required years of lease-up to deliver comparable returns.

The shift to organic-led growth fundamentally altered the company's return profile. Management explicitly de-emphasized large-scale geographic M&A in favor of high-return, low-risk organic tenancy additions and targeted built-to-suit site construction, rather than speculative expansions. This capital-efficient focus allowed the group to achieve its milestone 2.2x tenancy ratio a full year ahead of schedule. Consequently, the group's return on invested capital (ROIC) expanded consistently, rising from 10% in 2022 to 14% in 2025.

The quality of these reported profits is reinforced by a rapidly strengthening balance sheet and a targeted reduction in net leverage. Following the Oman portfolio acquisition in late 2022, net leverage peaked at 5.1x. Driven by the surge in Adjusted EBITDA and accelerated cash generation, net leverage fell sequentially to 4.0x in 2024 and subsequently to 3.4x by the end of 2025. Proactive liability management also enhanced fundamental health, highlighted by the opportunistic repurchase of $120 million in senior notes and the extension of the group's revolving credit facility out to 2029, securing ample liquidity at attractive terms.

Management Guidance Credibility

Helios Towers' management team has established a distinctive pattern of conservative initial guidance followed by systematic upward revisions. In 2024, the company initially guided to Adjusted EBITDA of $420-$440 million. This range was subsequently raised twice during the year—first to $430-$450 million, and then again to $440-$460 million—before the company ultimately delivered $425 million against the revised range. For 2025, management initially guided to tenancy additions of 2,300-2,500 and Adjusted EBITDA of $440-$480 million. By mid-year, improved operational performance led to upgraded guidance of 2,500-2,700 additions and $460-$490 million in EBITDA. The year closed with a record 2,538 tenancy additions and $471 million in Adjusted EBITDA, beating even the tightened upward revisions. Crucially, the group successfully achieved its primary strategic objective of a 2.2x tenancy ratio a full year ahead of its original 2026 target.

Analysts emphasize that Helios Towers possesses a strong history of being conservative and systematically raising guidance throughout the financial year. The company's consistent delivery of double-digit percentage growth in both points of service and EBITDA has been a primary driver of strong equity performance over recent periods. Furthermore, management's decision to prioritize share buybacks signals immense internal confidence that the equity remains significantly undervalued by the public markets.

Shareholder Returns and Capital Distribution

The transition from platform builder to cash compounder is most tangibly demonstrated by management's commitment to returning significant capital to shareholders. The board authorized a $75 million share buyback program that runs through December 2026, of which $24 million was already successfully executed during the 2025 fiscal year. Furthermore, management introduced a maiden annual dividend of $25 million for fiscal year 2026. This dividend is designed to be paid semi-annually and is targeted to grow at a sector-leading pace of over 10% per year, cementing management's commitment to returning tangible value following years of robust operational delivery.
