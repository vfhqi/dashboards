1. BRIEF COMPANY DESCRIPTION

Bufab AB (publ) is a Swedish-listed B2B trading company specializing in fastener and C-parts (components and small mechanical parts) distribution. Founded in 1977 and listed on Nasdaq OMX Stockholm since 2014, Bufab operates as a serial acquirer-driven platform business with 30+ acquisitions since IPO (50+ total since inception).

Revenue Model & Geography: Bufab generates revenue through trading and complementary niche services, sourcing primarily from Asian manufacturers (China, Taiwan, Vietnam, Singapore) and distributing to European, North American, and Asia-Pacific customers. End markets span telecommunications, automotive, furniture, offshore/energy, construction, aerospace, defence, railways, and pharmaceutical sectors. Geographic presence spans Sweden, Norway, Finland, Germany, France, Poland, UK, Netherlands, Austria, Italy, China, Taiwan, and Singapore, with material operations in both Europe West and Europe North & East regions. As of Q3 2025, trailing twelve-month revenue stood at ~SEK 7.8bn (approximately USD 786m at contemporary rates).

Business Evolution (2014-2026): The company has evolved from a regional Nordic distributor into a pan-European trading platform. Between 2014-2024, Bufab demonstrated capacity to grow organically despite cyclical headwinds, then pivoted into aggressive M&A mode. Recent strategy (2023-onwards) explicitly focuses on trading as core (divestment of legacy manufacturing units) and acquisition of complementary niche players—particularly in supply chain services and regional consolidation plays (Italy, Germany).

3-Year Outlook: Management is positioning Bufab for margin-accretive growth driven by three vectors: (1) organic recovery in cyclical markets, (2) disciplined add-on M&A building on platform acquisitions (VITAL in Italy, Novia in Germany), and (3) gradual improvement in working capital and purchasing efficiency. 2026 EBITDA margin target is 14% (vs. 12.5% in 2025). The thesis underpins a belief that fastener distribution is consolidating and that Bufab can capture share through local platforms + global sourcing leverage.
