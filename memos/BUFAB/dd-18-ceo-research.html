Objective

This memo captures the most material financial and strategic changes facing Bufab over the next 12–18 months. It is designed for an investor whose conviction lies in change—whether internally driven (strategy, M&A, costs, management) or externally driven (market demand, competition, regulation). The questions prioritise discussions that are likely to move the investment narrative, trading multiple, and share price.

Executive Summary

12 Key Observations:

1. Organic growth inflection: Bufab turned positive at Q1 2025 (+1.6% total growth, -0.1% organic) and Q3 2025 (+2% total, +1.4% organic), ending a 21-month negative organic growth streak from mid-2023. This is material for multiple expansion.

2. Margin improvement is real and consistent: Eight consecutive quarters of gross margin improvement; Q4 2025 gross margin reached 31.9% (full year) and 33.8% (Q4), a 240–280 bp swing since Q4 2023. Operating margin now at or above the 2026 target of 14% EBITDA (achieved 14.2% adjusted EBITA in Q3/Q4 2025).

3. But organic growth rate remains very low: 0.3% full-year 2025 organic growth is essentially flat. Market demand recovery is gradual and uneven by region. Americas struggled (defensive pricing action needed); UK/Ireland was severely weak (-4.1% organic in Q3). Only Asia Pacific (+6.8% organic in Q2, +17.2% in Q1) was genuinely strong.

4. Currency headwinds are meaningful: Q2 2025 saw -4.9% currency impact (roughly half of the -4.8% reported decline). This creates a false compression of reported growth and masks underlying momentum.

5. Two significant acquisitions in close succession: VITAL (Italy, EUR 48m sales, November 2024) and Novia Group (Germany/Switzerland/China, EUR 50m sales, October 2025) add scale but also integration burden. Both acquisitions must beat Bufab's historical mid-single-digit EBIT margins within 2 years to justify the cost of capital.

6. Strategic divestiture of US manufacturing unit (Q2 2025): Sale of Component Solutions Group manufacturing (~SEK 35m sales) signals a deliberate shift away from capital-intensive, lower-margin manufacturing toward pure trading and niche distribution. This may improve ROIC but will slightly depress reported growth.

7. New semiconductor/defense/infrastructure contract wins are real but quantification is opaque: Management highlights significant wins but provides no disclosed detail on revenue contribution, contract duration, or margin profile. These are key to believing the 2026+ growth narrative.

8. Leverage remains controlled but rising: Net debt/EBITDA was 2.5x in Q2 2025, improved to 2.3x in Q3 2025. Post-Novia acquisition, leverage was anticipated to rise to ~2.7x by Q4 2025. This is manageable but leaves limited headroom for further M&A or downturns.

9. CFO transition at a critical juncture: Pär Ihrskog departed in October 2025 (now at Vitrolife). Marcus Söderberg returned in March 2026. A new CFO always creates uncertainty around capex discipline, leverage targets, and whether acquisition appetite will remain disciplined.

10. Regional performance is divergent: Americas improved margins through pricing but faces soft demand and lost the manufacturing unit. Europe North & East organic growth remains near-flat. Europe West (post-VITAL) is unproven. Asia Pacific is the growth engine but from a small base. This regional volatility will drive quarterly surprises.

11. Working capital trends are positive but fragile: Improved leverage metrics suggest NWC is normalising, but tight credit conditions across manufacturing mean customer payment discipline and inventory turnover are critical. Any demand shock could flip this.

12. Long-term thesis hinges on: (a) sustained organic growth re-acceleration from 2026+, (b) margin sustainability near 14% EBITDA through a full-cycle demand environment, and (c) disciplined integration of VITAL and Novia without major mistakes or write-downs. None are guaranteed.

## Synthesis: Investment Narrative and Key Risks

Narrative

Bufab's story is one of margin improvement amid macro weakness, with an emerging optionality play on organic growth re-acceleration and niche segment penetration (semiconductors, defense). The company has demonstrated disciplined cost management and selective M&A, but organic growth has been stubborn. The critical inflection is whether 2026 will see a credible path to 3–5% organic growth (from 0.3% in 2025), driven by new contracts, market share gains, and macro stabilisation. If yes, multiple re-rating is likely. If not, the stock re-rates on margin sustainability and leverage reduction, a slower thesis.

The bear case: Organic growth disappoints (stays <2%), macro remains weak, new contracts underperform, tariff benefits reverse, and the company is forced to cut capex/dividends to maintain leverage. Margin compression ensues.

The bull case: Organic growth accelerates to 3%+ by 2027, VITAL/Novia integrate cleanly and contribute positively, new defense/semiconductor contracts ramp, and the company re-rates on a combination of margin (14%+ EBITDA) + growth (3%+ organic) + de-leveraging (to 2.0x).

Key Risks to Monitor

1. Execution risk on new contracts: If the semiconductor, defense, and infrastructure contracts underperform (customer delays, quality issues, pricing pressure), the growth thesis collapses.

2. Acquisition integration risk: VITAL and Novia integration must be flawless. Any major customer loss, margin compression, or capex overrun would be a negative surprise.

3. Currency and tariff volatility: A strong SEK or falling US tariffs could materially compress margins.

4. Leverage increase: If EBITDA disappoints and leverage rises above 3.0x, shareholder optionality (dividends, buybacks, M&A) will be constrained.

5. Macro weakness: If the global economy enters recession, C-parts/fastener demand could fall sharply, and Bufab's leverage would become a risk.

6. CFO transition: A new CFO always carries governance/execution uncertainty. Monitor Söderberg's first 100 days closely.

7. Regional underperformance: Americas and Europe remain weak. If recovery is slower than expected, the margin/deleveraging benefit will be offset.
