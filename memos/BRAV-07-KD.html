Business Mix & Demand Dynamics

The 50/50 service/installation split provides a defensive floor but leaves Bravida more cyclically exposed than Caverion; structural demand tailwinds from electrification and energy transition partially offset construction cycle weakness.

  • Structural driver: Service segment anchored by framework agreements and repeat-customer contracts, largely insulated from new construction volatility; Installation remains sensitive to the broader construction cycle #J
    • Mix: Service 49% of 2025 net sales; Installation 51%
    • Installation drag: Volumes declined 6% in 2025 on weak Nordic construction market
  • Peer benchmarking: Bravida's 50/50 mix is middle ground, Caverion leads at 66% service, Instalco historically installation-heavy but growing service to 38% by Q4 2025 #A
    • Technical edge: Bravida and Instalco stronger in traditional plumbing; Caverion holds edge in complex building automation and cooling
    • Competitive shift: ASCA merger (Apr 2024) created entity with deep service expertise and broad installation capability, materially altering the landscape
  • Energy transition tailwind: EU Green Deal mandates driving retrofitting, HVAC upgrades, EV charging, solar, and remote monitoring installations across Nordic region #A
    • Structural buffer: Capital deployment into infrastructure, defense, and industrial facilities partially offsetting residential construction weakness
  • Recovery trajectory: Organic growth -5% FY2025, analysts expect structural volume recovery H2 2026 only; Instalco already at +4.9% organic Q4 2025 #A
    • Constraint: Reduced headcount post-restructuring limits near-term volume recovery capacity
    • ASCA advantage: Nine-country geographic footprint absorbs isolated regional weakness; Bravida's four-country exposure more concentrated

Margin Resilience & Project Execution

The "margin over volume" strategy is structurally sound but the 5.9% consolidated margin trails peers: Denmark is the proof-of-concept turnaround, Finland is the drag, and full leverage of procurement scale remains unrealised.

  • Core strategy: "Grandfather principle" requires CEO approval for all contracts >SEK 50m, actively declining low-margin installation work to protect profitability #J
    • Effect: Disciplined bidding acts as structural defence against cyclical downturns; service mix now 50% of revenues
  • Procurement leverage: Purchasing volumes enable 25-30% material discounts vs smaller contractors, but unified procurement IT system only recently being implemented across decentralised branches #A
  • Peer margin gap: 5.9% EBITA vs ASCA 7.9% and Instalco 6.4%, gap attributed to broader legacy portfolio with underperforming branches requiring continuous restructuring #A
    • Expert view: Former Bravida executive attributes Instalco's higher margins to decentralised M&A model acquiring only high-performing boutique firms
    • Morgan Stanley view: Highly resilient network business; lifting underperforming regions and driving operational leverage is the primary margin expansion catalyst
  • Regional snapshot: Denmark 5% EBITA late 2025 (turnaround complete), Norway 7.7% EBITA Q4 2025 (cost cuts), Finland 2.1% EBITA 2025 (miscalculations and write-downs) #I
    • Sweden restructuring: North/Central/South merged into single entity Jan 2026; SEK 20m charge in late 2025, SEK 70-90m further in 2026, targeting SEK 65m annual savings
    • Sweden drag: SEK 100m Northvolt bad debt provision burdened Swedish margins in 2025
  • Margin target: DNB Carnegie views 7% EBITA group target as realistic for 2026 as Nordic construction enters multi-year recovery; requires Finland improvement and Sweden restructuring savings #A

M&A Strategy & Capital Allocation

M&A is a core growth pillar targeting 5-7% inorganic annual growth at attractive multiples, but 2025 was a deceleration year; the conservative balance sheet is the key differentiator versus leveraged peers, supporting both deal capacity and distribution potential.

  • M&A model: Bolt-on acquisitions at 5-6x EV/EBITA pre-synergies, targeting 5-7% annual top-line growth; full integration into "Bravida Way" platform within 3-12 months #I
    • 2025 activity: Only 4 deals completed, adding ~SEK 454m annual sales, significant deceleration vs prior years
    • Cause: Valuation expectation gaps in weak market; management confirms pipeline robust, expects acceleration in 2026
  • Legacy burden: Acquisition portfolio includes mix of high- and low-performing branches; heavier legacy portfolio vs Instalco's best-in-class-only acquisition filter, continuous restructuring required to lift consolidated margins #A
  • Peer comparison: Instalco paused M&A in 2025 (one acquisition + minority stake in Fabri AG for Germany entry), prioritising "execution before expansion"; ASCA focused on integration synergies before re-accelerating inorganic growth #I
  • Cash conversion: Bravida 10-year average 98-99%, but 2025 dipped to 79%, driven by advance payment reversal on major >SEK 1bn contracts cycling through to standard invoicing #A
    • Peer: Instalco delivered 108% cash conversion in 2025 via working capital reversal
    • Long-run target: Bravida targets >100% rolling 12-month cash conversion
  • Balance sheet: Net debt/EBITDA 1.1x vs Instalco 2.8x and ASCA 3.3x, well below 2.5x internal ceiling, providing substantial M&A and distribution capacity #I
    • Dividend: Minimum 50% net profit payout; 2025 dividend SEK 3.80/share (63% payout), 14% CAGR since IPO
    • Upside catalyst: DNB Carnegie: SEK 400m overdue receivables from Norwegian/Danish legal disputes could trigger extra remuneration via buybacks

Geographic Footprint & Valuation

Four-country decentralised branch network acts as a natural geographic hedge; Denmark's turnaround and Norway's resilience directly underpin the valuation premium, while Finland remains the outstanding operational risk.

  • Footprint: 192 branches across Sweden (47% of sales), Denmark (24%), Norway (21%), Finland (8%), top-two market position in all except Finland #I
  • Denmark: Turnaround complete: EBITA margin 6.6% Q4 2025 by systematically shedding loss-making contracts and enforcing project selectivity #J
  • Norway: 7.7% EBITA Q4 2025 via aggressive admin cost reduction and shift toward recurring service projects (now 60% of Norwegian sales) #I
  • Finland risk: 2.1% EBITA 2025, miscalculations and write-downs in specific branches; management enforcing stricter centralised bidding protocols, but no near-term fix #J
  • Competitive landscape: ASCA merger fundamentally altered Nordic competitive dynamics, creating entity with unmatched volume capability; Bravida/Instalco maintain advantages via localised entrepreneurship and specialist technical disciplines #A
  • Valuation: DNB Carnegie applies 13.5x 2026e EV/EBITA (vs Instalco 14.0x); premium justified by cash conversion, service mix, and recovery optionality; current market: EV/EBITDA 8.68x, P/E 15.21x #I
    • Multiple arbitrage: Morgan Stanley: listed premium enables bolt-on acquisitions at lower private market multiples, driving continuous inorganic growth cycle
    • Historical premium: Bravida has historically traded at premium vs European multi-technical peers due to resilient network model and superior cash generation
