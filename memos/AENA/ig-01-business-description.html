Business Model & Revenue Structure

AENA operates a four-segment model (Airports, Real Estate, AIRM concession, International) with regulated aeronautical fees balanced by higher-margin commercial and international revenues.

  • Revenue model: Four segments: Airports (aero + commercial), Real Estate, AIRM concession, International shareholdings I
    • Aeronautical: €3.35bn (2025), derived from regulated charges to airlines/passengers for airfield, terminal, security
    • Commercial: €1.98bn (2025), +11% YoY, driven by duty-free, F&B, mobility, VIP services
    • Real Estate: €131.9m (2025), +10.5% from cargo logistics and Airport Cities developments
    • International: €913.7m (2025) from 32 airports under concession models
  • Traffic: Post-pandemic traffic at all-time high: 384.8m pax globally in 2025, +4.2% YoY I
    • Spanish network: 321.6m pax, driven by +5.9% international surge offsetting -0.3% domestic dip
    • International mix shift positive: point-to-point international commands higher charges
    • 2026 guidance: +1.3% Spanish network growth (stabilisation phase)
  • Commercial intensity: Commercial revenue per pax growing faster than traffic: €6.19/pax in 2025, +8.2% YoY A
    • VIP services: Q1 2026 revenue +31%, exceptional demand elasticity regardless of pricing
    • MAG dispute resolution: +€28.4m positive one-off in Q1 2026 from pandemic-era settlements
    • Retail expansion: duty-free renovation, new F&B brands, premium mobility (parking, car rental)

Regulatory Framework (DORA)

The DORA regulatory cycle dictates AENA's investment obligations, tariff paths, and service quality targets over 5-year periods. DORA III (2027-31) represents an unprecedented €12.9bn capex commitment facing severe political and airline pushback.

  • Current regime: DORA II (2022-26): tariff freeze through 2025, then +6.31% increase approved for 2026 (IMAAJ → €11.02/pax) I
    • €2.25bn regulated investment baseline + €351m additional (July 2025) for urgent capacity/cybersecurity
    • 17 service quality indicators tied to tariff bonuses/penalties
  • Future regime: DORA III (2027-31): €12.88bn total capex, €9.99bn regulated, targeting 1.69bn cumulative passengers I
    • Regulated OPEX scaling €1.98bn (2027) → €2.31bn (2031), 36% from maintenance/security by cycle end
    • Proposed €0.43/pax average annual tariff increase to fund 9.0% WACC target
    • Quality mandate expanded: 17 → 26 compliance indicators
    • Board approved Feb 2026, now in stakeholder consultation
  • Stakeholder risk: ❌ Severe multi-front resistance: airlines, low-cost carriers, and political parties all opposing tariff increases J
    • ALA + carriers demand 4.9% annual tariff REDUCTION (propose 6.35% WACC vs AENA's 9.0%)
    • Ryanair retaliation: withdrew 1.2m seats from regional Spanish airports
    • Popular Party Senate amendment: statutory tariff freeze 2027-31
    • Management warning: freeze = suspension of €9.99bn regulated investment program
