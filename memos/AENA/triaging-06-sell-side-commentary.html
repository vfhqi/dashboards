Commercial Revenue and International Expansion Strategy

Sell-Side Coverage Gaps and Commercial Revenue Overview

While the research parameters targeted sell-side analyst commentary on Aena's commercial and international strategies, broker research covering these specific initiatives was not surfaced in the available data. Detailed operational and financial disclosures provided by the company, however, offer a comprehensive view of Aena's commercial revenue growth and international asset performance. Overall commercial and real estate revenue—encompassing fixed and variable rents as well as Minimum Annual Guaranteed (MAG) rents—increased by 7.9% year-over-year in the first quarter of 2026. Total commercial sales per passenger rose 4.6% during the same period, reaching €7.40. Looking forward, management has set long-term objectives to increase commercial revenue per passenger by at least 32% by 2026 compared to 2019 levels. The company also targets a 48% expansion in total commercial revenue by 2026 relative to the 2019 baseline.

MAG Contract Renewals and Retail Concessions

Aena has experienced substantial growth in its retail contract tenders, achieving significant upward revisions in Minimum Annual Guaranteed rents. For food and beverage operations, MAG rents from contracts awarded between July 2025 and March 2026 reflect a 33% increase for 2026 and a 39% increase for 2027 compared to prior 2025 levels. These updates were driven by the publication of 12 tenders covering 37 premises at key locations such as Málaga-Costa del Sol and Gran Canaria airports. Specialty shop tenders demonstrated even steeper rent recoveries, with contracts awarded in the second half of 2025 and the first quarter of 2026 generating MAG increases of 45% for 2026 and 66% for 2027.

Additionally, Aena has actively negotiated with commercial operators to resolve historical discounts applied to MAG contracts during pandemic-era traffic downturns. Agreements finalized after the close of the first quarter of 2026 are expected to yield a positive income statement impact of €28.4 million. The ongoing renovation of retail spaces with new brands and improved formats continues to drive commercial activity growth above underlying passenger traffic growth.

VIP Services and Adjacent Business Diversification

VIP and premium services represent one of the fastest-growing segments within Aena's commercial portfolio. Revenue from VIP services expanded by 31% year-over-year to €55 million in the first quarter of 2026. This performance was heavily driven by strong demand for VIP lounges, which alone saw a 25% revenue increase to €45 million. The upward trajectory in VIP revenue reflects both an improved penetration rate among travelers and successful price adjustments. Beyond terminal operations, Aena is pursuing diversification into adjacent business lines, primarily through its Airport Cities real estate project. Digital service expansion and enhanced customer loyalty programs are also core pillars of the company's strategy to capture value throughout the passenger journey.

International Expansion: London Luton and Brazil

Aena's international strategy relies on consolidating its major investments in the United Kingdom and Brazil, which contributed to the broader group's 81.3 million total passengers in Q1 2026. The regulatory environment at London Luton Airport has significantly improved, with maximum regulatory capacity being increased by one million to 19 million passengers in April 2024. A critical long-term growth catalyst was secured on April 3, 2025, when the UK Secretary of State for Transport approved a Development Consent Order to expand Luton's capacity to 32 million passengers. Aena manages Luton through a concession agreement that was originally set to expire in 2031, but the contract was extended to September 4, 2032, to compensate for the severe traffic disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

In Latin America, Aena's focus remains on integrating its recently expanded footprint in Brazil. Following the assumption of control over the Block of Eleven Airports in Brazil (BOAB), the company is executing mandatory infrastructure works according to the committed dates outlined in the Concession Agreement. This systematic integration aims to standardize operations across the newly acquired network and capture long-term traffic growth in the Brazilian market.

Capital Allocation, Dividends, and Valuation

Sell-Side Coverage Gaps and Baseline Remuneration Policy

Extensive broker research covering Aena's specific valuation models, target price adjustments, and detailed ESG considerations was not surfaced in the current survey data. However, baseline company disclosures confirm the primary elements of Aena's capital allocation strategy, most notably its shareholder remuneration policy. The company maintains a strict dividend payout ratio of 80% based on its annual individual net profit. This high distribution rate positions Aena as one of the most attractive entities within the broader airport sector regarding dividend policy.

Historical Distributions and Financial Positioning

Aena's historical capital returns demonstrate the scale of this payout commitment. Between 2015 and 2023, the company distributed a total of €4,857 million in dividends to its shareholders. Due to the company's ownership structure, the Spanish state—acting as the majority shareholder—received 51% of these distributions, which amounted to €2,477.1 million over that period.

Regarding debt and balance sheet stability, credit rating agencies have formally endorsed Aena's financial strength and creditworthiness. Moody's currently assigns the airport operator an A3 rating alongside a positive outlook, underscoring the company's resilient credit quality and capacity to sustain its robust capital allocation framework.
