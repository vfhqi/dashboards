Comet Holding AG is navigating a pivotal transformation as it shifts from a diversified industrial component

manufacturer into a focused semiconductor technology leader, anchored by its next-generation Synertia RF

platform and a strategic manufacturing pivot to Malaysia. While FY2025 financial performance was hindered by

severe Swiss Franc (CHF) headwinds and a sluggish industrial X-ray market, the company enters FY2026 with a

robust 1.4 book-to-bill ratio and a clarified "Strategy 2030" designed to capture accelerating AI-driven wafer

fabrication equipment (WFE) demand. [1] [2] [3] 1 • Earnings Presentation 2 • Company Publication

Brief Company Description

Comet Holding AG is a leading global technology company specializing in Plasma Control Technologies (PCT)

and X-ray Systems (IXS/IXM) . [16] Over the last three years, the company has pivoted its business model to

become a critical sub-system provider for the semiconductor value chain, with approximately 70-75% of revenue

now tied to this sector. [17] [18] 16 • PROXY 17 • Investor Presentation 18 • ARS

The PCT division provides RF power delivery components—including vacuum capacitors, matching networks, and

RF generators—essential for deposition and etching processes in wafer fabrication. [19] The IXS and IXM divisions

provide X-ray systems and modules used for non-destructive testing and inspection in back-end advanced

packaging, as well as in the automotive and aerospace industries. [20] Plausibly for the next three years, Comet

will prioritize its role as a "One-Stop Solution" for RF subsystems, integrating its previously standalone

BRIEF COMPANY DESCRIPTION

COMET Holding AG is a Swiss precision technology group, listed on SIX Swiss Exchange (COTN-CH), with approximately CHF 457m revenue in FY2025. It operates two divisions: Electronic Power Control (EPC) and Industrial X-Ray Systems (IXS). Both divisions share a common engineering heritage in high-precision, high-frequency electromagnetic systems, but serve materially different end-markets and exhibit very different financial characteristics.

EPC is the dominant division. It designs and manufactures radio-frequency (RF) and microwave power components and subsystems — principally power generators, impedance matching networks, and the proprietary Synertia plasma control platform. EPC's primary end-market is wafer fab equipment (WFE): semiconductor capital equipment manufacturers (OEMs) such as Applied Materials, Lam Research, and TEL embed COMET components in their etch, deposition, and CVD tools. PCT (plasma control technologies) is the core product line within EPC. This division is highly cyclical — it tracks WFE capex, which itself tracks semiconductor end-demand with a 6-18 month lag. EPC contributed the majority of COMET's revenue decline from the WFE downcycle (FY2023-24) and will drive the majority of the recovery upside now emerging.

IXS manufactures X-ray tubes, sources, and complete inspection systems for industrial NDT (non-destructive testing), security screening, and medical imaging. IXS is structurally less cyclical, with more recurring aftermarket revenue, longer product replacement cycles, and more diversified end-market exposure. IXS serves aviation security (airport baggage scanners), pipeline/weld inspection, electronics inspection, and food safety. Growth is slower but more predictable — mid-single-digit organic, with steady volume and reasonable pricing dynamics.

Business model: COMET is fundamentally a technology component manufacturer. It is not a pure capital equipment OEM — it sits one step back, supplying mission-critical sub-components to the OEMs. This gives COMET a durable position in the value chain (switching costs, qualification requirements, process integration) but limits its share of the economics relative to the full system. COMET's business model is asset-intensive: precision manufacturing requires significant capital investment (Penang fab expansion), and the IP moat is embedded in component design and system software (Synertia), not service contracts.

Geographically: the majority of revenue is generated in North America and Asia (driven by EPC/semiconductor exposure), with Europe more significant in IXS (NDT, security). Cost base is predominantly CHF (Swiss engineering, R&D, and head office), with Penang expanding the USD/MYR cost exposure. The currency mismatch — CHF cost base, USD/EUR revenue base — is a structural headwind that has intensified as CHF has appreciated since 2022.

Over the last three years (L3Y), COMET navigated a severe WFE downcycle. PCT revenue fell from CHF 381m at peak to approximately CHF 193m at trough — a 49% decline. This drove group revenue contraction, margin compression, and FCF deterioration. Management spent this period investing in the Penang facility (capex CHF 42.5m in FY2025 alone), developing and commercialising Synertia, refreshing the leadership team, and resetting financial targets.

Over the next three years (N3Y), COMET's investment case rests on: (1) WFE recovery driving EPC/PCT revenue recovery from trough toward CMD targets; (2) Synertia penetration expanding average selling prices and defensibility within the EPC division; (3) Penang capex cycle normalising, releasing FCF; (4) IXS delivering steady mid-single-digit growth as a portfolio ballast; and (5) management executing on operational leverage embedded in the fixed cost base. The CMD November 2025 peak revenue target of CHF 670-770m (from the current CHF 457m FY2025 base) implies ~50% revenue growth over 2-3 years at peak cycle — a large number that requires all five drivers to work simultaneously.

CHANGE IN STRATEGY/STRUCTURE

Rating: Yellow

The strategic change at COMET under Haferl is centred on the Synertia platform. Synertia is COMET's proprietary RF power and plasma control system, developed in collaboration with major semiconductor OEM customers. It represents a move from selling discrete components (RF generators, matching networks as separate units) to selling an integrated, software-defined platform that manages plasma process control. This is a meaningful strategic upgrade: integrated platforms command higher ASPs, create deeper customer integration (switching costs increase), and provide a path to software/service revenue alongside hardware.

The logic is sound. The risk is adoption velocity. Synertia competes not only against AEIS and MKSI RF components but against OEMs' own internal integration preferences. Some OEMs prefer to own the plasma control layer themselves and buy discrete components from COMET/AEIS/MKSI. Synertia requires the OEM to adopt COMET's integration philosophy — a longer sell cycle and more complex qualification process. At IG stage, the penetration of Synertia into the installed base is not publicly quantified. This is a critical data point for DD.

EPC vs IXS strategic priorities: Haferl has maintained both divisions as core — there is no divestiture signal for IXS. The rationale for retaining IXS is portfolio stability (non-cyclical revenue base, margin support through WFE troughs) and shared technology heritage (X-ray tubes and RF systems both require precision high-voltage/high-frequency engineering). The IXS business provides cash flow stability that partially offsets EPC cyclicality. This is defensible portfolio strategy, though some investors argue IXS is a distraction from the pure-play semiconductor story.

Penang manufacturing expansion: This is the single largest strategic capital allocation decision under Haferl. The strategic rationale is multi-layered: (1) cost reduction (Malaysian manufacturing cost base lower than Swiss), (2) geographic diversification (reduces Swiss concentration risk, provides APAC proximity to OEM customers in Taiwan, South Korea, Japan), (3) tariff mitigation (Penang-produced components may face different US tariff treatment than Swiss-produced). The differentiation claim is that Synertia reduces process variability in plasma etch/deposition — lower defect density, higher wafer yield, tighter process window. At advanced nodes (sub-5nm), process variability is increasingly the yield-limiting factor, so a platform that demonstrably reduces it has genuine commercial value. The critical unknown at IG stage: has Synertia been independently validated by OEM customers, and what market share has COMET gained relative to AEIS/MKSI as a direct result? DD must answer this.

Chinese semiconductor equipment entrants: Chinese domestic WFE manufacturers (NAURA, AMEC, ACM Research) are increasingly capable in mid-range processes. However, for advanced node plasma etch (the highest-value COMET target market), Chinese capabilities remain 2-3 generations behind TSMC-qualified processes. The risk is more relevant for COMET's legacy/mid-range PCT products than for Synertia-class advanced node products. Medium-term (3-5 year) risk if Chinese domestic semiconductor manufacturing becomes more self-sufficient.

Customer concentration: Applied Materials, Lam Research, and TEL are almost certainly COMET's top three EPC customers. The degree of concentration is critical — if AMAT alone represents 30%+ of EPC revenue, any AMAT capex programme cancellation, insourcing decision, or competitive displacement would be highly material. The Synertia co-development relationship with key OEMs is a concentration risk mitigant (deeper integration = lower substitution probability) but also a concentration amplifier (if COMET bets on Synertia with AMAT and AMAT internalises the function, the downside is severe).

Section 232 competitive dynamics: US-based RF power manufacturers (AEIS has manufacturing in the US and internationally) may gain a cost advantage over COMET's Swiss/Penang supply chain for US-consuming OEMs. The degree depends on tariff classifications, OEM pass-through negotiations, and whether tariffs are applied uniformly to US-destined vs globally sourced components. This is a genuine competitive disruption event — it changes the relative cost position of manufacturers in a cost-sensitive component business. COMET's Penang expansion may have been partly motivated by tariff risk mitigation (ASEAN country of origin), but this needs verification at DD.

Barriers to entry / moat assessment: COMET's moat in EPC rests on: (1) qualification barriers — RF power components must pass extensive qualification testing by each OEM, each new process node, each new tool platform; re-qualification is expensive and time-consuming (6-18 months typically); (2) process integration — COMET engineers are embedded in OEM development cycles, creating switching costs; (3) Synertia software/platform — proprietary IP with regulatory-style qualification; (4) manufacturing precision — RF power at WFE specifications requires extreme precision, quality systems, and materials expertise. These are real barriers. The question is whether they are sufficient to prevent share loss to AEIS/MKSI or Chinese entrants over a 5-year horizon. IXS has similar qualification-driven moats in aviation security X-ray.

CHANGE IN MARKET/MACRO CONTEXT

Rating: Yellow

The macro context for COMET in early 2026 is more complex than a simple WFE recovery narrative. Four macro forces are simultaneously active, with different signs.

CHF structural headwind: The Swiss National Bank has historically maintained CHF as a safe-haven currency with periodic appreciation episodes. Since 2020, CHF has appreciated approximately 15-20% against USD and EUR. COMET generates approximately 60% of revenue in USD/EUR but its cost base is predominantly CHF. This creates an automatic margin drag: as CHF strengthens, revenue translates at lower CHF values, while costs remain anchored. Every 5% CHF appreciation is approximately 200-300 basis points of EBITDA margin compression before any operational actions. Management cannot hedge this structurally — Penang expansion partially diversifies the cost base but does not eliminate the exposure. This is the single largest uncontrollable variable in COMET's financial model.

WFE cycle positioning: The consensus view in early 2026 is that the WFE cycle has troughed (CY2023-24 was the trough) and is entering an upcycle driven by advanced node demand. ASML, AMAT, LAM, and TEL have all guided to 2026 WFE growth. The magnitude of the up-cycle is debated — AI-driven demand suggests a higher ceiling than prior cycles, but China export controls cap some uplift. For COMET, the WFE positioning means it is at the early stages of an upcycle. The book-to-bill data confirms this. The risk is another false dawn — there were several brief WFE recoveries in 2023-24 that reversed. Q1 2026 data is stronger than prior false dawns, but one quarter is insufficient for confirmation.

AI-driven semiconductor capex: The structural driver behind the WFE recovery is AI chip demand. TSMC's $52-56bn 2026 capex guidance is the headline figure, but Intel's foundry ambition and Samsung's HBM ramp provide additional demand. AI-specific chips (NVIDIA H100/H200/B100, custom ASICs from hyperscalers) require advanced node foundry capacity (TSMC N3/N2) that uses plasma processes extensively. This is the most powerful macro tailwind in this memo. It creates a plausible structural floor under advanced-node WFE spend even if the consumer semiconductor cycle remains soft.

China export controls and geopolitical risk: US and allied export controls on advanced semiconductor equipment to China (Entity List restrictions, October 2022 and subsequent actions) have constrained China WFE spending at leading-edge nodes. COMET's exposure depends on whether it sells PCT to Chinese-bound tools — if Lam Research (China-restricted tools) is a significant EPC revenue source, this is a constraint. Separately, the Iran conflict context (current as of April 2026) creates oil price volatility and broader macro uncertainty. Direct impact on COMET from Iran is indirect: higher energy prices increase Swiss manufacturing costs, and broader risk-off reduces European equity valuations. This is a second-order headwind, not a primary driver.

CHF and tariff interaction: Section 232 tariffs (January 2026) compound the CHF headwind. Swiss-manufactured goods face a tariff wedge in the US market. If Swiss components shipped to US OEMs are now 10-15% more expensive due to tariffs, and CHF has appreciated 15-20% since 2020, COMET's effective cost position to US customers has deteriorated approximately 25-35% since 2020. This is substantial. Penang mitigates this if Malaysian-produced components receive ASEAN preferential treatment — but only for new, Penang-sourced production, not the existing Swiss-sourced installed base.

OVERALL CHANGE ASSESSMENT

Rating: Yellow

COMET presents a Yellow-rated change forces profile at the IG stage. The case is neither strongly positive (Green) nor deeply challenged (Red/Orange). The evidence for a genuine change thesis is:

  • Complete management team refresh (CEO, CFO, Chairman) with relevant semiconductor industry backgrounds
  • Proprietary Synertia platform representing a genuine strategic upgrade from commodity component to integrated solution
  • Penang capex investment timed correctly at cycle trough
  • WFE recovery underway (Q1 2026 B2B 1.4, orders +22.3% YoY)
  • AI-driven structural semiconductor capex creating a higher cycle ceiling than historical precedent
  • CMD target framework that is quantified and defensible (if conservative relative to prior target)

The evidence against a compelling change thesis:

  • Management credibility gap: two consecutive guidance misses, dividend cut -67%, no recovery delivery yet under Haferl
  • CHF appreciation is a persistent structural margin drag that offsets much of the operational improvement
  • Synertia adoption velocity and market share gain versus AEIS/MKSI are not publicly quantified
  • Section 232 tariff impact is opaque and potentially significant for near-term margins
  • CMD peak target cut from CHF 800-900m to CHF 670-770m signals ambition compression, not expansion
  • China exposure constraints limit the total WFE addressable market recovery

Net assessment: COMET passes the IG threshold to proceed to triage and ESA. The change thesis is plausible (new management, new platform, investment-phase transition to harvest-phase) but not yet proven. The demand recovery component (WFE cycle) may deliver IRR even without full execution of the internal change thesis. The combined case — WFE recovery + Synertia adoption + Penang cost benefit + FCF normalisation — has a credible path to 25%+ IRR over 24-36 months if entry price is right. The key risk is that the recovery is slower than expected (timeline extension destroying IRR) or that CHF appreciation offsets operational improvements dollar-for-dollar.

TRANSMISSION MECHANISM

For COMET's change forces to produce a step-change in earnings, the following chain must execute in sequence.

Step 1: WFE recovery sustains and accelerates. Q1 2026 order data (B2B 1.4, +22.3% YoY) confirms the cycle has turned. OEM tool orders need to convert to COMET component orders on a 3-6 month lag. EPC revenue should grow from FY2025's approximately CHF 290-310m toward CHF 380-430m over 12-24 months. This is purely cycle-driven and does not require management execution — it is demand-pull.

Step 2: Penang production ramp absorbs WFE demand growth. The new capacity must come online without quality or qualification issues. If Penang ramps successfully, COMET can service the order intake without Swiss capacity constraints — and at lower unit costs. This is an execution requirement that adds operational risk to the demand recovery.

Step 3: Synertia penetration grows within the expanding EPC revenue base. As PCT revenue recovers, a higher proportion of new orders should be Synertia-based (vs legacy discrete components). Synertia commands higher ASPs — a plausible 15-25% premium over discrete components, though this needs confirmation at DD. Mix shift toward Synertia within a growing EPC revenue base is the primary driver of EBITDA margin expansion above pure operating leverage.

Step 4: Operating leverage materialises. COMET has a significantly fixed cost base (Swiss R&D, head office, precision manufacturing). Revenue growth from Step 1-3 drops through to EBITDA at approximately 40-60% incremental margins (industry-typical for WFE component businesses at this scale). This leverage drives EBITDA from CHF 46m (FY2025) toward CHF 80-100m on the path to CMD peak targets.

Step 5: Penang capex normalises. As construction phases complete and the facility reaches operational capacity, capex drops from CHF 42.5m (FY2025) toward CHF 15-20m maintenance levels. FCF recovers from CHF 8.5m (FY2025) toward CHF 40-60m+ as operating cash generation is no longer consumed by capex. This FCF recovery drives re-rating from a capital-intensive investment phase company to a capital-returning industrial.

Step 6: Market re-rates from investment-phase to harvest-phase multiple. WFE component businesses at peak cycle trade at 12-18x EBITDA. COMET at CHF 120-150m EBITDA (CMD peak) would imply a market cap of CHF 1.4-2.7bn — compared to a current implied market cap that will be significantly below CMD peak embedded value. The re-rating from trough-cycle multiple to peak-cycle multiple on a growing EBITDA base is the IRR driver.

The transmission mechanism is visible. It is not exotic or reliant on speculative assumptions. The primary risks are: (1) the WFE recovery stalls or reverses before COMET's revenue reaches a level where operating leverage becomes material; (2) CHF appreciation offsets the Penang cost benefit and the incremental margin expansion; (3) Synertia adoption is slower than assumed, leaving COMET exposed to commodity RF component pricing; (4) Haferl fails to execute on Penang ramp and operational efficiency, meaning costs do not scale as expected.

The mechanism is clear enough to track quarter-by-quarter. Leading indicators: B2B ratio, order intake (quarterly, with YoY growth), Penang qualification milestones (periodic updates), Synertia as percentage of EPC orders (if disclosed), FX rates (weekly observable). This is a trackable transmission mechanism — not a false friend in the HBX sense.

SCEPTIC'S VIEW

A well-informed bear on COMET would construct the following case.

First, the management transformation story is incomplete and unproven. Jan Haferl has navigated a downcycle — any competent CEO would have made the Penang investment decision at cycle trough; it was the obvious thing to do. He has not yet demonstrated the ability to grow revenue, expand margins, or execute a complex operational ramp in a recovery environment. Two consecutive guidance misses and a -67% dividend cut are not signals of a management team that has earned credibility. The CMD targets are aspirational. Until Haferl delivers two or three quarters of beat-and-raise in a recovery environment, the leadership change premium is not justified.

Second, the CHF structural headwind is permanently devaluing COMET's financial profile. The Swiss manufacturing cost base is not going away. The Penang expansion helps at the margin but does not transform the cost structure. Every year, as CHF appreciates, COMET's reported margins appear lower than the operational reality — but the operational reality is what investors trade on. A management team that cannot change its cost base in a material way is running on a currency treadmill. COMET's reported EBITDA margin of 10.1% in FY2025 would look 200-300 basis points higher on a constant-currency basis — but constant-currency is not cash.

Third, Synertia's competitive differentiation claim needs verification. AEIS is not standing still — it is also investing in integrated RF and plasma control solutions. MKSI has deep process expertise. The claim that Synertia is materially superior to competitors' integrated solutions, and that this superiority is sufficient to drive durable ASP premiums and market share gains, is an assertion without peer-reviewed validation at IG stage. The risk is that Synertia is a good product in a competitive market, not a proprietary moat.

Fourth, the WFE cycle recovery may be front-loaded or smaller than AI excitement suggests. Hyperscaler AI capex is real but may peak earlier than expected (2026-27 rather than 2027-28+). If the WFE cycle is a 12-18 month phenomenon rather than a 36-month structural upcycle, COMET's CMD targets become unreachable in the relevant investment timeframe. The prior cycle (2021-22) showed how quickly WFE can overshoot and then correct. Assume 100% cyclical unless proven structural (investment philosophy principle).

Fifth, Section 232 tariffs introduce an unquantified but potentially material cost wedge precisely at the moment COMET needs revenue recovery to drive operating leverage. If Swiss-manufactured components face 10-15% tariffs in the US market and COMET cannot pass these through to OEMs (who face competitive pressure of their own), the margin recovery story is delayed or diminished.

Sixth, the dividend cut removes the income investor base at the worst time — when COMET needs patient capital. The share register will have been partially cleansed of long-term holders, potentially increasing volatility and reducing the valuation support that dividend-oriented European industrial investors provide.

Conclusion of sceptic's view: COMET could be a false friend. It looks like a classic WFE recovery play with a credible internal change story, but the transformation is slower than it appears, the currency drag is structural, the management track record is unproven, and the competitive moat (Synertia) is asserted rather than demonstrated. The risk of another guidance miss in 2026-27 — as CHF appreciation and tariffs offset the volume recovery — is material. Park and revisit if Haferl delivers two consecutive quarters of revenue beat with margin expansion confirmation.
