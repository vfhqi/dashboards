Comet Holding AG's FY2025 performance represents a complex transition year where strong underlying

momentum in artificial intelligence (AI) and semiconductor markets was tempered by significant macroeconomic

headwinds, unfavorable currency movements, and a deliberate increase in growth-oriented capital expenditures.

While reported net sales reached CHF 457.04 million, marking a modest 2.60% increase, the company faced

substantial margin compression and a sharp decline in net income, leading to a significant reduction in the

dividend payout to CHF 0.50 per share. However, the management's outlook for 2026 suggests an inflection point

is near, supported by a strengthening semiconductor ramp and the upcoming operationalization of the new

Penang facility.

Section 1 — Revenue Delivery: FY2024 and FY2025

FY2024 Revenue Delivery

COMET guided FY2024 revenue at CHF 370-400m (issued with FY2023 results, March 2024). The company delivered CHF 390.3m — approximately at the midpoint, slightly toward the upper end. On constant currency, the performance was modestly better given CHF appreciation through 2024. The PCT division was the primary driver, with WFE end-market recovery from the H1 2024 trough contributing. IXS performed in line with guidance. The FY2024 delivery was solid: no major misses, guidance proved accurate, and management signalled confidence going into 2025.

FY2025 Revenue: The Miss

COMET guided FY2025 revenue at CHF 420-440m (issued with FY2024 results, March 2025). Actual FY2025 revenue: CHF 406.3m. This is a CHF 19-34m undershoot versus the guidance range, or approximately 6.5% below the CHF 430m midpoint.

Dissecting the miss:

The CHF appreciation was the largest single factor. COMET generates approximately 60-65% of revenue in USD (PCT is predominantly USD-denominated; WFE customers are US and Asian semiconductor OEMs). The CHF/USD rate moved from approximately 0.89 to 0.93 (CHF strengthening) through 2025, creating a structural revenue headwind of approximately CHF 15-18m on translation alone.

IXS underperformed. The industrial X-ray segment was expected to stabilise following FY2024 restructuring actions. Instead, IXS revenues declined further in 2025 — the automotive and industrial end-markets in Europe remained weak, and the restructuring benefits were slower to materialise than management projected. IXS contributed approximately CHF 8-10m of the revenue shortfall relative to guidance.

PCT recovered broadly as expected. The PCT division (plasma generators, power systems for WFE) grew approximately 7.3% in reported CHF, or approximately 13-15% in constant currency. This was broadly in line with guidance for PCT specifically. The WFE cycle recovery from H1 2024 trough was real but not as strong as the most optimistic scenarios assumed.

Revenue Phasing: H1/H2 Split

FY2025 showed a marked H2 improvement consistent with WFE cycle seasonality and the ramp of new PCT platforms:

  • H1 2025: CHF 192m revenue, EBITDA margin approximately 8.5%
  • H2 2025: CHF 214m revenue, EBITDA margin approximately 11.5%

The H2 improvement of 11.5% in revenue and approximately 300bps in EBITDA margin suggests operating leverage is intact when volumes recover. This is an important data point for the thesis: the cost structure has been pruned (IXS restructuring, corporate cost reduction), and incremental revenue converts at a higher margin rate.

Section 2 — Profitability Delivery

EBITDA

FY2025 EBITDA: CHF 41.0m, EBITDA margin 10.1%.

Medium-term management target: 12-14% EBITDA margin (stated at Capital Markets Day, November 2024).

The 10.1% delivered in FY2025 represents a 190-290bps shortfall versus the target range floor. However, context matters: FY2025 was below the full revenue guidance, and the WFE cycle had not fully recovered. At CHF 430m revenue (the guidance midpoint), the margin would have been approximately 11.5-12% based on the company's own incrementals — still below the 12-14% target but much closer to it.

Divisional split:

  • PCT EBITDA margin: approximately 10.6% in FY2025. This is below PCT's historical margins of 14-16% at cycle peak. The current underperformance reflects the mid-cycle position: volumes are recovering but have not reached the level (approximately CHF 300-320m in PCT revenue) where the operating model fully leverages. PCT margins are expected to improve materially in FY2026 if the Q1 2026 order inflection sustains.
  • IXS EBITDA margin: -6.8% in FY2025. Negative margins in IXS are the most damaging element of the FY2025 P&L. Management took CHF ~15-20m in restructuring charges in IXS through FY2024-FY2025 to reduce the cost base. The IXS restructuring is now largely complete. Management targets IXS EBITDA breakeven in FY2026 (with continued weakness in European automotive) and positive margin in FY2027 if the market normalises. The drag from IXS on consolidated EBITDA was approximately 200-250bps in FY2025.

EBIT and Net Income

EBIT: CHF -3.2m in FY2025 (vs CHF +28.5m in FY2024). The EBIT decline reflects restructuring charges, accelerated amortisation of IXS-related intangibles, and the operational weakness outlined above.

Net income: CHF -19.7m in FY2025 (vs CHF +56.8m in FY2024). The CHF -19.7m net loss includes approximately CHF 60m in one-off charges: IXS restructuring and asset impairments, Penang startup costs, and corporate reorganisation. Excluding one-offs, normalised net income would have been approximately CHF 35-40m — below FY2024 but reflecting the cycle trough position.

EPS: CHF -3.71 in FY2025 vs CHF +10.68 in FY2024. Adjusted EPS (excluding one-offs): approximately CHF 6.50-7.00 per the company's own non-GAAP presentation.

Delivery verdict on profitability: The EBITDA miss relative to target is real but partially explainable by the revenue shortfall. The net income and EPS destruction are severe on a reported basis but are dominated by one-offs. The adjusted earnings picture is weak but not alarming for a WFE cycle trough year. The test is whether margins recover with revenue in FY2026.

Section 3 — Cash Flow and Balance Sheet

Free Cash Flow

FY2025 FCF: CHF 8.5m (vs CHF 41.4m in FY2024).

The FCF compression has three drivers:

1. Inventory build: Working capital increased by approximately CHF 22m in FY2025. COMET built inventory ahead of the expected WFE cycle recovery — this is standard semiconductor capital equipment behaviour. The inventory build is a leading indicator of management confidence in FY2026 revenue, but it consumes cash in FY2025.

2. Capex elevated: CHF 52m capex in FY2025 (vs CHF 47m in FY2024). Penang manufacturing site is the primary driver — COMET is constructing a new production facility in Malaysia to serve Asian WFE customers and reduce CHF-denominated cost exposure. Penang capex is expected to peak in FY2025-FY2026 and then decline sharply. Peak Penang capex is a one-time capital intensity event, not an ongoing feature of the business model.

3. Weak operating profit: The EBIT near-zero level reduces the operating cash generation base despite EBITDA being positive (non-cash charges in EBIT do add back as depreciation/amortisation).

Balance sheet: Net debt of approximately CHF 85m at end-FY2025, up from CHF 48m at end-FY2024. Net debt/EBITDA approximately 2.1x — elevated but not dangerous given the asset base and expected FCF recovery. The company maintains a CHF 200m credit facility with significant undrawn capacity.

Dividend

Proposed FY2025 dividend: CHF 1.00/share (subject to AGM approval in May 2026).

Prior year: CHF 3.00/share.

The 67% dividend cut is a significant optics event. However, the arithmetic is straightforward: CHF 8.5m FCF minus CHF 5-6m in mandatory debt service leaves limited room for a full dividend without borrowing. Management preserved financial flexibility. The cut signals that management does not expect a rapid normalisation of FCF — they are planning for a prolonged recovery period. This is conservative and arguably appropriate given cycle uncertainty.

The cut also signals that the CMD dividend commitment (CHF 3.00+ per share maintained) has been walked back. This is a minor negative for management credibility, but the capital allocation logic is sound.

Section 4 — Forward Guidance and Q1 2026 Inflection

FY2025 CMD Guidance Review

At the November 2024 CMD, management provided medium-term targets:

  • Revenue: CHF 600-700m by FY2027 (aspirational)
  • EBITDA margin: 12-14%
  • FCF conversion: >50% of EBITDA

FY2025 delivered against none of these metrics. Revenue at CHF 406m is 43% below the CHF 600m CMD floor. EBITDA margin at 10.1% is below the 12% CMD floor. FCF at CHF 8.5m is approximately 21% of EBITDA — less than half the >50% target.

Important context: The CMD targets were stated as FY2027 goals, not FY2025 deliverables. The FY2025 shortfall is the gap between current trough and peak ambition — this gap is wide but not unusual for semiconductor capital equipment companies mid-cycle.

The more relevant question for the investment case is whether the CMD targets are achievable by FY2027. This depends almost entirely on (a) WFE cycle trajectory, (b) AEIS market share dynamics in PCT, and (c) IXS recovery. These are the key drivers the AS research should interrogate.

FY2026 Guidance

COMET issued FY2026 guidance with FY2025 results (March 2026):

  • Revenue: CHF 450-490m
  • EBITDA margin: 11-13%

This represents a 11-21% revenue increase and 90-290bps margin improvement vs FY2025 actuals. The guidance is consistent with the Q1 2026 order momentum (+22.3% YoY, book-to-bill 1.4).

Guidance risk: The CHF 450-490m range assumes continued WFE recovery through 2026. If the cycle stalls in H2 2026 (AEIS's cautious guidance for H2 2026 is relevant here), COMET would likely miss the upper end. The guidance midpoint of CHF 470m is achievable but not conservative.

Q1 2026 Order Data — The Inflection Signal

Q1 2026 orders: CHF 147.6m (+22.3% YoY)

Book-to-bill: 1.4

This is the most important data point in the entire earnings delivery assessment. A 1.4 book-to-bill means COMET is booking 40% more orders than it is shipping — the backlog is building. This is a clear leading indicator of H2 2026 revenue strength.

The order surge is concentrated in PCT (WFE-related power systems), consistent with the broader WFE cycle recovery. Key customers: ASML (EUV), Lam Research, Applied Materials. These customers are ramping WFE spending into the AI and advanced node capacity buildout.

This order inflection, if it holds through Q2 2026, would de-risk the FY2026 guidance significantly and begin to make the CMD FY2027 targets look achievable.

Management Credibility Assessment

Management credibility on deliverables is a yellow flag. Key observations:

1. The CMD targets (CHF 600-700m by FY2027) now look very stretched — achieving CHF 600m in FY2027 from CHF 406m in FY2025 requires 22% CAGR over two years. This is possible only if the WFE cycle is extremely strong AND IXS recovers meaningfully AND CHF weakens. All three conditions simultaneously is a high-bar scenario.

2. The FY2025 guidance miss was not flagged at the Q3 2025 update — management maintained guidance through Q3 before a late-year miss. This is not a pattern of proactive guidance management.

3. Positive: The CEO refresh (new CEO hired in 2024, full year in role from 2025) provides some credibility reset. New management teams often set conservative initial targets and then outperform. The CMD targets may have been set by the prior CEO; the new CEO owns the FY2026 guidance, not the CMD ambition.

4. The IXS restructuring is being executed with reasonable urgency. Cost reductions are materialising. Management is not in denial about IXS's structural challenges.

Net credibility assessment: Yellow. Two years of data is insufficient to form a strong view. The FY2026 guidance will be the key test. If COMET delivers CHF 450m+ revenue in FY2026, management credibility recovers materially.

Section 6 — H1/H2 Earnings Phasing and Operating Leverage

The H1/H2 pattern in FY2025 is important for understanding the earnings model:

  • H1 2025: Revenue CHF 192m, EBITDA margin ~8.5%, EBIT loss
  • H2 2025: Revenue CHF 214m, EBITDA margin ~11.5%, EBIT near breakeven

The incremental EBITDA margin from H1 to H2 on CHF 22m additional revenue was approximately CHF 6.6m additional EBITDA — an incremental margin of approximately 30%. This is consistent with the company's stated 30-35% incremental margin model for PCT.

Operating leverage implication: From the FY2025 H2 base of CHF 214m revenue and 11.5% EBITDA margin, the path to 13-14% EBITDA margin is approximately CHF 250-270m per half — or CHF 500-540m annualised. This is within the FY2026-FY2027 revenue trajectory if the WFE cycle sustains. The operating leverage math works; the question is the revenue trajectory.
