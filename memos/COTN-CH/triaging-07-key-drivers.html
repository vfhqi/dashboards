Comet Holding AG (COTN.CH) is entering a critical 24-month window where the convergence of a cyclical

semiconductor recovery and internal structural transformations will dictate its equity valuation. While 2025 was

characterized as a "year of gradual recovery" impacted by significant foreign exchange headwinds and product

mix shifts, management has set the stage for a substantial inflection in 2026 and 2027 through the ramp-up of

the Synertia platform, the pivot of the X-Ray Systems (IXS) division toward semiconductors, and the

operationalization of the new Penang manufacturing hub. [1] 1 • ARS

Executive Summary: 24-Month Investment Thesis

The investment case for Comet rests on its high operating leverage to the Wafer Fabrication Equipment (WFE)

cycle, specifically within AI-driven advanced logic and memory segments. [2] After a challenging 2025 where

EBITDA margins contracted to 10.1% from 13.0% due to CHF strength and investment spending, the company is

executing a "Strategy to 2030" focused on regaining a 22-27% EBITDA margin corridor. [3] Key performance

indicators for the next 24 months include the transition of Synertia into high-volume manufacturing (HVM) orders

and the successful execution of a company-wide efficiency program expected to yield a 3 percentage point

margin improvement. [4] 2 3 • ARS 4 • Earnings Presentation

The Q1 2026 order inflection (+22.3% YoY, B2B 1.4) confirms the cycle has turned. The amplitude of the WFE recovery determines most of the investment case. #J
  • Multiple re-rating is plausible but front-loaded risk: COTN-CH trades at approximately 28x NTM earnings. A WFE recovery re-rating has already begun (stock +82% from 52W low). Entry above CHF 300 prices in meaningful recovery. The upside is real but the margin of safety has narrowed. #J
  • EBITDA margin expansion is the earnings compounding mechanism: From 10.1% (FY2025) to 13-15% (cycle peak target) represents 290-490bps of margin expansion. At CHF 470m FY2026 revenue, each 100bps of margin = CHF 4.7m EBITDA. The leverage is real; the timing is uncertain. #A
  • Synertia RF platform is the structural growth driver — proprietary RF power and plasma control systems for EUV and Gate All Around node transitions. If Synertia captures 35-40% attach rates in next-gen nodes, PCT revenue could exit the cycle at CHF 320-340m vs CHF 247m trough. Orange — not yet proven at volume. #J
  • IXS recovery is a free option — if management delivers EBITDA breakeven in FY2026 as guided, the IXS drag disappears. IXS was approximately 200-250bps EBITDA headwind in FY2025. The probability is 50-60% — European industrial end-market recovery is needed. #A
  • Penang optionality is mid-term, not immediate — the new Malaysia manufacturing site reduces cost exposure and enables Asian customer growth. Capex peaks in FY2025-FY2026; the benefits (cost savings, revenue access) materialise from FY2027. Not a near-term driver. #A
  • CHF/FX is a structural headwind that management cannot control — 60-65% of PCT revenue in USD, reporting in CHF. Every 1% CHF appreciation vs USD is approximately CHF 2.5-3.0m annual revenue headwind. This is a permanent feature of the investment case, not a one-off. Red. #J
  • Multi-variable thesis confirmed — proceed to ESA. No single driver is disqualifying, but the combination of WFE exposure, CHF headwind, management credibility gap, and stretched CMD targets means the stock demands careful monitoring at entry. The thesis is not yet conviction-grade. #J

Driver 1: WFE Cycle Recovery (Weight: ~65% of earnings case)

Nature of the Driver

PCT (Plasma Control Technologies) generated CHF 247m revenue in FY2025, approximately 61% of group revenue. This division supplies power generators, plasma control systems, and RF power amplifiers to WFE OEMs — principally ASML (for EUV), Lam Research (etch), Applied Materials (CVD/PVD), and Tokyo Electron. Driver 4: Synertia RF Platform — Structural Growth Driver (Weight: Future PCT growth)

What Is Synertia?

Synertia is COMET's proprietary RF power and plasma control platform, designed specifically for EUV lithography (ASML systems) and Gate All Around (GAA) transistor manufacturing (TSMC, Samsung, Intel). It integrates RF power generation, plasma monitoring, and process control into a single system — replacing multiple components that were previously sourced separately.

The strategic rationale: as semiconductor nodes shrink toward 2nm and below, plasma process control becomes increasingly critical. The tolerance for variation in RF power delivery narrows. COMET's Synertia platform offers higher precision than incumbent solutions.

Market Opportunity

COMET estimates the Synertia addressable market at CHF 400-600m annually by FY2027, based on assumed WFE spending levels and Synertia attach rates in EUV and GAA applications. Current Synertia revenue is small (CHF 20-30m estimated) — the platform is in early commercial deployment.

Attach rate thesis: If Synertia achieves 35-40% attach rates in EUV/GAA applications (i.e., specified by ASML or used in 35-40% of relevant WFE tools), PCT revenue could sustainably exceed CHF 320-340m at the next cycle peak — approximately 30-40% above current trough levels — even without WFE spend growth.

Risk Assessment

Synertia is an unproven platform at volume. COMET has historically competed against Cohu, AIMM, and AEIS in RF power delivery for WFE. AEIS in particular has a strong incumbent position in power delivery subsystems. Synertia's differentiation (integrated plasma monitoring) is technically compelling but the displacement of established AEIS relationships at key WFE OEMs will take time.

The AS research should probe: (1) customer qualification status of Synertia at ASML and key US OEMs, (2) competitive response from AEIS, (3) timeline to material revenue contribution.

Assessment: Orange. Synertia is strategically important but not yet commercially demonstrated at scale. It is an option on structural share gain, not a confirmed driver. Conviction on Synertia requires AS evidence of customer qualifications and design wins.

Driver 6: Penang Manufacturing — Medium-Term Optionality

The Strategic Rationale

COMET is building a new manufacturing facility in Penang, Malaysia. The strategic logic is threefold:

1. Cost reduction: CHF-denominated manufacturing in Switzerland is expensive given CHF appreciation. Shifting production to Malaysia (USD-denominated costs) reduces the structural FX headwind.

2. Asian customer proximity: WFE manufacturing is increasingly concentrated in Asia (TSMC in Taiwan, Samsung in Korea, SMIC in China). Penang reduces lead times and serves the supply chain localisation preference of Asian OEMs.

3. Capacity for cycle upswing: The Penang facility adds production capacity beyond current Swiss capacity — enabling COMET to capture volume in a strong WFE cycle without capacity constraint.

Timeline and Costs

Capex: CHF 52m in FY2025 (includes Penang). Peak Penang capex is FY2025-FY2026. Annual maintenance capex post-Penang buildout: CHF 25-30m (vs CHF 45-55m during construction phase). The capex normalisation alone will contribute approximately CHF 20-25m to annual FCF from FY2027.

Operational benefits: CHF 10-15m annual cost savings from Penang manufacturing vs Swiss equivalent (management guidance). Phased realisation: approximately 25% in FY2026, 75% by FY2027.

Assessment: Yellow. Penang is a strategically sound investment but a mid-term, not near-term, driver. The near-term effect is capex intensity and startup costs (net negative in FY2025-FY2026). From FY2027, Penang adds approximately 250-370bps of EBITDA margin and CHF 20-25m of additional FCF.

Driver 7: CHF/FX — The Structural Headwind

The Problem

COMET reports in CHF. Approximately 60-65% of PCT revenue is USD-denominated (US WFE OEM customers; Asian customers also billed in USD). CHF has strengthened materially since 2021:

  • CHF/USD in 2021: approximately 0.91
  • CHF/USD in 2025: approximately 0.93-0.94
  • CHF/USD trajectory: historically strong CHF bias vs USD

The translation headwind on USD revenue to CHF reporting: every 1% CHF appreciation vs USD is approximately CHF 2.5-3.0m annual revenue headwind and approximately CHF 1.0-1.2m EBITDA headwind at current margins.

FY2025 impact: approximately CHF 15-18m revenue headwind and CHF 5-7m EBITDA headwind from FX translation vs a flat-rate assumption.

Structural Nature

The CHF/FX headwind is a permanent feature of owning COMET. SNB (Swiss National Bank) policy historically maintains a strong CHF as anti-inflation insurance. There is no mean reversion thesis for CHF weakness available to a long-only investor.

Penang partially mitigates this by shifting some costs to USD-denominated currencies, but the structural revenue translation headwind does not disappear.

Assessment: Red. This is not a temporary risk — it is a permanent drag on reported results and a source of guidance miss risk whenever CHF appreciates unexpectedly. Any investment case must be stress-tested against CHF at 0.95-1.00 vs USD.
