Company Description and Context

AutoStore is the inventor/licensor of cubic ASRS technology with ~1,000+ deployed systems globally, operating a capital-light licensing model through 25+ integrators; FY2025 profile is $539m revenue, 72.4% gross margin, ~42% EBITDA margin.

  • Core business: Inventor and licensor of cubic Storage and Retrieval System (ASRS) technology; aluminium/steel Grid framework, storage bins, and Robots for high-density warehouse storage #I
    • Does not manufacture systems itself; licenses to 25+ integrators (Element Logic, Swisslog, Knapp, Vanderlande)
    • Cubic density: 4-6x more goods per sq metre than conventional shelving
  • Revenue streams: Three: (1) System sales (upfront CapEx); (2) Service/maintenance (recurring); (3) AaaS subscription (per pick/per month, multi-year recognition) #I
    • AaaS dilutive to near-term reported revenue but builds recurring base and lowers adoption barriers
  • Competitive advantages: Cubic density, integrator network (broad reach without installation risk), patent protection, land-and-expand economics #I
    • ~45-55% of revenue from existing customers expanding installed base; high attachment rates
  • L3Y revenue: $646m (2023) → $601m (2024, -7%) → $539m (2025, -10%); cumulative -16% from peak #I
    • 2022-23 distorted by post-COVID warehouse boom; 2024-25 = demand normalisation + macro softness
  • L3Y gross margin: 68% (2023) → 73.1% (2024) → 72.4% (2025); structural improvement as installed base grows; key positive in difficult revenue period #I
  • Share price: Listed Oct 2021 at NOK 20/share; under sustained pressure through 2022-25; trades well below IPO price; significantly underperformed European small/mid-cap #I
  • Strategic initiatives: (1) AaaS model; (2) Geographic expansion (NA, APAC priority); (3) $10m cost programme from June 2025; (4) Refreshed exec team; CEO Vikse (Jan 2023), CFO Harrison (Oct 2023), CCO White (Nov 2024) #I
  • Competitive landscape: Market growing ~14% CAGR; key threat is Ocado Solutions (settled patent dispute, now competitor); Swisslog, Knapp also have proprietary products; cubic density advantage remains differentiated #I

Margin Analysis

Gross margin at 72-73% is structural and durable (~1,500bps above 2022), but EBITDA margin compression to ~42% reflects a business that does not yet exhibit strong operating leverage at current revenue levels; the 2026 recovery will be the test.

  • Gross margin evolution: 58% (2022) → 68% (2023) → 73.1% (2024) → 72.4% (2025); ~1,500bps expansion is structural, not cyclical #J
    • Drivers: (1) shift from hardware to higher-margin software/service; (2) pricing power maintained despite demand softness; (3) mix toward premium verticals (pharma, grocery)
    • 70bps compression in 2025 vs 2024 = noise (modest AaaS mix effect); 72-73% is current structural level
  • EBITDA bridge: ~500bps compression from 2023 peak (47%) to 2025 (~42%) #A
    • Revenue deleverage: $107m decline from peak; +430bps gross margin improvement almost entirely offset by opex on lower revenue
    • R&D, sales, G&A relatively fixed; when revenue declines 16%, costs do not fall proportionally
  • Operating leverage: Does NOT exhibit strong positive leverage at current levels; needs revenue to grow to demonstrate it #J
    • Good news: in recovery ($580-620m FY2026), leverage should be highly visible; cost base managed, gross margin structurally higher, incremental EBITDA conversion strong
  • AaaS margin impact: Near-term dilutive (upfront hardware costs recognised immediately, revenue over years) #A
    • Years 1-2: negative vs conventional sale; Years 3+: highly margin-accretive (recurring, minimal incremental cost)
    • Temporary drag that will reverse as portfolio seasons; must distinguish from deterioration in underlying economics
  • Cost programme: $10m annualised savings from June 2025; real but modest (~185bps on $226m EBITDA base) #I
    • Signals cost discipline rather than restructuring ambition; no further programmes guided
    • Management noted cost base "appropriately sized" for anticipated recovery
  • Below-the-line drag: Net debt from Ocado settlement creating meaningful interest expense; primary reason EPS -51% exceeds revenue -10% #A
    • Until debt reduced/refinanced, interest costs continue to amplify EPS volatility
    • No clear debt reduction timeline from management
  • Margin risks: (1) AaaS scaling temporarily pressuring blended margins; (2) Ocado Solutions competitive pricing; (3) mix shift toward commodity 3PL; none imminent but relevant at DD stage #A
  • Rate: Yellow; gross margin structurally strong; EBITDA reflects deleverage not structural cost issue; AaaS near-term drag with long-term uplift; interest drag underappreciated #J
