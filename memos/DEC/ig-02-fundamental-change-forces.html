Section 1: Brief company description

JCDecaux SE operates as the undisputed global leader in outdoor advertising, reaching a massive daily audience of over 850 million people across 79 countries and 3,895 cities.

The company's foundational business model, pioneered by Jean-Claude Decaux in 1964, relies on providing high-quality, free street furniture to municipalities in exchange for the exclusive rights to market the associated advertising space.

This highly integrated and virtuous economic model ensures that brand communications finance innovative public infrastructure, smart city solutions, and sustainable mobility services without burdening local taxpayers.

The Street Furniture segment remains the company's largest and most historic business line, reliably generating 50.7% of total consolidated revenue in 2025.

This core segment encompasses lucrative advertising placements on bus shelters, free-standing information panels, multi-service columns, and increasingly within retail environments like shopping centres and supermarkets.

The Transport segment constitutes 35.8% of group revenue and involves marketing highly coveted advertising space across 154 airports and 257 contracts spanning metros, buses, trains, and tramways worldwide.

While Transport previously accounted for over 40% of group revenue in 2019, its current lower proportional share directly reflects a delayed recovery in international travel and commuter traffic within China.

Billboard advertising represents the smallest functional segment at 13.4% of total revenue, focusing on large-format traditional and digital displays strategically positioned in high-traffic urban corridors.

The structural strategy within the Billboard division heavily involves rationalizing legacy inventory to ensure strict regulatory compliance while selectively upgrading premium locations to highly profitable digital screens.

Beyond standard advertising space sales, JCDecaux derives meaningful non-advertising revenue through the dedicated sale, rental, and maintenance of street furniture, self-service bicycle networks, and automated public toilets.

Geographically, Europe remains the company's dominant core market, contributing 46.8% of total revenue in 2025, with France alone representing 16.7% and the United Kingdom accounting for a robust 10.6%.

The Asia-Pacific region contributed 20.5% of 2025 revenue, though this geography has been heavily influenced by China, whose share of overall group revenue has structurally decreased from 18% in 2019 to approximately 10% today.

North America and the Rest of the World generated 8.0% and 14.0% of 2025 revenue respectively, consistently serving as critical high-single-digit organic growth drivers for the overall geographic portfolio.

The ongoing operational shift toward Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) advertising has served as the central pillar of JCDecaux's growth strategy over the past three years.

In 2025, DOOH revenue experienced 10.0% organic growth and reached an all-time record of 41.7% of total group revenue, demonstrating the rapid market adoption of dynamic digital inventory.

Despite this impressive overall growth, digital revenue remains highly concentrated; the US, UK, Australia, Germany, and China combined account for roughly 59% of total DOOH sales, indicating massive headroom for future global expansion.

Programmatic advertising automates ad space buying and connects the company directly to digital demand-side platforms (DSPs), serving as a massive multiplier that allows the company to tap into a $300 billion digital advertising addressable market.

Showcasing explosive demand, programmatic revenue surged by 19.2% organically in 2025, reaching €180.5 million and constituting 10.9% of the company's total digital revenue.

Uniquely among global OOH operators, JCDecaux firmly controls its own programmatic technology ecosystem via its proprietary VIOOH supply-side platform (SSP) and a strategic majority stake in the Displayce demand-side platform (DSP).

Looking ahead over the next three years, the company intends to heavily target the booming retail media sector, recently exemplified by a major strategic partnership with Carrefour and Carmila to deploy indoor DOOH across European shopping hubs.

The underlying business model benefits from highly defensive low customer concentration and broad sectoral diversity, with no single client accounting for 10% of group revenue and the top 10 clients contributing less than 13% combined.

Crucially positioned as a sustainable medium for the next decade, nearly 50% of JCDecaux's revenue aligns directly with the EU Green Taxonomy, and its ambitious Net Zero Carbon by 2050 trajectory has been officially approved by the SBTi.

Section 2: Key components summary

1. CEO and Senior Leadership Stability: The executive leadership of JCDecaux remains anchored by the foundational family rotation system, providing immense strategic continuity over the next three years. At the May 2025 Annual General Meeting, Jean-François Decaux formally assumed the role of Chairman of the Executive Board for a one-year term, taking over from Jean-Charles Decaux in accordance with their established annual alternation principle.

Board and Committee Refresh: While executive leadership remains constant, the Supervisory Board experienced a notable change in May 2025 with the appointment of Guillaume Pepy to replace Michel Bleitrach. Pepy concurrently took over key committee roles, becoming Chairman of the Compensation and Nominating Committee and joining both the Ethics and ESG Committee and the Audit Committee.

2. M&A and Corporate Activity Shift: Management has clearly signaled that large-scale, transformative M&A is off the table for the next 12 to 18 months, instead focusing strictly on targeted, opportunistic bolt-on acquisitions. This strategy is actively playing out in Europe and Latin America, seizing upon the structural market consolidation triggered by competitors like Clear Channel exiting the European theater.

Strategic Asset Disposals and Consolidation: The company demonstrated active portfolio management by selling a 13.56% stake in APG|SGA to NZZ in Switzerland in May 2024, generating substantial capital gains. Concurrently, JCDecaux successfully completed the strategic merger of IGPDecaux and IGP (formerly Clear Channel) in Italy, boosting its nationwide reach and integrated offering.

Antitrust Hurdles in Core Markets: Despite the push for consolidation, regulatory friction remains a tangible near-term headwind for corporate activity. The proposed acquisition of Clear Channel's assets in Spain was subjected to a prolonged Phase 2 antitrust review lasting up to 16 months, highlighting the slow pace of European regulatory approvals for major sector mergers.

Retail Media Partnerships: Plausibly over the next three years, retail media will serve as a massive new frontier for corporate partnerships. JCDecaux recently launched a major strategic alliance with Carrefour, Carmila, and Unlimitail to deploy indoor Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) screens across European shopping centres, directly bridging physical assets with highly coveted retailer transactional data.

3. Margins and Financial Profile Acceleration: The company’s margin profile has fundamentally transformed, demonstrating profound operating leverage as the business digitized. In 2025, the operating margin rate hit 20.9%, an impressive 150 basis point increase year-on-year that structurally elevates the company's profitability baseline.

Free Cash Flow Generation Surge: The financial profile is now characterized by unprecedented cash conversion. Free cash flow surged by 47.9% in 2025 to reach an all-time high of €342.9 million, granting the company immense balance sheet flexibility for future capital allocation.

Deleveraging and Balance Sheet Strength: Rapid cash generation has systematically de-risked the balance sheet over the last 12 months. Net debt decreased by 22.3% in 2025 to €587.4 million, bringing the leverage ratio down to a highly conservative 0.7x operating margin, well below historical averages.

4. Shareholder Returns Policy Update: JCDecaux has officially transitioned into a phase of consistent, progressive shareholder remuneration. The proposed dividend for 2025 was raised to €0.65 per share, representing an 18.2% increase from the previous year, with explicit management guidance indicating intentions to gradually increase this payout going forward.

Tactical Share Buybacks: Beyond standard dividends, the company has actively deployed excess capital toward share repurchases. A newly authorized buyback program targets up to 10% of the share capital, with the specific intent to offset dilution from performance share plans and to partially finance future M&A transactions.

5. Demand and Growth Rate Dynamics: Topline demand remains highly resilient, defying broader macroeconomic stagnation. While reported 2025 organic revenue growth was 1.8%, the underlying growth rate—excluding the massive 2024 Olympic and UEFA Euro comparables—stood at a robust 3.2%, indicating healthy baseline advertiser appetite.

Digital and Programmatic Explosion: The velocity of digital demand is structurally reshaping the revenue mix. Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) revenue grew 10.0% organically in 2025, reaching 41.7% of group sales, while programmatic revenues surged 19.2% as agencies increasingly plug outdoor inventory into omnichannel digital buying platforms.

The China Recovery Pivot: The demand narrative in Asia-Pacific is shifting from a major drag to a potential tailwind. After suffering prolonged mid-single-digit declines due to depressed consumer demand and sluggish commuter traffic, management guided that revenue growth in China will finally turn positive in Q1 2026.

6. Company Goals and Targets Achievement: JCDecaux has notably accelerated its strategic timeline, achieving its medium-term financial ambitions well ahead of schedule. The company's explicitly stated 2026 financial targets—which called for an operating margin above 20% and free cash flow exceeding €300 million—were both successfully conquered by the end of 2025.

ESG and Climate Targets Ratification: Over the next three years, environmental commitments will act as a core operational mandate rather than just a marketing tool. The Science-Based Targets initiative (SBTi) officially validated the company's Net Zero Carbon by 2050 trajectory, backing a strategy that already reduced scopes 1, 2, and 3 emissions by 40.9% versus 2019.

7. Technological Disruption and Product Innovation: JCDecaux is actively disrupting traditional media buying silos by launching the world's first truly global programmatic DOOH media solution. Expanding beyond airports into street furniture and retail environments, this unified platform allows global brands to execute borderless, data-driven campaigns through a single programmatic touchpoint.

AI Defense and Optimization: While generative AI threatens to disrupt content creation and search-based advertising, JCDecaux's physical infrastructure provides a unique moat. Management views the company as "AI-insulated" due to its exclusive physical footprint, while simultaneously leveraging AI backend tools to optimize audience targeting, network maintenance, and campaign yield management.

8. Macroeconomic and Industry Changes: JCDecaux is successfully navigating a complex web of global uncertainties. Despite rising geopolitical tensions, including the ongoing Middle East conflict, and the looming threat of global trade tariffs, the company observed zero material negative impacts on advertiser demand heading into early 2026.

The Competitor Retreat: The European competitive landscape is undergoing a massive structural easing. Clear Channel's systematic withdrawal from European and Latin American markets has significantly reduced irrational pricing behavior, allowing JCDecaux to bid for concessions with higher targeted IRRs and less aggressive guaranteed rent structures.

Market Share Capture: Morgan Stanley asserts that the combination of high-impact physical presence, programmatic flexibility, and the structural decline of linear TV and print will allow the outdoor sector to take a larger overall share of advertising budgets over the next five years.

Australian Market Dynamics: Jefferies Research notes that out-of-home revenue growth has surpassed all traditional media in Australia over the past decade, a trend expected to continue as digital OOH further leverages data analytics to enhance yield.

Section 3: Change in leadership

The Co-CEO Rotation Model Remains Intact: At the apex of JCDecaux's leadership, the foundational family rotation system provides immense structural continuity while ensuring shared operational control. At the May 2025 Annual General Meeting, Jean-François Decaux formally assumed the role of Chairman of the Executive Board and Co-Chief Executive Officer for a one-year term. He took over from Jean-Charles Decaux in strict accordance with the company's established annual alternation principle.

Expansion of the CFO's Mandate: A highly significant shift in the executive team's operational structure occurred in mid-2025. David Bourg, who had served as the Group Chief Financial and IT Officer since 2015, was officially appointed as the Group Chief Financial, IT and Operations Officer, effective June 1, 2025. This expansion signals a powerful consolidation of internal functions under a single executive leader.

Consolidated Operational Control: In addition to his existing financial and IT responsibilities, David Bourg's newly expanded scope now encompasses the R&D Department, the Purchasing, Supply Chain and Production Department, the Design Department, the International Operations Department, and the Project Department. Management noted that this structural streamlining is designed to enhance agility, flexibility, and operational efficiency across the group's global footprint.

Executive Board Retirements and Downsizing: The size of the core Executive Board was actively reduced over the last 12 months following a planned retirement. Daniel Hofer, a longstanding Member of the Executive Board and the CEO for Germany, Austria, Central & Eastern Europe, and Central Asia, retired from his operational role effective August 31, 2025. The company explicitly announced that Hofer will not currently be replaced on the Executive Board, effectively shrinking the top leadership body to four members.

Regional Management Succession: Following Daniel Hofer's departure, regional management was seamlessly transitioned to a new leader to ensure continuity in a critical European theater. Jérôme d'Héré was officially appointed as the new head of the Germany, Austria, Central and Eastern Europe, and Central Asia region. Despite taking over this expansive and strategically vital territory, d'Héré will operate outside the formal Executive Board structure.

Supervisory Board Refresh: The 11-member Supervisory Board experienced targeted changes in 2025 aimed at injecting fresh perspectives while maintaining a 55.56% independent majority. A notable addition was the appointment of Guillaume Pepy, who was brought on to replace the departing Michel Bleitrach for a three-year term. Pepy's integration into the board is expected to bolster the company's governance and strategic oversight capabilities.

Committee Leadership Realignment: Guillaume Pepy's arrival triggered a immediate reshuffling of key board committee leadership. He was immediately named Chairman of the Compensation and Nominating Committee, and simultaneously joined both the Ethics and ESG Committee and the Audit Committee. Concurrently, Bénédicte Hautefort was elevated to the role of Chairman of the Ethics and ESG Committee, filling the vacancy left by Michel Bleitrach's departure.

Family Retention on the Supervisory Board: While independent voices were refreshed, the Decaux family maintained its strong foundational grip on the Supervisory Board's overarching direction. Gérard Degonse and Jean-Pierre Decaux both saw their mandates renewed for an additional year. Their renewals are conducted on an annual basis in accordance with Article 16 of the articles of association, which applies to board members aged 75 or older.

Second-Level Management Overhaul in ANZ: Plausibly reflecting a localized push to accelerate regional monetization, JCDecaux completely reshaped its executive leadership team in the Australia and New Zealand (ANZ) market in early 2026. John Harris was elevated to Chief Sales Officer after a 19-year tenure with the company, taking charge of national sales, trading, and programmatic initiatives. This move underscores the company's focus on elevating seasoned internal talent to drive advanced digital sales.

Product and Strategy Leadership Additions in ANZ: Alongside the sales leadership shift, the ANZ division appointed Jemma Enright as Executive General Manager of Product. Enright previously led the airport product strategy and brings 25 years of media and innovation experience to the table. Furthermore, Eddie Foo was appointed as Executive General Manager of Strategy, rounding out a comprehensive trio of hires designed to support the region's aggressive performance and growth agenda.

Data Leadership Expansion: While occurring slightly before the 12-month window (late 2023), the appointment of Sylvain Le Borgne as Group Chief Data Officer remains a defining factor in the company's current operational culture. His mandate actively drives the internal digital transformation, accelerating data usage across business lines to ensure greater operating efficiency. This role acts as the central nervous system for JCDecaux's programmatic push and AI integration.

As of December 31, 2025, the total global headcount stood at 11,320 employees, representing a year-over-year decrease of 1.0% (114 people). Management explicitly noted that this reduction reflects optimized resource management tied to the digital transformation, which is concurrently driving an increase in more highly qualified, technical employee profiles.

In 2025, the global employee turnover rate was 13.8%, a marginal increase from the 13.4% recorded in 2024. This uptick followed a successful period of proactive retention policies—particularly in the US and UK—that had previously driven the rate down from 14.5% in 2023.

To combat this, JCDecaux formalized a comprehensive Group-wide Social Policy focused on standardizing core social practices globally. This centralized approach actively replaced a previously decentralized HR strategy, ensuring all employees benefit from unified career development frameworks.

In 2025, the percentage of women serving on JCDecaux's executive management committees reached 37.0%, a meaningful improvement from 34.4% in 2024. This progress aligns directly with the company's strengthened Gender Balance Plan, which mandates achieving and maintaining female representation of at least 40% in governing bodies by 2030.

Safety Culture ments: Beyond corporate offices, the culture of safety for field workers has seen drastic improvements over the past year. The frequency rate of workplace accidents fell to 12.0 accidents per million hours worked in 2025, down significantly from 14.9 in the prior year. This 37.5% reduction compared to 2019 levels is attributed to new prevention practices and heightened safety performances in key markets like Brazil, the US, and Spain.

Alignment of Executive Pay with ESG: Plausibly over the next 36 months, executive behavior will be increasingly dictated by non-financial metrics. Section 5: Change in priorities/ambition

Accelerated Financial Ambitions: The most striking shift in JCDecaux's internal ambition over the past 12 months is the accelerated delivery of its medium-term targets. Management proudly highlighted to the market that the group conquered its stated 2026 financial targets—which mandated an operating margin above 20% and free cash flow exceeding €300 million—a full year ahead of schedule.

Elevated Margin Targets: Following this early success, the Co-CEOs immediately moved the goalposts higher, demonstrating a relentless, ratcheting intensity regarding profitability. Verbal commentary from the latest earnings transcripts reveals a new internal drive to push the operating margin rate "above 21% and going to 22%" while still absorbing the costs of ramping up new contracts.

The "DV360 of Out-of-Home": JCDecaux's technological ambition has fundamentally evolved from simply digitizing screens to wanting to own the underlying plumbing of the entire industry. The Co-CEOs explicitly articulated their goal to become the "DV 360 of out-of-home" by aggressively scaling their proprietary VIOOH supply-side platform (SSP) and Displayce demand-side platform (DSP).

Programmatic Penetration Urgency: Management's commentary conveys an intense urgency to capture omnichannel digital ad budgets that traditionally bypassed outdoor media. To this end, they have set a hard, ambitious target to double programmatic penetration from its current level of approximately 11% of digital revenue to well over 20% in the medium term.

Fear of Value Leakage: The intensity behind this programmatic push is driven by a deep-seated fear of value leakage to third-party tech intermediaries. The CEO bluntly warned that "if we had not moved up the value chain... we would leave a significant share of value on the table," underscoring why building proprietary ad-tech became an existential priority.

Retail Media as a New Frontier: The company's growth ambition has aggressively expanded beyond traditional transit and street furniture into the booming retail media sector. This shift in priority is epitomized by the massive new strategic alliance with Carrefour and Carmila ("Unlimitail") to deploy screens across 252 European malls, directly tying physical out-of-home assets to granular shopper transaction data.

AI as an Immediate Operational Mandate: Artificial intelligence is no longer treated as a distant, experimental concept; it has been rapidly institutionalized into the company's daily behaviors. Management commentary stresses that AI is now viewed as an essential, immediate enabler for campaign planning, automated creative checks, and yield optimization.

Productivity Over Pure Scale: The overarching operational behavior has pivoted from chasing pure top-line revenue growth to enforcing strict, leveraged productivity. This cultural shift was evident when management noted that a recent period of organic revenue growth resulted in operating margins expanding at more than double that rate, signaling a deeply ingrained focus on cost efficiency.

New Internal Conversion Benchmarks: To ensure this productivity mindset permeates the organization, leadership has established rigorous new internal performance standards. Achieving a 75%-plus conversion rate of incremental revenue directly into operating margin is now actively used internally as a baseline behavioral benchmark for regional managers.

Disciplined Capital Allocation: The behavioral approach to capital expenditure has become highly regimented and protective of cash generation. The Co-CEOs continually reiterate their strict commitment to keeping global CapEx constrained within a narrow band of 7% to 8% of total revenue.

Targeted Digitization Standards: Within that strict CapEx envelope, the company has abandoned any ambitions for blanket, uncalculated screen rollouts. The standard operating procedure now dictates highly selective digitization, meaning only premium, high-ROI sites are converted, while legacy analog sites in mature markets like France are actively rationalized to optimize municipal rent structures.

Ruthless Capital Recycling: Management's behavioral willingness to ruthlessly recycle capital from mature, low-growth assets into high-growth digital infrastructure represents a major shift.
