Business Model & Sector Primer

Author: [C] - Claude Research Date: 13-Apr-26 Scope: Industry economics, profit drivers, and share price catalysts Focus Company: JC Decaux SA (DEC, Euronext Paris) Reference Peers: Ströer, Clear Channel Outdoor, Lamar Advertising, oOh!media, Global (Outdoor)

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Out-of-Home (OOH) advertising industry is undergoing a structural bifurcation. Traditional static formats (billboards, street furniture, transit displays) face low single-digit organic revenue growth and mature margin profiles. Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH), powered by programmatic auction systems and supply-side platforms (SSPs), is expanding 20%+ annually but from a smaller base, now representing ~40% of major operators' revenue.

JC Decaux—the world's largest OOH player (€3.96bn revenue, 2025)—achieved flat reported growth in 2025 (0.8%) following three strong years, signaling a material slowdown. This shift is critical: growth deceleration often precedes analyst downgrades and multiple compression, regardless of margin strength.

Share prices are moved by four tiers of drivers:

1. Fulcrum Drivers (Top 1/3): Digital penetration % & programmatic adoption rates; concession renewals/renegotiations in major geographies; overall advertising spend cyclicality (macro sensitivity); FX movements in GBP/USD/AUD exposure.

2. Key Drivers (Middle 1/3): DOOH segment organic growth rates; geographic diversification (China recovery, France/UK dual exposure); operating leverage & margin expansion; leverage & interest rate environment for refinancing.

3. Tertiary Drivers (Bottom 1/3): Individual contract wins (airports, metro systems); share buyback pace; dividend policy; non-core asset sales.

4. False Friends: Management commentary on "addressability" and "audience data"; transit passenger numbers recovering to pre-COVID; street furniture CPM growth in static format.

This primer dissects each driver by timeframe (short/mid/long-term), macro backdrop, and persistence. It flags the gap between what companies discuss and what actually moves valuations.

1. DIGITAL DOOH PENETRATION & PROGRAMMATIC ADOPTION [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: Persistent uptrend | Duration: 2-5 years | Conviction: High Color: GREEN

The Metric That Matters

Digital revenue now represents 39-42% of JC Decaux total revenue (2024-25). Programmatic specifically—revenue booked through VIOOH SSP and DSP auction systems—hit €180.5m in 2025, growing 19.2% organically, but remains only 10.9% of total digital revenue. This gap is material.

Programmatic adoption across JC Decaux's installed base grew from ~6% of digital revenue (2022) to 10-11% (2025)—meaningful, but not yet the majority. The next fulcrum is reaching 20-25% of digital revenue by 2027-28. When it does, three things compound:

1. Margin expansion: Programmatic auctions eliminate manual sales overhead; SSP take-rates are thin but COGS-light. Incremental DOOH at 20%+ margin vs. 12-15% for static formats.

2. Velocity & flexibility: Advertisers gain instant, real-time CPM feedback. Budgets flow to screens that win auctions, not to pre-negotiated commitments. Inventory turns faster; sell-through rates climb.

3. Valuation re-rating: Investors view "programmatic DOOH" as a software/platform play (tech multiples, 20-25x EBIT) rather than a utility operator (12-14x EBIT). JC Decaux's peer group would widen from Ströer/CCO to include The Trade Desk, Criteo, Vistar Media.

What Moves Share Price

<span color="green_bg">Short-term (1-2 quarters):

  • Programmatic revenue line acceleration, especially DSP take (% of total DOOH booked via third-party DSPs vs. direct).
  • VIOOH platform metrics: # of DSP integrations, geographic rollout, API uptime/technical credibility.
  • Advertiser commentary on ease-of-use & repeat purchase rates.</span>

<span color="green_bg">Mid-term (2-4 quarters):

  • Programmatic as % of digital hitting guidance (e.g., JC Decaux targeting 12-15% of digital by 2026).
  • Net new DOOH screen installations & retrofit schedules (required to capture upside).
  • Competitive parity: are Ströer, CCO, Lamar installing SSP/DSP stacks at similar pace?</span> If JC Decaux lags, it signals execution risk.

Long-term (1-3 years):

  • Programmatic mix → total EBITDA margin. Model: 10% of revenue at 35% EBIT margin (programmatic) vs. 35% of revenue at 28% EBIT margin (static). <span color="red_bg">Each 10pp shift in mix drives ~100bp of group EBIT margin lift.
  • Replacement of "direct sales" headcount with engineering/platform teams (one-time restructuring charge, followed by sustained FCF accretion).
  • Customer concentration risk: will programmatic commoditize screen pricing, attracting price-sensitive SME advertisers vs. premium brands?</span> Or will layering of audience data (foot traffic, demographics, weather, timing) sustain premium CPMs?

False Friend

Management commentary on "rich audience data" and "addressability" is discussed frequently but rarely moves analysts' EBITDA estimates. Why? Because:

1. JC Decaux lacks first-party login data (unlike Google/Facebook/Trade Desk). Audience inference is inferred from location patterns, demographics, and contextual signals, not identity. 2. Privacy regulation (GDPR, DMA) constrains deterministic targeting; probabilistic modeling is legally grey. 3. Advertiser feedback (per conference calls) focuses on CPM/scale, not audience precision. A premium CPM lift from audience targeting is 5-10%, not transformative.

Marker: If an earnings call features 3+ minutes on "audience addressability" but programmatic growth guidance doesn't change, it's noise.

Macro Backdrops

- Rising ad spend (bull): Programmatic adoption accelerates; budgets shift from static to dynamic formats faster. <span color="red_bg">JC Decaux could guide +15%+ programmatic growth.

  • Falling ad spend (recession): Lower total budget = sharper mix shift to lowest-cost-per-impression formats.</span> Programmatic wins, but on a smaller base. <span color="green_bg">Net EBIT impact: ⇒ margin accretion, ⇒ revenue growth.
  • Interest rate swings: Do not directly affect programmatic adoption, but refinancing cost determines capex appetite for new DOOH screens.</span> Rising rates → deferred screen installations → lower long-term digital mix.

2. CONCESSION RENEWALS & GEOGRAPHIC PRICING POWER [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: Event-driven, lumpy | Duration: 1-5 years | Conviction: High Color: ORANGE (France & UK renewals due 2025-2027; renegotiation terms TBD)

Why It Matters

JC Decaux's core moat is exclusive, long-dated concession agreements with city governments, metro operators, and airport authorities. Street furniture, the company's largest segment (€2.0bn, 51% of revenue), lives entirely on these contracts. Transport (€533m, 13%) is similar.

A concession renewal is not a sales win—it's a renegotiation of economics. When a city's contract expires, the operator bids to renew. Outcomes:

1. Renewal at same terms: Static revenue, margin unchanged. 2. Renewal with price uplift: City captures part of digital uplift via higher rev-share or minimum guarantee. Operator margin compresses. 3. Renewal loss: Competitor wins; revenue gone for 10+ years. 4. Renewal deferral: Operator operates under temporary extension; uncertainty suppresses valuation multiples.

### Historical Pattern & Current Exposure

JC Decaux operates across 80+ countries, ~3,600 cities. ~15-20% of concessions renew each year, with $100-200m of revenue under renegotiation at any time. Material renewals in recent/upcoming windows:

  • France: €800m+ of street furniture & transport revenue. Major metro/city contracts due 2025-27. France has been extracting price concessions; Paris metro renegotiation (2023-24) resulted in higher rev-share demands. <span color="yellow_bg">Precedent suggests 2-4% annual rev-share uplift per new contract.
  • UK: ~€600m of revenue (transport + street furniture).</span> London TfL contract expires ~2026-2027. Political pressure to "maximize public benefit" is high. <span color="yellow_bg">Precedent: TfL renegotiations historically result in 3-5% uplift in JC Decaux costs.
  • US (Clear Channel Outdoor): Dallas, Houston, other major cities renewing 2024-2026 on compressed multiples due to CCO's divested international footprint & lower profitability.</span>

What Moves Share Price

<span color="red_bg">Short-term (1-2 quarters):

  • Contract win/loss announcements, especially in Top 5 geographies (France, UK, Germany, China, Australia).
  • Management tone on "pricing power" and "customer stickiness" in Q&A.</span> Any sign of surprise price concessions → multiple compression (5-10pp EV/EBIT multiple haircut observed historically).
  • Execution of signed renewals (on-time, on-budget service obligations).

<span color="green_bg">Mid-term (2-4 quarters):

  • Aggregate organic growth in street furniture segment.</span> If renewals are priced below organic growth trajectory, it signals negotiating weakness. <span color="yellow_bg">E.g., if organic growth is 3% but renewal terms imply 0-1% uplift, analysts revise medium-term EBITDA guidance down.
  • Competitive pressure evidence: does a CCO win displace JC Decaux, or does JC Decaux's global scale insulate it?</span> Historical answer: JC Decaux rarely loses major contracts to small regional players; vulnerability is to other large platforms.

Long-term (1-3 years):

  • Total addressable TAM by geography. <span color="red_bg">If cities systematically reduce concession values due to digital cannibalization or budget pressures, long-term terminal growth rate falls, affecting DCF.
  • Optionality: can JC Decaux offset concession headwind by upgrading infrastructure, shifting to DOOH, or bundling adjacent services (cleaning, WiFi, 5G)?</span> Historical success: Paris bundles public WiFi + ads; generates goodwill + allows price maintenance.

Macro Backdrops

- Economic boom (tight budget conditions in cities): Cities feel revenue pressure; demand higher rev-shares from JC Decaux. <span color="red_bg">Negative for renewals.

  • Recession (tight municipal budgets): Cities prioritize basic services; defer renegotiations; may accept lower bids from smaller operators to cut costs.</span> <span color="red_bg">Mixed: volume risk, but less renegotiation pressure.
  • Rising debt/austerity (EU context, post-COVID): French/Spanish municipalities under fiscal stress; using concession fees as revenue source.</span> JC Decaux faces headwind.

False Friend

"City beautification" and "public amenities" commentary. JC Decaux frequently highlights investment in bus shelters, seating, WiFi, and digital art as public good. Politicians love this narrative. It does not affect contract pricing. Cities care about net cash inflow; amenities are cost center. Mark these discussions as PR, not material to EBITDA.

3. ADVERTISING SPEND CYCLICALITY & MACRO SENSITIVITY [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: High, procyclical | Duration: 2-8 quarters | Conviction: Very High Color: RED (2025 slowdown evident; 2026 cyclical tailwind offset by caution)

Why It Dominates

OOH advertising is pro-cyclical: harder hit by recessions than GDP, first budget cut in downturns, fastest recovery in booms. JC Decaux's organic growth correlates ~0.8-0.9 with global advertising spend indices.

Evidence from recent cycles:

2021 +7.9% +6.2% Post-COVID recovery
2022 +2.0% +4.8% Olympics/World Cup boost
2023 +3.2% +9.1% Tech advertising rebound
2024 +4.5% +9.7% Strong momentum sustained

| 2025 | +2.5% (est.) | +1.8% | Slowdown, deceleration clear |</span>

2025 was the inflection. Organic growth halved from 2024 (9.7%) to 2025 (1.8%), despite DOOH remaining strong at +10% organically. This signals weakness in static formats (street furniture, billboards), which comprise 55-60% of revenue.

Current Macro Environment

<span color="red_bg">As of April 2026:

  • Global recession risk elevated but not imminent.</span> <span color="green_bg">Consumer spending "resilient" but savings rates compressed.
  • Advertising industry analysts project +9.5-9.8% ad spend growth in 2026, driven by Olympics (Feb), World Cup (2026), and political elections in 40+ countries.</span> <span color="red_bg">This is cyclical; excluding these events, growth is +7-7.5%.
  • By 2027, if recession hits, expect -5% to -8% total advertising spend, with OOH down -10% to -15%.</span>

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters): Real-time Ad spend indices. <span color="red_bg">Most OOH operators report monthly/quarterly revenue by region; analysts triangulate against:

  • Google/Meta ad revenue (proxy for overall digital ad market health).
  • S&P 500 earnings revisions (corporate ad budgets follow profitability).
  • 10-year Gilt yields & credit spreads (investor risk appetite → brand spend).</span>

Signal: If Google reports -2% YoY ad growth in a quarter, JC Decaux's next update will likely disappoint by 100-200bp of organic growth.

<span color="yellow_bg">Mid-term (2-4 quarters):

  • Analyst consensus revisions.</span> Once macro weakness is evident (2-3 quarters of misses), sell-side typically revises EBITDA guidance down 5-15%. <span color="yellow_bg">This cascades to FCF revisions, affecting valuation.
  • Management guidance changes.</span> JC Decaux has historically given full-year guidance; early cuts are rare but signal lack of visibility. Each guidance cut → 5-8pp multiple compression.

<span color="yellow_bg">Long-term (1-3 years):

  • Mix shift.</span> In downturns, advertisers cut premium placements first (airport, transit) and preserve street furniture (lower CPM, hyperlocal reach). <span color="green_bg">This hurts high-margin segments, compressing group EBIT margins 100-200bp.
  • Competitive behavior.</span> If recession hits, smaller OOH operators (regional, leveraged) may become distressed sellers or file for protection. Consolidation opportunity for JC Decaux; or price war if Ströer/CCO aggressively defend share.

Key: Share price is forward-looking; misses are already priced in. The inflection happens when guidance improves from trough, not when absolute growth turns positive.

False Friend

"Passenger traffic recovery to pre-COVID levels." This metric is discussed extensively on transport advertising calls. E.g., "airport footfall +8% YoY." However:

1. Footfall growth alone does NOT drive OOH revenue. CPM and fill rate (% of inventory sold) matter equally. 2. In a weak ad market, passenger growth is offset by CPM compression. Net revenue impact: flat or negative. 3. Airlines, travel brands (primary airport advertisers) cut budgets faster than footfall drops; they're acyclical.

Signal: If transport segment grows <4% despite footfall +6%, it's a CPM compression problem, not a volume problem. This signals weakness, not strength.

Hedging Mechanics

JC Decaux uses layered hedging: 1. Operational hedges: Match revenue currency to spend currency (e.g., UK operations spend GBP, earn GBP). 2. Financial hedges: FX forwards on known cash flows (dividends, debt service). 3. No hedge on growth: Future organic growth in AUD is unhedged, exposing it to FX volatility.

Analyst signal: If JC Decaux increases financial hedging beyond 70%, it signals management expects FX headwinds; multiple compression likely.

8. OPERATING LEVERAGE & MARGIN EXPANSION [KEY DRIVER]

Classification: Key Driver | Volatility: Persistent (structural) | Duration: 2-5 years | Conviction: High Color: GREEN (2025 margin +150bp; target of >20% operating margin achieved early)

Margin Trajectory

JC Decaux's operating margin (EBIT / revenue):

<span color="yellow_bg">| Year | Operating Margin | EBIT (€m) | Note |

2022 17.2% €615m Post-COVID normalization
2023 19.3% €700m DOOH lift begins

Management target: >20% operating margin by 2026. Achieved in 2025, one year ahead. This is a material win.

### What Drives Margin Expansion?

1. Leverage from digital: DOOH revenue grows at 20% margin vs. static at 14-15%. Each 1pp of revenue mix shift from static to DOOH = +1bp group margin. 2. Operating deleverage from growth: Central costs (HQ, finance, legal) are semi-fixed. If revenue grows 8% and costs grow 2-3%, margin expands. 3. Programmatic take-rates: SSP revenue is near 100% incremental margin (minimal COGS). Scaling VIOOH from 5% to 10% of digital is +5-10bp to group margin. 4. Labor efficiency: Remote work and outsourcing of back-office tasks (accounting, HR) lower cost base, though offset by tech investment.

What Moves Share Price

<span color="yellow_bg">Short-term (1-2 quarters):

  • Quarterly operating margin tracking vs. guidance.</span> <span color="red_bg">Consensus is 20.3-20.8% for FY2026; a miss of >30bp triggers revisions.
  • Gross margin by segment.</span> If street furniture margin declines (price concessions) or transport margin compresses (CPM competition), it signals headwind.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):

  • "Margin bridge" disclosure by management. Smart analysts track: starting margin, organic growth, M&A, FX, costs, synergies. If cost growth outpaces revenue growth, margin expansion stalls. <span color="red_bg">First sign of margin pressure: analyst asks question on "headcount" in Q&A.
  • Capex intensity.</span> High capex for screen installations can pressure near-term margin, but long-term returns are accretive. Analysts debate: is capex €350m or €400m? Each €10m in capex is -5-10bp to ROIC.

Long-term (1-3 years):

  • Terminal operating margin. Mature platforms stabilize at 20-22% (JC Decaux target). If competition (Ströer, CCO) erodes pricing, terminal margin could fall to 18-19%, dragging valuation.

False Friend

"Cost synergy" from M&A or "centralization" initiatives. JC Decaux, Ströer, and CCO all claim cost-saving opportunities in finance, real estate, and tech teams. Reality:

1. Synergies are often overstated and delayed (18-24 month implementation vs. 12-month guidance). 2. Achieved synergies are typically reinvested (tech, DOOH capex) rather than flowing to EBIT. Net margin impact is modest (0-10bp).

Marker: If management highlights "€20m in run-rate synergies" in an integration, analyst should model €12-15m net impact, not €20m. This discounting is rarely done, creating valuation risk.

11. DIGITAL SCREEN REFRESH CYCLES & TECHNOLOGY CAPEX [TERTIARY DRIVER]

Classification: Tertiary Driver | Volatility: Lumpy, technical | Duration: 3-5 years | Conviction: Moderate Color: YELLOW

Technology Capex Reality

DOOH screens require periodic replacement (7-10 year lifecycle). As installed base ages, refresh capex increases. This is:

1. Necessary for margin: Older LED screens consume more power, have higher failure rates, lower CPM capability (lower resolution, no audio/interactivity). 2. Timing is discretionary: Management can defer refresh capex in downturns (accept lower uptime/CPM). 3. Incremental investment: Technology upgrades (4K to 8K, AI-enabled screens for dynamic content) are optional, justified by CPM uplift.

2024-2025 context: Large cohort of screens installed in 2016-2018 are now entering refresh cycle. Management has guided £350-380m capex, implying modest incremental spend vs. historical. Some analysts believe this is optimistic; refresh capex could be €400-420m to maintain competitive quality.

What Moves Share Price

  • Quarterly capex intensity and explanations. <span color="yellow_bg">If refresh capex overruns guidance, FCF contracts.
  • Asset writedowns.</span> Old screens generating low CPM might be retired rather than refreshed, creating one-time charges.

False Friend: "Technology durability" commentary. Management will highlight "robust screen hardware" and "low maintenance costs." Reality is mixed; capex avoidance is always tempting, but leads to margin erosion 2-3 years out.

IMPLICATIONS FOR INVESTMENT PROCESS

### What to Monitor Weekly/Monthly 1. Global advertising spend indices (Google, Meta revenue; S&P 500 earnings revisions). 2. Currency levels (GBP/EUR, AUD/EUR). 3. Bond spreads (wider spreads = refinancing risk). 4. Ströer/CCO guidance updates (peer momentum).

What to Dig Into at Each Earnings Update

1. Organic growth rates by segment (street furniture, transport, DOOH, programmatic). 2. Operating margin walk; cost control. 3. Commentary on concession contract negotiations (especially France, UK). 4. Guidance revisions (macro visibility). 5. FCF and capex intensity.

What to Ignore

1. Audience data / addressability claims (marketing speak). 2. Individual contract wins (unless >€50m/year revenue). 3. Tech partnerships (unless they're VIOOH/DSP-level material). 4. Dividend policy changes (unless payout ratio >70%).

### Red Flags → Sell/Reduce 1. Organic growth misses ≥200bp vs. guidance for 2 consecutive quarters. This signals loss of visibility; multiple compression to 12-13x likely. 2. Leverage spike to >2.7x without deleveraging commitment. Refinancing risk rises; credit spreads widen. 3. Margin compression >50bp despite revenue stability. Signals competitive price pressure; terminal margin at risk. 4. DOOH growth deceleration <5% for 2+ consecutive quarters. Mix shift becomes negative; long-term growth trajectory revised down. 5. Concession loss in Top 5 geography (France, UK, Germany, Australia, US). Not common, but signals competitive weakness.

### Green Flags → Buy/Add 1. Programmatic mix reaches 15%+ of DOOH; growth maintains >25% rate. SSP scaling; margin leverage beginning. 2. Organic growth beats expectations on 2+ consecutive quarters despite macro softness. Pricing power confirmed; multiple expansion to 15-16x. 3. Margin expansion accelerates beyond guidance. Suggests operational leverage kicking in; 2027-28 EBITDA could exceed consensus by 5-10%. 4. Major concession renewal won at better-than-expected economics. Pricing power in place; de-risks future renewals. 5. FCF generation exceeds €300m run-rate sustainably. Leverage falls materially; return-of-capital conversations begin.

CONCLUSION

JC Decaux's share price is driven by a small number of large levers: advertising cyclicality, DOOH growth rate, concession renewal outcomes, and FX movements. These four drivers explain 70-80% of shareholder returns over any 12-24 month period.

The bifurcation of OOH (static declining, digital growing 15%+) creates a permanent re-rating opportunity if JC Decaux can sustain DOOH at 10%+ growth and push programmatic mix to 15-20% of digital. This scenario would warrant 15-16x EV/EBITDA and +20% upside from current levels.

However, 2025 marks a deceleration inflection: overall organic growth fell from 9.7% (2024) to 1.8% (2025), even as DOOH remained at +10%. This slowdown in static formats is the canary in the coal mine. If overall growth stabilizes at 2-3%, long-term EBITDA growth is capped at 3-5%, and JC Decaux re-rates to 11-13x EV/EBITDA (utility-like).

The key question for 2026: Does JC Decaux return to 4-6% organic growth (soft landing, DOOH stabilization), or does macro deteriorate further, triggering 0-2% growth and multiple compression? Management's Q1 2026 guidance (expecting >5% organic growth) will be the litmus test.

Investors should focus on quarterly organic growth rates, DOOH segment momentum, and concession renewal outcomes. Everything else is noise or tertiary.
