6. Counter-Arguments to Bull Case

Bull thesis: SATS is the dominant Nordic fitness operator with structural market share.

Bear rebuttal: SATS's premium/mid-market positioning sits in the most competitive segment. Budget operators (Evo Fitness, Fresh Fitness at lower price points) encroach from below. SATS's response — expanding into yoga via HiYoga brand and Fresh Fitness budget concept — is logical but creates brand complexity and potential cannibalisation.

Bull thesis: Leverage at 1.4x is conservative; company is deleveraging into a dividend/buyback regime.

Bear rebuttal: The 1.4x leverage is pre-IFRS 16. SATS's cost of debt is floating or near-floating in a market where Norwegian and Swedish central banks have maintained elevated rates. Interest rate sensitivity on the debt facility is meaningful. A 100bps rate increase adds approximately NOK 15-20m of annual interest cost at a NOK 1.5bn debt load, which is material against NOK 526m EBIT (FY2024).

Bull thesis: Strong member growth (755,000+) and improving visit frequency show brand loyalty.

Bear rebuttal: SATS actively manages churn by monitoring inactive members and intervening. This is good management but also signals that churn is a live risk. The key metric — members inactive for more than two weeks — requires active intervention. If digital engagement tools fail or management focus shifts, churn could accelerate. Nordic fitness penetration is already relatively high; the total addressable market for incremental members is less elastic than the bull case assumes.

Bull thesis: Norway/Sweden are high-income markets with stable fitness culture.

Bear rebuttal: Nordic consumer confidence is correlated with energy prices, housing markets, and real wage growth. A sharp deceleration in Norwegian housing (significant correction risk given mortgage market dynamics) could suppress discretionary spending including gym memberships. SATS is not immune to macro shocks.
