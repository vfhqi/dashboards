KEY FINDINGS

1. The primary share price driver is CMD credibility: the market's confidence that SATS will reach NOK 1.1bn EBITDA in a specified timeframe. Each quarterly EBITDA print that stays "in line" with the CMD trajectory extends the re-rating opportunity. Each miss compresses the multiple.

2. ARPM compounding is the engine of the CMD story. If ARPM compounds at 5–6% annually and member growth is flat to modestly positive, the entire revenue and margin expansion story works mathematically. ARPM is the swing variable.

3. The dividend is a yield anchor. At NOK 1.34/share annualised (extrapolating the H2-25 distribution), the yield of approximately 3.1% provides valuation support that BFIT and GYM cannot match at this stage of their capital return cycle.

4. FCF normalisation post-remodelling capex is a medium-term upside catalyst. When the club remodelling programme matures, maintenance capex will decline as a percentage of revenue, driving further FCF improvement beyond the current NOK 506m run rate.

5. The Sweden recovery has contributed meaningfully to FY2025 results. Continued Sweden margin expansion toward Norway-level economics (Norway implicit EBITDA margin approaching 20%) could add 100–200 basis points to the group margin over FY2026–27.

6. Treningshelse is the most underappreciated risk to the bull case. Its Norwegian expansion strategy could slow SATS's new member acquisition in Norway and — in a worst case — trigger a mild margin compression in the domestic market.

7. The Q1-2026 result (30 April 2026) is the most important near-term catalyst. Management pre-guided that member growth may soften. If ARPM compensates and EBITDA still grows year-on-year, the bull case survives intact.

8. The 12-month MA200 uptrend (the longest in the gym trio) and 43.1 NOK price at only 10% above the MA200 confirm the technical underpinning of the fundamental thesis. The market has been consistently buying weakness.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SATS AS has six identifiable key share price drivers for the next 24 months. They are not equal in magnitude or probability. The primary driver — CMD credibility — is binary in the sense that quarterly misses or beats compound non-linearly. The supporting drivers (ARPM, dividend, FCF, Sweden, market share) are incremental compounders that reinforce or undermine the primary thesis.

Stack rank by expected 24-month impact:

1. CMD NOK 1.1bn EBITDA trajectory (highest impact, binary nature)

2. ARPM compounding (primary mechanical driver of CMD path)

3. FCF normalisation and dividend sustainability (yield anchor + capital efficiency)

4. Sweden recovery continuation (margin uplift lever)

5. Treningshelse competitive response (downside risk containment)

6. Market share consolidation in Nordics (upside optionality)

Overall thesis rating: GREEN — dominant technical and fundamental alignment. Differentiated macro backdrop. CMD on track. First dividend paid. The investment case is credible.

DRIVER #1: NOK 1.1BN EBITDA CMD TARGET — PATHWAY AND CREDIBILITY

Definition: The mid-term EBITDA ambition set at the CMD (7 May 2025) to reach NOK 1.1bn before IFRS 16 from the FY2025 base of NOK 871m.

Plausibility: HIGH. The pathway is through ARPM compounding, operating leverage, 8–12 new clubs per year, and club remodelling. No transformational M&A required. The FY2025 operating leverage (18% EBITDA growth on 9% revenue growth) has already demonstrated the mechanics. At the current rate (12–15% EBITDA CAGR), the target is achievable in FY2027.

Guidance: No formal year has been attached to the target. This is a deliberate management choice that avoids hard accountability but also avoids setting an aggressive commitment that market conditions might invalidate.

Credibility factors: Three consecutive quarters (Q2-Q4 2025) where management specifically cited "in line with NOK 1.1bn ambition" in earnings releases. The CMD presenters (CEO Sondre Gravir, CFO Cecilie Elde) are the same management team that has consistently delivered in FY2024 and FY2025. The strategy is organic and within demonstrated competency.

Bear case: The gap between FY2025 actual (NOK 871m) and target (NOK 1.1bn) is 26%. If cost inflation accelerates (energy, wages, club leases are the key inputs), if Sweden moderates, or if Treningshelse forces pricing concessions in Norway, the EBITDA growth trajectory could slow to 8–10% CAGR. At 8% CAGR, the target is reached in FY2029 — a 4-year horizon that would require material re-rating of the stock's growth multiple.

Monitoring metrics: Quarterly EBITDA before IFRS 16 trend. Management qualitative language ("on track" vs "reassessing"). Any CMD revision.

DRIVER #2: ARPM COMPOUNDING — PRICING POWER AND UPSELL

Definition: Average Revenue Per Member per month. FY2025 year-end trajectory: NOK 630 Q4-25 (blended), Norway NOK 607, Sweden NOK 651.

Guidance: No formal ARPM target. CMD implies ARPM must grow at 4–6% per annum to support the NOK 1.1bn EBITDA target through revenue growth.

Plausibility: HIGH. ARPM has grown consistently above CPI in Norway (approximately 2–3% inflation) and at a premium to the global gym industry. SATS's pricing power is structural: it operates at the mid-to-premium tier where consumers are less price-sensitive, corporate wellness contracts provide base-load stability, and the digital platform (AI coaching, app integration) provides upsell vectors. The 8% ARPM growth in Sweden in Q4-25 is the most impressive recent data point — a market where the physical footprint was actually shrinking.

Bear case: If the 2026 price increase (the one management flagged as potentially dampening Q1-2026 member growth) causes churn above approximately 3–5% of the member base, the member count would decline and partially offset ARPM gains. At member count below 730,000 combined with ARPM growth of only 3%, revenue growth slows to 1–2%. This is the most plausible near-term risk.

Monitoring metrics: Q1-2026 ARPM (due 30 April 2026). Q1-2026 net member count. Country-level ARPM splits (Norway and Sweden are the key indicators).

DRIVER #3: DIVIDEND SUSTAINABILITY AND GROWTH — YIELD ATTRACTION

Definition: SATS initiated dividends in H2-2025 at NOK 0.67/share (61% payout ratio). Policy: at least 50% of net profit via dividends and buybacks.

Guidance: The dividend policy is formal and stated. No specific annual dividend growth target, but the policy implies dividend growth tracks net profit growth.

Plausibility: HIGH. FCF of NOK 506m easily covers a NOK 1.34/share annualised dividend (approximately NOK 437m implied dividend on 326m shares). Net debt at 1.1x provides ample headroom. Share buybacks (3.8% of capital in 2025) provide additional yield optionality.

Yield calculation: At NOK 43.1/share and NOK 1.34 annualised dividend, yield = approximately 3.1%. This is materially above the Norwegian 10-year government bond rate (approximately 3.3–3.5% in early 2026) and competes effectively for income-oriented capital.

Bear case: If FCF declines (elevated capex year, NWC headwind, or EBITDA miss), the dividend could be cut or suspended. At NOK 506m FCF, the NOK 437m dividend implies a very thin FCF buffer. However, management has been clear that the business generates sufficient cash to sustain the policy, and the conservative leverage position provides a balance sheet backstop.

Monitoring metrics: FCF trajectory. Net debt trend. Any change to dividend policy language.

DRIVER #4: FCF CONVERSION — CAPEX NORMALISATION

Definition: After the current club remodelling programme is largely complete, maintenance capex should normalise to the CMD target of 5% of revenues, and the incremental FCF generation should accelerate.

Guidance: CMD maintenance capex guidance: ~5% of revenues. High cash conversion target: 55%.

Plausibility: MEDIUM-HIGH. Current cash conversion is already 58% — above target. As the remodelling programme matures, the elevated capex associated with club upgrades and relocations will reduce. The FCF trajectory should therefore improve further from the current NOK 506m base.

Bear case: If the club remodelling programme is extended, or if new club growth accelerates above 12 clubs per year, growth capex could remain elevated. This would not reduce EBITDA but would reduce FCF conversion, potentially limiting dividend growth and buyback capacity.

Monitoring metrics: Annual capex vs guidance. Distinction between maintenance capex and growth capex. FCF as a percentage of EBITDA.

DRIVER #5: TRENINGSHELSE COMPETITIVE RESPONSE — MARKET SHARE DEFENCE

Definition: Treningshelse Holding, operating 93+ Norwegian clubs under the Sporty brand with 115,000+ members, is the primary domestic challenger to SATS in Norway.

Guidance: SATS management has not formally commented on Treningshelse as a competitive threat in investor communications reviewed.

Plausibility of threat materialising: MEDIUM. Norway's gym market at 30%+ penetration is mature. In a mature market, incremental growth is share gain, not market expansion. Treningshelse is explicitly targeting market leadership. Its clubs operate at the sub-NOK 400/month tier — the budget end. Direct competitive overlap with SATS at NOK 600–700/month is limited. However, SATS also operates the Fresh Fitness budget brand in Norway, which directly competes with Treningshelse at the price-sensitive end.

Bear case: Aggressive Treningshelse new club openings in catchments adjacent to SATS clubs slow SATS's new member acquisition. SATS is forced to increase promotional activity, dampening net ARPM growth in Norway. This would be a multi-year structural headwind, not an immediate shock.

Monitoring metrics: Treningshelse club count and member growth announcements. Fresh Fitness brand performance. Norwegian market pricing surveys.

DRIVER #6: SWEDEN RECOVERY — RETURN TO GROWTH FROM RESTRUCTURED BASE

Definition: Sweden delivered 12% revenue growth and 25% Country EBITDA growth in Q4-25 from a 93-club base (reduced from 95 in 2024). ARPM grew 8% to NOK 651. Country EBITDA margin reached 18%.

Guidance: No formal Sweden-specific target. Sweden is managed within the group CMD framework.

Plausibility: HIGH. The portfolio optimisation has delivered clear results. The question is whether the Sweden margin can approach Norway-level economics (Norway implied EBITDA margin approaching 20%) from the current 18%. The mathematical uplift from Sweden margin improving by 200 basis points is approximately NOK 30–40m in group EBITDA — meaningful at the margin.

Bear case: Swedish economic weakness (higher interest rates, more exposed to European cyclicality than Norway) slows the ARPM growth trajectory. Sweden members are less wealthy and more price-sensitive than Norwegian members. A Swedish consumer downturn would test the 8% ARPM growth run rate.

Monitoring metrics: Sweden quarterly ARPM. Sweden Country EBITDA margin. Swedish economic data (consumer confidence, real wage growth).

SECTION 10: DRIVER INTERDEPENDENCIES

The six drivers are tightly interconnected. ARPM compounding (D2) is the prerequisite for CMD achievement (D1). CMD achievement underpins FCF generation (D4). FCF generation funds dividends and buybacks (D3). Sweden margin expansion (D6) directly contributes to group EBITDA, helping close the CMD gap (D1). Treningshelse defence (D5) is a pre-condition for Norwegian ARPM growth to continue unimpeded (D2). The chain: D5 → D2 → D1 → D4 → D3.

The critical dependency: ARPM (D2) is the linchpin. If ARPM growth stalls, every other driver weakens. The CMD target requires ARPM growth. FCF requires EBITDA growth driven by ARPM. Dividend sustainability requires FCF. The entire model is built on a pricing power assumption that has been validated in FY2025 but must be re-validated every quarter.

SECTION 15: THESIS FRAGILITY

The thesis is fragile in exactly one way: ARPM must keep growing. Everything else is a derivative of this. If ARPM growth stalls due to competitive pressure (Treningshelse), economic softness (Swedish consumer), or an over-aggressive 2026 price increase causing outsized churn, the CMD target becomes unreachable in the medium term. The stock's multiple is predicated on continued earnings compounding. Multiple compression risk on a stalled ARPM trajectory could be 20–25% downside from current levels.

All other risks (leverage, liquidity, management execution) are secondary and well-managed given the current balance sheet.

SECTION 17: SCEPTICAL / BEAR THESIS

The fullest bear case requires five things to go wrong simultaneously: (1) the 2026 price increase causes an unexpected spike in member churn (above 5% net attrition), (2) Treningshelse's Norwegian expansion accelerates faster than expected, forcing SATS to defensively reduce price increases, (3) the Swedish consumer weakens materially (housing market, rate exposure), compressing Sweden's 8% ARPM growth, (4) energy and wage inflation re-accelerates, preventing the operating leverage from flowing through to EBITDA, and (5) management revises the CMD timeline implicitly by declining to reaffirm the NOK 1.1bn ambition in FY2026 commentary.

No single one of these events alone is sufficient to break the investment case. Combined, they produce the bear scenario: EBITDA growth below 5% in FY2026, CMD credibility damaged, multiple compression, share price to NOK 35–38.

The probability of this combined scenario is low — approximately 15–20% in Watson's assessment. The base case (CMD on track, ARPM grows 4–6%, EBITDA grows 10–15%) has approximately 60% probability. The bull case (EBITDA reaches NOK 1.0bn in FY2026, earlier than expected) has approximately 20–25% probability.

Downside risk rating: YELLOW — material risks exist but are specific, identifiable, and currently not evidenced in reported data.
