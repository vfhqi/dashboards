AS IG Q01 | 06-May-26

Key Findings

  • MOCVD dominance under threat: [AS] Aixtron commands a 71–77% global market share in MOCVD systems, but faces rapidly emerging Chinese domestic competitors like AMEC capturing 4–12% share alongside legacy rival Veeco holding 8%; competitive intensity is intensifying as AMEC aggressively launches government-backed localization efforts.
  • Severe cyclical downturn: [AS] Revenue declined 12% from €633.2M in 2024 to €556.6M in 2025, while gross margins contracted from 44% to 40% and EBIT margins collapsed from 27.5% in 2023 to 18% in 2025; the 300 bps gross margin erosion highlights vulnerability to sudden cyclical downturns.
  • Single-pillar near-term narrative: [AS] Optoelectronics expected to double in 2026, driven by aggressive AI datacenter optical interconnect capacity expansions; however, this surge carries significant downside risk as optoelectronics revenue has historically never grown linearly and could result in severe over-ordering followed by a demand vacuum by 2027.
  • SiC market collapse: [AS] Management projects a catastrophic 70% year-over-year SiC revenue contraction for 2026 as Western EV markets digest excess capacity; structural slowdown in global EV adoption creates a prolonged headwind restricting SiC equipment revenue predictability over the next 36 months.
  • Structural margin deficit vs. peers: [AS] Aixtron's gross margins of 40–44% structurally trail higher-quality semi-cap names like BESI (63–65%) and ASML (51–52%), reflecting fundamental lack of pricing power; historical 5% revenue CAGR from 2008–2024 was the slowest among major WFE players versus the industry's 9%.
  • Valuation expansion disconnected from fundamentals: [AS] Share price rallied from a 52-week low of €8.45 to near €42, driven entirely by multiple expansion rather than earnings beats, pushing 2027 forward EV/EBITDA to approximately 19x — a 5-turn premium over historical average; zero room for execution missteps.
  • Multiple strategic execution risks: [AS] Geopolitical tensions and shifting US trade policies threaten global supply chains while AMEC aggressively targets compound semiconductor deposition through localization mandates; strategic pivot toward 300mm GaN tools risks stranding massive R&D investments if top-tier manufacturers select competing architectures.
  • Business model core: [AS] Aixtron designs, manufactures, and sells highly complex MOCVD systems used to deposit thin material layers onto semiconductor wafers; primary competitive advantage is its proprietary Planetary Reactor architecture, which allows for high-throughput batch processing with exceptional material efficiency.
  • Revenue model: [AS] Revenue is heavily concentrated in Asia (60% of 2025 sales), Americas and Europe at 20% each; 57% of equipment revenue came from power electronics (GaN and SiC), 23% from optoelectronics, 15% from LEDs; recurring services and spare parts reached 20% of total revenues in 2025.
  • Capital allocation shift: [AS] Primary near-term priority is rebuilding cash position following intense capex cycle; dividend cut to €0.15/share; €40M committed for new modular manufacturing facility in Malaysia to lower long-term unit costs; company structurally debt-free with €225M cash and €200M undrawn RCF.

Business Description, Strategy, and Organisational Structure

Aixtron SE is a leading global provider of deposition equipment, primarily supplying MOCVD and CVD systems to the compound semiconductor industry; the company's strategic objective is to maintain technological leadership in core markets while leveraging its platform strategy to diversify across uncorrelated end markets.

  • Core business: [AS] Aixtron SE is a leading global provider of deposition equipment, primarily supplying Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD) and Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) systems to the compound semiconductor industry.
  • Technology portfolio: [AS] The company's technology portfolio focuses on complex material deposition for end markets such as power electronics (Silicon Carbide and Gallium Nitride) and optoelectronics (lasers, Micro LEDs).
  • Governance structure: [AS] Structurally, Aixtron operates as a European Company (Societas Europaea) with a dual management system comprising an Executive Board for daily operations and a Supervisory Board for oversight.
  • Strategic objective: [AS] The company's strategic objective is to maintain technological leadership in core markets while leveraging its platform strategy to diversify across uncorrelated end markets like consumer electronics, AI data centers, and electromobility.
  • Headquarters and global footprint: [AS] Aixtron is headquartered in Herzogenrath, Germany, which serves as the central hub for R&D, manufacturing, sales, and service; the company maintains additional facilities in Cambridge (UK) for R&D and manufacturing, and sales/service offices in the USA, South Korea, China, Taiwan, and Japan.
  • Business model focus: [AS] The organisation operates a highly focused business model, deriving its competitive strength from clear technology leadership in fast-growing segments of the compound semiconductor market.
  • Key performance indicators: [AS] Management relies on order intake, revenue, gross margin, and EBIT margin as its most significant financial performance indicators to steer operations.
  • ESG integration: [AS] Aixtron integrates sustainability into its organisational structure, with environmental and social targets explicitly tied to the Executive Board's Long-Term Incentive (LTI) program.
  • R&D investment: [AS] A key component of Aixtron's operational strategy involves continuous, heavy investment in research and development, particularly for new equipment generations like 300mm GaN tools.
  • Operational efficiency: [AS] The company actively manages organisational efficiency through a standardised employee suggestion scheme and a Group-wide compliance management system led by a Chief Compliance Officer.
  • Competitive vulnerability [Bearish]: [AS] Aixtron's strategy relies heavily on maintaining an incumbent technological advantage, which may be vulnerable if competitors achieve breakthroughs in alternative deposition methods.
  • Portfolio dilution risk [Bearish]: [AS] The company's decision to maintain a broad technology portfolio across diverse markets could dilute focus and strain R&D resources compared to more specialised peers.
  • Labour execution risk [Bearish]: [AS] Aixtron's reliance on temporary agency workers to cover production peaks introduces operational execution risks if labour market conditions tighten.
  • Peer rating — A-: [AS] Aixtron holds a dominant market share in MOCVD (over 70%) and a leading position in SiC CVD, demonstrating superior technological execution compared to direct peers like Veeco; plausible rating changes would depend on Veeco or Chinese competitors successfully penetrating the 300mm GaN or 8-inch SiC markets.

  • GaN for AI power delivery: [AS] Gallium Nitride (GaN) is emerging as a critical secular trend for AI power delivery networks, as hyperscalers seek energy-efficient high-voltage direct current (HVDC) architectures.

  • 800V HVDC GaN catalyst: [AS] Jefferies notes that the eventual ramp of 800V HVDC architectures in AI datacenters will be a major catalyst for GaN adoption in the 2027 to 2028 timeframe.
  • 8-inch wafer transition: [AS] The broader industry shift from 6-inch to 8-inch wafers in SiC manufacturing forces customers to upgrade their epitaxial equipment, driving a structural equipment replacement cycle.
