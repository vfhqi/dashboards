DORA Regulatory Framework

The DORA III proposal represents Aena's most aggressive capex cycle in two decades, but faces severe airline and political pushback that could force suspension of the investment programme.

  • DORA II (2022-2026): Baseline €2.25bn regulated investment; tariff freeze through 2025 #I
    • IMAP: Initial maximum annual revenue per pax set at €9.89
    • Additional investment: €351m approved July 2025 for urgent infrastructure/cybersecurity
    • 2026 tariff: CNMC authorised 6.31% increase; IMAAJ adjusted to €11.02
    • Quality monitoring: 17 service quality indicators tied to tariff bonuses/penalties
  • DORA III (2027-2031): €12.88bn total investment (€9.99bn regulated) #I
    • Traffic model: 1.69bn cumulative passengers; peak 347m in 2031
    • Tariff ask: Average annual increase of €0.43 per passenger
    • WACC target: 9.00%
    • OPEX trajectory: Regulated OPEX scaling from €1.98bn (2027) to €2.31bn (2031)
    • Cost drivers: Maintenance and security rising to 36% of regulated costs by end of cycle
    • Quality: Expanded from 17 to 26 compliance indicators

Stakeholder Pushback and Political Risk

Severe three-way friction between Aena, airlines, and political actors threatens the DORA III investment programme and introduces material regulatory uncertainty.

  • Verdict: Regulatory risk is the single largest overhang on the investment case; outcome will determine whether €9.99bn capex proceeds or is suspended #J
  • Airline resistance: ALA and major carriers contesting proposed fee hikes #I
    • Counter-proposal: Demanding 4.9% annual tariff reduction (vs Aena's proposed increases)
    • WACC dispute: Airlines propose 6.35% vs Aena's 9.00%
    • Ryanair retaliation: Withdrew 1.2m seats across regional Spanish airports
  • Political risk: Popular Party passed Senate amendment to freeze tariffs through 2031 #I
    • Management warning: Statutory price ceiling would force suspension of €9.99bn investment
    • Legal defence: Spanish Constitution grants central state exclusive regulatory competence over airports
  • Ownership structure: Hybrid 51% state / 49% private; management defending model against regional co-management demands #I
