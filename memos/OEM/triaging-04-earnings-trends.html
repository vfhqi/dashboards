OEM International AB: Recent Earnings and Financial Performance

Sales Growth and Order Intake Dynamics

OEM International AB demonstrated robust financial momentum in the first quarter of 2026, recording historically high quarterly sales and order intake despite a mixed macroeconomic environment. Net sales for Q1 2026 increased by 7% year-over-year to SEK 1,472 million. The top-line expansion was driven by a solid organic growth rate of 5% and a 6% contribution from recent acquisitions, which successfully offset a 4% negative impact from currency fluctuations.

This strong start to 2026 builds upon the more tempered growth seen in the full year 2025. During FY 2025, total net sales grew by 1% to SEK 5,317 million, as 3% organic growth and 1% acquired growth were largely offset by a 3% currency headwind. Forward-looking visibility appears strong, as Q1 2026 incoming orders surged by 10% to SEK 1,543 million. This order intake exceeded net sales by 5%, driving an increase of SEK 71 million in the order backlog during the quarter.

Regional Performance and Product Categories

The company's regional operations displayed broad-based momentum in Q1 2026, with the core Sweden segment continuing to function as the primary growth engine. Region Sweden, which accounts for 59% of total Group sales, posted a 6% increase in net sales to SEK 862 million. Organic growth in the Swedish market stood at a robust 6%, complemented by a 3% boost from acquisitions, while incoming orders outpaced sales by 6%.

Regional sales trends per quarter up to Q1 2026

Region Finland, the Baltic States, and China generated net sales of SEK 258 million in Q1 2026, representing a 5% increase year-over-year. This region delivered the highest organic growth across the Group at 9%, largely powered by standout performances from Akkupojat (up 47%) and OEM Electronics (up 18%).

Region Rest of Europe reported the highest absolute growth rate in Q1 2026, with sales rising 14% to SEK 352 million. This expansion was entirely acquisition-driven, as M&A contributed 21% to regional growth, masking a 3% organic decline and a 4% negative currency impact. Within this region, OEM matic Hungary and OEM matic Slovakia were the top organic performers, expanding by 45% and 19% respectively.

From a product category perspective, OEM maintains a diverse industrial component portfolio, with distinct revenue weightings across its operating geographies. The company's recent strategic moves highlight a continued focus on strengthening its electronics, hydraulics, and automation portfolios. For instance, the acquisition of Multicomponent Norden AB bolstered the Swedish electronics components offering. Furthermore, the purchase of Cre8 System AS in Norway expanded OEM's exposure to hydraulics and chemical injection systems for the energy sector. Similarly, the addition of Motion29 Ltd in the UK specifically reinforced the Group's industrial automation capabilities.

Margin Evolution: Gross and EBITA Trends

OEM International's profitability metrics showed notable improvement in early 2026 following a period of margin compression in the previous year. In Q1 2026, Group EBITA increased by 16.1% to SEK 238 million. This robust earnings growth translated to an EBITA margin expansion to 16.2%, up from 14.9% in the corresponding quarter of the prior year. The margin improvement was primarily driven by higher sales volumes combined with a strengthened gross margin and strict cost control.

This first-quarter expansion represents a reversal from the full-year 2025 performance, where the EBITA margin contracted slightly to 15.0% from 15.3% in 2024. During 2025, a slightly lower gross margin—driven by currency fluctuations and a higher cost base from completed acquisitions—resulted in a 1% overall decline in EBITA to SEK 795 million. However, by Q1 2026, profitability recovered across all divisions, with Region Sweden reporting a 19% EBITA margin, Finland/Baltics/China at 15%, and Rest of Europe at 14%.

Cash Flow Generation and Capital Allocation

OEM's highly cash-generative financial profile continues to serve as the foundation for its dual capital allocation strategy of acquisitive growth and steady shareholder returns. Operating cash flow in Q1 2026 amounted to SEK 96 million, maintaining adequate liquidity to fund aggressive expansion. This follows a strong FY 2025, where cash flow from operations reached SEK 763 million, fortifying the Group's unleveraged balance sheet.

The company's M&A pacing has accelerated significantly following EQT's assumption of ownership in March 2025. The updated corporate strategy actively broadens the acquisition mandate to encompass product-owning niche companies alongside traditional technical trading distributors. This accelerated pace was evident in Q1 2026, during which OEM completed five acquisitions that contributed SEK 46 million in quarterly net sales.

Despite the increased capital deployment toward acquisitions, OEM has maintained its commitment to returning capital to shareholders. For the 2025 financial year, the Board proposed an unchanged dividend of SEK 1.75 per share, representing a total distribution of SEK 243 million. This payout constitutes 41% of earnings per share after tax, reflecting management's confidence that the Group's cash reserves and unutilized credit facilities provide ample capacity for simultaneous dividend continuity and acquisition-driven growth.

Fundamental Trends in Industrial Distribution

The European industrial distribution sector is navigating a complex macroeconomic environment characterized by subdued overall economic growth, elevated interest rates, and structural shifts in global trade. Despite a challenging manufacturing backdrop across major economies like Germany, strategic realignments and targeted investments are providing strong counter-cyclical growth opportunities. Chief among these is the ongoing reconfiguration of global supply chains, as businesses increasingly prioritize operational resilience over pure cost efficiency.

The escalation of geopolitical tensions, including disruptions along the Red Sea corridor and the Middle East, has exposed the vulnerabilities of extended international logistics networks. In response, European industrial companies are accelerating nearshoring and "friendshoring" strategies to bring production closer to end consumers. For industrial distributors, this localization trend supports sustained demand for automated warehousing and customized regional supply chain solutions, particularly within Central and Eastern Europe.

End-Market Demand Drivers

The industrial distribution market is currently experiencing significant divergence across its primary end-markets, with robust growth in defense and energy offsetting persistent weakness in traditional construction and automotive sectors.

Defense and SecurityThe geopolitical security situation has triggered a structural rearmament cycle across Europe, leading to historic increases in national defense budgets. European governments are executing multi-year investments to replenish depleted ammunition stockpiles and modernize armed forces. This has generated surging demand for highly specialized, ruggedized electronic components, tactical IT infrastructure, and precision land systems. The defense sector remains highly price-inelastic and insulated from short-term economic cycles, providing high-margin visibility for distributors with established military certifications.

Energy Infrastructure and Data CentersThe rapid expansion of artificial intelligence and cloud computing has transformed data centers into a structural source of incremental electricity demand across Europe. Recent estimates suggest European data center capacity could more than triple by 2030, necessitating massive investments in grid modernization, cooling technologies, and uninterrupted power supply systems. Concurrently, the broader energy transition is driving significant capital expenditure into battery energy storage systems, smart grids, and renewable generation infrastructure, creating a sustained tailwind for electrical and automation component distributors.

Medtech and HealthcareThe medical technology sector continues to provide stable, defensive growth, underpinned by evolving patient needs, technological advancements in diagnostics, and an aging demographic profile. Industrial distributors serving this market benefit from the high switching costs associated with rigorous product qualification processes and the demand for biocompatible, high-precision electronic modules.

Construction and motiveConversely, the European automotive industry faces a challenging transition period, marked by intense price competition from Asian imports, fluctuating EV adoption rates, and a volatile tariff environment. Similarly, the residential construction market remains severely depressed due to high financing costs and inflation, though this is partially mitigated by government-subsidized infrastructure projects and energy-efficiency building retrofits.

Shift Towards Value-Added Solutions and Sustainability

To combat margin pressure in commoditized product lines, industrial distributors are increasingly transitioning from traditional wholesale models to becoming integrated, full-service partners. Customers are consolidating their vendor bases and demanding complex, customized engineering solutions that include sub-assembly, predictive maintenance, and digital supply chain analytics. This shift toward customized full-service models allows distributors to execute value-based pricing strategies and embed themselves more deeply into OEM manufacturing processes.

Simultaneously, environmental, social, and governance (ESG) mandates have evolved from voluntary guidelines into strict regulatory requirements, such as the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD). Original equipment manufacturers now require their distribution partners to provide transparent documentation of Scope 3 emissions and life-cycle carbon assessments for supplied components. Distributors that successfully integrate sustainable sourcing and circular economy solutions are securing a distinct competitive advantage in securing long-term contracts with major industrial clients.

Peer Benchmarking: Bufab, Bossard, Addtech, and Indutrade

OEM International operates in a highly competitive Nordic and European industrial distribution landscape against established serial acquirers like Addtech and Indutrade, as well as C-parts specialists Bufab and Bossard. Benchmarking these peers reveals distinct differences in organic growth resilience, margin execution, and acquisition strategies during a period of macroeconomic uncertainty.

Organic Sales Growth and Order Backlogs

OEM International delivered a robust 5% organic sales growth in the first quarter of 2026, significantly outpacing its core peers in a sluggish broader market. Bufab followed with a respectable 2.2% organic growth in early 2026, driven primarily by volume increases despite a cautious underlying market.

Conversely, the larger decentralized compounders faced stronger growth headwinds. Addtech posted a more modest 1% organic growth in its recent quarter, as variations between customer segments weighed on overall volumes. Indutrade saw flat (0%) organic sales development in Q1 2026, though its organic order intake grew by 1%, indicating a slightly strengthening backlog. Bossard faced notable normalization challenges, reporting a 1.3% organic sales decline in the first half of 2025 before recovering to 2% organic growth for the full year.

Margin Execution: Gross and EBITA Margins

Profitability metrics highlight varying degrees of pricing power and cost control across the peer group. Bufab has demonstrated exceptional margin execution, expanding its first-quarter 2026 gross margin to 32.9% and its adjusted EBITA margin to 15.3%. Addtech similarly maintains high profitability, posting a 15.6% EBITA margin driven by a strong product mix, active pricing strategies, and high value-add engineering solutions.

OEM's recent margin recovery brought its Q1 2026 EBITA margin to 16.2%, firmly positioning it at the top end of the peer group. Indutrade's EBITA margin stood at a resilient 13.3% in early 2026, dampened slightly by flat organic sales and marginally higher expense levels, though its gross margins remained strong. Bossard operates at a slightly lower profitability tier, recording a 10.2% EBIT margin in 2024 as it navigated wage inflation and IT implementation costs.

Decentralized Growth Models and M&A Pacing

Indutrade exemplifies the decentralized growth model, overseeing more than 220 independent companies and completing 13 acquisitions in 2025 that added SEK 1.3 billion in annual sales. Addtech also operates a highly decentralized structure with over 150 subsidiaries, adding eight new companies in its recent fiscal year to contribute approximately SEK 1.5 billion in revenue.

OEM is rapidly adapting to this high-volume serial acquirer playbook following EQT's assumption of ownership, completing five acquisitions in the first quarter of 2026 alone. Bufab utilizes a "pull integration" strategy for its M&A, focusing on revenue synergies rather than rigid cost-cutting, as evidenced by its successful 2025 acquisition of the German novia Group. Bossard pursues a more targeted M&A approach, acquiring companies like Ferdinand Gross and Aero Negoce to secure strategic footholds in specific European geographies and aerospace verticals.

End-Market Exposures

Across the peer group, energy infrastructure and defense remain standout counter-cyclical growth drivers. Addtech has benefited from structural tailwinds in grid infrastructure and defense, which effectively offset persistent weaknesses in the building and installation segments. Indutrade similarly reported robust order growth from medical technology, pharmaceuticals, and the energy sector, while demand in traditional engineering and construction remained subdued.

Bufab noted solid demand in energy, agriculture, and defense, while facing persistent headwinds in construction, furniture, and automotive markets. Bossard has successfully pivoted toward sunrise industries, leveraging acquisitions to strengthen its presence in aerospace and railway sectors to counteract weakness in electromobility and agriculture.
