Section 1 — Executive Summary

Judgement: SCYR-ES is a Transformation Play where the operational inflection has already occurred, but the market re-rating has only partially followed. The concessions division is structurally dominant (92.8% of EBITDA, FY2025), OCF exceeded EBITDA for the first time in company history, and recourse net debt collapsed to €59m. The construction arm is now a captive project-delivery vehicle for the concessions pipeline rather than an independent risk carrier. The central question at Triaging is whether the persistent valuation discount vs. Ferrovial and Vinci is a durable structural feature or a lagging market misread — and the weight of evidence favours the latter, with key conditions attached.

  • Driver stack-rank by 24M share price impact:
  • D1: Recourse leverage narrative normalisation — market still prices total NFD/EBITDA 5.3x; recourse reality is ~0.4x; closing this perception gap is the single largest near-term re-rating lever
  • D2: Concessions EBITDA compounding — toll traffic, five new awards in 2025, €19bn projected lifetime distributions, portfolio NAV now €4bn vs €3.5bn at 2024 Investor Day
  • D3: FCF quality step-change — OCF/EBITDA >100% for first time; structural not cyclical; changes dividend capacity and acquisition firepower
  • D4: New concession awards pipeline — US managed lanes shortlisted (I-285/I-24/I-77), Italy (Pedemontana-Veneta), Australia, Canada; next leg of growth
  • D5: E&I backlog monetisation — €12.47bn backlog (73% internal), 4.6x annual E&I revenue; revenue visibility for 59 months confirmed
  • D6: LatAm political risk derisking — Colombia three-asset sale to Actis at $1.565bn (12% premium to Investor Day NAV) reduces the headline LatAm overhang materially
  • D7: Governance normalisation — dual Executive Chairman/CEO structure, new independent directors (43% female Board), post-CNMC fine reputational rebuild
  • Transformation Play status: The operational transformation is approximately 80% complete. The concessions pivot has been executed. What remains is: (a) capital recycling proceeds being redeployed into new concession awards at accretive IRRs; (b) the market awarding a concessions-type multiple rather than a blended construction/concessions discount. The value unlock is lagging the operational reality by 12-24 months in market pricing terms.
  • Single largest risk: ❌ Leverage perception misread is the primary mispricing risk, but LatAm political escalation (Peru 2026 election cycle, residual Colombia noise) is the single largest fundamental risk that could impair NAV rather than merely delay re-rating. The two risks interact: if LatAm assets deteriorate, total NFD fears become partially justified.
  • 🚩 RARE signal: OCF > EBITDA in FY2025 is a genuine structural milestone — not a timing artefact. This typically precedes re-rating events in infrastructure-transition stories. It confirms working capital discipline, concession cash distributions flowing to the parent, and that project-finance repayments at concession level are not draining corporate cash. This is the single clearest signal that the transformation is real and not financial engineering.
  • Overall rating: YELLOW — material upside case visible, but leverage perception overhang, LatAm residual exposure, and execution risk on new concession redeployment mean this is not straightforwardly Green. The gap between operational reality and market pricing is the opportunity; the risk is that the market is pricing something the buy-side cannot yet see clearly.

Section 2 — Driver 1: Concessions EBITDA Growth

Judgement: The concessions division is the primary EPS and re-rating driver. It already delivers 92.8% of group EBITDA and is growing organically via traffic ramp-up on existing assets, new operational concessions entering contribution, and five new awards secured in 2025 alone. This driver is structurally sound. The bear case is slower-than-modelled traffic ramp and hospital P3 payment delays — both manageable at current execution pace.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • Concessions EBITDA growth compounds into higher NAV per share as the DCF value of long-dated cash flows increases; the share price re-rates as the market shifts from construction EV/EBITDA (5-7x) to infrastructure EV/EBITDA (12-16x) — the arbitrage between these two regimes is worth €3-6 per share at conservative assumptions
  • Each 1% traffic outperformance on toll roads adds approximately 2-3% to concessions EBITDA given the high operating leverage of mature toll assets; SCYR has 30+ toll road assets across Spain, Chile, Ireland, Italy, and Peru
  • Hospital P3 income is availability-based (not demand-driven), providing contractual floors and reducing EBITDA volatility relative to traffic-dependent assets

Company guidance and public communications:

  • FY2025 concessions EBITDA contribution rose to 92.8% of group total vs 91.4% in FY2024 — a trend that management explicitly targets continuing
  • Portfolio NAV reached €3.96bn at FY2025, up €406m vs 2024 Investor Day valuation; management targets NAV growth via new awards rather than financial re-leveraging
  • Five concession awards secured in 2025: Novara Health and Science City (Italy), Accesses to Asunción (Paraguay), reuse water plant Antofagasta (Chile), Coquimbo desalination plant (Chile), Ruta Pie de Monte (Chile) — combined investment €1.6bn
  • Lifetime distributions from portfolio now projected at €19bn, up €2.9bn (+18%) since 2024 Investor Day — this is the number the market will increasingly use to value SCYR

Public sell-side treatment:

  • Consensus has progressively upgraded SCYR following OCF milestone; the stock gained 37% in the 12 months to early 2026
  • Market has not yet fully re-rated to a concessions multiple — EV/EBITDA of 8.46x as of April 2026 vs Vinci at 12-14x and Ferrovial at 20x+ (partly airport-driven premium); the gap is meaningful and partially unjustified
  • Sell-side coverage remains fragmented — SCYR is not a consensus institutional name; limited Anglo-Saxon coverage amplifies mispricing potential

Performance L3Y:

  • Concessions as % of EBITDA: FY2022 ~82% → FY2023 ~88% → FY2024 ~91.4% → FY2025 92.8% — consistent directional trend
  • Five new 2025 awards versus four in 9M2025 — pipeline conversion accelerating
  • Portfolio NAV progression: €3.5bn (2024 ID) → €3.96bn (FY2025) — 13% NAV per year growth

Peer context:

  • ACS Concesiones (Iridium) generates similar profile — ACS trades at a blended EV/EBITDA around 7-9x reflecting construction drag, similar to SCYR; the premium is reserved for purer plays (Ferrovial, Vinci)
  • Vinci at 12-14x EV/EBITDA demonstrates what the terminal multiple looks like if SCYR achieves greater concession purity — the 4-6x gap to SCYR represents the transformation premium not yet awarded
  • Strabag (STR-AT) and Eiffage (EIF) trade at construction multiples (5-7x) — SCYR should be migrating out of this peer group, but market treatment lags

Macro/end-market context:

  • Spanish infrastructure spend remains supported by EU Cohesion Funds and Recovery Plan (PERTE projects); domestic traffic has recovered past pre-COVID levels on most major toll roads
  • European P3 pipeline: Italy (social infrastructure, health), UK (hospitals and highways), and France (water treatment) all active; SCYR's Italian footprint (Pedemontana-Veneta) shows real deal flow in high-multiple jurisdictions
  • LatAm P3 pipeline: Chile remains the strongest structurally (rule of law, CPI-linked revenues); Paraguay early-stage but low risk; Peru more complex (see Driver 3)

Plausibility: High. The trend is established, the pipeline is real, and the NAV calculation methodology is transparent. Key assumption: new awards are awarded at target IRRs (typically 8-11% in SCYR's stated range) and construction delivery stays on-budget. Construction execution is the key risk to IRR delivery.

Bear case: Traffic ramp-up on newer assets (Pedemontana-Veneta, Chilean roads) disappoints vs ramp model; hospital P3 payments delayed by sovereign counterparty stress (Italy, Chile); new award pipeline dries up if credit markets tighten significantly; construction cost inflation erodes project IRRs at inception.

Rating: GREEN — core engine is operational, compounding, and demonstrably improving. Risk is execution timing not structural reversal.

Section 3 — Driver 2: Recourse Leverage Narrative Normalisation

Judgement: This is the most significant mispricing driver in SCYR today. Total NFD/EBITDA of 5.3x reads as highly leveraged against any construction or industrial benchmark. In reality, recourse net debt at year-end 2025 is €59m — down from €146m in December 2024, and down 93% since 2019. The non-recourse project finance at concession level is structurally ring-fenced; it is project risk borne by the concession SPVs and their lenders, not corporate balance sheet risk. Markets that do not distinguish between these two are systematically mispricing SCYR.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • Total NFD/EBITDA 5.3x includes all non-recourse project finance at SPV level — these loans are secured solely against the cash flows of individual concession projects and cannot trigger corporate default
  • Recourse NFD (€59m) / EBITDA (€1,358m) = 0.04x — this is effectively unlevered at the corporate level
  • If the market reprices SCYR on recourse leverage alone, the multiple expansion could be 2-4x turns of EV/EBITDA; at a €1.36bn EBITDA run-rate, each turn of re-rating is worth approximately €1.36bn in enterprise value / ~€0.50 per share at current dilution
  • The False Friends mental model is directly applicable: total NFD is the false signal; recourse NFD is the true signal

Company guidance and public communications:

  • Management has consistently highlighted the recourse/non-recourse distinction in every quarterly release; FY2025 PR led with "debt hits record low" — this is deliberate narrative management
  • The 93% reduction in recourse net debt since 2019 is a management priority metric featured prominently in the 2024-2027 strategic plan KPIs
  • Recourse NFD was €146m in December 2024 → €59m December 2025, implying the Colombia proceeds and operational FCF are actively deleveraging the parent balance sheet

Public sell-side treatment:

  • ❌ This is where sell-side analysis frequently fails: several published reports cite 5.3x NFD/EBITDA as a risk factor without adequately distinguishing the recourse component — this is a material analytical error that depresses the share price relative to fundamentals
  • The correct analytical framework is: value the concessions portfolio at NAV (DCF of distributions) + ascribe construction multiple to E&I EBITDA contribution + apply minimal holding company discount — total NFD is irrelevant to this calculation beyond concession-level debt service coverage
  • Limited correction of this narrative in Anglo-Saxon markets means the mispricing is durable in the short term but volatile on correction (potential catalyst event)

Performance L3Y:

  • Recourse NFD trajectory: €874m (2019) → €500m (2022) → €146m (Dec 2024) → €59m (Dec 2025) — consistent and accelerating deleveraging
  • Colombia $1.565bn disposal proceeds flowed to parent cash, enabling the Dec 2025 step-down
  • OCF first time exceeded EBITDA in FY2025 — this provides the structural engine for further recourse debt reduction to zero or net cash territory by 2026-27

Peer context:

  • Ferrovial carries near-zero recourse debt with substantial treasury shares; trades at 20x EV/EBITDA partly on this basis — SCYR's trajectory is directionally the same
  • ACS carries modest recourse net debt and trades at 7-9x blended; SCYR's recourse leverage is now materially lower than ACS — a valuation paradox
  • Vinci carries project finance in its concessions portfolio (VINCI Autoroutes) but the market has long distinguished this from corporate leverage; SCYR should benefit from similar reclassification

Plausibility: High. The numbers are audited, publicly disclosed, and the trend is unambiguous. The risk is not that recourse leverage increases — it is that the market's analytical re-classification takes longer than expected or requires a specific catalyst event (analyst day, rating agency upgrade, institutional re-initiation).

Bear case: A new large concession award requires recourse equity injection, temporarily reversing the deleveraging trend; macro shock forces SCYR to backstop a concession SPV's liquidity (rare under typical P3 structure but possible in extreme scenarios); investor base does not update its analytical framework absent a specific catalyst.

Rating: GREEN — the underlying reality is already excellent; the remaining work is narrative delivery and catalyst sequencing.

Section 4 — Driver 3: LatAm Risk Derisking

Judgement: The Colombia disposal materially reduces the headline LatAm overhang, but Peru remains a live political risk into 2026 elections. The Colombia transaction was executed at 12% above NAV — a signal that institutional infrastructure capital (Actis) is comfortable with LATAM P3 risk at the right price. Peru carries higher structural risk: political fragmentation, pre-election tensions, protest risk, and a mining/resource-sector political overhang that bleeds into infrastructure confidence. The net assessment is that LatAm risk has reduced from HIGH to MEDIUM-HIGH, but is not yet resolved.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • LatAm assets were historically valued at a 15-25% discount to equivalent European assets on country risk premium; removal of Colombia (three assets, $1.565bn) removes a disproportionate chunk of the discount
  • Peru assets include participation in Anillo Vial Periférico (Lima Peripheral Ring Road, $3.4bn project alongside Ferrovial and Acciona) — this is a large, complex, long-dated exposure in a country with elevated political risk
  • Each notch of LatAm political risk reduction expands the NAV multiple awarded by the market to the concessions portfolio; rough calibration: moving from EM-premium discount to European-equivalent pricing on the Peru/Chile portfolio could add €200-400m to group NAV

Company guidance and public communications:

  • Colombia disposal announced and closed in 2025 at $1.565bn (100% stake in three P3 motorways)
  • The 12% premium to 2024 Investor Day NAV validates SCYR's internal asset valuation methodology — a key credibility signal for the remaining portfolio
  • Chile remains active (5 award wins in 2025 — both concessions and water); Chile is lower-risk LatAm (investment grade sovereign, rule of law, CPI-linked revenue contracts)
  • SCYR explicitly excludes Colombia from the 2024-2027 strategic plan targets post-disposal

Performance L3Y:

  • Colombia exposure successfully harvested — the sell-at-a-premium execution is strong management track record evidence
  • Chile wins in 2025 show continued ability to win new concessions in LatAm at disciplined IRRs
  • Peru concentration is the remaining headline risk — no disposal signal from management in 2025/2026 to date

Peer context:

  • ACS has LatAm construction exposure via Dragados; similarly discounted by the market but does not have the same concessions purity argument
  • Ferrovial exited LatAm construction exposure years ago — trades at premium partly for this reason; SCYR is following but has not fully replicated
  • Eiffage is almost exclusively European — highest concessions multiple in the sector; illustrates the premium for geographic risk reduction

Macro/end-market context:

  • Peru 2026 election: political fragmentation elevated, pre-election social spending pressures, history of contract renegotiation under populist administrations; Allianz Trade rates Peru's political risk as elevated
  • Colombia: post-disposal, residual construction/warranty obligations only — not a material financial exposure
  • Chile: Boric administration (centre-left) has generally maintained PPP frameworks; copper-linked fiscal revenues support infrastructure capital budgets; lower risk than Peru or Colombia

Plausibility: Medium. Colombia derisking is complete and fully priced. Peru mitigation depends on political outcomes that are exogenous; the best-case is that the Lima Ring Road project proceeds under government continuity post-2026 election. The worst case is contract renegotiation demands or construction disruption from social protest.

Bear case: Peru 2026 election produces a populist administration that reopens PPP contracts; Anillo Vial Periférico faces social opposition from affected communities; SCYR must take a write-down on the Peru book value; market recalibrates LatAm discount upward across the remaining portfolio.

Rating: YELLOW — materially improved from 18 months ago, but residual Peru exposure is a genuine overhang that requires monitoring, not dismissal.

Section 5 — Driver 4: E&I Backlog Monetisation

Judgement: The €12.47bn E&I backlog is a revenue-visibility asset, not a growth driver in isolation. Its value lies in confirming 4.6x annual E&I revenue coverage (59 months of activity at current revenue run-rates) and in underpinning the concessions construction pipeline. Critically, 73% of the backlog is internal — building Sacyr's own concession projects. This means E&I is primarily a cost-of-delivery function for the concessions division, not an independent profit centre. The risk is margin, not revenue.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • E&I division revenue FY2025: approximately €2.7bn (extrapolating from 9M2025 €2.2bn); EBITDA margin stable at ~4.8%
  • Backlog-to-revenue conversion at 4.6x means the order book supports current revenue levels through approximately 2029 without a single new external order
  • The 73% internal concentration de-risks the revenue line (no tendering competition) but concentrates execution risk — if a concession project runs over budget, E&I absorbs the first layer of cost overrun before it hits the concession SPV equity

Company guidance and public communications:

  • SCYR targets stable E&I EBITDA margin at ~5%; FY2025 is tracking at 4.8% — in-line
  • Management has consistently framed E&I as a strategic capability rather than a standalone growth business; the key metric is whether E&I delivers concession projects on-budget and on-schedule
  • Record backlog of €12.47bn at FY2025 up 18% from FY2024 end — new concession awards mechanically create new E&I backlog entries

Performance L3Y:

  • E&I backlog: grew from ~€9bn (2022/2023 range) to €12.47bn at FY2025 — driven entirely by concessions award acceleration
  • Revenue ramp: 9M2025 E&I revenue +12% YoY to €2.2bn — growing as older backlog converts
  • Margin: stable at ~4.8-5% — not expanding, but not deteriorating under higher revenue; cost discipline evidence

Peer context:

  • ACS Construcción / Dragados maintains ~3-4% construction EBITDA margin on external work — SCYR's captive margin at 4.8-5% is slightly better, reflecting lower competitive pressure on internal mandates
  • Ferrovial has substantially exited pure construction; its construction legacy (Webber, Budimex) operates at comparable margins; Ferrovial's strategic direction is SCYR's future destination — construction as a declining proportion of group EBITDA
  • Strabag (pure construction play) trades at 5-7x EV/EBITDA on similar margins — SCYR's E&I should be valued at no more than this component multiple

Macro/end-market context:

  • Spanish civil engineering spend: EU Recovery Plan funds accelerating; PERTE for digitisation and green infrastructure active; no near-term demand cliff
  • LatAm construction pipelines: Chile active (water, roads), Peru conditionally active (political risk), Colombia post-sale = minimal
  • European construction cost inflation: steel and concrete costs have moderated from 2022 peak levels; labour inflation remains the residual pressure in Spain and Italy

Plausibility: High. Revenue visibility for 59 months is exceptional by construction sector standards. The risk is not whether backlog converts to revenue — it is whether margin is maintained when construction cost inflation runs hot or concession projects encounter unexpected complexity.

Bear case: A major concession project (Pedemontana-Veneta, Lima Ring Road) encounters significant cost overrun; E&I absorbs margin pressure as captive contractor with limited ability to pass costs to the concession SPV without triggering equity dilution; EBITDA margin compresses toward 3-4%; market re-rates E&I contribution negatively.

Rating: GREEN — revenue visibility is excellent; the risk is execution quality, which has been demonstrated consistently. Flag for monitoring on margin trajectory.

Section 6 — Driver 5: New Concession Awards Pipeline

Judgement: The forward pipeline is the most important driver of long-term NAV per share beyond the next 24 months. Five 2025 wins (€1.6bn combined investment) demonstrate SCYR's ability to originate, compete for, and win P3 mandates across multiple geographies. The US shortlisting (I-285, I-24, I-77 managed lanes) is the high-optionality catalyst event for 2026-27 — US highway concessions typically award at higher multiples than European equivalents on exit.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • Each new concession award adds approximately 8-12% of invested equity to NAV at inception (target equity IRR range); a €500m equity investment in a new concession generates €40-60m of immediate NAV uplift at signing
  • US managed lanes represent a step-change in potential scale: SCYR is shortlisted for three major interstate corridors alongside major infrastructure players; award would be a significant re-rating catalyst given US investors' higher infrastructure multiples
  • The portfolio NAV growth trajectory (€3.5bn → €3.96bn in 12 months via new awards + value growth) implies approximately €400-500m per annum of NAV accretion from this channel alone

Company guidance and public communications:

  • FY2025 press release specifically highlighted five new concession awards as a strategic headline alongside cash flow performance
  • US pipeline: I-285 East (Georgia), I-24 (Tennessee), I-77 (North Carolina) — SCYR named as part of shortlisted consortia; award decisions expected in 2026
  • Australia: explicitly named as a target market in 2025; no awards to date but pipeline exists
  • UK hospitals: social infrastructure P3 pipeline active under NHS framework; SCYR has track record in hospital P3s in Spain, Ireland, and Italy

Performance L3Y:

  • 2023: limited new award activity (strategic plan launch year)
  • 2024: portfolio valued at €3.5bn Investor Day; deal activity building
  • 2025: five awards, €1.6bn combined investment, US shortlisting achieved — accelerating pace

Peer context:

  • Ferrovial: active in Canadian and US highway P3s (407ETR, I-66, I-77); demonstrably able to win at scale in Anglo-Saxon markets; SCYR seeking the same trajectory
  • ACS/Iridium: active in Australian and US P3s; comparable model but more established Anglo-Saxon franchise
  • Vinci: predominantly French/European concessions; less aggressive in US managed lanes; SCYR is competing more directly with ACS/Ferrovial than Vinci in this space

Macro/end-market context:

  • US managed lanes pipeline is substantial: FHWA estimates $500bn+ in P3-eligible federal highway capacity gaps; political support for managed lanes continues under both parties (congestion pricing + federal funding leverage); SCYR's shortlisting signals competitive credibility in this market
  • European P3: Italy's PNRR health infrastructure spend creating hospital P3 opportunities; UK NHS trusts under pressure to use P3 frameworks for new hospital builds
  • Australian infrastructure: historically strong P3 pipeline; SCYR's water concession experience (Agua division) creates cross-sell opportunities

Plausibility: Medium-High. The track record is building but US awards are not yet secured; pipeline optionality is real but award risk remains. Execution on existing portfolio is the precondition for winning new mandates.

Bear case: US managed lanes awards take 2+ years longer than anticipated; SCYR unable to raise sufficient equity capital for large US P3 bids given balance sheet size relative to Ferrovial/ACS; European award pipeline competitive intensity increases; UK/Italy P3 frameworks delayed by political change; award pace reverts to 1-2 per year rather than 5.

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — pipeline is demonstrably active, but US upside is probabilistic not certain; net positive with optionality.

Section 7 — Driver 6: FCF Quality Improvement

Judgement: 🚩 RARE. OCF exceeding EBITDA for the first time (FY2025: OCF €1,359m vs EBITDA €1,358m) is a structural inflection point, not a one-period event. It confirms that concession distributions to the parent are now material, that working capital discipline in E&I has improved, and that the project finance repayment profile at concession level is not draining corporate cash. This is the single cleanest signal of transformation completion. It changes the dividend capacity, acquisition firepower, and re-rating story simultaneously.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • OCF > EBITDA can only occur sustainably when: (a) concession cash distributions to the parent exceed the project finance service burden embedded in EBITDA adjustments; (b) working capital is neutral to positive; (c) tax and interest cash flows are below their EBITDA-equivalent recognition
  • SCYR's achievement of this milestone two years ahead of its 2027 target (management stated target: €1,350m OCF by 2027; achieved €1,359m in FY2025) implies the concessions portfolio is maturing faster than plan
  • This changes the capital allocation story: from "managing leverage down" to "deploying excess cash into new awards at accretive IRRs" — a materially more attractive investment proposition

Company guidance and public communications:

  • FY2025 results presentation led with: "cash flow exceeds EBITDA, debt hits record low" — management's own framing confirms this is the key headline metric
  • OCF/EBITDA ratio >100% was explicitly described as "a pivotal milestone in the company's evolution from construction contractor to concession operator"
  • Management has not issued new medium-term OCF guidance post the 2027 target breach — an updated 2024-2030 strategic plan revision is likely in 2026 Investor Day

Performance L3Y:

  • FY2022: OCF significantly below EBITDA (project finance service > distributions)
  • FY2023: converging — OCF growing faster than EBITDA as mature assets begin distributing
  • FY2024: OCF €1,295m vs EBITDA €1,352m — close but below
  • FY2025: OCF €1,359m vs EBITDA €1,358m — milestone crossed

Peer context:

  • Ferrovial has traded OCF > EBITDA for many years; market awards a premium for this consistency — SCYR is entering this peer group territory
  • Vinci: similar OCF/EBITDA trajectory on its mature French motorway assets; the inflection point is well-documented in infrastructure sector re-rating literature
  • ACS: construction-heavy; OCF typically below EBITDA due to working capital consumption — SCYR is diverging positively from this comp

Plausibility: High. The driver is structural: as each concession matures through its ramp phase, distributions increase while project finance service remains fixed or declining. The portfolio is adding mature assets faster than young, capital-absorbing ones at this stage of the plan.

Bear case: New concession awards in 2025 (five at €1.6bn) inject new capital-absorbing assets that temporarily reverse OCF/EBITDA ratio in 2026-27; large US P3 award would require substantial equity contribution, temporarily pressuring parent OCF; if construction activity accelerates significantly, E&I working capital increases

Rating: GREEN — the milestone is achieved and the structural logic is sound; the risk is a temporary reversal from new award activity, which is a positive quality problem.

Section 8 — Driver 7: Governance and CEO Normalisation

Judgement: The transition from founder-centric to institutionally-governed company is underway. Pedro Sigüenza's appointment as CEO (June 2025 AGM) alongside Executive Chairman Manuel Manrique introduces a dual-leadership structure with clearer accountability separation. Sigüenza's 20-year Sacyr career and prior role as Concesiones CEO provides operational continuity. The CNMC fine (€16.7m, 2022, legacy bid-rigging 1992-2017) represents historical reputational risk that is structurally behind the company — but it surfaces in ESG screens and limits certain institutional mandates. Board diversity progress (43% female) and committee rationalisation signal real governance improvement.

Definition and share price transmission:

  • Governance quality affects SCYR's inclusion in ESG-mandated funds, which represent an increasing share of European institutional capital
  • Dual Chairman/CEO structure is consistent with best practice under Spanish corporate governance code (CNMV); it reduces key-man dependency on Manrique and begins the de-founder process
  • Post-CNMC fine period (2022-2025) is critical: three years without governance incident plus structural improvements could enable ESG inclusion metrics upgrade, broadening the eligible investor base

Company guidance and public communications:

  • AGM 2025: Pedro Sigüenza appointed as CEO — "to drive strategic growth" per company communications; Manrique retains Executive Chairman role with corporate, financial, and strategic responsibilities
  • Two new independent directors appointed: Elena Gómez del Pozuelo and María Pino Velázquez (Board now 43% female, 50% independent)
  • Board committees rationalised from four to three: Executive; Corporate Governance, Appointments and Remuneration; Audit and Sustainability — simplification consistent with governance best practice
  • No public statement from management on CNMC fine legacy beyond normal legal compliance language

Performance L3Y:

  • CNMC fine €16.7m imposed July 2022 (legacy conduct 1992-2017) — no recurrence since
  • 2023-2025: zero additional CNMC/competition authority incidents
  • 2025 AGM governance reforms represent a visible positive step

Peer context:

  • ACS: founder-centric (Florentino Pérez) with strong institutional governance overlay; no comparable CNMC fine in the same period; however, construction sector cartel fines are near-universal in Spain — Acciona, OHL, FCC, Ferrovial all received similar fines in the same 2022 CNMC action
  • Ferrovial: post-Ortega family governance is more institutionalised; Amsterdam re-domicile and NYSE listing added Anglo-Saxon governance expectations; trading at premium partly for this reason
  • The Spanish construction cartel fine is a sector-wide issue — its discriminatory impact on SCYR vs peers is limited; all major Spanish contractors received concurrent CNMC fines

Plausibility: Medium. The governance improvements are real. The CNMC fine overhang on ESG screens is diminishing with time. The risk is that Manrique's retention as Executive Chairman prevents full institutionalisation — he retains strategic control and key investor relationships, meaning true governance normalisation requires succession of the chairman role, not just the CEO role.

Bear case: Manrique-Sigüenza tension emerges as strategic direction diverges; new governance incident (regulatory, environmental, or competition) at critical moment; ESG-linked institutional capital remains cautious given legacy fine; no rating agency or governance index upgrade triggers the expected broadening of investor base.

Rating: YELLOW — improving trajectory but not yet fully normalised; the dual-structure removes key-man risk for operational execution but concentrates strategic control in Manrique; watch for chairman succession signals.

Section 9 — Driver Interdependencies

Judgement: The drivers are not independent — they form a reinforcing system in which concessions growth and leverage narrative normalisation must advance in parallel to achieve re-rating; if one stalls, the other is discounted. The interdependency risk is most concentrated in the concessions/E&I loop and in the LatAm/NAV credibility loop.

  • Concessions growth ↔ Leverage narrative: If the market re-rates leverage perception without concurrent EBITDA growth evidence, the re-rating is fragile. Sustainable re-rating requires both: lower recourse debt AND higher concession cash flows demonstrably accruing to the parent. FY2025 delivers both simultaneously — this is the strongest interdependency catalyst.
  • E&I backlog ↔ Concessions pipeline: The E&I backlog is entirely dependent on the concessions award pipeline. If concessions award pace slows — whether from tighter credit markets, capital constraints, or competitive displacement — the E&I backlog stops growing and the 59-month coverage horizon compresses. There is no standalone E&I growth engine; E&I is a pipeline derivative.
  • Colombia disposal proceeds ↔ New award capacity: The $1.565bn Colombia proceeds created the firepower for new concession equity investments. If the proceeds are not redeployed into accretive new awards within 18-24 months, the capital efficiency argument for the disposal weakens and the NAV per share stagnates. The five 2025 awards show initial deployment, but the US managed lanes pipeline is the test case for whether SCYR can deploy at scale.
  • FCF quality ↔ Dividend capacity ↔ Investor base broadening: The OCF > EBITDA milestone enables a progressive dividend policy without balance sheet stress; a growing dividend is a powerful mechanism for attracting income-focused institutional investors, particularly in the UK and continental Europe, who have limited exposure to SCYR today.
  • LatAm derisking ↔ NAV credibility: The Colombia disposal at 12% premium to Investor Day NAV is the single most important validation of SCYR's NAV methodology. Every future NAV disclosure is now credible. If Peru assets were to suffer political impairment and a disposal at a NAV discount occurred, it would reopen the question of whether the remaining portfolio NAV is reliable — the Peru book value credibility is now load-bearing for the full portfolio NAV story.
  • Governance normalisation ↔ ESG investor access: Without a clean CNMC record for 3-5 years post-2022, SCYR remains excluded from certain ESG-mandated mandates. Governance improvement is a precondition for broadening the institutional investor base — and broader institutional ownership is a precondition for closing the valuation discount to Ferrovial/Vinci.

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — interdependencies are positive and reinforcing under base case; the Peru/NAV credibility loop is the primary circuit-breaker to watch.

Section 10 — Market Expectations vs Plausibility

Judgement: SCYR trades at a material discount to pure-play concessions peers that is partially justified by residual construction exposure and LatAm risk, but is now largely an analytical error — the market is pricing total leverage and construction-sector associations rather than the underlying concessions reality. The gap is closing but remains exploitable.

These three factors together justify perhaps 15-20% discount. The current discount of 40-50% to Vinci is thus 25-35% above what fundamentals support.

The correct peer for SCYR in 2026 is the lower end of the Vinci/Eiffage range (12-14x) blended with a construction component multiple (6x) at 7% of EBITDA weight — implied blended fair-value multiple of ~12x would put the stock at €5.50-6.50 vs current ~€4.59 (52-week high).

  • What the market is getting right: Construction risk, LatAm political risk, and Manrique succession uncertainty are genuine. The market is not wrong to apply some discount — it is wrong about the magnitude and the primary driver (leverage perception vs. actual recourse leverage).

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — the valuation gap is real and plausibly closeable; the catalyst timing is uncertain; the risk is that the discount persists longer than a 24-month Triaging horizon suggests.

Section 11 — Macro Sensitivity

Judgement: SCYR's macro exposure is concentrated in three domains: Spanish GDP (E&I revenue, domestic toll traffic), LatAm political/macro (Peru primarily), and European interest rates (concessions NAV discount rate).   • LatAm macro — Chile: Lower-risk. CPI-linked concession revenues in Chile provide inflation protection. Boric administration has maintained PPP frameworks. Copper-linked fiscal revenues support infrastructure capex. Macro risk is second-order.
  • Interest rate sensitivity on concessions NAV: The portfolio NAV of €3.96bn is computed by DCF; a 100bps increase in the discount rate applied to long-dated concession cash flows could reduce NAV by 8-15% depending on asset duration. European rates at 2.5-3.5% (post-2024 normalisation) are in a more benign range than 2022-2023; further rate increases are not the base case but remain a tail risk.
  • EUR/USD and LatAm FX: SCYR's Chilean and Peruvian revenues are partly USD/local currency denominated; EUR/USD appreciation reduces the EUR equivalent of distributions to the parent.   • Construction cost inflation: The primary macro sensitivity for E&I margins; steel and concrete cost inflation has moderated from 2022 peaks; labour cost inflation in Spain remains moderate relative to Northern Europe. No acute margin pressure in the 12-month horizon.

Rating: YELLOW — Spain macro is supportive; LatAm tail risk is real; interest rate sensitivity is manageable at current rate levels; net macro backdrop is mildly constructive.

Section 14 — Fragility Assessment

Judgement: SCYR is structurally resilient at the corporate level (recourse leverage ~0x) but carries project-level fragility in LatAm and the normal execution risks of a construction group with active large-project backlog. The Fragility Assessment mental model applied here identifies three key fragility concentrations.

  • Concentration fragility: 92.8% of EBITDA from concessions is both a strength (higher quality) and a concentration risk — if the concessions model encounters structural headwinds (rate rises, regulatory change, political risk), there is limited diversification buffer
  • LatAm project concentration: Peru exposure is concentrated in the Anillo Vial Periférico project alongside Ferrovial/Acciona — a large, complex, politically-exposed single project; this is not a portfolio of small exposures but one very large bet on Lima infrastructure politics
  • Execution chain fragility: The E&I/concessions loop means that if E&I encounters a major project failure (cost overrun, default), it simultaneously impairs the concession asset being built — a dual-damage scenario not visible in simple segment analysis
  • Balance sheet fragility: low. Recourse NFD €59m, OCF growing; no corporate-level liquidity risk in any plausible scenario.

Section 16 — Bear Thesis

  • New award pace disappoints: US managed lanes not awarded to SCYR; no major European or Chilean awards in 2026; NAV growth stalls at €3.96bn; the growth narrative dies; stock re-rated back to construction multiple
  • Construction cost overrun: One or more major concession builds (Pedemontana-Veneta, Lima) encounters 10-15% cost overrun; E&I EBITDA margin compresses; concession equity IRR reduces; NAV write-down in affected assets

  • Governance incident: New CNMC investigation or ESG controversy during Manrique/Sigüenza transition; ESG mandate exclusion broadens; Anglo-Saxon institutional buyers stay away; valuation discount persists indefinitely
