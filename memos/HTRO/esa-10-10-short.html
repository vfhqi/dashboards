Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines the bearish case surrounding Hexatronic Group AB, a Swedish fiber optic infrastructure provider currently navigating a severe cyclical downturn in its core fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) market. Notably, there are no documented short-seller reports from major activist firms such as Muddy Waters, Citron, Gotham City, Hindenburg, Viceroy, or ShadowFall targeting Hexatronic Group AB, and specific quantitative short interest metrics, including current short interest as a percentage of float, days to cover, and historical short interest peaks, are not present in the provided research data. The absence of coordinated short-seller campaigns, however, does not diminish the fundamental operational challenges that have attracted significant market skepticism.

The bearish thesis centers on the company's core Fiber Solutions business area transitioning into a turnaround situation due to a challenged fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) market, with this segment experiencing weak demand, declining margins, and lower-than-expected performance in both Europe and North America throughout 2025. The severity of these pressures materialized when severe overcapacity forced the company to issue a profit warning in July 2025 and initiate a performance improvement program to reduce the cost base by approximately SEK 110 million annually. Financially, Hexatronic has increasingly relied on non-GAAP adjustments to its reported profitability due to major operational restructuring, recognizing SEK 202 million in non-recurring costs in the third quarter of 2025 linked to a performance improvement program, followed by an additional SEK 97 million in restructuring charges burdening reported group EBITA in the fourth quarter of 2025.

From a valuation perspective, Redeye's internal bear case modeling assigns a Bear Case fair value ranging from SEK 12.0 to SEK 15.0 per share, which implies a potential downside of 40% to 50% from the stock's recent trading levels. This downside scenario assumes a meager 0% sales Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) from 2024 through 2030, eventually turning to negative 2% to 3% terminal growth, combined with a terminal EBIT margin of merely 4% to 5%, a stark contrast to the 15% to 17% EBITA margins Hexatronic achieved during peak boom periods.

Structurally, the bearish case is reinforced by several fundamental vulnerabilities. The company operates a highly capital-intensive manufacturing business characterized by commodity-like products in a relatively fragmented market, which heavily limits sustainable competitive advantages and long-term pricing power. Additionally, Hexatronic faces limited ability to increase prices above inflation without risking significant customer churn and concerns regarding inconsistent historical returns on invested capital compared to the company's cost of capital.

However, the bearish thesis faces meaningful counterarguments. The core bearish claim regarding severe vulnerability to FTTH market cyclicality has transitioned from speculation to undisputed fact, substantiated when Hexatronic was forced to launch a massive performance improvement program, with the Fiber Solutions segment's adjusted EBITA margin collapsing to just 5.2% in the fourth quarter of 2025. Yet the bearish assertion that the Data Center segment will inevitably suffer a similar "bust" cycle remains entirely speculative, as current financial data directly contradicts this bearish fear with the Data Center division continuing to accelerate rather than contract.

Bear Case Credibility: Orange — The fundamental operational challenges are thoroughly documented and substantiated by corporate restructuring actions, profit warnings, and margin compression. However, the absence of coordinated short-seller campaigns and the speculative nature of claims regarding newer business segments prevent a "Red" rating.

Downside Risk if Bears are Right: Red — The quantified downside scenario of 40% to 50% from current levels, combined with potential margin compression to terminal levels of 4% to 5% and negative revenue growth, represents severe financial deterioration with meaningful wealth destruction for shareholders.

Fundamental Bearish Pressures

While formal short-seller campaigns are not documented in the provided reports, fundamental bearish pressures and operational challenges have recently materialized, which typically attract market skepticism. The company's core Fiber Solutions business area has transitioned into a turnaround situation due to a challenged fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) market. This segment experienced weak demand, declining margins, and lower-than-expected performance in both Europe and North America throughout 2025.

A primary catalyst for bearish sentiment is the volume shortfall in microduct products, which has caused under-absorption in Hexatronic's factories and subsequent margin pressure. This severe overcapacity forced the company to issue a profit warning in July 2025 and initiate a performance improvement program to reduce the cost base by approximately SEK 110 million annually. Specifically, the company decided to close one of its duct facilities to address the market overcapacity, which management expects to persist into 2026. Additionally, the company has faced sudden shifts in customer ordering patterns, including North American customers abruptly slowing down orders due to overstocking in prior quarters.

Redeye's Fundamental Bear Case Modeling

While Redeye maintains a generally positive base case, their internal bear case modeling outlines a severe downside scenario for Hexatronic. Under these pessimistic assumptions, Redeye assigns a Bear Case fair value ranging from SEK 12.0 to SEK 15.0 per share, which implies a potential downside of 40% to 50% from the stock's recent trading levels.

The core driver of this bearish valuation is the assumed maturity and subsequent stagnation of Hexatronic's primary strategic growth markets: the United States, the United Kingdom, and Germany. In the downside scenario, Redeye models that Hexatronic will fail to offset the severe downturn in the Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) market through expansion into new segments like Data Centers or Harsh Environment.

If the bear thesis materializes, the financial implications are profound. Redeye's bear case assumes a meager 0% sales Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) from 2024 through 2030, eventually turning to negative 2% to 3% terminal growth. Furthermore, intense pricing pressure and lower factory utilization are projected to crush profitability. The bear case models a terminal EBIT margin of merely 4% to 5%, a stark contrast to the 15% to 17% EBITA margins Hexatronic achieved during peak boom periods.

Strategic and Operational Vulnerabilities

Beyond the explicit financial modeling, several operational vulnerabilities form the foundation of a bearish view on the company.

Commoditization and Pricing Power: Hexatronic operates in a relatively fragmented market characterized by commodity-like products, which inherently limits sustainable competitive advantages. The company has demonstrated a limited ability to increase prices above inflation without risking significant customer churn, leaving it highly vulnerable to competitive pricing pressures.

Cyclicality and Overcapacity: The company is heavily dependent on cyclical FTTH investments. The recent boom-and-bust cycle has resulted in severe market overcapacity, particularly in microduct production, forcing Hexatronic to close facilities and incur massive restructuring costs.

Return on Capital Concerns: Skeptics point to Hexatronic's historically inconsistent returns on invested capital compared to its cost of capital. Return on Equity (ROE) has remained below 20% for the last five years, suggesting inefficient use of shareholder capital and challenging the narrative of long-term sustainable outperformance.

Leadership and Governance Uncertainty: The appointment of a new CEO in early 2025 with limited prior industry experience creates tangible execution risk during a highly sensitive turnaround phase. Additionally, the lack of a controlling owner with a 20%+ voting stake and relatively limited board member personal shareholdings reduces the concentrated, long-term stewardship typically desired by institutional investors.

Industry Expert Skepticism

Interviews with telecommunications executives reveal additional layers of skepticism regarding Hexatronic's recent growth trajectory.

Industry insiders note that Hexatronic's massive revenue surge in 2022 and 2023 was heavily subsidized by severe supply chain shortages from Tier-1 incumbents like Corning and CommScope. As global supply chains have normalized and lead times for tier-1 fiber have plummeted, Hexatronic is losing this artificial tailwind. Consequently, smaller broadband service providers and private equity-backed overbuilders—Hexatronic's core customer demographic—are entering a prolonged "digestion phase," halting new equipment orders because they already hold excess inventory.

Furthermore, experts have flagged Hexatronic's aggressive M&A strategy as a potential fundamental risk. As the core FTTH market slows, Hexatronic has rapidly acquired companies in adjacent but distinct markets, such as subsea cabling and harsh environment connectivity. Observers question whether the company is spreading its management attention too thin, attempting to juggle too many disparate integrations simultaneously while its core fiber cash cow undergoes a painful structural contraction.

Accounting, Governance & Historical Patterns Governance Structure and Leadership Risks

Hexatronic's corporate governance profile exhibits several structural characteristics that may concern defensively minded investors. The company lacks a dominant controlling shareholder with a voting stake exceeding 20 percent, which reduces the concentrated, long-term stewardship often associated with corporate outperformance. Furthermore, less than half of the board members possess substantial personal shareholdings in the company. Board and executive management combined hold approximately 13 percent of the company's total outstanding shares. The appointment of CEO Rikard Fröberg in March 2025 introduced a degree of execution risk, as he brought relatively limited specific industry background to the role despite his strong credentials from previous large enterprise roles.

From a related-party perspective, the group leases premises from Fastighets AB Balder. Hexatronic board member Erik Selin exercises significant influence over Fastighets AB Balder. Management maintains that this rental contract, which amounts to approximately SEK 7 million annually, was entered into strictly under normal commercial terms.

Quality of Earnings and Non-GAAP Adjustments

Regarding earnings quality, Hexatronic has increasingly relied on non-GAAP adjustments to its reported profitability due to major operational restructuring. In the third quarter of 2025, the company recognized SEK 202 million in non-recurring costs linked to a performance improvement program primarily focused on its struggling Fiber Solutions division. These adjustments included severance pay, facility closure costs, and significant write-downs of both tangible assets and existing inventory.

This trend of adjusting earnings continued into the fourth quarter of 2025, where reported group EBITA was burdened by an additional SEK 97 million in restructuring charges. A significant portion of this fourth-quarter adjustment, specifically SEK 67 million, was attributed to sudden capacity reductions at the company's duct manufacturing plant in Clinton, South Carolina. The company estimates that these extensive restructuring efforts will eventually yield SEK 122 million in total cost savings, translating to a net positive EBITA effect of SEK 110 million on an annual basis.

Revenue Recognition Practices

Hexatronic's revenue recognition policies generally dictate that sales of standard goods are recognized at a specific point in time when control is transferred to the customer. However, revenue derived from long-term or highly customized projects is recognized over time, provided there is a substantial contract value and a prolonged completion period. For example, in the first quarter of 2024, revenue recognized over time accounted for SEK 120 million out of total net sales of SEK 2,115 million.

Sceptical Rebuttal to the Bear Case

By the fourth quarter of 2025, the fast-growing Data Center and Harsh Environment business areas had expanded to account for 37% of the group's total net sales. More importantly, these two segments now generate 60% of the group's adjusted EBITA, proving they can effectively replace legacy profit pools. The Data Center segment demonstrated an exceptional 62% organic sales growth in the fourth quarter of 2025. Concurrently, the Harsh Environment segment delivered a 15% organic growth rate, driven by highly resilient defense and energy investments.

Cash Generation and Balance Sheet Strength: Skeptics frequently highlight the capital intensity of manufacturing microducts and the risks of elevated debt during a cyclical demand trough. However, Hexatronic's cash generation profile has proven remarkably resilient during the ongoing FTTH downturn. The company generated an impressive operating cash flow of SEK 349 million in the fourth quarter of 2025, representing an exceptional cash conversion rate of 235%. This robust cash flow allowed the company to successfully reduce its adjusted leverage ratio to 1.9x EBITDA, completely absorbing the strategic acquisition of Communication Zone without stressing the balance sheet.

Proactive Margin Defense: Bears argue that persistent overcapacity in the North American and European duct markets will permanently impair group margins. To directly counter this, management has aggressively executed a targeted performance improvement program within the struggling Fiber Solutions division. This initiative includes consolidating production facilities in Europe and actively downsizing duct operations in Clinton, South Carolina, to better match depressed market demand. These decisive restructuring efforts are expected to yield SEK 120 million in permanent annual cost savings, with the full run-rate effect materializing by the end of the first quarter of 2026.

Valuation Floor: Finally, bearish analysts may be ignoring the substantial margin of safety already embedded in Hexatronic's depressed trading multiples. Redeye notes that the company currently trades at a steep 35% discount to its sector peers based on 2026 and 2027 earnings estimates. At an implied multiple of roughly 9.2x EBITA for 2026, the current valuation heavily prices in the legacy FTTH weakness while arguably giving investors a free option on the accelerating, high-margin growth of the Data Center division.
