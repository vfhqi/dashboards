AFG-NO - Earnings Trends

[C] AFG-NO | Triaging #4 | Earnings & Fundamental Trends | 04-May-26

KEY FINDINGS

  • Margin inflection is real but fragile. AFG group EBIT margin expanded from 3.8% in 2024 to approximately 5.2% EBT margin in 2025, driven almost entirely by the Civil Engineering segment running at 9%+ margins and strong Q4 Building execution. This is a genuine improvement, but the margin base remains narrow at group level — one bad project cluster can move the needle materially in a ~NOK 32bn revenue, ~NOK 1.6bn pre-tax profit business.
  • Order backlog is a false friend at face value. Backlog of NOK 44.7bn at end-2025 looks excellent (~1.4x LTM revenue), but Q4 2025 order intake fell sharply to NOK 9.6bn from NOK 12.5bn a year earlier, suggesting the book-to-bill has rolled below 1x for the most recent quarter. Backlog duration and margin quality must be interrogated — headline growth obscures the deceleration in new winning rates.
  • Offshore is the open wound. AF Offshore Decom reported a project loss of approximately NOK 100 million in Q4 2025, dragging the "Other/Offshore" segment to a loss of NOK 33 million versus analyst expectations of NOK 130 million profit. This fits the Cockroaches Never Alone pattern — one disclosed project loss in a segment running fixed-price decommissioning contracts historically precedes further provisions.
  • Civil Engineering is the margin engine. The segment delivered ~9.5% EBIT margins in Q4 2025 and ~7% for full-year 2025, underpinned by Norway's infrastructure investment cycle (NTP 2025-2036, near-doubling of rail maintenance budgets). This is the genuinely differentiated element of AFG's story — construction peers run 3-5% EBIT margins. The key risk is whether Civil sustains this as the project mix rotates.
  • CQ-C4 (Fulcrum Driver) is Claxton Engineering + offshore decommissioning scale. The April 2026 acquisition of Claxton Engineering Services (GBP 63.5m, ~14% operating margins, ~GBP 82m average revenues) is the identifiable company-specific catalyst for above-peer EPS growth. If integrated cleanly and offshore losses are project-specific rather than structural, this expands the addressable decom market materially. The risk is that it deepens exposure to the problematic offshore segment at exactly the wrong time.
  • Property remains a drag with limited near-term resolution. The segment suffered from high Norwegian interest rates and collapsed housing sales throughout 2024. While Q4 2025 saw improvement via land sales, this is transactional rather than operational recovery. Norwegian residential construction contracted 3-4% in real terms in 2024; the recovery to modest growth in 2025-2026 is gradual.
  • Peer benchmarking places AFG mid-table. Strabag runs 6.1% EBIT margins; Skanska Construction runs ~4.5%; NCC runs 3%. AFG's civil-driven profile is competitive at the EPC/infrastructure end but Building segment margins are below group requirements per management's own admission.
  • The consensus "Hold" and NOK 173 average target (vs ~NOK 178 current) implies limited near-term re-rating. Analysts see EPS growth of ~12% ahead from the 2025 base, but upside is contingent on Claxton integration and no further offshore provisions. This is essentially a "show me" story rather than a momentum buy.

1. Executive Summary

Rating: Yellow

  • AF Gruppen is a NOK 32bn revenue Norwegian EPC contractor operating across Building, Civil Engineering, Energy/Environment, Offshore, Property, and Sweden. The group is emerging from a two-year earnings trough driven by weak residential construction, a problematic Building segment, and Property impairment. The 2025 full-year results represent a genuine step-change: EBT of NOK 1.65bn versus NOK 1.09bn in 2024 and NOK 0.70bn in 2023, implying a near-doubling of earnings over two years.
  • Within the construction peer group for this analysis (VEI, STR, NCC, SKA), AFG ranks third on margin trajectory. Strabag leads at 6.1% EBIT margin, Skanska Construction at ~4.5%, AFG group at ~5% EBT equivalent, Veidekke at ~4.1% pre-tax, NCC at ~3%. AFG's aggregate position looks middle-of-pack, but the Civil segment at 7-9% EBIT is class-leading among Nordic contractors.
  • The sector tailwind is Norway's National Transport Plan 2025-2036 committing approximately USD 40bn to rail and a doubling of rail maintenance budgets for 2025. This directly feeds AFG's Civil segment, which is the earnings quality anchor of the group.
  • The sector headwind is the Norwegian housing market. Dwelling permits fell 16.8% in 2023 and construction output contracted in both 2023 (-1.8%) and 2024 (-3.9%). Recovery in 2025 is tentative (+2% expected output growth), underpinned by 20%+ new home sales volume growth. This matters for AFG's Building and Property segments.
  • The Claxton Engineering acquisition (April 2026, GBP 63.5m EV) is the single most important forward-looking development. Claxton brings 400 employees, GBP ~82m revenues, and ~14% operating margins in offshore decommissioning and wind enablement. If successfully integrated, it accelerates the offshore segment recovery and diversifies AFG's revenue base away from residential construction cycles.
  • Management credibility is adequate but not exceptional. Building margins were described as "below requirements" in 2024 yet the segment delivered good Q4 2025 results — implying execution quality is volatile. The offshore project loss in Q4 2025 (despite strong group results) is a caution flag on management's ability to consistently execute in technically complex fixed-price environments.
  • EPS consensus forward growth of ~12% is achievable if: (1) Civil margins hold, (2) Building continues Q4 2025 momentum, (3) no further offshore provisions, (4) Claxton integrates cleanly. Any one of these failing reduces the bull case materially.
  • Free cash flow is strong: NOK 3.04bn operating cash flow in 2025 versus NOK 1.65bn pre-tax earnings implies cash conversion well above 100% of stated profits, a genuine positive in a sector where cash conversion is often poor due to working capital timing.
  • The stock has outperformed the Norwegian Construction index over the past 12 months, suggesting the market is pricing in some recovery. This limits upside from current levels unless earnings delivery exceeds the ~12% consensus EPS growth target.
  • The peer ranking on combined revenue/margin/backlog momentum: (1) Strabag — best in class margins, record backlog; (2) Skanska — record backlog, Construction at target margin; (3) AFG — strong civil, offshore drag, good backlog; (4) Veidekke — solid infrastructure, housing recovery lagging; (5) NCC — lowest margins in cohort, stable.
  • Overall: AFG sits in a "recovery credible but not yet proven" zone. The next 2-3 quarters must show Building margins sustaining, Offshore stabilising, and Claxton not introducing new cost surprises. Until then, this is a Yellow — interesting enough to continue investigating, not yet Green.

2. Revenue Growth & Organic Dynamics

Rating: Yellow

  • Full-year 2025 revenue of NOK 31.99bn compares to NOK 30.64bn in 2024, growth of 4.4%. This is below headline Norwegian construction sector nominal growth and below peer Strabag. The topline growth rate is modest.
  • Full-year 2024 revenue of NOK 30.64bn was itself down from NOK 31.2bn in 2022, meaning the business has been broadly flat to marginally declining in revenue terms over three years — 2023 saw a contraction as Building dropped 13%, before the 2025 partial recovery. This is not a revenue growth story.
  • The Building segment's 2024 revenue decline of 13% year-on-year is the dominant negative driver of the three-year revenue trend. Building is the largest single segment and its contraction outweighed Civil growth during 2023-2024. Q4 2025 results confirm Building delivered "very good results" suggesting the trough is behind — but the 13% prior-year contraction has left a low base.
  • Civil Engineering is the structural growth engine. The segment delivered 12% revenue growth in Q4 2025 versus the prior year, consistent with the NTP-driven infrastructure pipeline. Civil Engineering revenues grew 19% in Q2 2025 versus Q2 2024, indicating the acceleration is not a one-quarter phenomenon.
  • Revenue concentration risk: Norway accounts for the substantial majority of AFG revenue, with Sweden representing a second geography (~NOK 2-3bn revenue implied). The Sweden segment delivered EBIT of NOK 110m with 5% margins in Q4 2025 — acceptable but volatile.
  • Order intake of NOK 36.4bn for full-year 2025 implies a book-to-bill ratio of approximately 1.14x on 2025 revenues — positive. However, the Q4 2025 intake of NOK 9.6bn versus Q4 2024's NOK 12.5bn is a notable sequential deceleration. Approximately 23% less new business was signed in the most recent quarter compared to a year earlier.
  • Backlog of NOK 44.7bn at end-2025 represents approximately 1.4x LTM revenues — healthy for a contractor of this scale. However, the False Friends model applies: backlog growth from NOK 40.4bn to NOK 44.7bn year-on-year does not tell us whether the new work was signed at better or worse margins than the work executing off the book.
  • Norwegian residential construction recovery in 2025 has been driven primarily by price increases (new home sales volumes up 20%+) rather than starts volumes. The pipeline of physical units commencing construction in 2025-2026 remains below the 2021-2022 peak. This means the Building segment's workload recovery is gradual, not sharp.
  • Pricing power assessment: AFG has structural pricing power in Civil Engineering where its track record on large Norwegian public infrastructure projects creates oligopolistic competition with VEI. In Building, pricing power is minimal — the sector is fragmented and fixed-price contracts with inadequate escalation clauses created the 2022-2023 margin compression episode.
  • Sceptical interpretation: the "record revenue in Q4 2025" (NOK 9.25bn) narrative obscures the fact that full-year revenue growth of 4.4% is not exceptional, the order intake trend is weakening at the margin, and the revenue base has been essentially flat for three years. There is no sustained organic growth story here — the investment case is entirely about margin expansion, not revenue acceleration.
  • Section rating: Yellow

3. Margin Profile & Evolution

Rating: Yellow/Green

  • Group EBIT margin in 2024 was 3.8% (NOK 1,149m on NOK 30,638m revenues). In 2025 the EBT margin expanded to ~5.2% (NOK 1,653m on NOK 31,992m revenues). This is a 170 basis point improvement — material for a contractor, representing roughly a 50% earnings increase on roughly flat revenues.
  • Three-year margin trajectory: 2022 ~4.5% profit margin; 2023 a compression year; 2024 partial recovery at 3.8% EBIT; 2025 near-full recovery at ~5% EBT. The trend is unambiguously improving, but the recovery is still in progress rather than complete.
  • Civil Engineering is the margin anchor at 7-9% EBIT margins. This significantly outperforms typical civil construction peers and reflects AFG's established position in Norwegian public infrastructure work. The ~9.5% Q4 2025 margin is exceptional and likely to moderate toward 7-8% on a full-year sustainable basis as new work enters the portfolio at competitive tender margins.
  • Building segment margins were described as "below requirements" in 2024 but delivered "very good results" in Q4 2025. The commentary suggests recovery from negative/near-zero territory in 2024 toward the 2-3% range. Management has indicated a shift toward alliance/partnering contract models in Building, which if executed improves risk allocation.
  • Offshore segment was loss-making in Q4 2025, with a project loss of approximately NOK 100m. The Cockroaches Never Alone model is highly relevant: when an offshore contractor discloses a material project provision, the probability of further provisions within 6-12 months is elevated.
  • Peer margin comparison: Strabag 6.1% EBIT (2024), Skanska Construction ~4.5%, AFG ~5% (EBT 2025), Veidekke ~4.1% (pre-tax 2024), NCC ~3.0%. AFG sits roughly at or slightly above the peer median, led by its Civil outperformance, penalised by its Offshore drag.
  • The Worser, Odder, Longer, Further model applies specifically to the Offshore segment. The ~NOK 100m Q4 2025 project loss at AF Offshore Decom was disclosed as a single project event, but the nature of offshore decommissioning — technically complex, weather-dependent, logistically unpredictable — means initial cost estimates are inherently unreliable.
  • Input cost inflation: Norwegian construction input costs have been elevated through 2022-2024 alongside broader Nordic inflation. Civil Engineering's public contracts with escalation clauses provided protection; Building's fixed-price residential work did not. The inflation cycle is moderating in 2025 as Norges Bank has begun easing, which is constructive for Building margin recovery.
  • Sceptical interpretation: the 2025 margin improvement from 3.5% to 5.2% EBT is highly concentrated in Civil (structural, durable) and in Q4 Building (potentially reversionary, driven by project completions). The full-year 2026 margin trajectory depends critically on no new offshore provisions and Building maintaining Q4 2025 momentum — neither is guaranteed.
  • Section rating: Yellow/Green

4. EPS Trajectory & Delivery

Rating: Yellow

  • Full-year 2025 earnings before tax of NOK 1,653m implies basic EPS (on approximately 120m shares outstanding) of roughly NOK 9.8-10.5 per share after tax. The consensus forward EPS estimate per available data is approximately NOK 11.07, implying ~12% growth from the 2025 base.
  • The 2023-2025 EPS arc shows substantial recovery: from the trough year 2023 (EBT ~NOK 700m) through 2024 (NOK 1,085m EBT) to 2025 (NOK 1,653m EBT), earnings have more than doubled over two years. This is a genuine cyclical recovery in EPS, driven by Civil margin expansion and Building trough exit.
  • Dividend delivery is a positive signal: management proposed a 30% dividend increase to NOK 6.50 per share for 2025, up from NOK 5.00 for 2024. The increase signals board confidence in the cash generation profile. On a NOK 178 share price the indicated dividend yield is approximately 3.6%.
  • The Q4 2025 result missed Kepler Cheuvreux forecasts at the EBIT level by approximately 9%, driven by Offshore losses and Sweden underperformance relative to consensus. This is a meaningful miss rate that reduces confidence in the precision of near-term earnings forecasts and management delivery consistency.
  • No buyback program has been identified — AFG's capital returns are dividend-centric. This contrasts with Skanska, which runs active buyback programs. In the absence of buybacks, EPS growth is entirely dependent on earnings growth rather than share count reduction.
  • Leverage is conservative. Net interest-bearing receivables of NOK 1,274m at end-2025 means AFG is in a net cash/receivables position. This provides headroom for the Claxton acquisition (GBP 63.5m / ~NOK 800m) without material balance sheet stress.
  • Capital allocation: the Claxton acquisition at GBP 63.5m EV for a business with ~GBP 82m revenues and ~14% margins implies an acquisition multiple of approximately 5-6x EBIT. This is reasonable for a niche offshore services business with proprietary technology.
  • Working capital dynamics in construction are inherently favourable for strong contractors. AFG's NOK 3.04bn operating cash flow against NOK 1.65bn pre-tax profit (cash conversion ratio approximately 184%) reflects this structural working capital benefit.
  • Forward EPS risk factors: (1) further Offshore provisions in H1 2026 as the problematic project works through; (2) Claxton acquisition costs and integration friction in 2026; (3) Norwegian residential construction recovery slower than anticipated; (4) Civil margin normalisation from the current ~9.5% back toward ~7%.
  • Sceptical EPS case: if Offshore provisions total another NOK 150-200m in 2026, and Building fails to sustain Q4 2025 margins, and Civil moderates to 7%, EBT for 2026 could be flat to down marginally on 2025 rather than growing the expected 12%. This is not the base case but is a plausible downside.
  • Section rating: Yellow

5. Guidance & Forecast Changes

Rating: Yellow

  • Management does not provide explicit quantitative EPS or EBIT guidance, which is standard practice for Nordic contractors. The Q4 2025 release stated the company "ended the year with solid results and a strong order intake" — directionally positive but lacking precision.
  • The 30% dividend increase to NOK 6.50 per share is the strongest forward-looking signal management has provided. This implies the board believes the earnings recovery is durable rather than one-off.
  • Order backlog of NOK 44.7bn at end-2025 provides approximately 16-17 months of forward revenue visibility at current run rates. This is a qualitative guidance signal — clients cannot easily cancel, and execution against the existing book gives reasonable earnings predictability for H1 2026.
  • Guidance pattern over the last 18 months: Q4 2024 guidance signalled continued strength in Civil and partial Building recovery, which was delivered. Q3 2025 narrative described "good profitability and solid order backlog." Management has not materially misled the market on the macro trajectory, but the Q4 2025 Offshore loss was not flagged in Q3 commentary.
  • The Claxton acquisition (announced April 2026) was not in sell-side models and is expected to complete Q2 2026. It will add approximately GBP 82m (~NOK 1,050m) to revenues and potentially ~NOK 115-130m to EBIT (at 14% margin) on a full-year annualised basis.
  • Norwegian interest rates: Norges Bank began cutting rates in late 2024, which is constructive for the Property segment and for Building volumes via housing market recovery. However, Norway's rate cuts have been slower and shallower than ECB or Riksbank, meaning Property margin recovery is gradual.
  • Infrastructure spending guidance from the Norwegian government: the 2025 budget included approximately NOK 2 trillion in total spending, up 6.8% year-on-year, with rail maintenance nearly doubled. This provides a multi-year forward visibility for AFG's Civil segment that is government-policy-backed rather than demand-driven.
  • The forward consensus EPS growth of ~12% implies analysts are modelling continued Civil strength, Building stabilisation, and no material Offshore provisions. This is a plausible but not conservative set of assumptions.
  • Sceptical interpretation: management's qualitative guidance language is deliberately vague. The pattern of Q4 Offshore surprises warrants monitoring. Investors should assign a non-trivial probability to guidance disappointment in H1 2026 as the Claxton integration costs flow through alongside any legacy Offshore project closeouts.
  • Section rating: Yellow

6. Comparative Peer Ranking

Rating: Yellow

  • Peer group ranked on combined 2024-2025 EBIT margin: (1) Strabag 6.1%, (2) AFG Civil-weighted ~5% group EBT, (3) Skanska Construction 4.5%, (4) Veidekke ~4.1%, (5) NCC ~3.0%. This places AFG second on pure profitability, but the Strabag comparison reflects different geographies.
  • On order backlog as % of LTM revenue: Strabag 146% (EUR 25.4bn on EUR 17.4bn revenues), Skanska record backlog SEK 285bn on SEK 177bn revenues = 161%, AFG 140% (NOK 44.7bn on NOK 32bn), NCC approximately 86%. On this metric AFG is mid-table: better than NCC, behind Skanska.
  • Book-to-bill trend: Skanska achieved a rolling 12-month book-to-bill of 123% in 2024, indicating strong new business momentum. AFG's Q4 2025 order intake fell 23% year-on-year, and the full-year book-to-bill of 1.14x, while above 1x, is trending down. This is a relative deterioration in momentum versus Skanska.
  • On revenue growth momentum L12M: Strabag +1%, Skanska +13%, AFG +4.4%, Veidekke -4%, NCC stable. AFG is middle of the cohort. Veidekke's revenue decline is a forward comparator for AFG's Building exposure — both are working through the same market dislocation.
  • Cash conversion (operating cash flow vs pre-tax earnings): AFG ~184% is best-in-class versus peers. This is a genuine AFG differentiator.
  • Dividend growth: AFG 30% dividend increase to NOK 6.50 for 2025 is the most aggressive dividend growth action among the identified peer group in the relevant reporting period.
  • Strabag's record EUR 25bn order backlog (8% increase year-on-year) suggests the pan-European infrastructure cycle is broadly supportive for the sector. This is directionally positive for AFG's Civil segment, but AFG lacks Strabag's geographic diversification into Central/Eastern Europe.
  • AFG rank change L12M: broadly stable at mid-table. The Civil margin outperformance is not new information. The improvement in overall ranking is contingent on the Offshore segment being fixed. Without that fix, AFG remains structurally capped at mid-table.
  • Sceptical interpretation: the headline metrics flatter AFG because Civil Engineering's 7-9% margins boost the group average. Strip out Civil and look at the remaining AFG portfolio (Building at thin margins, Offshore at loss, Property at weak, Sweden at 5%) and the underlying business quality is closer to NCC than to Strabag.
  • Section rating: Yellow

7. Sector & Value Chain Context

Rating: Yellow/Green

  • Norwegian construction output contracted in both 2023 (-1.8%) and 2024 (-3.9%) in real terms — the first two-year consecutive contraction in over a decade. The primary driver was the collapse in residential construction as the sharp Norges Bank rate cycle (benchmark rate from 0% to 4.5% between 2021 and 2023) crushed housing affordability.
  • The 2025 recovery is expected to be modest at +2% output growth, recovering further to +3.4-3.6% annually through 2026-2029. Building contractors face 2-3 more years of below-peak volumes even in the base case.
  • Norwegian infrastructure spending is the structural counterweight. The National Transport Plan 2025-2036 includes approximately USD 40bn in rail investment and NOK 6.6bn in rail maintenance for 2025 alone (nearly doubling the 2024 budget). The E39 highway project (NOK 33bn+ approved) provides a decade-long Civil Engineering order pipeline.
  • The competitive intensity in Civil is bifurcated. Large infrastructure projects (>NOK 500m contract value) are contested by AFG, Veidekke, and international majors (Strabag, Skanska, occasionally NCC and Peab). AFG's scale and track record positions it well for the large project tier where rational competition limits price erosion.
  • Energy transition tailwinds: Norway's ambitious renewable energy targets and offshore decommissioning requirements are growing the addressable market for AFG's Energy & Environment and Offshore segments. The Claxton acquisition is a direct bet on this theme.
  • Input cost dynamics: Norwegian construction cost inflation peaked in 2022-2023 and is moderating. Labour costs remain elevated as the Norwegian labour market is tight, but materials have deflated from peak levels. This is constructive for fixed-price Building contract margins on work signed in late 2024/2025.
  • The Demand Pulse Reversion model is relevant here. The Norwegian Civil Engineering order book build in 2023-2025 is partly structural (NTP commitments) and partly cyclical (pent-up public spending post-COVID). The NTP structural commitment is legally appropriated and unlikely to reverse under any plausible political scenario.
  • European construction sector context: Strabag's record backlog, Skanska's record backlog, and NCC's stable order book all confirm that the pan-European infrastructure cycle is supportive. Defence infrastructure spending (NATO commitments) is adding a new demand driver that AFG is well-positioned to capture.
  • Section rating: Yellow/Green

8. Quality of Earnings & Accounting

Rating: Yellow

  • Cash conversion is the standout quality metric. Operating cash flow of NOK 3.04bn versus EBT of NOK 1.65bn implies a cash conversion ratio of approximately 184%. For a contractor, this is exceptional — it confirms that reported profits are not being held in receivables or work-in-progress, but are converting to cash.
  • The negative working capital structure typical of large contractors (advance payments, milestone billing ahead of cost incurrence) explains much of the cash conversion premium. However, it also creates a risk in volume decline scenarios: if revenue declines, the advance payment benefit reverses, potentially creating a temporary cash outflow even as earnings hold up.
  • Net interest-bearing receivables of NOK 1,274m at end-2025 indicates the balance sheet is net cash positive — a clean position for a contractor. This is consistent with Norwegian construction contractors operating in a sovereign-backed infrastructure market with reliable payment terms.
  • One-off items: the Q4 2025 Offshore project loss of approximately NOK 100m is described as project-specific. Management characterisation of project losses as one-off is standard in the sector but should be treated sceptically — examination of the L3Y earnings history for AFG shows episodic impairments are recurrent rather than truly isolated.
  • Revenue recognition on long-duration construction contracts follows IFRS 15 (percentage of completion). For Civil Engineering's large-ticket infrastructure projects, this creates the potential for front-loading revenue recognition followed by margin haircuts on project close.
  • The Property segment's land sales in Q4 2025 are economically real but lumpy. The two land sales drove the segment's Q4 2025 recovery — these are non-recurring and tied to specific asset disposals rather than operational income.
  • Dividend sustainability: the proposed NOK 6.50 dividend for 2025 represents approximately 60-65% of estimated EPS (at NOK 10-11 EPS). This is a sustainable payout ratio for a contractor with positive net cash and strong cash conversion.
  • Sceptical accounting view: the combination of (1) percentage-of-completion revenue recognition on large Civil projects, (2) episodic offshore provisions, and (3) Property earnings driven by land disposals creates a picture where "core" recurring earnings are potentially lower than reported group profits in any given year.
  • Section rating: Yellow

9. Sceptical Summary

Rating: Orange

  • The single largest risk is the Offshore segment. AF Offshore Decom is running fixed-price decommissioning contracts in the North Sea — technically complex, weather-exposed, logistics-intensive work with limited ability to pass cost overruns to clients. The Q4 2025 NOK 100m project loss is most likely not the final word on this contract, and the Claxton acquisition adds more complexity to an already strained segment before the existing problems are resolved.
  • The False Friends model destroys the simple backlog narrative. AFG's NOK 44.7bn order backlog looks compelling on its face, but: Q4 2025 order intake fell 23% year-on-year; the backlog composition is unknown; and backlog booked at thin margins (Building) or loss-adjusted margins (Offshore) contributes revenues without proportionate earnings.
  • The Building segment's "very good" Q4 2025 results follow a period of deliberate volume reduction (13% revenue decline in 2024). There is a risk that Q4 2025 performance reflects favourable project completion timing rather than a structural improvement in the quality of new work being signed. Sustainable Building margin improvement requires 2-3 more quarters of consistent delivery.
  • Management guidance is qualitative and backward-referencing. The absence of quantitative forward guidance means investors cannot independently verify the earnings trajectory. The Q4 2025 EBIT miss versus Kepler Cheuvreux was driven by Offshore and Sweden underperformance — segments where execution risk is inherently higher.
  • Early warning signals to monitor: (1) any Q1 or Q2 2026 disclosure of further Offshore project provisions — the Cockroaches Never Alone model says one is rarely enough; (2) Order intake in Q1 2026 — if the Q4 2025 weakness continues, the backlog will erode; (3) Civil margin normalisation below 7% on a full-year 2026 basis; (4) Claxton integration costs and any revenue disruption post-acquisition completion in Q2 2026.
  • Bear case synthesis: Offshore provisions total another NOK 150-250m in H1 2026; Building margin recovery stalls; Civil margins normalise from 9.5% to 7%; Claxton integration creates NOK 50-100m of friction costs. On this scenario, group EBT for 2026 could fall to NOK 1.2-1.4bn versus the consensus of ~NOK 1.85bn implied by 12% growth. That would represent a 15-25% earnings disappointment, likely resulting in a 15-20% share price decline.
  • Overall confidence level on the investment case at this Triaging stage: LOW-MEDIUM. The macro context and Civil segment justify continued investigation. The Offshore segment is an open liability, the Building recovery is unproven over multiple quarters, and the Claxton acquisition adds execution risk at a sensitive time. This is not a PARK — the earnings trajectory and dividend growth are genuine positives. But it is not a PROGRESS to ESA without seeing at least Q1 2026 results, Offshore provision clarity, and Claxton completion without major surprises.
  • What causes underperformance vs peers: AFG underperforms Strabag if (1) Offshore losses persist; (2) Civil margins normalise faster than expected; (3) Property fails to recover. The Offshore segment is the primary differentiator — negatively. It is the one element of AFG's portfolio with no structural quality argument and real downside scenario asymmetry.
  • Overall rating: Orange
