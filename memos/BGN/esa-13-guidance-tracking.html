Conservative "sandbagging" philosophy drives Banca Generali's guidance strategy, with CEO Gian Maria Mossa explicitly acknowledging the bank prefers to under-promise and over-deliver, ensuring initial targets are comfortably achievable and progressively upgraded throughout the year.

Consistent net inflow outperformance over the past three years, with FY 2025 delivering €6.8 billion versus the €6.0 billion baseline target and higher-margin AUI reaching €4.0 billion against the €3.5 billion expectation.

Historical Guidance Track Record (L3Y) Management Behavior: A Philosophy of Sandbagging Over the past three years, Banca Generali's management has established a clear and consistent track record of under-promising and over-delivering on key financial and commercial targets. The bank's executive leadership has explicitly acknowledged this strategy during earnings calls, with CEO Gian Maria Mossa stating that the bank prefers to start with a conservative approach and then overdeliver. This "sandbagging" methodology ensures that initial market expectations are comfortably achievable, allowing the bank to progressively upgrade guidance throughout the year as visibility improves. This approach has fostered strong credibility with the market, as targets are structurally set to be overachieved rather than merely met. 1 2 3 4 • Transcript - Earnings Calls Net Inflows: Consistent Outperformance Banca Generali has systematically beaten its net inflow guidance across the trailing three-year period.
