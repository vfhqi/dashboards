Value-Chain Relationships and Dynamics Value Chain Architecture and Core Operations The structural design of Banca Generali's value chain is engineered to maximize operational efficiency while delivering a broad spectrum of high-quality services to its client base. The architecture is formally divided into upstream, core, and downstream operational activities. Upstream activities encompass the sourcing of strategic goods, IT services, and the establishment of partnerships for both financial and non-financial solutions. Core operations are performed internally and focus heavily on managing and developing the proprietary workforce, which includes both corporate employees and the extensive network of Financial Advisors. Downstream activities consist of direct business interactions with the end-customer, encompassing holistic advisory services, investment product distribution, and relationship management. This structure is underpinned by an open banking and open architecture approach that allows the bank to leverage best-of-breed partnerships rather than relying solely on proprietary product manufacturing. 1 2 3 5 7 • ARS 8 • Investor Presentation

Distribution Network and Client Dynamics Banca Generali's distribution model is heavily dependent on a specialized network of 2,405 Financial Advisors, which includes Senior Partners, Wealth Managers, Private Bankers, and Relationship Managers. This network consistently ranks at the top of the Assoreti industry index for per-capita productivity and assets under management. The structural dynamics between these advisors and the high-net-worth client base are highly personalized, relying on physical presence and a profound understanding of complex family and business needs. To maintain this competitive distribution advantage, the bank operates a flexible remuneration model linked directly to revenue generation. The overall total payout ratio to the network, excluding NII distributions, was tightly managed and successfully brought down to 51.5%. 9 • ARS 10 • Investor Presentation 11 12 13 • Transcript - Earnings Calls The 'Insurbanking' Strategic Partnership A pivotal element in the expansion of Banca Generali's value chain is the 'Insurbanking' partnership with Alleanza Assicurazioni, launched to unlock growth within the affluent customer segment. Alleanza possesses a highly capable distribution network of 10,000 insurance advisors and a client base of nearly 2 million individuals. Internal estimates suggest these clients hold approximately €170 billion in targetable financial wealth that is currently managed by third-party institutions. The partnership aims to internalize these assets through integrated product offerings, specifically the "Conto Unico" current account and the "Stile Unico" unit-linked insurance wrapper. By 2030, Banca Generali projects this initiative will generate between €7.0 billion and €8.5 billion in total volumes. Despite periods of positive business momentum, the stock trades at a higher price-to-earnings multiple compared to its historical averages. Because of this elevated valuation premium, brokers often conclude that there are more attractive risk-reward opportunities available elsewhere within the Italian asset gathering space. Analysts point out that Banca Generali has periodically underperformed its direct peers in generating robust assets under management (AuM) inflows, making its high valuation harder to justify in volatile markets. 35 36 • Jefferies Research 37 • Jefferies Research 38 • Jefferies Research Vulnerabilities to Net New Money and Market Fluctuations A primary fundamental vulnerability identified in bear case analyses is the bank's acute sensitivity to lower-than-anticipated net new money (NNM). Any shortfall in net new money generation directly and adversely impacts the bank's core commission income. Furthermore, Banca Generali's revenue model is highly geared to the broader financial markets through the fees it earns on managed and administered assets. Consequently, worse-than-expected market performance triggers a rapid repricing of AuM stocks, which structurally depresses both recurring management fees and lucrative performance fees. Analysts have previously been forced to cut earnings per share estimates by up to 8 percent during periods of market jitters simply due to the mark-to-market impact on the bank's asset base. 39 40 41 • Jefferies Research 42 43 • Jefferies Research Margin Compression and Competitive Pricing Pressures Margin compression represents a severe headwind within bearish sell-side frameworks. Through its deep operational ties with its parent company, Assicurazioni Generali, the bank is indirectly exposed to broader life margin compression driven by falling asset yields. Analysts explicitly warn of intensifying competitive pressures specifically targeting the pricing structures of unit-linked and asset management products. This structural margin pressure could be rapidly accelerated by technological disruption. Brokers highlight that the emergence of sophisticated, AI-driven tax and wealth planning tools threatens to disintermediate traditional financial advisors, potentially pulling retail savings away from incumbents and severely compressing advisory margins. Additionally, potential regulatory changes from the European Commission aimed at reducing or banning inducement payments could further erode the profitability of the investment industry. 44 45 46 • Jefferies Research 47 48 • Jefferies Research 49 • Jefferies Research The Reversal of M&A Speculation A unique idiosyncratic risk heavily emphasized in sell-side research is Banca Generali's reliance on corporate takeover narratives. The stock has frequently been the subject of intense M&A speculation, which has historically injected a speculative premium into its share price. Analysts explicitly caution that any reversal, delay, or collapse of this M&A speculation could trigger a sharp derating and negatively affect the stock. Removing this speculative premium would force the market to value the bank strictly on its standalone fundamentals, which are already constrained by the aforementioned AuM inflow and pricing pressures.50 52 • Jefferies Research 51 • Jefferies Research Downside Scenarios and Target Price Risks To quantify these overlapping threats, broker models include severe downside scenarios that project material target price contractions. In a standard bearish model, analysts assume that interest rates fall 50 basis points below the base case, drastically reducing net interest income over average assets. Under this stressed framework, projected fee compound annual growth rates drop significantly, pushing the cost-to-income ratio up to 44 percent. This combination of lower rates, compressed fees, and adverse provision cycles results in modeled downside risks of 15 percent to over 20 percent from base target prices. Furthermore, these downside scenarios project a steep drop in the bank's return on tangible equity, falling to roughly 24 percent in stressed years. 53 • Jefferies Research 54 55 56 57 • Jefferies Research Critical Views and Fundamental Risks Macroeconomic Vulnerabilities and Spread Sensitivity Banca Generali's balance sheet structure exposes the institution to specific macroeconomic vulnerabilities, particularly regarding sovereign debt fluctuations. The bank was forced to allocate a net €61.5 million in non-recurring provisions and value adjustments during the year to address repayment issues on illiquid products sold to customers. These legacy products, which included securitized healthcare receivables, suffered severe liquidity and valuation pressures. 66 67 • ARS To protect its reputation and restore potential customer losses, the bank deployed €45.5 million specifically for commercial initiatives and the issuance of financial guarantees. Advanced AI algorithms and digital agents present emerging risks related to technological dependence and the potential disintermediation of traditional banking channels. 73 • ARS Jefferies notes that the sector experienced a sharp sell-off following the launch of an AI-powered tax and wealth planning platform by a US fintech competitor. This event crystallized market fears that sophisticated AI tools could eventually disintermediate incumbent wealth managers by directly serving retail savers. They compare the current threat landscape to the historical introduction of price comparison websites, which permanently altered distribution dynamics in other financial sectors. 77 78 • Morgan Stanley Despite these bearish structural views, Banca Generali's management strongly rejects the narrative that AI will commoditize its core business. Because these holistic advisory needs demand strict confidentiality and deep personal trust, management insists clients will not entrust this sensitive data to automated machines.
