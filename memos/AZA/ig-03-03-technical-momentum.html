This is a meaningful cautionary signal. In the Minervini framework, a healthy stage 2 advance should exhibit:

  • Above-average volume on up-days (institutional accumulation)
  • Below-average volume on down-days (lack of distribution)
  • Volume expansion on breakouts (confirmation of directional moves)

The current volume profile suggests the opposite pattern is emerging — volume is declining as price approaches resistance. This is consistent with a lack of institutional buying pressure at current levels. It does NOT invalidate the stage 2 thesis, but it suggests that any breakout attempt at SEK 365-381 may fail unless accompanied by a volume surge.

2. Pullback volume: If AZA pulls back to the 200-DMA at ~SEK 349, volume should contract (below 200k shares) to indicate the pullback is orderly and not distribution.

3. Capitulation volume: A spike in volume on a sharp down-day (>500k shares) would be a potential exhaustion/washout signal and could mark a tradeable bottom.
