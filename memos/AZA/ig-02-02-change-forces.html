[W] Avanza Bank (AZA-SE) — Change Forces [C]

Query #2-C | IG Stage | 19-Apr-26

Key Findings

  • Rate cycle bottoming out. Riksbank cut from 4.0% to 1.75% between mid-2024 and Sep 2025. Rate now stable at 1.75% through 2026; first hike expected Q1 2027 (Handelsbanken) or Nov 2026 (DNB Carnegie). Avanza's NII proved resilient through the cutting cycle — volume growth offset rate compression.
  • Record FY2025 results. Revenue SEK ~4.96bn (+16% YoY), operating profit SEK 3.08bn, net profit SEK 2.63bn (+17%). Return on equity 40%, exceeding 35% target. Cost/savings capital ratio improved to 14.1bps from 14.5bps.
  • Customer growth re-accelerating. Added 171,000 net new customers in 2025, reaching 2.24m. Savings capital crossed SEK 1,079bn (8.3% Swedish savings market share, up from ~7.5% at start of 2025).
  • Platform model generating operating leverage. Since 2019, income up ~180% vs costs up ~30%. Mainly fixed cost base (~70% staff). Revenue mix diversifying: brokerage, NII, fund commissions, currency income, insurance/pensions all contributing.
  • Occupational pensions = major growth vector. 10.6% market share of non-collective-agreement occupational pension premiums. Lifeplan partnership for advisory. Pension savings capital growing but market share slipped slightly (11.0% to 10.6%). Strategic priority with dedicated 2026 investment.
  • Private Banking + DPM launch imminent. Sigmastocks acquisition (May 2025, SEK 21m) enables digital discretionary portfolio management for 35,000+ Private Banking clients. Targeting SEK 1,000bn traditional DPM market.
  • Swedish structural tailwinds powerful. Nearly 4m Swedes hold ISK accounts (~40% of population). ISK tax-free threshold doubling to SEK 300,000 in 2026. Household financial assets = 329% of GDP (2nd highest in EU). 70% fund saving participation rate.
  • Competitive moat deepening. 16 consecutive years as Sweden's most satisfied savings customers (SQI). NPS of 49. Avanza + Nordnet together dominate, and traditional banks (SEB, Swedbank, Handelsbanken) unable to compete on fees or UX.
  • PFOF ban is a non-issue for Avanza. EU ban on payment for order flow effective 2026 — primarily impacts zero-commission neobrokers. Avanza charges explicit commissions and does not rely on PFOF revenue.
  • 2030 strategic targets ambitious. Savings capital target SEK 2,000bn (vs 1,079bn today = 13% CAGR needed). Average annual cost growth below 8%, trending to 5% by 2030.

1. Swedish Rate Environment

The cutting cycle is over. Rates stable at 1.75% through 2026.

The Riksbank halved its policy rate from 4.0% to 2.0% between mid-2024 and early 2025, then delivered a final 25bps cut to 1.75% in September 2025. The December 2025 meeting held rates unchanged. The January 2026 meeting also held. The Riksbank's forward guidance: rates flat at 1.75% through 2026, with slow increases thereafter.

Rate path expectations from key forecasters:

  • Riksbank: Policy rate expected to remain at 1.75% in 2026, then begin "slow increases."
  • Handelsbanken: First rate hike forecast for Q1 2027.
  • DNB Carnegie: More hawkish — first hike November 2026, second January 2027, terminal rate 2.50% in late 2027.

The rationale for extended pause: inflation outlook benign, spare capacity in the economy, but growth recovering. Governor Erik Thedeen characterised the current stance as appropriate given the balance of risks.

Implications for Avanza: The 225bps of rate cuts from peak represented a material headwind to NII. However, the stabilisation at 1.75% removes further downside. Any future rate hikes would be a tailwind. The key question is whether Avanza can continue growing deposit and lending volumes fast enough to offset the lower rate environment. Evidence from 2025 suggests yes — NII held up despite 100bps of cuts during the year, thanks to volume expansion.

2. Equity Market Activity & Trading Volumes

Swedish equity markets buoyant. Trading activity elevated.

The OMX Stockholm 30 index stood at ~3,137 in mid-April 2026, up ~17% over the trailing twelve months and ~31% versus a year prior. Swedish equity markets outperformed European peers in 2025, supported by tech/industrials exposure and a weaker krona.

Nasdaq Stockholm was the number one trading venue in Nordic equities in 2025. Key activity metrics:

  • Retail investors account for more than 30% of turnover, especially in the growth market (First North).
  • International banks and brokers represent ~70% of turnover — important context for understanding who drives volume.
  • Nordic single stock options hit record traded notional value and open interest in 2025.
  • Derivatives activity surging: Danish SSO trading +209% YoY, Norwegian +55%.

Avanza-specific activity data:

  • Avanza is the largest Swedish player by number of transactions and turnover on Nasdaq Stockholm including First North.
  • Brokerage margin stable at approximately 11.2-11.4bps through 2025.
  • Private Banking and pro customers' share of brokerage rose to 26% (from 24%), compressing the blended margin slightly.
  • Foreign trading's share of turnover returned to high levels.

Assessment: Elevated equity markets and strong retail participation are direct tailwinds for Avanza's brokerage income. The shift toward larger-ticket Private Banking customers is margin-dilutive per trade but volume-accretive. Rising derivatives activity in the Nordics could present future product expansion opportunities.

3. Customer Acquisition & Market Share

Steady customer acquisition. Market share grinding higher.

Avanza ended 2025 with 2,242,700 customers, having added 171,000 net new customers during the year. Monthly net additions varied significantly:

  • March 2025: 22,100 (strong month, likely driven by Q1 market activity)
  • May 2025: 7,600 (post-Easter lull)
  • August 2025: 13,400
  • September 2025: 15,000
  • October 2025: 19,500
  • November 2025: 8,900

Average monthly run-rate: ~14,300 net new customers. The variability correlates with market sentiment and seasonal patterns. High-activity months (January, March, October) consistently outperform.

Market share progression:

  • January 2025: 7.5% of Swedish savings market
  • August 2025: 7.9%
  • Year-end 2025: 8.3%

This 80bps market share gain in a single year is substantial. Savings capital crossed SEK 1,079bn at year-end, driven by SEK 54bn net inflows plus market appreciation.

Avanza vs Nordnet comparison (year-end 2025):

| Metric | Avanza | Nordnet |

|--------|--------|---------|

| Total customers | 2.24m (Sweden only) | 2.35m (Nordics) |

| Savings capital | SEK 1,079bn | SEK 1,183bn |

| Net savings 2025 | SEK 54bn | SEK 60.2bn |

| Sweden savings capital | SEK 1,079bn | SEK 389bn |

| Geographic scope | Sweden only | Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland |

| Customer satisfaction | #1 (16 consecutive years) | Strong but trails Avanza |

Critical point: Avanza's SEK 1,079bn is entirely Swedish, versus Nordnet's SEK 389bn in Sweden (out of SEK 1,183bn total). Avanza holds roughly 2.7x Nordnet's Swedish savings capital. This is the dominant market position that matters for competitive analysis.

4. Net Interest Income Dynamics

NII resilient through rate-cutting cycle. Volume growth the key offset.

Avanza's NII proved more durable than feared during 2025. Despite 100bps of Riksbank rate cuts during the year (from 2.75% to 1.75%), full-year NII increased year-on-year. The mechanism:

Deposit side:

  • Customer deposits stood at SEK 76.9bn as of Q1 2025, growing meaningfully through the year.
  • Deposit volumes increased by close to SEK 10bn in Q2 2025 alone.
  • Avanza is self-financed through equity and customer deposits — no wholesale funding dependency.
  • Only a small share of deposits is used for lending; Avanza maintains a structural liquidity surplus.

Lending side:

  • Internally financed lending volumes increased 20% in Q1 2025.
  • Average internal lending rate declined from 3.40% (Q1) to 3.01% (Q3) to 2.81% (Q4) as policy rate cuts passed through.
  • Margin lending rate reduced by 13bps following the latest rate cut.
  • Mortgage rate reduced by 25bps (fuller pass-through on mortgages).
  • Mortgage volumes increased by over SEK 1bn in Q4 alone, mainly from Private Banking customers.

Historical sensitivity benchmark: When the Riksbank hiked by 75bps in a prior cycle, Avanza estimated the annualised NII benefit at ~SEK 50m. This implies sensitivity of roughly SEK 65-70m per 100bps change — though the relationship is not perfectly linear given deposit beta dynamics and lending volume changes.

Net assessment: The worst of the NII headwind is behind. Rates stable at 1.75% through 2026 means no further compression. Lending volume growth (mortgages, margin) continues to build the earning asset base. Any rate hike from 2027 onward would be incrementally positive. The risk is if a recession forces emergency rate cuts below 1.75%.

5. Fee Income Diversification

Revenue mix broadening. Multiple growth vectors beyond brokerage.

Avanza's full-year 2025 revenue reached approximately SEK 4.96bn (+16% YoY). The company reports operating income across these line items:

1. Net brokerage income — the largest single contributor, driven by number of trades and average commission. Grew strongly in 2025 thanks to elevated equity market activity and rising customer numbers. Brokerage margin stable at ~11.2-11.4bps.

2. Net interest income — second-largest contributor. Held up despite rate cuts (see Section 4). Growth driven by deposit and lending volume expansion.

3. Net fund commissions — increased in 2025. Fund margin stable at ~25bps on average (24.8bps at September 2025). Driven by growth in savings capital allocated to funds.

4. Net currency-related income — significant and growing contributor. Driven by customer trading in non-SEK securities (US equities, European stocks). As international allocation by Swedish retail investors increases, this income stream benefits.

5. Other income — declined in 2025. Includes miscellaneous fees.

6. Insurance/pension income — embedded within fund commissions and other lines. Occupational pension premiums growing but market share recently slipped (11.0% to 10.6%).

Operating cost structure:

  • FY2025 operating expenses increased 20% to SEK 402m (quarterly figure; annualised ~SEK 1.6bn).
  • Cost increase driven by higher other expenses, personnel costs, and marketing.
  • But cost/savings capital ratio improved to 14.1bps (from 14.5bps), demonstrating operating leverage.
  • 2026 cost guidance: +9% increase, driven by Private Banking, DPM launch, and pension investments.

Revenue diversification trajectory: The direction is clear — Avanza is moving from a pure brokerage platform toward a comprehensive financial platform. The Private Banking push, DPM launch, occupational pensions, mortgages, and currency trading income all reduce dependency on any single revenue line. This is strategically important given brokerage income's inherent cyclicality.

6. Competitive Landscape

Avanza dominates in Sweden. Nordnet is the only credible challenger. Traditional banks failing to compete.

Avanza vs Nordnet:

  • Avanza is the undisputed #1 in Sweden with 2.7x Nordnet's Swedish savings capital.
  • Nordnet's diversification across four Nordic markets is both a strength (geographic risk) and a distraction (management bandwidth).
  • Pareto Securities explicitly prefers Avanza to Nordnet due to Sweden-only focus and lower NII dependency.
  • Both offer commission-free trading for first SEK 50,000, ISK accounts, mobile-first UX.
  • Avanza perceived as "simpler and more pedagogical"; Nordnet has Shareville (social trading) and more advanced tools.

Traditional Swedish banks (SEB, Swedbank, Handelsbanken, Nordea):

  • Consistently unable to compete on fees, UX, or product breadth for retail savings/investing.
  • Avanza's stated position: "It is difficult for traditional banks to compete against [Avanza and Nordnet]."
  • Traditional banks retain advantages in corporate banking, advisory, and full-service relationships.
  • Mortgage competition is the one area where traditional banks remain formidable — Avanza's mortgage product is growing but still niche.

Neobroker threat:

  • EU neobrokers (eToro, Trade Republic, etc.) have limited penetration in Sweden.
  • The ISK account wrapper is Sweden-specific and creates a natural moat for domestic platforms.
  • PFOF ban from 2026 actually hurts neobrokers more than Avanza (see Section 7).
  • One emerging Swedish platform reportedly positioning to challenge Avanza/Nordnet, but no meaningful traction yet.

Competitive moat assessment: Avanza's moat is multi-layered: (1) brand/trust built over 25+ years, (2) ISK/pension product complexity creates switching costs, (3) ecosystem breadth (trading, funds, pensions, mortgages, insurance) increases stickiness, (4) scale advantages in cost-to-serve, (5) 16-year customer satisfaction streak creates powerful referral dynamics.

7. Regulatory & Policy Forces

Net positive regulatory environment. ISK tax benefit expansion is a direct catalyst.

ISK tax reform (major tailwind):

  • The ISK (Investeringssparkonto) is Sweden's tax-advantaged investment wrapper.
  • Nearly 4 million Swedes (~40% of population) hold ISK accounts.
  • The tax-free threshold on ISK and endowment insurance was set at SEK 150,000 for income year 2025.
  • Doubling to SEK 300,000 for income year 2026. This is a significant incentive for incremental savings into ISK accounts, directly benefiting platforms like Avanza.

PFOF ban (neutral to positive for Avanza):

  • EU MiFIR amendments banned payment for order flow, effective from 2026.
  • Zero-commission neobrokers that derived 25%+ of revenue from PFOF face existential business model challenges.
  • Avanza charges explicit commissions (from SEK 1 per trade) and does not rely on PFOF.
  • The ban actually strengthens Avanza's competitive position versus PFOF-dependent European neobrokers.

Swedish pension reform:

  • Target retirement age (riktalder) introduced at 67 from 2026, with increases forecast for later cohorts (68 for those born 1967-1981).
  • AP fund consolidation: five buffer funds merging to three. AP1 assets split between AP3 and AP4; AP2 and AP6 merging.
  • Premium pension system unchanged structurally, but longer working lives = more years of contributions = larger pension pots.
  • Occupational pension market remains large and growing — positive for Avanza Pension.

Employer contribution changes:

  • From 1 January 2026, employers pay only old-age pension contribution (10.21%) on compensation to workers 67+ at start of year.
  • Encourages longer working lives, incrementally positive for pension savings volumes.

MiFID II / III:

  • Ongoing European regulatory evolution. Key impact areas: transparency requirements, best execution rules, suitability assessments.
  • Compliance costs absorbed more easily by scale players like Avanza than by smaller competitors.

8. Platform Stickiness & Ecosystem

Exceptional customer satisfaction. Ecosystem depth creates high switching costs.

Customer satisfaction metrics:

  • SKI (Swedish Quality Index) award for most satisfied savings customers: 16 consecutive years (through 2025). No other company has ever won it.
  • Customer satisfaction score: 76.3 (2024 data).
  • NPS: 49 (strong for financial services, where NPS typically ranges 20-40).
  • Avanza ranks #1 in image, value for money, and service quality within the category.

Ecosystem breadth:

Avanza's platform now encompasses:

  • Stock and ETF trading (Swedish, Nordic, US, European markets)
  • Fund marketplace (proprietary and third-party)
  • ISK accounts (tax-advantaged wrapper)
  • KF (kapitalforsakring / endowment insurance)
  • Occupational pensions
  • Private pensions
  • Savings accounts (instant-access and fixed-term)
  • Mortgages
  • Margin lending
  • Currency conversion for foreign trading
  • Private Banking (35,000+ clients)
  • Placera (financial media/content platform)
  • Borsveckan (stock magazine, acquired 2006)
  • Digital discretionary portfolio management (Sigmastocks acquisition, launching 2025-26)
  • Corporate occupational pension administration
  • Independent pension advisory (via Lifeplan partnership)

Switching cost analysis:

  • Moving an ISK account to a competitor requires transferring all holdings — operationally friction-heavy.
  • Pension accounts (occupational and private) are particularly sticky. Transferring pension capital involves long processing times and regulatory paperwork.
  • Mortgage customers face standard Swedish refinancing costs.
  • The breadth of services means a customer with trading, ISK, pension, and mortgage at Avanza faces meaningful coordination costs to replicate this elsewhere.
  • Habit/familiarity: daily users of the Avanza app develop muscle memory and workflow preferences.

Retention inference: Avanza does not publicly disclose a retention rate, but the combination of 16-year satisfaction leadership, NPS of 49, and ecosystem breadth implies retention well above the financial services benchmark of 75-85%. Customer base growth is net of churn and consistently positive.

9. Technology & Product Innovation

Investing in growth. Key launches in DPM, Private Banking, and pensions.

Sigmastocks acquisition and DPM launch:

  • Acquired Sigmastocks entirely for SEK 21.1m (closed May 2025).
  • Sigmastocks offers digital discretionary portfolio management based on quantitative factor investing.
  • Target portfolio: 40 stocks across Sweden, Europe, and US.
  • Clients choose risk level and can exclude specific sectors.
  • Launch to Avanza's 35,000+ Private Banking clients in 2025-26.
  • Strategic target: challenge the SEK 1,000bn traditional DPM market in Sweden.

Private Banking expansion:

  • New online service launched specifically for Private Banking clients.
  • Ambition to become market-leading in Private Banking.
  • Mortgage volumes growing, particularly from Private Banking segment (SEK 1bn+ added in Q4 2025).
  • Private Banking and pro customers now represent 26% of brokerage (up from 24%).

Platform and technology:

  • Banking license obtained 2005; full regulatory infrastructure in place.
  • First in Sweden to offer real-time deposits from other banks (2016).
  • Mobile-first platform design — critical given Swedish smartphone penetration rates.
  • Ongoing investment in API infrastructure, though specific open banking initiatives not prominently disclosed.

2026 investment focus:

  • 9% cost increase budgeted for 2026.
  • Priorities: Private Banking development, DPM product launch, occupational pension business investment, platform scalability improvements.
  • Cost increases front-loaded, trending toward 5% annual growth by 2030 as scalability improves.

Assessment: Avanza is making targeted, capital-light acquisitions (Sigmastocks at SEK 21m is trivial relative to SEK 2.6bn net profit) and investing organically in higher-value segments. The DPM launch opens an entirely new TAM. The risk is execution — digital DPM at scale with good client outcomes is not easy. But the optionality is real.

10. Structural & Macro Tailwinds

Sweden is one of the world's best structural markets for a retail savings platform.

Savings culture:

  • Swedish households' financial assets = 329% of GDP, second-highest in the EU (after Denmark).
  • 73% of Swedes have private fund savings. Including occupational pensions: 80%. Including premium pensions: 100%.
  • One in four Swedes holds directly-quoted shares (average portfolio ~EUR 38,000, median ~EUR 8,000).
  • Household savings rate normalised post-pandemic but remains structurally healthy.
  • Q2 2025: household liquid savings of SEK 157bn, driven by bank deposits and fund purchases.

ISK account proliferation:

  • Nearly 4 million ISK accounts (~40% of population).
  • Tax-free threshold doubling to SEK 300,000 in 2026 — directly incentivises more savings into ISK.
  • ISK accounts are overwhelmingly held at Avanza and Nordnet, not traditional banks.

Demographic tailwinds:

  • Sweden's population ~10.5m, median age ~41. Working-age population stable.
  • Millennials and Gen Z are digital-native investors — Avanza's core demographic.
  • Rising pension target age (67 in 2026, 68 for those born 1967+) means longer accumulation periods.
  • Shift from defined benefit to defined contribution in occupational pensions increases individual savings responsibility.

Wealth inequality dynamic:

  • 5% of Swedes hold ~50% of financial assets. Most Swedes hold modest amounts.
  • This means the addressable market for Avanza extends well into the mass-market segment — most Swedes are still under-saved.
  • Private Banking serves the high end; the platform model scales across the full wealth spectrum.

Macro context (2026):

  • Avanza management expects improved momentum from: lower rates (vs 2023-24 peak), expansionary fiscal policy, and real wage growth in Sweden.
  • Debt-to-income ratios have declined from 177% (2021 peak) to 154% (mid-2025), improving household balance sheet health.
  • Household savings shifting from bank deposits toward risk assets (funds, equities) as rates declined — positive for Avanza's fee-generating AUM.

Net change forces assessment: Overwhelmingly positive. Eight of ten forces are positive, two are neutral-to-positive. No single force is a clear negative at this stage. The primary risk factors are: (1) a severe equity market drawdown reducing activity and savings capital, (2) a recession forcing rates below 1.75%, and (3) execution risk on new product launches (DPM, occupational pensions). These are cyclical risks against a structural growth story.
