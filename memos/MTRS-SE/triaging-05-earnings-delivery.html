[W] MUNTERS GROUP AB (MTRS-SE) — QUERY #5: EARNINGS DELIVERY

[C] Native Research | 28-Apr-26

1. KEY FINDINGS

Overall Rating: YELLOW — Strong top-line delivery and order momentum obscure a structural margin disappointment in 2025. The investment case remains intact but near-term earnings quality is compromised.

  • The core tension is real and unresolved. Munters delivered record FY2024 margins (15.1% adj. EBITA) and then gave most of that back in 2025 (~12.7%), finishing below the CMD target range of >14%. This is not a minor variance — it is a 240bps year-on-year margin decline driven by three simultaneous headwinds (AirTech battery, Geoclima tariffs, DCT dual-site transition). The question is whether these are truly temporary or whether 2025 revealed structural fragility in the margin model.
  • Order delivery is exceptional; earnings delivery is not. DCT order intake +85% full-year 2025, book-to-bill 1.6x, single-quarter record of BSEK 2.1 in Q4. The backlog is building correctly. But converting that backlog into margin at speed has proven harder than management implied. The Q4 2025 adj. EBITA margin of ~10% was a significant miss versus consensus expectations and prompted a 10% share price drop on results day.
  • Q1 2025 and Q4 2025 were both consensus misses; Q2 was a revenue miss but EBITA beat; Q3 beat strongly. The beat/miss pattern is erratic — not the profile of a management team with tight near-term visibility.
  • Management's forward guidance language has become more hedged and backward-looking. The Q4 2025 title — "Exceptional demand, while earnings weakened" — is unusually candid. This is not sandbagging; it is a management team managing expectations downward after previously anchoring the market at record FY2024 levels.
  • Cash flow quality deteriorated in 2025. Operating cash flow fell year-on-year despite record order intake. Working capital consumed cash as DCT customer advances were deployed. Net debt/EBITDA rose to approximately 2.9x at year-end 2025 — above the stated target range.
  • The recovery narrative for 2026 is plausible but front-loaded with risk. Management's articulated path is: Q1 2026 still impacted by dual-site costs and tariffs, then H2 substantially better as Virginia ramps and AirTech restructuring savings (MSEK 250-300) phase in. This is a hockey-stick profile. If Virginia ramp or AirTech recovery slips, consensus EPS upgrades (+20% over 6M) unwind rapidly.
  • CEO succession adds an additional layer of near-term uncertainty. Klas Forsström stepping down in Q3 2026 with Stefan Aspman (DCT head) named successor is strategically coherent but creates execution risk during a critical operational transition year.
  • Sceptical read: The EPS revision cycle (+20.1%/6M) reflects DCT re-rating on order momentum, not proof of earnings delivery. The market is pricing a significant 2026 recovery that has not yet been demonstrated.

2. LTM EARNINGS DELIVERY OVERVIEW

Section Rating: YELLOW

  • LTM earnings quality is mixed. The last twelve months (Q1 2025 through Q4 2025) tell a story of record demand meeting impaired operational gearing. Group adj. EBITA margin for the LTM period is approximately 12.7% — 240bps below FY2024 and outside the CMD target of >14%.
  • The revenue/margin divergence is the defining LTM characteristic. Munters is growing its top line — the problem is that three margin drags landed simultaneously in 2025, creating a year where operational leverage failed to manifest despite double-digit revenue growth.
  • DCT: tariff headwind of approximately -4 percentage points on divisional EBITA margin in Q4 (Geoclima production in Italy into US customers).
  • AirTech: battery sub-segment collapse from ~17% EBITA margin to ~7% in H1. Structural demand destruction from European battery gigafactory slowdown. AirTech battery revenue declines dragged group margin by an estimated -2 percentage points in Q4.
  • Dual-site transition costs: Amesbury factory relocation to Virginia created underutilisation drag throughout 2025. These are genuine one-offs but ran longer than initially indicated.
  • DCT divisional earnings were positive but fell short of margin potential. DCT's order intake trajectory is extraordinary — the division should be a high-teens EBITA margin business at scale. The Virginia ramp and tariff drag have deferred the margin realisation. DCT is not an earnings quality concern at a structural level; the timing mismatch between order intake and margin delivery is real and material.
  • FoodTech SaaS retained segment is small but high-quality. Post-Equipment divestiture, FoodTech contributes pure SaaS/digital revenue (ARR growth 46%+ in Q4 2024). This is immaterial to group margin in the near term but directionally correct.
  • LTM earnings adjustments remain consistent. Munters reports both reported and adjusted EBITA. Adjustments are principally transaction costs (Geoclima), restructuring charges (AirTech battery rightsizing), and dual-site transition costs. These are not egregious. The adjustments policy is transparent.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The FY2025 margin of 12.7% versus the CMD target of >14% represents a 130bps minimum miss against published targets. If management's H2 2026 recovery narrative requires flawless execution across three simultaneous recovery vectors, the probability of all three landing on schedule simultaneously is meaningfully less than 100%.

3. QUARTERLY BEAT/MISS HISTORY

Section Rating: YELLOW

  • The 2025 quarterly delivery pattern is erratic. Two clear misses (Q1 2025, Q4 2025), one mixed (Q2 2025 — revenue miss, EBITA beat), and one strong beat (Q3 2025). This is not the profile of a management team with clear near-term earnings visibility.
  • Q4 2024 (FY2024 results, reported January 2025): BEAT. Record full-year — adj. EBITA margin 15.1% vs. prior year 13.2%. Revenue SEK 15.45bn (+11%). Management described as "overall record performance achieved for the year while investing for the future." Stock reaction: positive. Consensus entered 2025 expecting continued margin expansion.
  • Q1 2025 (reported April/May 2025): MISS. Revenue SEK 3.56bn vs. consensus approximately SEK 3.81bn — a ~7% top-line miss. EPS approximately SEK 1.05 versus consensus SEK 1.30. Adj. EBITA margin declined to approximately 13.5%. Despite the miss, the stock rallied ~11.5% — reflecting that DCT order intake remained strong and the miss was attributed to known transitional factors.
  • Q2 2025 (reported July 2025): MIXED — Revenue miss, EBITA beat. Net sales approximately 3% below consensus. Adjusted EBITA beat by approximately 2%. Order intake +22% YoY, 5% above consensus estimates, DCT-driven. CEO Forsström stated "closer to $10bn than $5bn" on DCT opportunity size. Stock fell ~2% post-results.
  • Q3 2025 (reported October 2025): BEAT. Strong performance — net sales +15% YoY. Adjusted EBITA margin recovered to approximately 13.5%. Order intake +57% YoY (70% in comparable currency), DCT-driven. Book-to-bill above 1 in DCT confirmed. Stock surged 19.26% on results day — the most significant single-day reaction of the year.
  • Q4 2025 (reported January 29, 2026): MISS. Adj. EBITA margin fell to approximately 10% — a sharp sequential decline from Q3's 13.5%. Revenue declined approximately 8% sequentially (flat on a currency-adjusted basis). Stock dropped 10% on results day. Three concurrent margin hits: Geoclima tariff impact (-4pp on DCT divisional margin), AirTech battery underutilisation (-2pp on group), dual-site transition costs. Order intake +191% organically in the quarter — a record — which limited the stock decline. Report titled "Exceptional demand, while earnings weakened."
  • Pattern summary: Beat in Q4 2024 → Miss Q1 2025 → Mixed Q2 2025 → Strong beat Q3 2025 → Miss Q4 2025. Two misses and a mixed result in four 2025 quarters. This is a below-average beat rate for a company trading at an AI-infrastructure growth premium.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The Q3 2025 beat occurred partly because expectations had been revised sharply lower following Q1 and Q2 disappointments. The 19% stock reaction to Q3 is a measure of how far consensus had capitulated, not proof of genuine earnings quality.

4. REVENUE DELIVERY ANALYSIS

Section Rating: YELLOW-GREEN

  • Top-line delivery has been solid but not exceptional. Full-year 2025 revenue reached approximately SEK 16.3bn, a new group record. The underlying growth engine — DCT — is performing exactly as the investment thesis requires.
  • DCT is the dominant positive volume driver. DCT order intake +85% in full-year 2025, reaching a record BSEK 2.1 in Q4 alone. Book-to-bill of 1.6x means DCT revenue recognition will accelerate through 2026 and 2027 as capacity comes online.
  • Volume vs. price decomposition: DCT growth is primarily volume-driven — more hyperscaler projects, larger project scopes. Pricing has been stable to slightly positive, reflecting Munters' position as a specification-driven supplier. AirTech volume has declined in battery and been approximately flat outside battery.
  • FX has been a modest headwind. The SEK strengthened against USD in parts of 2025. Q4 2025 showed flat revenue on a currency-adjusted basis versus reported -8% sequentially — the FX impact was meaningful.
  • Mix shift is structurally positive. The portfolio is migrating toward higher-value products: DCT (complex, engineered, margin-accretive), FoodTech SaaS (high-margin, recurring), away from AirTech battery (commoditised, margin-compressed).
  • The Geoclima acquisition (Oct 2024, MEUR ~40) added chiller capability and European manufacturing. It contributed to revenue in 2025 but also created the Italy-to-US tariff exposure that hit Q4 2025 margins.
  • AirTech battery revenue has been a structural drag. The margin collapse from ~17% to ~7% in H1 2025 was accompanied by volume decline. Battery gigafactory construction in Europe slowed materially — this is an exogenous demand destruction event, not market share loss.
  • Management guided "at least 30%, potentially up to 40%" DCT revenue uplift from backlog in 2026 invoicing. That is extraordinary guidance if delivered.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The LTM revenue record is partly an artefact of Geoclima consolidation and DCT mix. Organic revenue growth ex-acquisitions and ex-battery recovery would show a less impressive picture.

5. MARGIN DELIVERY ANALYSIS

Section Rating: ORANGE

  • The 2025 margin story is the weakest element of Munters' recent earnings delivery. FY2025 adj. EBITA margin of ~12.7% represents a 240bps decline from FY2024's record 15.1% and a clear miss versus the CMD target of >14%. This is the most material single-year margin miss in Munters' post-IPO history (2017).
  • Three concurrent margin headwinds is an unusual configuration. The simultaneous arrival of AirTech battery collapse, Geoclima tariff exposure, and dual-site transition costs created a margin convergence that management did not adequately guide investors toward in H1 2025.
  • Q4 2025 was the low watermark. Adj. EBITA margin of approximately 10% in Q4 compares with the prior-year Q4 (FY2024) exit margin of approximately 15%+ — a 500bps sequential and year-on-year deterioration.
  • Geoclima tariffs: approximately -4pp on DCT divisional margin in Q4. Virginia production ramp eliminates most of this after Q1 2026.
  • AirTech battery: approximately -2pp on group margin in Q4. Recovery trajectory into 2026 is unclear but management guided subdued through the year.
  • Dual-site costs and underutilisation: quantified in restructuring charges. Virginia transition mostly resolved by Q2 2026 per management.
  • DCT margins were positive but below potential. DCT should be a high-teens EBITA margin business at scale. The underlying economics remain intact and Virginia resolution should restore DCT margins to normal levels in 2026.
  • AirTech ex-battery is structurally healthy. The battery sub-segment is a ring-fenced issue. AirTech outside battery — semiconductor, pharmaceutical, general industrial — appears to have maintained mid-teens EBITA margins.
  • Q3 2025 recovery to 13.5% matters. It demonstrates that when transition costs normalise and DCT tariff drag eases, margins can recover quickly. Q3 showed that the underlying business can deliver in the low-to-mid teens — confirming the current weakness is timing-related, not structural.
  • Sceptical interpretation: Management's framing of all three headwinds as "temporary" is too convenient. AirTech battery demand recovery into 2026 remains uncertain. Geoclima tariffs may persist in reduced form even after Virginia ramps. Dual-site costs were supposed to be largely resolved in Q3 2025 but still impacted Q4. The pattern of each temporary headwind lasting one quarter longer than indicated creates a credibility issue.

6. EPS DELIVERY AND ADJUSTMENTS

Section Rating: YELLOW

  • Reported EPS has been volatile; adjusted EPS has been more stable but still disappointing versus 2024 levels. The beat/miss pattern on EPS mirrors the margin delivery — Q1 miss, Q2 mixed, Q3 beat, Q4 miss.
  • The adjustment policy is consistent and not aggressive. Munters' adjustments encompass: M&A transaction costs (Geoclima), restructuring charges (AirTech battery rightsizing), and dual-site transition costs. These are legitimate one-time charges. The company has not introduced new adjustment categories mid-cycle. This is a positive earnings quality signal.
  • EPS revisions have been strongly positive on a 6M basis (+20.1%). This is DCT-driven — the market re-rated the DCT earnings power following the order intake surge. It is important to distinguish: EPS revisions reflect upward revision to 2026 and 2027 estimates (based on DCT backlog conversion) rather than upward revision to 2025 actual delivery.
  • The 3M EPS revision of +3.3% is more modest. This reflects the Q4 2025 miss, which prompted some 2025 and early-2026 estimate reductions, partially offset by DCT momentum upgrades for 2026/2027.
  • Q1 2025 EPS miss was significant: reported ~SEK 1.05 vs. consensus ~SEK 1.30. This is a ~19% shortfall on the headline EPS line. The stock's +11.5% reaction on that day illustrates the AI infrastructure re-rating effect — fundamentals were irrelevant in the face of DCT order flow.
  • Cash EPS quality: Working capital consumed cash in 2025 as DCT customer advance payments were received and deployed. Operating cash flow fell year-on-year. The gap between reported adj. EBITA and actual cash generation widened. True cash EPS quality was lower than the adjusted EPS figures imply.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The 80% buy rating and +20% EPS revision over 6M are based almost entirely on DCT backlog extrapolation, not demonstrated earnings delivery. If DCT margin recovery from Virginia is slower than assumed, or if hyperscaler capex decelerates even modestly, the consensus EPS curve for 2026/2027 will need significant downward revision.

7. GUIDANCE EVOLUTION

Section Rating: YELLOW

  • Guidance has consistently slipped in precision through 2025. The progression from "record year, investing for the future" (FY2024) to "exceptional demand, while earnings weakened" (FY2025) reflects a management team that has moved from confident forward projection to damage limitation commentary.
  • CMD May 2024 targets: adjusted EBITA margin >14%, net sales growth >14% CAGR. The EBITA margin target was not met in 2025 (12.7% actual vs. >14% target). Net sales growth was met (approximately 6-7% on a continuing operations basis, with FoodTech Equipment divestiture complicating the comparison).
  • Q1 2025 reporting cycle: guidance was cautiously optimistic. Management flagged AirTech battery weakness as a known headwind but characterised it as manageable. No specific margin guidance for 2025 was provided.
  • Q2 2025 reporting cycle: tone was bullish on DCT, cautious on near-term. The CEO's "closer to $10bn than $5bn" comment on DCT opportunity size was the headline message. Near-term margin pressure was acknowledged but framed as transitory.
  • Q3 2025 reporting cycle: positive surprise, guidance for recovery becoming more specific. The Q3 beat allowed management to articulate a clearer recovery path: Virginia completion Q2 2026, tariff drag diminishing after Q1 2026, AirTech restructuring savings phasing in through 2026. This was the clearest guidance communication of the year.
  • Q4 2025 / FY2025 reporting cycle (January 2026): recovery narrative reconfirmed with more hedging. Management stated: "After Q1, when short-term holdbacks will diminish, Munters is set to deliver in 2026 with historical high turnover and strong margins in H2." This is directional but not quantified.
  • The guidance pattern shows a management team that is better at signalling the direction of change than the magnitude. They correctly identified DCT as the growth engine and AirTech battery as structurally challenged. They were slow to quantify the combined margin impact of tariffs plus battery plus transition costs.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The shift to H2-weighted 2026 guidance creates a structural problem: any H1 underperformance can be dismissed as "we said H2 would be the recovery." Investors should demand more specific margin trajectory guidance at the Q1 2026 results — specifically: DCT margin normalised level post-Virginia, AirTech ex-battery run-rate margin, and quantum of restructuring savings already booked.

8. CASH FLOW AND BALANCE SHEET QUALITY

Section Rating: ORANGE

  • Cash flow quality deteriorated in 2025. Operating cash flow fell year-on-year despite record order intake and record revenue. The primary drivers: lower operating earnings (margin compression), less favourable working capital development, and increased capital expenditure (Virginia facility construction).
  • Net debt/EBITDA rose to approximately 2.9x at end-2025. This compares with approximately 2.3x at end-2024. The increase reflects: Geoclima acquisition payments, Virginia capex, and lower EBITDA generation. At 2.9x, Munters is above its stated target leverage range.
  • FoodTech Equipment divestiture (MEUR 97.5, May 2025) was a material cash inflow. This reduced gross debt by a meaningful amount and improved the leverage trajectory.
  • Working capital discipline has been historically strong. The CMD target for OWC/net sales is 10-13% — the company achieved 10.2% at FY2024. In 2025, the DCT backlog buildup and Virginia construction created temporary working capital pressure.
  • Capex intensity increased in 2025. Virginia construction represents a step-change in capex. Post-completion (Q2 2026), maintenance capex should revert to normalised levels.
  • Sceptical interpretation: A company with 2.9x net debt/EBITDA, declining operating cash flow, and a management transition faces balance sheet risk if the H2 2026 recovery narrative disappoints. The leverage ratio leaves limited cushion for further operational disappointment.

9. MANAGEMENT COMMENTARY TRACK RECORD

Section Rating: YELLOW

  • Management's directional signalling has been good; magnitude signalling has been poor. Forsström correctly identified DCT as the transformational growth vector. Where he has fallen short is in communicating how severely these headwinds would compound in H2 2025.
  • The "closer to $10bn than $5bn" comment (Q2 2025) is the clearest example of guidance optimism bias. This forward-looking market size claim for DCT is plausible over a multi-year horizon, but making this statement in the context of a quarter where revenue missed and margins were under pressure prioritised narrative management over financial transparency.
  • The "temporary" characterisation of headwinds has been repeatedly tested. AirTech battery weakness was flagged as temporary in Q1 2025. By Q4 2025, it was still a material drag and management guided subdued through 2026. Geoclima tariffs were flagged as manageable in Q2 2025. By Q4 2025, they were quantified at -4pp DCT divisional margin. Each individual characterisation of "temporary" has turned out to be too optimistic on timing.
  • Forsström's track record overall is positive. He executed the FoodTech Equipment divestiture cleanly. He positioned Munters correctly in the AI datacentre thematic early. The 2025 execution disappointment is more about the macro environment (tariffs, EV slowdown) than a fundamental strategic misjudgement.
  • The CEO succession announcement creates ambiguity on the forward guidance owner. Aspman (DCT head, named successor) has deep domain expertise in the division driving the growth story. He has not publicly guided on AirTech margins or group-level targets. There will be a period of guidance uncertainty as Aspman establishes his communication cadence.
  • No evidence of systematic sandbagging. Munters does not appear to set low targets to guarantee beats. The CMD target of >14% margin was genuine ambition and was missed. Management is better characterised as slightly overconfident on transition timing than as sandbagging.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The pattern of overly optimistic transition timing is itself a systematic bias. If Munters management consistently underestimates how long "temporary" headwinds persist, the H2 2026 recovery framing deserves a significant credibility discount.

10. RISKS TO FORWARD DELIVERY

Section Rating: ORANGE

  • The 2026 recovery thesis rests on three simultaneous improvements — each individually uncertain, together compounded. Virginia ramp-up completing and eliminating tariff drag; AirTech battery stabilisation and restructuring savings materialising; DCT backlog converting to revenue at assumed margin levels.
  • Risk 1: Virginia production ramp slower than planned. The 200,000 sq ft facility in Virginia is scheduled for completion in Q2 2026. Construction/commissioning risk, labour availability, and qualification processes for new DCT production lines could delay the ramp. A 1-2 quarter delay in Virginia commissioning directly extends the Geoclima tariff drag.
  • Risk 2: AirTech battery demand remains depressed through 2026. The restructuring programme (MSEK 250-300 run-rate savings) is in motion, but savings are phasing in gradually. If AirTech battery volumes remain flat or decline further, the savings may not offset the volume deleverage.
  • Risk 3: DCT order-to-revenue conversion slower than consensus models. DCT book-to-bill of 1.6x is exceptional. But the backlog is not yet revenue — projects are complex, long lead-time, customer-specific. If hyperscaler customers reschedule or reduce scopes, Munters' revenue recognition schedule shifts.
  • Risk 4: Tariff escalation beyond Geoclima. The current tariff exposure is primarily Geoclima (Italy to US). The broader US tariff environment could expand to cover additional Munters product categories. Chinese-manufactured components entering the US market could face escalating duties.
  • Risk 5: CEO succession disruption. The transition period creates risk in H2 2026 — precisely when the recovery narrative requires flawless delivery. Aspman's strength is DCT; AirTech recovery leadership may fall between the cracks during the transition.
  • Risk 6: Hyperscaler capex deceleration. Munters has a 1.6x book-to-bill and substantial backlog providing partial insulation, but new order growth would slow and market consensus assumes continued strong intake.
  • Risk 7: Balance sheet headroom. At 2.9x net debt/EBITDA, Munters has limited capacity for further operational disappointment or incremental investment.
  • Risk 8: FX. The DCT business is predominantly USD-denominated. A significant SEK appreciation versus USD would compress reported revenues and margins.
  • Sceptical interpretation: The combination of Risks 1-3 interacting simultaneously is the scenario the market is not pricing. The Q4 2025 experience — where three headwinds arrived simultaneously — should inform investors' prior for multi-vector recovery scenarios.

Overall Earnings Delivery Rating: YELLOW

The DCT story is structurally intact — order momentum, backlog quality, and end-market demand are genuine. The AirTech battery drag and Geoclima tariff exposure are real but their character is more transitional than structural. The balance sheet is elevated but manageable. The risk is concentrated in the H2 2026 recovery narrative — if that slips, the EPS revision cycle reverses sharply.

Three specific monitoring points:

1. Q1 2026 results (published 28-Apr-26): Does management demonstrate that Q4 2025 was genuinely the margin low? DCT margin improvement post-Virginia ramp-up is the single most important datapoint.

2. Virginia facility completion (Q2 2026): Physical operational confirmation of the Amesbury transition ending and US production starting.

3. AirTech battery Q2 2026 order intake: Any uptick in battery would confirm the restructuring programme is not fighting a permanent volume hole.

Sceptic's one-line summary: Munters has exceptional order delivery and poor recent earnings delivery — and the market is paying for the orders while discounting the pattern of margin disappointment. At some point, the market requires earnings proof. Q1 2026 is the first real test.
