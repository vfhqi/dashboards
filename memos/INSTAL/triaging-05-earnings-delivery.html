Overall Summary

Instalco AB is a leading decentralized Nordic installation group comprising over 150 subsidiaries across Sweden, Norway, and Finland, with operations focused on mid-sized electrical, heating and plumbing, ventilation, and industrial projects. Over the past three years, the company transitioned from rapid acquisition-driven growth to a phase of significant operational and market challenges, culminating in a 6.5 percent organic decline for the 2024 financial year and severe margin compression well below the company's historical 8 percent EBITA target. The broader Nordic construction market suffered from rising interest rates, inflation, and intense price pressure, forcing the company to undertake a fundamental strategic reset under new leadership.

Following the return of founder Per Sjöstrand as CEO in mid-2025, Instalco reorganized into a country-based structure with newly appointed Chief Operating Officers to enforce stricter accountability and operational discipline. This abrupt leadership change successfully refocused the market's attention onto internal efficiency improvements and a clear roadmap back to historical profitability. The strategic priority has decisively shifted from aggressive acquisition volume toward margin restoration, deleveraging, and operational excellence under the comprehensive "Instalco 2.0" framework.

Management framed the fourth quarter of 2025 as a critical inflection point, highlighting that adjusted EBITA grew year-over-year for the first time in seven consecutive quarters, breaking a prolonged pattern of margin degradation. The quarter delivered a significant beat across the board, with revenue arriving 7 percent ahead of consensus expectations and the adjusted EBITA margin of 7.1 percent representing a 30 basis point year-over-year expansion, positioning absolute adjusted EBITA a full 15 percent above market forecasts. However, leadership maintained a distinctly cautious tone, emphasizing that while proud of the progress, they remain entirely unsatisfied with the absolute level of profitability.

A notable operational bright spot was the company's exceptional cash conversion rate of 108 percent for the full year, comfortably exceeding internal targets and driven by concentrated efforts to optimize the percentage-of-completion ratio and enforce stricter payment terms. Despite this success, financial leverage remains a vulnerability, with net debt to EBITDA exiting the year at 2.8x, which exceeds the stated long-term ceiling of 2.5x, although management emphasized the positive trajectory given that leverage had peaked at a concerning 3.3x at the end of the third quarter.

To accelerate the deleveraging process and fortify the balance sheet against ongoing macroeconomic uncertainty, the Board of Directors proposed a reduced dividend of SEK 0.50 per share, signaling that capital deployment will remain highly selective in the near term. In a significant strategic pivot, management confirmed that the company's historically aggressive acquisition engine remains temporarily paused as they prioritize organic consolidation, stressing that lower indebtedness and improved working capital must be achieved before eventually resuming a value-creating acquisition agenda.

Over the past twelve months, sell-side financial forecasts for Instalco have experienced a volatile trajectory, characterized by a series of conservative downgrades throughout late 2025 as the company struggled with under-absorption of resources and delayed profitability in key growth initiatives. The most severe negative forecast revisions occurred in late October 2025 following a highly disappointing third-quarter earnings release, when analysts aggressively slashed adjusted EPS for 2025e by 11 percent and cut 2026e and 2027e adjusted EPS by 7 percent and 5 percent respectively, reflecting higher-than-anticipated net financial costs and operational shortfalls.

The persistent trend of negative estimate revisions was decisively broken in February 2026 following the fourth-quarter earnings report, when analysts upgraded adjusted EPS expectations for both the 2026 and 2027 financial years by 3 percent, catalyzed by an adjusted EBITA delivery that arrived a staggering 15 percent above market consensus, which analysts interpreted as definitive proof that the underlying margin recovery and operational turnaround were finally gaining sustainable traction. Despite this positive inflection, consensus models remain conservative, assuming margins meaningfully below the company's stated 8.0 percent long-term target.

Instalco's share price has experienced significant volatility and broad underperformance relative to both the wider market and its sector peers over the last 12 months, with the stock falling by approximately 20 percent from January 2025 through early 2026, primarily driven by investor concerns surrounding persistent margin degradation, potential project writedowns, and elevated leverage levels. While the share price witnessed a brief 20 percent rebound in the two months leading up to January 2026, it remains substantially de-rated over the trailing 6- and 12-month periods, contrasting sharply with the broader European installation sector where many competitors enjoyed substantial positive returns during the same timeframe.

Sell-side price targets for Instalco have fluctuated in tandem with the company's turbulent operational performance, with one prominent broker adjusting its target price from SEK 50.00 in October 2024 down to SEK 40.00 by January 2026, before raising it to SEK 42.50 following the strong fourth-quarter results. Despite the reduced price targets, analysts largely maintain a "Buy" rating on the stock, citing the highly depressed valuation levels as an attractive entry point and arguing that if management successfully executes its margin recovery plan, the stock possesses nearly 100 percent upside potential as valuation multiples normalize toward historical averages.

When benchmarked against European installation peers, Instalco is currently trading at a severe and historically anomalous discount, with its valuation compressed to approximately 10x estimated 2026 EBITA, representing a 30 to 35 percent discount compared to rivals such as SPIE, Bilfinger, and Burkhalter. Historically, Instalco and its Nordic peer Bravida traded at an average premium of 20 percent over these continental competitors due to superior regional growth dynamics, and analysts attribute this current valuation gap directly to the market's severe penalization of Instalco's high financial leverage and recent inability to defend its operating margins during the construction downturn.

Bravida serves as Instalco's closest publicly listed Nordic competitor and shares many of the same end-market challenges, having also suffered from the weak residential and commercial construction market leading to industry-wide price pressure and depressed margins. However, Bravida boasts a higher proportion of recurring service revenue at roughly 48 percent of its sales in 2024 compared to Instalco's 37 percent, providing it with slightly better earnings resilience, and analysts note that Bravida has managed to slowly increase its revenue per full-time employee over recent years, whereas Instalco's productivity metrics have stagnated.

The sell-side narrative surrounding Instalco has fundamentally shifted over the last six months from a growth-focused story to a complex turnaround and self-help case, with earlier concerns in 2024 primarily focused on rising debt levels and dilutive new greenfield investments, but the leadership change successfully refocused attention onto specific operational KPIs such as revenue generated per full-time employee and the normalization of bad debt provisions as the primary catalysts for future earnings upgrades.

Among sell-side analysts, those holding "Hold" or neutral ratings have maintained highly skeptical narratives regarding Instalco's valuation premium and structural leverage, emphasizing the negative strategic implications of current financial constraints that create severe uncertainty around the company's ability to resume its active acquisition agenda. Following the CEO transition, the narrative explicitly pivoted entirely from acquisition-driven growth to margin restoration and balance sheet deleveraging, with concerns over financial leverage dominating analyst reports throughout 2025 as they closely tracked the net debt to EBITDA ratio against banking covenants.

Against the company's established long-term targets, full-year 2025 delivery fell significantly short across multiple dimensions: net sales decreased by 0.7 percent versus a 10 percent annual growth target; the adjusted EBITA margin reached only 6.4 percent, remaining well below the 8 percent ambition; and year-end net debt to EBITDA ratio of 2.8x exceeded the strict ceiling of 2.5x. The notable operational bright spot relative to internal targets was cash generation, which aggressively outperformed the 100 percent goal to deliver a 108 percent cash conversion rate for 2025.

The absolute highest strategic priority under the new leadership is strict margin recovery, with management explicitly pivoting away from historically aggressive, acquisition-driven volume growth to prioritize operational excellence and highly disciplined project selection, with the overarching goal of returning to the historical adjusted EBITA margin target of at least 8 percent. To operationalize this strategy, the company launched "Instalco 2.0," a comprehensive framework designed to refine the decentralized business model with stricter central governance, which formally transitioned from conceptual planning into active daily execution, introducing clearer accountability, more consistent follow-up across the subsidiary network, and earlier identification of project deviations and risks.

In tandem with structural reorganization, management initiated aggressive cost-saving measures including the merger and definitive closure of eight distinctly underperforming subsidiaries that had been dragging down consolidated earnings, along with targeted layoffs of approximately 110 full-time employees and the termination of contracts for approximately 100 subcontractors to better align operating capacity with current market demand. Analysts estimate that the closure of these underperforming subsidiaries will mathematically lift the group's adjusted EBITA margin by approximately 35 basis points heading into the next financial year.

Despite the stabilization in fourth-quarter financial performance, management sought to temper market expectations by issuing highly cautious forward-looking commentary, reminding investors that the Nordic installation market remains late-cyclical and that they expect continued weakness and variation across different geographical regions. As a result, the company explicitly stated that it is not relying on a broader macroeconomic recovery, but rather depending entirely on self-help measures to drive earnings improvement.

The forward-looking analysis suggests that Instalco's recovery trajectory depends critically on successful execution of internal efficiency initiatives rather than macroeconomic tailwinds. The depressed valuation and conservative analyst forecasts indicate substantial upside potential if management delivers on margin restoration targets, but execution risks remain elevated given the company's historical decentralized model now requires significantly more central oversight and control. The temporary M&A pause, while prudent from a balance sheet perspective, removes a historically important driver of growth and earnings, placing even greater pressure on organic improvements to deliver shareholder returns. Investors should closely monitor quarterly progression toward the 8 percent margin target, continued working capital discipline, and the pace of deleveraging as key indicators of whether this turnaround story will ultimately succeed or require further strategic recalibration.

Business Overview: Instalco AB is a leading decentralized Nordic installation group comprising over 150 subsidiaries across Sweden, Norway, and Finland, with a recent strategic entry into the German market. The company focuses primarily on mid-sized projects ranging from SEK 1 million to SEK 75 million, offering end-to-end solutions in electrical, heating and plumbing, ventilation, and industrial disciplines. Instalco's business model is built on acquiring highly profitable local entrepreneurs while allowing them to retain their local brand, culture, and operational autonomy. The central organization remains deliberately small, focusing instead on facilitating cross-selling, joint purchasing, and sharing best practices among the independent subsidiaries.
