## 2. European Telecom Sector Framing

### 2.1 Structural Headwinds (what we expect to find on every stock)

**Revenue erosion from technology substitution.** Fixed-voice revenues are structurally declining across Europe — OTT voice/messaging (WhatsApp, Teams) has largely killed PSTN voice ARPU. This is now complete for most incumbents and is background noise, not a change driver.

**Cable and fixed-wireless competition.** In most Western European markets (Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Sweden), cable operators (Liberty Global/Telenet, Magenta/T-Mobile) or fixed-wireless providers (Starlink, local ISPs) provide credible broadband alternatives. Competitive intensity varies sharply by market: Netherlands (moderate, KPN has structural fibre lead), Belgium (high, Orange Belgium faces VOO/Orange joint venture competition), Austria (moderate, TKA-AT has network quality advantage but pricing pressure), Sweden (moderate, consolidation ongoing).

**Capex treadmill.** Fibre rollout is mandatory across Europe (EU Gigabit Infrastructure Act targets, national broadband plans). Most incumbents must spend €300-600 per premise passed to upgrade from copper to fibre, with payback periods of 8-12 years. During the rollout phase, FCF is depressed and dividends are at risk unless balance sheets are robust. Key question for each stock: where are they in the rollout cycle, and is the capex inflection already in the rearview mirror?

**OTT content competition.** B2C content bundling (TV, streaming) as a retention lever is expensive and often dilutive. Incumbents that are spending heavily on content without clear ARPU uplift evidence are destroying capital. Flag any stock where content investment is a major P&L item.

### 2.2 Structural Tailwinds (what could be a genuine change driver)

**Fibre monetisation inflection.** Operators that have completed or largely completed fibre rollout (KPN is the leading example in Europe) are entering a phase where incremental subscriber migration to fibre drives ARPU uplift (fibre plans typically command €5-15/month premium over copper) with declining incremental capex. This is the most credible EPS growth lever in the sector. The key test: is ARPU per fibre subscriber actually rising, or is pricing pressure offsetting the mix benefit?

**B2B / enterprise / cloud infrastructure.** Larger operators with enterprise divisions are benefiting from cloud connectivity demand growth (SD-WAN, private 5G, IoT connectivity, managed services). This is a higher-margin, stickier revenue stream than consumer. Elisa's software/SaaS platform exports (Finland → international) are a specific version of this thesis.

**Converged bundle pricing power.** Fixed + mobile convergence (FMC) allows operators to bundle services at a modest discount vs standalone, improving churn and ARPU per household. Operators with strong FMC penetration (KPN, Swisscom) have empirically lower churn than single-service operators. The key test: is FMC penetration still growing, or has it plateaued?

**Market consolidation.** Several European telco markets are consolidating (Belgium: Orange-Proximus discussions historically; Austria: post-3/Hutchison M&A dynamics; Sweden: Tele2-Com Hem). Consolidation reduces competitive intensity and typically improves ARPU discipline across the surviving operators. Any consolidation signal is a material change force.

**Corporate restructuring / disposal.** Some incumbents are divesting non-core assets (towers, fibre infrastructure SPVs, IT subsidiaries) to unlock balance sheet value and refocus capital allocation. These are event-driven setups that can crystallise value independently of underlying trading performance.

### 2.3 The Goldilocks Test for European Telco

European telecoms rarely produce 20-30% EPS growth (Richard's Goldilocks zone). The realistic range for quality incumbents is 5-12% EPS CAGR over 3 years, driven by: (a) modest revenue growth from fibre ARPU uplift + B2B mix shift, (b) operating leverage from copper-to-fibre migration (lower field engineer cost per customer), (c) declining capex post-rollout completion releasing FCF.

At Triaging stage, the right question is not "will this stock grow earnings 20-30%?" but "is there a credible scenario where EPS grows 10-15% CAGR over 3 years AND the stock is pricing in less than that?" — i.e., a genuine re-rating opportunity rather than a yield story. Flag any name where the answer is plausibly yes.

## 3. Wisdom Library Model Summaries (Cohort Precommits)

The following models are precommitted across the cohort. Per-stock sub-agents run Rule #37 in DELTA-ONLY mode (load only WL models NOT in this list).

**`flywheel-vs-treadmill` (Gold) — PRIMARY TRIAGE LENS.** The central question for every telco in this cohort: is the business model a flywheel (network investment → better product → higher ARPU → more investment capacity → repeat) or a treadmill (run hard just to maintain market share, no compounding dynamic)? Most European telcos are treadmills. Look for evidence that breaks this pattern: demonstrated ARPU uplift from fibre, improving NPS → improving churn → improving revenue, or structural market consolidation removing the competitive treadmill.

**`incumbent-dilemma` (Silver) — COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS LENS.** European telco incumbents face a textbook incumbent dilemma: the technologies disrupting them (cable, FWA, OTT) require them to cannibalise their own legacy revenues to compete. Those that act decisively (KPN's early fibre commitment, Elisa's digital-first transformation) tend to outperform those that delay. Look for evidence of decisiveness vs delay.

**`red-queen-effect` (Silver) — TREADMILL DETECTION.** The Red Queen runs to stand still. In European telco, the treadmill version of this is: heavy capex on fibre, intense price competition, flat or declining ARPU — and the operator ends up with the same revenue but a worse balance sheet. Detection signal: multi-year revenue flat + capex elevated + no ARPU improvement = Red Queen. Exit criterion: market structure improving (competitor exits or capacity discipline) or capex cycle turning.

**`pricing-power` (Gold) — ARPU QUALITY TEST.** True pricing power in telco is rare and usually comes from: (a) network quality monopoly in a specific geography, (b) FMC bundle lock-in, (c) B2B mission-critical connectivity. Test every stock for evidence of actual ARPU per customer trending up or stable at a premium level. Declining ARPU with growing subscriber count is a Red Queen pattern, not pricing power.

**`economies-of-scale` (Gold) — OPERATING LEVERAGE TEST.** Post-fibre-rollout, incumbents should exhibit genuine operating leverage: fewer truck rolls, lower field engineer cost per customer, reduced legacy network maintenance. Test each stock for evidence of this inflection. If scale benefits are absent 2-3 years post-rollout completion, the thesis is weaker.

**`predictability-obsession` (Gold) — EPS FORECASTING TEST.** Can we build a credible 18M-3Y EPS forecast for this stock? For regulated utilities, this should be high. For competitive mobile markets, it is much lower. Explicitly rank the 7 stocks by EPS predictability at Triaging close. Highest predictability + most undervalued = best candidate for ESA.

**`right-to-left-thinking` (Gold) — FINANCIAL OUTPUT ANCHOR.** Start every per-stock analysis from the required financial output: what EPS trajectory justifies attention? Work backwards. Do not get lost in network architecture or regulatory detail without connecting to what this means for EPS in 18-36 months.

**`animal-ceo` (Gold) — OPERATOR QUALITY (Sub-B specific, but scan Sub-A too).** European telco CEOs range from aggressive operators (transformational, cost-focused, decisive) to utility managers (consensus-driven, slow to change, dividend-first). Flag any CEO that exhibits Slootman-archetype characteristics. This is rare in telco and a positive differentiator.

**`cockroaches-never-alone` (Gold) — DETERIORATION DETECTION.** Any single deterioration signal in a telco (unexpected churn spike, margin miss, capex overrun, CFO departure) is likely tip-of-iceberg. European telcos have long investment cycles and complex regulatory environments — problems compound. Treat early warning signs seriously at Triaging stage; do not wait for confirmation.

**`second-order-effects` (Gold) — CEE EXPOSURE (TKA-AT specific, but scan for any cross-border exposure).** Telkom Austria has significant CEE exposure. Second-order effects of geopolitical instability (Belarus: direct regulatory risk; Serbia/Bulgaria: macro sensitivity; Croatia: EU member but still emerging-market characteristics) matter. Do not analyse TKA-AT purely on Austrian domestic metrics — the CEE mix is load-bearing.

## 4. Cohort-Level Expected Priors

**What we expect to find on every stock:**
- Flat to low-single-digit revenue growth (1-3% organic) driven by ARPU pressure offsetting volume growth
- EBITDA margins of 30-45% depending on segment mix and competitive intensity
- Elevated capex / revenue ratios (15-25%) driven by ongoing or recently completed fibre rollout
- Dividend yields of 3-6%, acting as a floor but also a constraint on balance sheet optionality
- Sell-side consensus clustered around hold/neutral; few strong buy or sell convictions
- Low Minervini scores (most of these names are not in Stage 2 momentum setups — KPN is the exception)

**What would genuinely surprise us (signals worth escalating to ESA):**
- Evidence of ARPU per fibre subscriber rising meaningfully (>5% year-on-year) and this being sustainable
- A CEO with a clear operational transformation agenda, measurable KPIs, and evidence of delivery
- A market structure shift (consolidation signal, competitor financial distress, regulatory relief on wholesale access pricing) that changes the competitive dynamics materially
- FCF yield >6% with capex already declining — suggesting the dividend is under-covered only in the market's model, not in reality
- B2B/enterprise revenue growing >10% and at structurally higher margins than consumer — mix shift thesis
- Elisa-specific: software platform revenues growing internationally at scale (not just Finnish domestic)

## 5. Cohort-Level Invalidation Candidates

These are ACHs that could fire across multiple stocks simultaneously and lead to cohort-level parking rather than individual-stock parking:

**Invalidation 1 — Sector-level regulatory deterioration.** If EU or national regulators tighten wholesale access pricing rules (forcing incumbents to offer cheaper access to competitors on newly built fibre networks), the fibre monetisation thesis breaks across the board. Monitor for: BEREC consultations, national NRA decisions on fibre access pricing in Netherlands/Belgium/Austria/Finland.

**Invalidation 2 — Macro rate re-pricing.** European telcos are effectively bond proxies at current yield levels. If long-end European rates rise materially (10Y Bund >3.5%), the relative attractiveness of telco yields compresses and multiple re-rates downward regardless of fundamental delivery. This is a portfolio-level risk, not a company-level one — but it would invalidate the entry timing thesis.

**Invalidation 3 — Fixed-wireless access (FWA) acceleration.** If 5G FWA (Starlink, T-Mobile, Vodafone) takes significant share in fixed broadband in any of these markets, the fibre ARPU uplift thesis is undermined. The key test: are we seeing genuine subscriber losses from FWA in the data, or is this a theoretical risk? At Triaging stage, assess FWA penetration rate and trajectory.

**Invalidation 4 — América Móvil strategic action (TKA-AT specific).** América Móvil owns ~51% of TKA-AT. Any signal of a delisting, forced restructuring, squeeze-out, or capital extraction (special dividend funded by leverage) would change the investment thesis materially. This is a governance risk specific to TKA-AT but worth flagging at cohort level as it affects Sub-B risk profile.
