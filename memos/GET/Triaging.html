Text: Progress to ESA. Getlink is a natural monopoly with a 60-year concession runway (to 2086) — the Channel Tunnel is an irreplaceable fixed-link asset protected by bilateral Treaty of Canterbury. HSR passenger inflection is the genuine volume catalyst: Virgin Trains (2029-30) and Trenitalia (2029) entering as new operators will structurally increase Eurotunnel rail path utilisation at >90% incremental EBITDA margins via fixed-cost infrastructure. But ElecLink revenue normalisation (€558m peak 2023 to €225m 2025), persistent truck shuttle weakness (-5.3% Jan 2026), and stretched valuation (~33x P/E) create tension. Two gates: (i) can freight volumes stabilise and HSR new entrant timelines hold? (ii) will ElecLink spreads stabilise at €200-250m run-rate or compress further?

The fulcrum question is whether the HSR entrant timeline holds given rolling stock procurement complexity and whether freight volume weakness is cyclical (EU IP stagnation) or structural (supply chain restructuring away from UK-EU corridors post-Brexit).
