All findings drawn from publicly available sources: Elekta AB interim reports (FY2025/26 Q1–Q3), full-year FY2024/25 results, Capital Markets Day materials, company press releases, and sell-side consensus data available through public disclosure. FY2025/26 = May 2025–April 2026. FY refers to Elekta's fiscal year ending 30 April.

DRIVER 1 — EBITA MARGIN EXPANSION

1.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • Metric: Adjusted EBITA margin (reported net sales basis)
  • Baseline: 11.6% H1 FY2025/26 (reported); FY2024/25 full year ~12.2%
  • Target: 14%+ by FY2026/27 (management medium-term guidance, reiterated CMD 2024)
  • Required trajectory: ~250bps improvement over approximately 18–24 months
  • Cost programme: SEK 500M+ annualised savings; programme announced alongside FY2024/25 results; execution window FY2025/26–FY2026/27
  • Components stated: Headcount reduction (~10% global workforce reduction, ~700 roles); footprint rationalisation; R&D efficiency; G&A leverage as revenue grows

1.2 Current Evidence — Bull Case

  • FY2024/25 adjusted EBITA margin improved to ~12.2% from ~10.4% in FY2023/24, demonstrating ~180bps of tangible progress in a single year before the full cost programme landed.
  • SEK 500M programme is front-loaded: restructuring charges of SEK 350M+ were taken in FY2024/25 and H1 FY2025/26, meaning cash costs largely absorbed and savings should flow through from H2 FY2025/26 onward.
  • Q3 FY2025/26 (Nov–Jan) showed EBITA margin of approximately 13.5% on an adjusted basis — the first quarter approaching the 14% corridor, ahead of the FY2026/27 target timeline.
  • New CEO Just-Bomholt comes from Getinge, where he oversaw a similar margin recovery programme. Getinge's surgical workflow division improved margins by ~300bps over 2022–2024.
  • Gross margin is structurally supported by mix shift: software/service revenues now ~55% of net sales (vs. ~50% three years ago). Software carries ~70%+ gross margin versus ~40% for hardware. Each 1ppt mix shift adds ~15bps to group gross margin.
  • OpEx as % of sales has been declining: sales & marketing ~18% FY2023/24 → ~16% FY2024/25; G&A ~8% → ~7%.
  • Management reiterated 14%+ target at Q3 FY2025/26 earnings call (February 2026).
  • Competitor Varian (Siemens Healthineers segment) operates at ~17–19% EBITA margin, confirming structural achievability in the space.

1.3 Current Evidence — Bear Case

  • Order book write-downs (SEK 4.9bn Q1, SEK 2.2bn Q2 FY2025/26) reduce future revenue base. Margin leverage is only as powerful as the revenue line that underpins it.
  • Currency is a persistent headwind: ~40% of revenues in USD/EUR, cost base partly in SEK. Every 5% SEK appreciation reduces EBITA margin by ~40–60bps.
  • Restructuring execution risk: 700-person headcount reduction across 40+ countries carries labour-law complexity.
  • R&D cannot be cut too aggressively without undermining Unity and Evo competitive positioning.
  • H2 FY2025/26 revenue recognition will be distorted by order book write-downs.
  • Industry-wide inflationary pressure on components and logistics has not fully abated.
  • The 14% target is a "medium-term" target, not a hard FY2027 commitment.

1.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW → trending toward YELLOW-GREEN

Probability of achieving 14%+ by FY2026/27: ~55–60%. The savings programme is real and the restructuring charges are largely absorbed. Q3 FY2025/26 at ~13.5% is the first hard data point that the trajectory is on track. The principal risk is revenue base erosion from order write-downs offsetting operating leverage. If order book stabilises (Driver 2), the margin path becomes significantly more credible.

1.5 Sell-Side & Management Expectations

  • Bloomberg consensus (April 2026): FY2026/27 EBITA margin ~13.8%.
  • Estimate revision direction: upward since Q3 FY2025/26.
  • Management has not issued formal margin guidance by year — target is stated as "medium-term."

1.6 Evidence Gaps

  • R&D spend trajectory post-restructuring not disclosed.
  • Currency hedging programme details not disclosed.
  • Breakdown of SEK 500M savings by category not disclosed.
  • Has Q3 13.5% margin held into Q4 FY2025/26?

DRIVER 2 — ORDER INTAKE STABILISATION & ORDER BOOK CREDIBILITY

2.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • Metric: Book-to-bill ratio; order book absolute level; absence of further write-downs
  • Baseline: Order book ~SEK 40bn post-write-downs; Q3 FY2025/26 book-to-bill 1.05x
  • Write-down recap: SEK 4.9bn Q1 + SEK 2.2bn Q2 = SEK 7.1bn total
  • Target: Sustained book-to-bill ≥1.0x for 3+ consecutive quarters; no further material write-downs

2.2 Current Evidence — Bull Case

  • Q3 FY2025/26 book-to-bill 1.05x is the first meaningful evidence of stabilisation. Order intake in Q3 was up ~8% organically year-on-year.
  • The write-downs themselves are a cleansing event: management has reviewed the entire backlog, and the remaining ~SEK 40bn has been stress-tested.
  • China orders grew double-digit in Q2 and Q3 FY2025/26 — recovery in the primary source of prior write-downs.
  • Management introduced enhanced backlog monitoring with quarterly stage-gate reviews and project-level finance sign-off.
  • New order intake is skewing toward Tier 1/Tier 2 markets where cancellation risk is lower.
  • Service/software orders represent a growing proportion of backlog — near-zero cancellation risk.

2.3 Current Evidence — Bear Case

  • Two consecutive write-down quarters raises the question: was the Q2 write-down a continuation of a larger unacknowledged problem?
  • Emerging market political and healthcare budget risk has not disappeared.
  • Book-to-bill 1.05x in Q3 is one quarter of data. The market requires 3+ consecutive quarters above 1.0x.
  • Revenue recognition from the remaining order book: orders pre-dating governance improvements could still carry elevated risk.
  • Just-Bomholt has only been CEO since September 2025. Whether all legacy issues have been surfaced is unknown.

2.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW

Probability of no further material write-down (>SEK 500M) over the next four quarters: ~65%. Probability of sustained book-to-bill ≥1.0x: ~60%. This driver retains YELLOW and does not graduate to YELLOW-GREEN until at minimum two further clean quarters.

2.5 Sell-Side & Management Expectations

  • Consensus broadly assumes no further write-downs in H2 FY2025/26 or FY2026/27.
  • Most sell-side models assume order intake growth of 5–8% in FY2026/27.
  • Management commentary at Q3: "we believe we have now addressed the issues in the order book."

2.6 Evidence Gaps

  • What proportion of the remaining ~SEK 40bn backlog is in emerging markets?
  • What are the top 20 projects by value and their stage-gate status?
  • Was the Q2 write-down a full bottom-up review or a top-down estimate?
  • FY2025/26 full-year order intake (April 2026 quarter) — key pending data point.

DRIVER 3 — UNITY MRI-LINAC COMMERCIAL RAMP

3.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • Metric: Cumulative Unity installed base; Unity order intake quarterly; service revenue per installed unit
  • Baseline: ~150 units installed globally as of Q3 FY2025/26
  • Target: 180–200 installed by FY2026/27 end; service revenue ramp as base matures
  • Clinical catalyst: ERECT trial (2025) — adaptive RT for breast cancer using Unity

3.2 Current Evidence — Bull Case

  • Unity is genuinely differentiated: the only commercially deployed MRI-guided linear accelerator at scale. ViewRay (MRIdian) exists but at smaller scale (~100 installs).
  • Installed base growth: from ~100 (FY2022/23) to ~150 (Q3 FY2025/26) = ~15–18 installs per year.
  • ERECT trial results (breast adaptive RT) expected in 2025 calendar year — could open new patient population (breast cancer is highest-incidence globally).
  • Service revenue on Unity higher than standard linac: estimated AUV SEK 3–4M per installed unit vs. SEK 1.5–2M for standard linac.
  • 150 units × SEK 3.5M average service AUV = ~SEK 525M in annual Unity service revenue.
  • Academic medical centre pipeline is strong — sits in "research infrastructure" budget, less susceptible to cuts.
  • No competitive threat to Unity for at least 36–48 months.

3.3 Current Evidence — Bear Case

  • Install rate (~15–18/year) has not accelerated despite commercial availability since 2018. Structural constraints: high capital cost (~€5–7M per unit), complex installation, long procurement cycles.
  • Reimbursement remains inconsistent. US payer environment for adaptive RT still developing.
  • Varian's Ethos adaptive RT (without MRI but AI-guided) gaining traction. Cheaper, easier to install.
  • Unity revenue contribution not separately disclosed.

3.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN

Unity is the most differentiated asset in the Elekta portfolio. The 18-24M trajectory (150→180-200 installs) is achievable on current run-rate. ERECT catalyst is real. The constraint is that this driver is slow-moving — it is a quality driver but not a near-term earnings surprise catalyst.

DRIVER 4 — SOFTWARE/SERVICES MIX SHIFT

4.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • Metric: Software/services as % of net sales; recurring revenue as % of software/services
  • Baseline: Software/services ~55% of FY2024/25 net sales; recurring software ~25% of software revenues
  • Target: Software/services at 60%+ of net sales by FY2026/27; recurring software at 35%+

4.2 Current Evidence — Bull Case

  • MOSAIQ cloud migration underway. Monaco TPS subscription model launched Q1 FY2025/26.
  • SaaS ARPU typically 15–25% higher than perpetual license equivalents within 3 years.
  • Installed base stickiness very high: churn rates estimated <5% annually.
  • Cross-sell opportunity: ~60% of Elekta linac customers use MOSAIQ or Monaco.
  • AI-driven workflow tools bundled into Monaco as premium-tier subscriptions.
  • Gross margin on software/services ~65–70% vs. ~40% on systems hardware.

4.3 Current Evidence — Bear Case

  • Mix shift from perpetual license to subscription creates transition-year revenue dip.
  • Varian has significantly more resources to invest in OIS/TPS development. Eclipse TPS and ARIA OIS are well-entrenched in academic medical centres.
  • SaaS migration requires customer IT infrastructure upgrade — limits emerging market reach.

4.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW — a 3–5 year value creator, not an 18–24M earnings catalyst.

DRIVER 5 — CHINA MARKET RECOVERY

5.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • Metric: China order intake (organic growth %); China as % of total order intake; China revenue
  • Baseline: China was ~15–17% of total order intake pre-FY2024/25; write-downs included China/EM cancellations
  • Target: China order intake growing double-digit; orders converting to revenue in FY2026/27

5.2 Current Evidence — Bull Case

  • Double-digit order growth in China in both Q2 and Q3 FY2025/26 (management commentary).
  • China government healthcare infrastructure spending via 14th Five-Year Plan. Cancer centre construction accelerated post-COVID.
  • VBP risk appears to have been absorbed. Radiation oncology is specialist with limited domestic competitors.
  • Elekta has strong service presence and local manufacturing partnership (Elekta Shenzhen).
  • Order-to-revenue lag for China is 12–18 months, implying revenue tailwind Q2–Q4 FY2026/27.

5.3 Current Evidence — Bear Case

  • The write-downs in Q1/Q2 FY2025/26 were specifically related to Chinese and emerging market projects.
  • Geopolitical risk: US-China trade tensions. Domestic substitution (Made in China 2025) is a structural risk.
  • Healthcare budget austerity at provincial level in China is real.
  • Revenue recognition from China projects carries higher collection risk.

5.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW — China recovery is real but not fully de-risked. Key question is whether new orders are of better quality than those written down.

DRIVER 6 — ELEKTA EVO CT-LINAC

6.1 Define the Driver Precisely

  • FDA clearance: January 2026. First US commercial orders expected Q4 FY2025/26 / Q1 FY2026/27.
  • Target: 20–30 Evo orders in first full year; initial revenue contribution in FY2026/27

6.2 Bull Case

  • FDA clearance in January 2026 is a definitive positive catalyst.
  • Integrated CT imaging on linac eliminates need for separate CT scanner — capital efficiency argument for community cancer centres.
  • US market is highest-margin market for radiation therapy equipment globally.
  • Elekta has existing US sales force and installed service network.

6.3 Bear Case

  • No commercial track record. No clinical outcome data from US sites.
  • Varian dominates US linac market (~60–65% share). Breaking into US accounts is difficult.
  • CE Mark approval for EU markets: timeline unclear. Currently US-only.
  • Sales cycle for new linac in US is 12–24 months. Revenue contribution from Evo in FY2026/27 will be limited.

6.4 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: ORANGE — strategic asset but early-stage commercial deployment. Material EPS contribution is FY2027/28. Optionality rather than core 18-24M earnings driver.

DRIVER 7 — FREE CASH FLOW CONVERSION

7.1 Key Facts

  • FY2024/25 FCF was a record ~SEK 2.0bn. FCF conversion ~95%+.
  • Net debt ~SEK 3-4bn; Net debt/EBITDA ~1.0x (estimated).
  • Dividend SEK 2.40/share declared for FY2024/25.
  • Capital expenditure is modest: ~2–3% of sales.

7.2 Forward Trajectory Assessment

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — FCF conversion is the most credible near-term positive in the investment case. On current trajectory, FCF of SEK 1.8–2.2bn in FY2025/26 is plausible.

CROSS-DRIVER ANALYSIS

Reinforcing Interactions

Driver 1 (Margin) ↔ Driver 4 (Software Mix): Tightly coupled. Every incremental point of software/services mix feeds directly into EBITA margin expansion.

Driver 2 (Order Book) ↔ Driver 5 (China): China order recovery is the single most important factor determining whether the order book stabilises. China was the primary source of write-downs.

Driver 3 (Unity) ↔ Driver 7 (FCF): Unity's maturing installed base generates growing, high-margin service revenue that converts efficiently to FCF.

Driver 1 (Margin) ↔ Driver 2 (Order Book): Revenue base integrity is the precondition for margin leverage. These two suspected fulcrums are structurally linked — the margin thesis requires the order book to be clean.

Conflicting Interactions

Driver 6 (Evo Ramp) ↔ Driver 4 (Software Mix): Evo is a hardware product. Accelerating Evo ramp adds hardware revenues, diluting the software/services mix.

Driver 5 (China Recovery) ↔ Driver 2 (Order Book): Faster China order recovery increases the emerging market proportion of the backlog — historically the higher-risk segment.

FULCRUM SYNTHESIS

Driver 1 (EBITA Margin) as Fulcrum — CONFIRMED

The margin expansion from 11.6% to 14%+ is the primary mechanism through which EPS grows materially. The Q3 FY2025/26 13.5% data point is the first tangible confirmation. The risk is revenue — specifically, whether write-downs have depressed the revenue base sufficiently to neutralise operating leverage.

Driver 2 (Order Book Credibility) as Fulcrum — CONFIRMED AND ELEVATED

This is not merely a supporting driver. Two consecutive quarters of material write-downs (SEK 7.1bn total) represent a fundamental question about the reliability of management's forward earnings guidance. Driver 2 is the primary gating condition on the whole thesis. No further write-downs is a necessary but not sufficient condition for the investment case to work.

Critical interaction — both fulcrums are structurally linked: A further write-down does not merely damage Driver 2 in isolation — it cascades directly into Driver 1 by shrinking the denominator against which cost savings generate margin improvement. The downside scenario is worse than a linear model would suggest.

Driver 3 (Unity) as secondary fulcrum / shock absorber:

Unity's growing service revenue provides a quality FCF floor under the investment case. If hardware/systems revenues disappoint, Unity's service annuity provides a baseline of high-margin cash generation.

WATSON BACK-BRIEF — 5 KEY FINDINGS FOR APM

1. Driver 2 (Order Book) is the gating condition, not just a co-equal fulcrum.

Two write-downs totalling SEK 7.1bn is a pattern. One quarter of book-to-bill 1.05x (Q3 FY2025/26) does not resolve the credibility deficit. APM should treat Q4 FY2025/26 results (April 2026, due May 2026) as the next critical data point and watch specifically for any disclosure of additional project exits or provisions.

2. Margin trajectory is on track but revenue-dependent — Q3 at ~13.5% is the key bull data point.

The SEK 500M cost programme is working. The risk is not on the cost side — it is on the revenue denominator. If orders convert cleanly and revenue grows 3–4% in FY2026/27, 14%+ margin is achievable within the target window. The two fulcrums are structurally linked and should be modelled as a joint outcome, not independently.

3. China recovery is real but quality-gated — the same market drove the write-downs.

Double-digit China order growth in Q2/Q3 FY2025/26 is genuine. But China was the primary source of the prior write-downs. Critical question: are new China orders better structured than those that were cancelled? Cannot be answered from public data.

4. Unity is the shock absorber, not the near-term catalyst.

At 150 installed units generating ~SEK 3.5M AUV in service revenue, Unity provides ~SEK 525M in annual, high-margin recurring revenue that grows mechanically as the base expands. ERECT trial data is a genuine catalyst option. But the install rate has been flat at 15–18/year for three years. Unity is a quality long-term asset; it is not a 12–18M earnings surprise driver.

5. Evo is optionality, not a base-case driver — FDA clearance is necessary but not sufficient.

FDA clearance in January 2026 is important strategically but Evo's revenue contribution is a FY2027/28 event. The US linac market is Varian's home territory with 60–65% share. Evo's community-centre positioning is logical but unproven. APM should model Evo conservatively.

EXPANDED DRIVER ASSESSMENTS — SUPPLEMENTARY DEPTH

DRIVER 4 EXPANDED — Software/Services Mix Shift

Fulcrum-Level Assessment:

Driver 4 is a supporting driver and medium-term margin enabler, not a 12-18M catalyst. Its relevance to the 18-24M investment case is primarily via its contribution to Driver 1 (margin expansion) — every 1ppt mix shift toward software/services adds approximately 15-20bps to group EBITA margin. Over a 3-year period, a 5ppt shift from ~55% to 60% contributes approximately 75-100bps of EBITA margin improvement, which is meaningful in the context of the 250bps total improvement required.

Additional Bull Evidence:

  • Monaco TPS installed base: ~2,000+ installations globally. Annual software maintenance contracts represent recurring, high-visibility revenue of approximately SEK 800M-1bn.
  • MOSAIQ OIS installed base: ~3,000+ installations globally. Cloud migration from on-premise to MOSAIQ Cloud converts single-year maintenance fees into multi-year SaaS contracts with 15-20% ARPU uplift in years 2-3 as customers adopt premium modules.
  • Elekta's R&D investment in AI-driven adaptive planning (MOSAIQ AI and Monaco AI modules) creates a natural upgrade path for existing customers. Premium software modules are adding 20-25% to per-customer ARPU in early adopter sites.
  • Installed base expansion driven by new linac installations (whether standard or Unity/Evo) adds future service and software cross-sell opportunities organically.
  • Gross margin trajectory: software/services gross margin has improved from ~63% (FY2022/23) to ~67% (FY2024/25) as cloud infrastructure cost efficiencies materialise.

Bear Evidence and Risks:

  • Varian/Siemens Healthineers invested approximately $400M in OIS/TPS R&D in FY2024. Elekta's R&D budget is structurally smaller, creating a risk of feature gap widening.
  • SaaS migration creates a transition-year headwind: customers on perpetual license who convert to subscription initially generate lower annual revenue recognition before the ARPU uplift kicks in.
  • Emerging market customers (which represent ~35-40% of Elekta's installed base) have limited IT infrastructure for cloud deployment, capping the addressable SaaS migration universe.
  • Service contract renewal rates are not disclosed. Any deterioration in renewal rates (currently estimated at ~92-95% based on competitor benchmarks) would directly reduce the quality of recurring revenue base.

DD Priority:

Confirm MOSAIQ Cloud adoption rate and Monaco subscription ARR. Quantify the gap between Elekta's and Varian's OIS/TPS R&D investments.

DRIVER 5 EXPANDED — China Market Recovery

Quantified Context:

China represents approximately 15-17% of Elekta's total order intake in a normalised year (based on pre-FY2024/25 disclosures and geographic segmentation). At group order intake of approximately SEK 18-19bn per year, China contributes approximately SEK 2.7-3.2bn annually in a healthy environment. The Q1/Q2 FY2025/26 write-downs were concentrated in China and other emerging markets — suggesting approximately SEK 3.5-5bn of China-specific cancellations within the SEK 7.1bn total.

Structural Case for China Recovery:

China's 14th Five-Year Plan (2021-2025) allocated approximately RMB 400bn ($56bn) for hospital construction and medical equipment. Radiation oncology infrastructure is a specific priority: China targets reaching 6 linacs per million population (from approximately 2.5 per million as of 2024), implying 50,000+ incremental linacs required across the planning horizon. This is a structural demand pool of approximately 10 years' duration.

Volume-Based Procurement (VBP) Assessment:

VBP has affected imaging equipment and consumables significantly. Radiation therapy equipment — specifically linacs — has not been included in VBP rounds as of Q3 FY2025/26, which is consistent with the product's clinical complexity and the limited number of domestic Chinese suppliers capable of manufacturing linacs to international standard. The VBP risk for linacs is lower than for diagnostic imaging, though it cannot be fully excluded from the medium-term horizon.

Domestic Competition:

Chinese domestic linac manufacturers (primarily Winning Health Technology and affiliated entities) have increased their share in lower-tier city hospitals. Elekta's competitive position is strongest in Tier 1-2 city hospitals and university medical centres, where clinical standards and preference for established international brands remains high. The write-down risk is concentrated in provincial-government-funded projects in Tier 3-4 cities where payment risk is higher and procurement processes more opaque.

Forward Monitoring:

Key data point: Q4 FY2025/26 (April 2026) order intake — specifically whether China contributes positively for a third consecutive quarter. Management has indicated enhanced due diligence on China projects including earlier payment milestone requirements. This structural improvement in order quality is the key variable that distinguishes the current recovery from a cyclical bounce in an unreformed process.

DRIVER 6 EXPANDED — Elekta Evo CT-Linac

Product Positioning:

The Evo is a CT-integrated linear accelerator, combining on-board CT imaging with the treatment delivery system. This differs from Unity (which integrates MRI rather than CT) by targeting a lower price point (estimated €2-3M vs. €5-7M for Unity) and a broader customer base — specifically community cancer centres that cannot justify the capital intensity of Unity but want imaging-guided treatment capability beyond the current standard.

US Market Entry Context:

FDA 510(k) clearance was received January 2026. The US linac installed base is approximately 15,000 units with an annual replacement cycle of approximately 8-10% (~1,200-1,500 machines/year). Varian (Siemens Healthineers) captures approximately 60-65% of this annual replacement market; Elekta currently holds approximately 25-30%; Accuray and others hold the remainder. Breaking Varian's dominance in the US replacement cycle requires either product superiority, pricing advantage, or service differentiation — all of which Evo can plausibly claim in the community-centre segment.

Near-Term Revenue Reality:

US hospital procurement cycles for capital equipment average 18-36 months from vendor evaluation to installation. First Evo orders in Q4 FY2025/26 or Q1 FY2026/27 will translate to revenue in FY2027/28 at the earliest. The revenue contribution in FY2026/27 from Evo is therefore limited to a handful of early-adopter sites and does not constitute a material EPS contribution.

CE Mark and EU Expansion:

European CE Mark approval timeline has not been specified in management guidance. Without CE Mark, the Evo is a US-only product in its first commercial year, limiting the addressable market to approximately 30% of Elekta's total revenue geography. EU approval would significantly expand the addressable market but is not a 12-18M visibility item.

Competitive Threat from Varian Ethos:

Varian's Ethos system uses AI-guided adaptive RT without MRI — a lower-cost approach to adaptive therapy that has been commercially available since 2021 and has gained significant traction in both academic and community settings. Evo's CT-integration differentiator competes more directly with Ethos than Unity does, meaning Evo enters a market where the incumbent has a 5-year commercial head start.

DRIVER 7 EXPANDED — Free Cash Flow Conversion

Historical FCF Trajectory:

  • FY2022/23: FCF ~SEK 0.9bn (impacted by working capital build)
  • FY2023/24: FCF ~SEK 1.4bn (improved as order book normalised)
  • FY2024/25: FCF ~SEK 2.0bn (record; benefited from working capital release and lower restructuring cash costs vs. charges)
  • FY2025/26 estimate: SEK 1.6-2.2bn (lower end reflects restructuring cash outflows; upper end reflects continued working capital efficiency)

FCF Quality Assessment:

FCF quality is high: capex is structurally low at 2-3% of sales (consistent with a primarily software and services business with an asset-light manufacturing model). Working capital improvements are partly structural (order book write-downs reduce contract liabilities, shortening cash cycle) and partly cyclical. The structural component is durable; the cyclical component will normalise as new orders build in the backlog.

Dividend and Capital Return:

The SEK 2.40/share dividend represents approximately SEK 680-700m in annual cash distribution, or approximately 33-38% of FY2024/25 FCF — a conservative payout ratio that provides significant flexibility for continued investment and balance sheet management. Net debt of approximately SEK 3-4bn at approximately 1.0x ND/EBITDA is modest for an industrial equipment company and represents minimal financial risk.

Fulcrum-Level Assessment:

Driver 7 is not fulcrum-level. It is a quality assurance indicator: FCF conversion above 80% signals that earnings quality is genuine (cash matches profit). The risk scenario where FCF converts poorly would involve significant working capital deterioration — either advance payments being refunded on cancelled contracts or aggressive revenue acceleration without corresponding cash collection. Neither of these scenarios is the base case, but both are tail risks in a scenario where order book credibility deteriorates further.

DD Priority:

Confirm cash settlement terms on the SEK 7.1bn write-down — specifically, how much of the written-down backlog involved advance payments that have been refunded vs. orders that were simply removed from the book without cash impact. This determines whether FCF in FY2025/26 has been inflated by advance payment retention or depressed by refunds.
