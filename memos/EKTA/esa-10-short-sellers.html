Elekta’s equity has historically been subjected to entrenched bearish patterns, driven by a succession of operational and accounting crises that institutional skeptics argue have masked deeper, structural market share losses.

The FY2018/19 IFRS 15 Scare

In 2018, Elekta’s transition to the new IFRS 15 accounting standard served as a major catalyst for bearish sentiment. Under the previous standard, the company recognized revenue for treatment solutions when the risks and rewards were transferred, which typically occurred at the point of shipment. The implementation of IFRS 15 fundamentally shifted this recognition trigger to the start of installation at the customer site. While management framed this accounting shift as a move toward greater revenue stability, the retroactive restatement artificially boosted the company’s fiscal year 2017/18 EBITA margin by approximately 240 basis points, lifting it to 21 percent.
