Q4 2024 Financial Performance

Flat top line masked by underlying service growth and disciplined mix shift; EBITA margins beat consensus despite restructuring charges and Northvolt provisions; strong cash conversion the standout metric.

  • Revenue: Q4 net sales SEK 8,108m, flat YoY; organic growth -4%, fully offset by +4% from acquisitions #I
    • FY2024: Total net sales +1% to SEK 29,653m; acquired growth +5% offsetting -3% organic decline
    • Mix shift: Q4 service sales +5%, installation -4%, consistent with deliberate margin-over-volume strategy
  • EBITA margin: Q4 reported 7.5% (vs 7.4% prior year); underlying 8.3% excluding comparability items, 15% above consensus #J
    • Restructuring charge: SEK 41m for staff layoffs and branch closures in southern Sweden
    • Northvolt provision: Final SEK 30m bad debt provision; full-year total SEK 100m exposure
    • FY2024 margin: Reported 5.2%, adjusted 5.7%
  • Order intake: Q4 order intake -26% YoY; management attributed to selective tender strategy and tough base (SEK 1.3bn Stockholm metro contract in Q4 2023) #A
    • Backlog: Total order backlog decreased SEK 1,681m in Q4
    • Context: Selective strategy designed to protect margin quality of future backlog, not volume
  • Cash generation: Q4 operating cash flow SEK 756m; FY2024 operating cash flow SEK 1,896m, 105% conversion; net debt/EBITDA 1.0x at year-end #I
    • Dividend: Board proposed SEK 3.75/share, 73% payout ratio

Geographic Segment Results

Denmark executed the standout turnaround of the quarter; Norway delivered strong margins via service mix shift; Finland improved modestly; Sweden dragged by southern weakness and restructuring charges.

  • Denmark: EBITA margin 4.0% (from 0.1% prior year), supported by +9% net sales growth driven entirely by organic momentum and service business #J
    • Mechanism: Careful project selection and deliberate shift away from unprofitable contracts
    • Order backlog: Order intake -20% YoY but total backlog +8%; new orders carry significantly better margins
    • Outlook: Management views 2025 as transition year toward full margin potential by 2026
  • Norway: EBITA margin 7.5% (from 5.9% prior year) alongside Thunestvedt integration completing better than expected #I
    • Revenue: Net sales -2% YoY (organic -7%, acquisitions +6%)
    • Mix: Service sales +13%, installation -18%; service now 60% of Norwegian net sales
    • Order intake: +10% in Q4, primarily service assignments; total backlog -23% YoY, indicating volume risk ahead
  • Finland: EBITA margin 6.4% (from 6.1%); net sales +4% (organic -3%, acquisitions +6%) despite very soft demand #I
    • Risk: Q4 order intake -11%; order backlog -33% YoY; market expected to remain weak into 2025
    • Driver: Margin improvement attributable to better installation project margins
  • Sweden: Net sales -4% (organic -7%); reported EBITA margin 9.6% (from 11.3%), underlying 11.4% ex restructuring and Northvolt provision #A
    • Southern drag: -20% sales volume in southern Sweden; restructuring programme (branch closures + layoffs), SEK 41m charge
    • North/Central: Continued to perform well with stable demand and high profitability
    • Order intake: -40% YoY, but -18% adjusted for SEK 1.3bn Stockholm metro comparable; underlying trend less severe

Market Outlook & Strategy

Management calls the bottom of the Nordic installation cycle; continued "margin over volume" execution through 2025, positioning for recovery via high-quality backlog, Swedish restructuring savings, and M&A acceleration.

  • Cycle call: Management expects Nordic installation market to have bottomed; gradual recovery through 2026; early signs visible in southern Sweden and Finland by late 2025 #J
    • Third-party: Installation volumes forecast to grow ~4% annually in 2026-2027
    • Service: Demand for service and maintenance stable, growing ~1% per year
  • Growth segments: Bravida well-positioned in infrastructure, defense facilities, industrial projects, and data centers; specialized technical requirements and favorable pricing dynamics #A
  • Sweden restructuring: Swedish operations consolidated Jan 2026 (North/Central/South into single entity); SEK 20m charge in Q4 2025, SEK 70-90m further in 2026, targeting SEK 65m annual savings #I
  • M&A pipeline: New Head of M&A (Alexandra Johansson, appointed early 2026); deal pace expected to accelerate toward historical averages; ABEKA El och Kraftanlaggningar AB sold to Vidia Climate Fund I for completion H1 2026 #A
  • Sustainability: Carbon neutrality target by 2045; EV fleet share 45% of 8,400 vehicles, driving 20.5% reduction in vehicle CO2 YoY; LTIFR 4.9 (below 5.5 target) #I

Broker Perspectives & Estimate Revisions

Sell-side reaction positive on margin quality; order intake miss was absorbed given the base-effect explanation; both DNB Carnegie and Carnegie revised EPS upward on higher margin assumptions, with Carnegie upgrading to BUY.

  • DNB Carnegie: Reiterated BUY, TP SEK 112; adjusted EBITA 8.3% came in 15% above consensus; 7% EBITA target for 2026 viewed as "highly realistic" #J
    • Framing: Strong underlying margins and free cash flow viewed as strong counterweight to weaker-than-expected order intake
    • 2025 outlook: ~75% of 2025 EPS improvement expected to come from Danish recovery
    • Risk flag: Installation backlogs in Finland and Norway "very thin", volume risk for 2025
  • Carnegie upgrade: HOLD to BUY, TP raised SEK 85 to SEK 110; Q4 underlying margins in Sweden, Norway, and Denmark stronger than expected #I
    • EPS revisions: 2025-2026 EPS estimates revised +2-3%; higher EBITA margin assumptions fully offset lower organic growth assumptions
    • Southern Sweden: Persistent weakness and restructuring absorbed most YoY earnings growth generated by Danish recovery
  • Subsequent action: By early 2026 DNB Carnegie raised TP to SEK 115, anticipating multi-year Nordic recovery; SEK 400m dispute receivables collection flagged as extra remuneration catalyst #A

Valuation Framework

Bravida commands a premium to traditional construction peers but trades in line with direct installation peers; sell-side argues this premium is structurally warranted given service mix, cash conversion, and lower project risk.

  • Target multiple: Carnegie's revised TP of SEK 110 implies 2025e EV/EBITA of 13x, in line with historical average and at premium to Nordic business services peer group (~11x 2025e EV/EBITA) #I

  • Premium rationale: Carnegie argues Bravida warrants tangible premium over Skanska, NCC, PEAB due to greater earnings stability, structurally higher margins, and significantly lower individual project risk #J
