Margin Profile and Commodity Risk (KQ2)

2026 margins are hedged and operating leverage is real, but 2027 commodity re-hedging at elevated prices is the primary forward risk; consumer K-shape favours premium but is not immune.

  • Verdict: 2026 is protected by hedging; 2027 is the unhedged risk cliff if commodity prices remain elevated #J
  • COGS structure: ~45% packaging materials (aluminium ~45% of packaging, glass, PET, paper) + barley + hops + energy + logistics #I
  • 2026 hedging: CEO confirmed "hedged against cost increases this year"; consistent with 12-18 month rolling hedge practice #I
  • 2027 exposure: No early hedging disclosed at Q1 2026 #I
    • Risk: If aluminium/oil remain elevated through H2 2026, 2027 hedge costs materially higher
    • Aluminium: LME hit $3,269/tonne in March 2026 (+~15% YoY)
    • Scenario: 5-8% COGS inflation into 2027 plausible if prices don't normalise
  • Oil-COGS transmission: Multiple channels of exposure #A
    • Aluminium: Energy-intensive smelting directly correlated to oil; can-heavy mix = direct exposure
    • Transport: Diesel-linked freight, most acute in emerging Asia
    • Barley: Indirect via fertiliser (gas-linked) and energy cost of production
  • Operating leverage: Q1 positive; 5% OP growth on ~3-4% revenue growth #I
  • Consumer demand: K-shaped polarisation (premium or economy; middle squeezed) #A
    • Premium holding: Carlsberg brand +10%, AF brews +7%
