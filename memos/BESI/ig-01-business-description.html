Company Overview and Business Model Core Business Focus and Global Footprint

BE Semiconductor Industries (Besi) operates as a highly specialized manufacturer engaged exclusively in a single line of business: the development, manufacturing, marketing, sales, and service of semiconductor assembly equipment. The company provides a broad portfolio of advanced packaging solutions to the global semiconductor and electronics industries.

Headquartered in Duiven, the Netherlands, Besi manages a highly optimized global footprint that strategically divides its research and manufacturing functions. Product ownership and advanced new product development activities are concentrated at its European operations in the Netherlands, Austria, and Switzerland. Conversely, substantially all system production, supply chain sourcing, product applications engineering, and tooling operations have been successfully transferred to Asian facilities. The company operates eight primary development and production facilities across Asia and Europe, supplemented by 13 regional sales and service offices. This Asian-centric production model, which utilizes major manufacturing hubs in Malaysia, China, and Vietnam, places Besi in close proximity to its core customer base. Reflecting this operational structure, approximately 68% of Besi's worldwide workforce is based in Asia.

Overarching Strategy and Product Lifecycle

Besi employs a disciplined, technology-led product lifecycle strategy to maximize profitability. The company initially enters new product markets by offering leading-edge technology to industry first-movers, typically top-tier global semiconductor manufacturers and foundries. Once commercial acceptance is firmly established, Besi shifts its focus toward reducing the cost of ownership to make the equipment appealing to a broader mainstream customer base, which largely consists of Asian assembly subcontractors. Finally, the company actively exits product markets once the underlying technology becomes commoditized and investment returns fall below attractive thresholds. Under its recently updated long-term strategic plan, Besi is targeting future through-cycle revenue of €1.5 billion to €1.9 billion while maintaining an addressable market share above 40%.

Value Chain Positioning: Blurring Front-End and Back-End

Traditionally, semiconductor manufacturing is cleanly divided into front-end wafer processing and back-end assembly and test phases. Besi has historically operated exclusively as a back-end equipment provider, supplying die attach and packaging solutions after the wafer fabrication process is complete. However, the advent of advanced packaging technologies is actively blurring the traditional boundaries between front-end and back-end manufacturing environments. Direct copper-to-copper hybrid bonding creates high-density interconnects between vertically stacked chips directly within the stringent front-end cleanroom environment.

To capitalize on this value chain shift, Besi established a strategic partnership with Applied Materials in 2020 to co-develop integrated equipment solutions. This collaboration effectively combines Applied Materials' expertise in front-end wafer processing with Besi's leadership in back-end bonding precision. Applied Materials recently validated the success of this strategy by acquiring a 9% strategic ownership stake in Besi, cementing their joint approach to the hybrid bonding market.

Competitive Moat

Besi's competitive advantage is deeply rooted in its extreme sub-micron placement accuracy and its highly scalable production model. The company's equipment achieves industry-leading precision, exemplified by the 50-nanometer placement accuracy demonstrated by its latest hybrid bonding prototype systems. Furthermore, Besi is highly regarded for its deep application support and its willingness to customize tools to meet the specific integration requirements of complex customer production lines. This technical differentiation allows Besi to command premium pricing, resulting in a structural target gross margin range of 64% to 68%.

Morgan Stanley notes that Besi's industry-leading margin profile should enable the company to generate exceptionally strong earnings momentum as its core revenue accelerates.

Product Portfolio and Technological Leadership Comprehensive Assembly Equipment Portfolio

Besi's overarching product strategy centers on supplying highly precise assembly solutions that span both substrate and wafer-level packaging processes. The company categorizes its equipment into three primary product groups: Die Attach, Packaging, and Plating. Die Attach systems constitute the overwhelming majority of the business, representing approximately 80% of total revenue in 2025. This segment features a diverse array of equipment including single chip, multi-module, flip chip, epoxy, soft solder, and advanced wafer-level bonding systems. Packaging equipment, which accounts for roughly 17% of revenue, encompasses conventional, ultra-thin, and wafer-level molding, as well as trim, form, and singulation systems. Finally, Plating solutions contribute approximately 3% of revenue and include systems for tin, copper, precious metal, and solar plating.

Advanced Packaging: The Shift to Heterogeneous Integration

The semiconductor industry is rapidly transitioning toward 2.5D and 3D chiplet architectures to overcome the physical limitations of traditional Moore's Law scaling. This heterogeneous integration demands extreme miniaturization, forcing assembly equipment to deliver tighter placement accuracies, manage lower power consumption, and handle highly complex device structures. Advanced packaging has therefore become a critical enabler for next-generation artificial intelligence applications. Besi generated roughly 70% of its 2025 revenue from advanced packaging applications. Systems capable of sub-7-micron placement accuracy alone accounted for approximately 60% of the company's total revenue.

Hybrid Bonding: Pioneering Front-End Interconnects

Hybrid bonding represents the pinnacle of advanced 3D interconnect technology, enabling direct copper-to-copper connections between chips without the use of traditional micro-bumps. This process yields up to 15 times greater interconnect density and dramatically enhances bandwidth and energy efficiency compared to standard packaging methods. To maintain its technological edge, the company recently completed its first prototype system capable of 50-nanometer placement accuracy. Customer adoption has scaled rapidly, with cumulative orders exceeding 150 systems across 18 unique clients, including top-tier logic and memory producers. The company has also deepened its integration into front-end manufacturing workflows through its strategic partnership with Applied Materials. Together, they have installed six integrated hybrid bonding production lines comprising 30 platforms at a leading logic customer.

Morgan Stanley highlights that Besi currently serves as the dominant global leader in these hybrid bonding tools.

Thermo Compression Bonding (TC Next)

While hybrid bonding dominates the highest-density architectures, Thermo Compression Bonding (TCB) bridges the critical gap between traditional mass reflow processes and direct copper bonding. Besi developed its next-generation platform, TC Next, to address the stringent requirements of advanced chiplet integration and High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) stacking. The TC Next system achieves an impressive 700-nanometer placement accuracy while handling bump pad pitches as tight as 5 microns. This platform provides crucial flexibility by supporting both standard flux-based and advanced fluxless bonding processes in a micro-inert chamber. Fluxless bonding is increasingly vital because traditional chemical flux can leave residues that reduce reliability at ultra-fine pitches.

Morgan Stanley notes that TCB presents a more competitive cost structure than hybrid bonding because it does not require the same highly stringent cleanroom environments.

Flip Chip and Multi-Module Attach Systems

Besi also maintains a robust portfolio of high-accuracy flip chip and multi-module attach (MMA) systems, which remain the workhorses of the 2.5D logic and mobile markets. The company's versatile EVO 2200 platform is highly configurable and executes precision substrate attach processes for complex applications like CoWoS. By expanding the capabilities of its traditional flip chip and MMA platforms, Besi effectively addresses the continuous demand for high-throughput, sub-micron precision across broader semiconductor manufacturing workflows.

Morgan Stanley emphasizes that these multi-module systems are increasingly pivotal in the emerging market for co-packaged optics (CPO) and silicon photonics.

Morgan Stanley further observes that the optical industry is shifting from slow, active alignment to passive optical alignment using grating couplers.

According to Morgan Stanley, this passive alignment process requires 1-micron placement accuracy, a specification that fits squarely within the proven capabilities of Besi's EVO die bonding tools.

End Markets and Industry Dynamics The Rise of Computing and AI Datacenters

BE Semiconductor Industries serves a diverse array of end-user markets, but the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence has fundamentally restructured its revenue profile. The computing and datacenter segment has surged to become the company's largest end market, accounting for approximately 50% of total revenue in 2025, up from 40% in 2024. This explosive growth is heavily driven by the massive build-out of AI infrastructure and the industry-wide transition toward 2.5D and 3D heterogeneous integration. The datacenter AI chip market alone is projected to reach $643 billion by 2030, representing a compound annual growth rate of 22% between 2025 and 2030. High-bandwidth memory (HBM) architectures are critical components of this new computing paradigm, requiring advanced wafer-level assembly to stack memory chips vertically. Driven by the escalating performance requirements of generative AI, the broader HBM market is forecast to grow by a factor of ten to reach approximately $33 billion by 2027.

Morgan Stanley analysts emphasize that AI datacenter demand will act as the primary catalyst for Besi's cyclical acceleration. They note that while hybrid bonding adoption in the HBM market may experience a more fragmented path than initially expected, Besi's dominant position in 2.5D CoWoS-like processes provides immediate upside as customers invest heavily to meet surging AI demands.

Silicon Photonics and Co-Packaged Optics

Another major growth vector within the datacenter ecosystem is the accelerated adoption of silicon photonics and co-packaged optics (CPO). As AI workloads dramatically increase bandwidth requirements, the power dissipation and latency associated with traditional copper wiring have become unsustainable, forcing the industry to transition to light-based data transmission. Silicon photonics semiconductor shipments are consequently expected to reach 367 million units by 2030, growing at a 30% compound annual rate from 2025.

Morgan Stanley highlights that optics are emerging as a critical bottleneck in AI datacenters, driving a projected 40% compound annual growth rate for Europe's addressable optical market through 2028. The firm points out that the optical industry is actively shifting from slow, active optical alignment to passive alignment utilizing grating couplers. This architectural transition transforms fiber attachment into a precision die placement process, opening up a previously inaccessible assembly step to Besi's high-accuracy EVO tools. Furthermore, integrating a single CPO package requires multiple precision die attach steps, multiplying the demand for Besi's equipment with each successive architectural generation.

Traditional Markets: Mobile and motive Evolution

Beyond high-performance computing, Besi maintains significant exposure to traditional mainstream markets such as mobile and automotive electronics. The mobile internet segment historically represented Besi's largest market, but its revenue contribution has moderated to roughly 17% in 2025. Future growth in the mobile sector now relies heavily on the integration of on-device AI, 5G/6G advanced antennas, foldable form factors, and increasingly sophisticated camera modules. Similarly, the automotive market currently accounts for 10% to 15% of the company's revenue. motive semiconductor demand is fundamentally supported by long-term megatrends, including vehicle electrification, autonomous driving systems, and the transition toward software-defined vehicles.

Industry Cyclicality and Market Recovery

The semiconductor assembly equipment market is inherently cyclical, often lagging broader semiconductor market recoveries by three to six months due to equipment production lead times. Historically, Besi's order patterns exhibited strong seasonality, with capacity build-outs peaking in the first half of the year to meet retail-oriented holiday demand. However, the surging, non-seasonal demand for AI-related datacenter and photonics infrastructure has actively moderated this traditional seasonality. Following an extended four-year downturn in mainstream applications, the industry is now showing definitive signs of normalization. Semiconductor inventory-to-booking ratios have improved significantly, dropping from above 2.0 in 2022 to below 1.5 entering 2026. Consequently, "green shoots" of recovery are emerging across traditional mobile and automotive markets, positioning the broader assembly industry for a renewed structural upcycle.

Jefferies Research notes that analog and mature node chips are experiencing supply tightness due to the immense capacity soaked up by AI datacenter demand. This dynamic is prompting industrial and automotive supply chains to increase their component orders to avoid shortages, which should further support the ongoing cyclical recovery in mainstream semiconductor equipment.

Financial Performance and Strategic Outlook Historical Revenue and Order Trends

BE Semiconductor Industries (Besi) has demonstrated significant financial resilience through recent industry cycles by leveraging its strong position in advanced packaging. In 2024, the company generated €607.5 million in revenue, representing a 4.9% increase over 2023, driven by high demand from computing end-user markets for hybrid bonding and photonics applications. However, for the full year 2025, revenue slightly decreased by 2.7% to €591.3 million. This decline was principally due to broad-based, ongoing weakness in mainstream assembly markets, particularly within the mobile, automotive, and industrial sectors.

Conversely, Besi's order intake has highlighted a robust structural shift toward artificial intelligence infrastructure. Total orders for 2025 reached €685.0 million, an increase of 16.8% compared to 2024. This growth was heavily concentrated in the second half of the year, with orders surging 63.6% versus the first half of 2025. Demand was primarily fueled by Asian subcontractors expanding their 2.5D datacenter capacity and making renewed investments in photonics applications. Consequently, orders strictly tied to AI applications grew to represent approximately 50% of the company's total order intake in 2025.

Morgan Stanley notes that the setup for Besi's core business has materially improved, driven by a material acceleration of compute demand in datacenters. The firm forecasts top-line growth for Besi's core business of 60% in 2026 and 15% year-over-year in 2027.

Industry-Leading Margin Profile

Besi operates with an exceptionally lean and scalable production model that consistently yields industry-leading profitability metrics. In 2024, the company achieved a gross margin of 65.2%, reflecting a highly favorable advanced packaging product mix. During 2025, the gross margin normalized slightly to 63.3%. This 1.9-point reduction was primarily attributed to adverse foreign exchange effects, specifically a 12% decrease in the value of the US dollar against the euro during the first half of the year.

Despite these currency headwinds and lower mainstream volumes, the company maintained an operating margin of 29.3% and a net margin of 22.3% in 2025. Net income for 2025 stood at €131.6 million, decreasing 27.7% year-over-year due to the lower gross margins, an increased effective tax rate, and higher interest expenses related to a €350 million Senior Notes issuance in July 2024.

Morgan Stanley highlights that when revenue growth fully materializes, Besi's baseline gross margins of around 65% and lean operating expense base should ensure massive operating leverage.

Updated Long-Term Strategic Roadmap

To align with the generative AI era and its expanding product portfolio, Besi recently updated its strategic roadmap covering the 2025-2029 period. The Board of Management increased the company's long-term through-cycle revenue target from a baseline of "€1 billion+++" to a new structural range of €1.5 billion to €1.9 billion. Under this updated model, Besi is targeting a total addressable market share of over 40%.

To support this revenue expansion, the strategic plan establishes a target gross margin range of 64% to 68% and a targeted operating margin of 40% to 55%. Achieving these goals relies heavily on maintaining technological leadership in wafer-level assembly, scaling Asian production capabilities, and deepening its strategic partnership with Applied Materials. Furthermore, to prepare for this growth, Besi intends to approximately double the cleanroom capacity of its Malaysian production facilities and expand process development capabilities in Singapore.

Capital Allocation Policy

Besi remains highly committed to returning excess cash to its shareholders through a balanced mix of dividends and share repurchases. The company's dividend policy targets an annual payout ratio of 40% to 100% of net income, depending on ongoing financing needs and market conditions. For the 2025 fiscal year, the Board of Management proposed a cash dividend of €1.58 per share, representing a 95% payout ratio relative to net income.

In addition to dividends, the company actively utilizes share buybacks to optimize its capital structure. Besi successfully completed a €100 million share repurchase program in October 2025, acquiring 870,825 shares at an average price of €114.83. Immediately following this completion, the company initiated a new €60 million share repurchase program intended for general capital reduction and to offset dilution from employee stock plans and convertible notes. In total, Besi distributed €254.8 million to shareholders in 2025 across dividends and buybacks.

Sustainability and ESG Initiatives

Besi has integrated a comprehensive Climate Transition Plan into its overarching corporate strategy. The company has committed to achieving Net Zero greenhouse gas emissions for its internal operations (Scope 1 and 2) by 2030. By 2050, the company aims to reach absolute Net Zero emissions across its entire upstream and downstream value chain (Scope 1, 2, and 3).

The company has already made substantial progress against its interim milestones. Since its 2021 baseline, Besi has successfully reduced absolute Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 97%. A critical driver of this reduction is the company's aggressive transition to renewable energy; in 2025, Besi procured 99% of its global electricity from renewable sources, up from just 18% in 2019. Furthermore, the company launched a "Design-to-X" initiative, which actively embeds sustainability directly into the product engineering phase to lower the downstream energy consumption of its die-attach platforms.
