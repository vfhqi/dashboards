Financial Performance & Q1 2026 Outlook Q4 2025 Financial Highlights

BE Semiconductor Industries (BESI) delivered a robust financial performance in the fourth quarter of 2025, exceeding the favorable end of its prior guidance. Revenue for the quarter reached €166.4 million, representing a 25.4% sequential increase from Q3 2025 and an 8.5% year-over-year growth. This strong top-line performance was primarily driven by higher shipments tailored for 2.5D AI-related computing and photonics applications.

The most notable metric from the quarter was the significant surge in bookings, with total orders reaching €250.4 million. This order intake marked a 43.3% sequential increase and a 105.4% jump compared to the fourth quarter of 2024. The sharp acceleration in bookings was principally fueled by a broad-based increase in demand from Asian subcontractors for 2.5D datacenter applications, renewed capacity purchases for photonics, and a substantial rise in hybrid bonding orders.

Profitability metrics also showcased meaningful sequential improvement during the fourth quarter. BESI reported a gross margin of 63.9%, which expanded by 1.7 percentage points sequentially due to a more favorable advanced packaging product mix. Net income climbed 69.2% sequentially to €42.8 million, benefiting from the higher revenue base, improved gross margins, and lower-than-anticipated operating expense growth. Consequently, the company's net margin expanded by 6.7 points sequentially to reach 25.7%.

Full Year 2025 Performance Overview

For the full year 2025, BESI's financial results reflected the contrasting dynamics of a booming AI infrastructure market offset by prolonged weakness in traditional segments. Total revenue for FY 2025 was €591.3 million, a modest 2.7% decline versus 2024. This slight contraction was attributed to broad-based weakness in mobile, automotive, and industrial end-user markets, which was only partially offset by the surge in revenue from Asian subcontractors for AI-related datacenter and photonics applications.

Despite the slight revenue dip, total orders for the year grew by 16.8% to €685.0 million. Order momentum heavily skewed toward the latter half of the year, with second-half bookings increasing 63.6% compared to the first half. Furthermore, AI applications became the dominant driver of BESI's business, representing approximately 50% of total orders in 2025. Revenue from the computing end-user market correspondingly grew to comprise 50% of total revenue, up from roughly 40% in 2024. Full-year net income stood at €131.6 million, declining 27.7% year-over-year primarily due to lower gross margins earlier in the year, higher net interest expenses, and an increased effective tax rate.

Q1 2026 Outlook and Market Sentiment

Management enters 2026 with increased optimism, driven by the strong order momentum established in late 2025 that has continued into the first quarter. For Q1 2026, BESI expects revenue to increase between 5% and 15% sequentially compared to the €166.4 million reported in Q4 2025. Gross margins are projected to remain strong, ranging between 63% and 65%. Operating expenses are anticipated to grow by 10% to 15% from the €50.0 million reported in the fourth quarter, primarily reflecting increased research and development spending to support long-term growth opportunities.

Morgan Stanley notes that while the Q4 2025 print and Q1 2026 guide were in-line with consensus estimates, the lack of positive revisions or immediate upside surprises resulted in a 7% drop in the company's share price following the announcement.

Morgan Stanley views this market reaction as a buying opportunity, emphasizing that the lack of near-term revenue upside stems from equipment lead times, as approximately €250 million in orders arrived late in the fourth quarter.

Furthermore, Morgan Stanley estimates that BESI has built a backlog exceeding €300 million, which positions the company for sharply accelerating revenue growth in the coming quarters as these AI-driven 2.5D and photonics orders translate into recognized sales.

Strategic Plan 2025-2029 & Operational Changes Upgraded Long-Term Financial Targets

To capitalize on the next semiconductor market upcycle and the accelerating adoption of advanced packaging, management has introduced an updated Strategic Plan for the 2025-2029 period. The centerpiece of this updated framework is a significantly upgraded long-term revenue target, which has been raised from the previous €1 billion baseline to a new range of €1.5 billion to €1.9 billion. This enhanced growth trajectory is expected to be fueled by Besi's leading market position in wafer-level assembly, specifically driven by AI-related 2.5D and 3D applications, hybrid bonding, and silicon photonics. Alongside the top-line expansion, the company has set ambitious profitability objectives, targeting gross margins between 64% and 68%. Operating margins are projected to reach between 40% and 55%, reflecting the inherent operational leverage in Besi's financial model as next-generation product shipments scale.

Structural Cost Reduction & Scalability ments

A critical component of the 2025-2029 Strategic Plan is the implementation of new structural cost reduction initiatives designed to further lower the company's break-even point and enhance through-cycle profitability. Management has identified opportunities to extract between €15 million and €30 million in structural cost savings over the planning period. These savings will be realized through several targeted efforts, including the realization of design efficiencies by utilizing common parts, platforms, and modules across equipment families. Furthermore, Besi is expanding its internal use of automation and artificial intelligence to improve operational productivity and reduce direct labor costs.

To support these margin targets, the company has continued to refine its highly scalable production model. Strategic initiatives have been executed to increase the flexibility of Besi's manufacturing footprint through the utilization of temporary Asian production personnel and the cultivation of localized, high-quality supply chain networks. By simplifying and harmonizing manufacturing and IT processes, Besi has successfully reduced material and overhead costs while accelerating delivery times and improving inventory turnover.

Asian Capacity Expansions and Realignments

In response to changing global supply chain dynamics and customer migration patterns, Besi has fundamentally reorganized its global operations by transferring substantially all European production and tooling capacity to its Asian facilities. This strategic realignment places production, sourcing, and process support closer to the core customer base, resulting in Asian personnel representing approximately 68% of the company's total fixed headcount at the end of 2025.

To accommodate the anticipated surge in demand for hybrid bonding and Thermo-Compression (TC) Next systems, Besi is executing significant capacity expansions across key Southeast Asian hubs. In Malaysia, the company is successfully doubling its cleanroom capacity to eliminate production bottlenecks for advanced wafer-level assembly systems. Simultaneously, Besi is significantly expanding its advanced packaging capabilities in Singapore, which serves as a central node for customer support, R&D, and process development. The Singapore facility now houses a dedicated customer application lab for hybrid bonding and chip-to-wafer technologies, facilitating closer collaboration with foundries and logic customers.

Vietnam has emerged as another critical growth pillar as semiconductor manufacturers increasingly diversify their footprints outside of mainland China. Besi is establishing a new tooling and support facility in Vietnam to directly support this geographic migration. Long-term, the company is considering a massive $1 billion investment in Vietnamese manufacturing infrastructure over the coming years. This initiative will commence with the construction of a new production facility in 2026, backed by an initial capital expenditure estimated at €10 million to €12 million. These synchronized expansions across Malaysia, Singapore, and Vietnam ensure that Besi possesses the necessary infrastructure to support its €1.5 - €1.9 billion revenue ambitions while maintaining optimal cost efficiency.

Market Dynamics & Technology Drivers (AI, Hybrid Bonding, Photonics) AI-Driven 2.5D Demand and OSAT Expansion

The global semiconductor industry is experiencing a profound architectural shift as AI workloads transition from generative models to agentic AI, driving unprecedented demand for both compute power and high-speed data transfer.

This massive expansion in AI infrastructure has created severe capacity bottlenecks in advanced packaging, particularly for 2.5D configurations.

To alleviate these constraints, leading foundries are increasingly outsourcing back-end assembly processes to outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) providers. Besi has capitalized heavily on this dynamic, experiencing a broad-based surge in bookings from Asian subcontractors for its flip-chip and multi-module die attach systems tailored for 2.5D datacenter applications.

Jefferies highlights that Taiwanese equipment vendors and local suppliers are gaining favor as foundries seek flexible and responsive partners to build out this critical OSAT capacity.

The Evolution of Silicon Photonics and Passive Alignment

As AI clusters scale, optical connectivity is replacing traditional copper to overcome bandwidth and power efficiency limitations. This transition is driving the adoption of co-packaged optics (CPO), which integrates optical engines directly alongside the ASIC and memory on a shared substrate. Historically, photonic assembly required active optical alignment, a slow and precise serial process utilizing specialized equipment outside of Besi's traditional product range.

The industry is now transitioning toward passive alignment, enabled by vertical grating couplers and fiducial marks that can be read by a standard die bonder's vision system. This innovation transforms fiber attachment from an optical alignment challenge into a precision die placement task, squarely fitting the 1-micron accuracy capabilities of Besi's EVO systems. Furthermore, advanced platforms like TSMC's Compact Universal Photonic Engine (COUPE) utilize Besi's hybrid bonding tools to stack electronic integrated circuits directly onto photonic integrated circuits.

Hybrid Bonding Trajectory and the HBM Thickness Debate

Hybrid bonding remains a cornerstone of the industry's roadmap for extending Moore's Law, allowing for ultra-fine pitch interconnects without the use of microbumps. While adoption in advanced logic devices is progressing rapidly, the timeline for hybrid bonding integration in High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) has sparked significant industry debate. Recent media reports indicate that the industry is considering relaxing the JEDEC standard for HBM thickness in future generations, potentially increasing the limit from 775 microns to 825-900 microns for 20-high stacks.

Morgan Stanley notes that easing these thickness constraints could extend the lifespan of existing Thermo-Compression Bonding (TCB) technologies, which initially caused a sharp pullback in Besi's share price as investors feared a delay in hybrid bonding orders. However, Morgan Stanley emphasizes that hybrid bonding provides critical performance benefits that physical thickness adjustments cannot replicate, including improved heat dissipation, superior signal integrity, and higher bit density. Furthermore, Morgan Stanley observes that custom HBM designs driven by hyperscalers often bypass standard physical specifications to achieve maximum performance, suggesting hybrid bonding will remain essential for high-end SKUs.

An Advanced IC Packaging R&D Engineer at Samsung Semiconductor echoed this sentiment, viewing hybrid bonding as an inevitable and necessary evolution for advanced memory packaging despite current yield and process challenges.

Corporate Governance & Capital Allocation Capital Return Programs: Dividends and Share Repurchases

Besi's capital allocation policy is structurally designed to optimize shareholder value through a balanced combination of cash dividends and share repurchases. Since 2011, the company has distributed approximately €2.4 billion to shareholders, a figure that represents roughly 33% of its total revenue generated over that period.

For the fiscal year 2025, the Board of Management has proposed a cash dividend of €1.58 per ordinary share. This proposed distribution represents a high dividend payout ratio of 95% relative to the company's net income, underscoring Besi's commitment to substantial capital returns despite the extended assembly market downturn. The 2025 dividend proposal is subject to final shareholder approval at the Annual General Meeting (AGM) scheduled for April 23, 2026.

In addition to its dividend policy, Besi actively manages its outstanding share count to offset dilution from employee stock plans and its convertible notes. After successfully completing a €100 million share repurchase program in October 2025, the company immediately initiated a new €60 million share repurchase program effective October 24, 2025. This new program is funded entirely through the company's available cash resources and is expected to be completed by October 2026.

Execution of this €60 million mandate has continued steadily into the first quarter of 2026. Recent company disclosures highlight multiple daily transactions conducted throughout January and February 2026, with block repurchases executed at average prices ranging broadly between €160 and €185 per share.

Supervisory Board Reappointments and Strategic Oversight

On the governance front, Besi's upcoming AGM on April 23, 2026, will feature the reappointment of two critical Supervisory Board members. The Supervisory Board has proposed reappointing Mr. Carlo Bozotti and Mr. Niek Hoek for new two-year terms ending immediately after the 2028 AGM.

Mr. Bozotti, who has served on the board since 2018 and acts as the Chair of the Remuneration Committee, is a highly experienced semiconductor executive and the former CEO of STMicroelectronics. Mr. Hoek, who was also appointed in 2018, serves as the Chair of the Audit Committee and brings extensive financial management and executive experience to the board.

The Supervisory Board has emphasized that retaining both Bozotti and Hoek is of decisive importance for the company's near-term future. Their continued oversight is viewed as essential for the successful implementation and governance of Besi's newly updated 2025-2029 strategic plan, ensuring continuity in leadership as the company scales its advanced packaging operations.
