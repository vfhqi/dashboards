THE CHANGE THESIS — WHAT HAS CHANGED?

The "Bravida Way" operational model is working. CEO Mattias Johansson's "margin over volume" strategy, articulated since Q1 2024, is now visibly delivering. The company has deliberately sacrificed top-line growth (-2.2% organic in FY2025) to maintain and expand margins. This is not market weakness masquerading as discipline; it is genuine management choice.

Denmark turnaround is the crown jewel evidence. From 0-1% EBITA trough (2022-2023) to 6.6% (Q4 2025) is a 560bp+ margin recovery. This is not accounting-driven; it reflects resolution of legacy loss-making projects, cost discipline, and a genuine shift toward service revenue (up to 59% of Danish revenue from 50% in prior year). New Danish CEO Christian Alsø (ex-ISS, May 2024) has been the execution engine.

New CFO Petra Vranjes signal. A CFO with Ericsson and Telia background — both capital-intensive, investor-relations-sophisticated businesses — signals that Bravida's board is prioritising financial discipline, investor communication, and capital allocation. Vranjes made an early share purchase post-appointment. This is an alignment signal.

Nordic construction recovery approaching inflection. H2 2026 is the base case for market recovery. Bravida's order intake +11% in Q4 2025 is the early signal. Sweden's LTV rule easing (April 2026: 85%→90%) and Riksbank at 1.75% provide the macro backdrop.

BEAR CASE

Revenue decline is -2.2% organic in FY2025. This is not a growth story yet. The margin improvements are real but earned by shrinking — rejecting low-margin work and focusing on service. If the market doesn't recover by H2 2026, Bravida faces a structural shrinkage problem.

Assemblin Caverion (ASCA) is now 1.5x larger by revenue. The merger creates a competitor with procurement advantages, scale, and geographic breadth that Bravida cannot easily match. Competitive pressure in the Nordics will intensify as ASCA completes integration.

EPS quality concerns. EPS +17% YoY in FY2025 against EBITDA +8-10% implies EPS growth is front-loaded by financial engineering (deleveraging, WC improvement). Structural EBITDA growth is more modest.
