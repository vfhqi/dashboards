SECTION 1 – GENERAL Description and Strategy

Delivery Hero operates as a global online food delivery and quick commerce platform, connecting millions of consumers with restaurants and local shops across more than 70 countries.

The company is strategically transitioning from a pure food delivery marketplace into an "Everyday App" that captures multi-vertical consumer needs including groceries, household essentials, and health products.

Organisationally, the group structures its reporting and operations across four primary geographic segments—Asia, MENA, Europe, and the Americas—alongside an Integrated Verticals segment dedicated to quick commerce (Dmarts).

The overarching strategic objective is to drive sustainable, profitable growth by leveraging network density, increasing order frequency through subscriptions, and expanding high-margin Advertising Technology (AdTech) revenues.

At the business level, Delivery Hero primarily generates revenue by charging partner restaurants commission fees based on a contractually specified percentage of the gross order value.

In addition to standard marketplace commissions, the company earns consumer delivery fees, product sales from its own grocery micro-fulfillment centers, and non-commission income like AdTech and subscription fees.

The Asia segment historically represented the largest portion of the business, operating under the Foodpanda and Baemin brands, though the region has faced intense competition and recent strategic divestments.

The MENA region, operating primarily under the Talabat brand, acts as the company's highest-margin profit engine, showcasing robust category leadership and consistent volume growth.

Delivery Hero's European operations have expanded significantly, heavily driven by the integration of Glovo, though they face complex regulatory environments regarding gig-economy labor classifications.
