Business Overview and Regulatory Framework (RP4)

Core Business Model and Operations

ENAV S.p.A. operates as Italy’s sole commercial Air Navigation Service Provider (ANSP), ensuring the safety, efficiency, and punctuality of air traffic across 751,742 square kilometers of controlled airspace. The company’s core regulated business is structurally divided into two primary service categories: en-route services and terminal services. En-route services involve the management of air traffic crossing Italian airspace, including overflights and national/international transit, operated from four Area Control Centers (ACCs) located in Brindisi, Milan, Padua, and Rome. Terminal services provide assistance during the critical phases of approach, takeoff, and landing, coordinated from control towers across 45 national civil airports. Revenues for these services are generated by charging airspace users a regulated tariff based on the volume of airspace used, measured conventionally in Service Units (SUs).

The RP4 Performance Plan (2025-2029)

ENAV’s operations are heavily regulated at the European Union level through the Single European Sky performance and charging scheme. In May 2025, the European Commission formally approved Italy's Performance Plan for the fourth Reference Period (RP4), which spans from 2025 to 2029. This approval establishes the economic, operational, and capacity targets that ENAV must meet over the five-year cycle, fully defining the chargeable cost base and tariff structures.

A major structural shift introduced in RP4 is the consolidation of terminal charging zones. Under the new framework, the previous three zones have been reduced to two. The former Zones 1 and 2 have been merged into a single new Zone 1, while the former Zone 3—which historically operated under a national cost-recovery scheme for lower-traffic airports—has been converted into the new Zone 2 and brought entirely under the EU performance and risk-sharing regulation. This regulatory evolution subjects all of ENAV's airport operations to the performance-based incentive mechanisms.

Tariff Setting and Revenue Stability Mechanisms

The EU regulatory framework provides significant protection against external shocks, ensuring high revenue visibility and financial stability through specific balance mechanisms. Tariffs are determined annually by taking the ratio between planned chargeable costs and planned traffic volumes (Service Units). When actual variables deviate from the plan, a "balance mechanism" compensates ENAV or refunds airlines through tariff adjustments in year N+2.

The framework mitigates risk across several distinct areas:

Traffic Risk Sharing: ENAV is insulated from severe traffic volatility through a symmetrical risk-sharing mechanism with airlines. If actual traffic deviates by up to +/- 2% from the plan, ENAV absorbs 100% of the upside or downside. For traffic deviations between 2% and 10%, the risk is shared, with ENAV absorbing 30% of the impact and airlines covering the remaining 70%. Any traffic drop exceeding 10% triggers full cost recovery, ensuring ENAV is completely shielded from catastrophic demand shocks.

Inflation Protection: EU regulations guarantee full protection against inflation discrepancies. ENAV fully recovers any variance between the actual inflation rate and the forecasted inflation rate built into the Performance Plan.

Cost Recovery: ENAV is granted full cost recovery for depreciation and amortization (CAPEX) and the actual cost of debt. Furthermore, any operational expenditure (OPEX) efficiencies achieved above the regulatory target are fully retained by the company.

Capacity Targets and Quality Performance

To incentivize high service quality, the RP4 framework includes a bonus/malus mechanism tied directly to capacity targets and flight delays. The European Commission establishes a strict cap on the average en-route air traffic flow delay per flight. For 2025, the regulator assigned ENAV a stringent target of 0.14 minutes of delay per assisted flight.

Demonstrating its position as a top-performing European ANSP, ENAV successfully managed record traffic volumes while achieving an average delay of just 0.010 minutes per flight. This significant outperformance of the 0.14-minute benchmark secured a "capacity bonus" for the company, contributing approximately €13 million in positive balance revenues for the year. This quality-driven incentive structure strongly aligns ENAV's operational excellence with continued value creation and revenue enhancement.

Earnings Performance and Fundamental Financial Trends

Revenue Dynamics and Service Unit Growth

ENAV's financial profile in 2025 demonstrated a robust underlying expansion in air traffic, successfully offsetting the expected regulatory headwinds associated with the start of the Reference Period 4 (RP4). The company reported total operating revenues of €1,173.1 million, representing a significant 11.2% year-over-year increase. This top-line momentum was directly fueled by record traffic volumes across Italian airspace, with en-route Service Units (SUs) growing by 5.9% and terminal SUs increasing by 3.4% compared to 2024. ENAV's en-route performance notably outpaced the broader European market, tracking higher than growth rates in Spain (+5.6%), the UK (+4.2%), Germany (+4.0%), and France (+3.6%).

Despite this underlying operational strength, total consolidated revenues officially printed at €1,024.7 million, a slight 1.2% decrease from 2024. This accounting contraction was entirely anticipated and was driven by the regulatory balance mechanisms typical of the first year of a new performance plan, alongside the inclusion of the former third terminal charging zone into the standard performance and risk-sharing scheme. Concurrently, ENAV continued to aggressively scale its non-regulated revenue streams, which reached €52.1 million in 2025, a 5.8% increase year-over-year. This non-regulated growth was supported by international contracts, including aeronautical consulting in Saudi Arabia, airspace modernization for the Bulgarian ANSP, and radar installations at military bases.

Balance Mechanisms and Capacity Bonus Achievements

The regulatory balance mechanism, which protects ENAV from severe traffic and inflation shocks, had a substantial net negative impact of €190.4 million on the 2025 income statement. This dynamic was primarily driven by the "N-2" reversal effect, which incorporated a negative €207.7 million into the 2025 unit rate. This large deduction represents the scheduled reimbursement to airlines of the significant positive balances that ENAV accrued during the pandemic-impacted 2020-2021 combined period.

Conversely, ENAV generated a positive period balance of €8.8 million for operations strictly relating to 2025. A critical driver of this positive generation was the company's exceptional quality of service. By maintaining an average en-route delay of just 0.010 minutes per assisted flight—drastically below the stringent 0.14-minute target set by the European Commission—ENAV unlocked a capacity bonus worth approximately €13 million. This bonus effectively insulated the company against a negative €5 million inflation balance, caused by actual inflation tracking lower than the rate embedded in the RP4 plan.

Cost Efficiency and EBITDA Margin Expansion

Total operating costs for the year increased by 6.4% to €772 million. Personnel expenses rose by 6.8% to €632.6 million, reflecting both fixed contractual adjustments (a 2% revaluation of minimum salaries and inflation differentials) and higher variable remuneration tied directly to the record volumes of assisted traffic. Other operating costs grew by 7.6%, driven by heightened maintenance requirements and increased Eurocontrol contributions.

Through proactive managerial cost control and internal efficiency initiatives, ENAV successfully reported an EBITDA of €252.7 million. While this figure is nominally down 18.7% from 2024 due to the regulatory balance reset, it successfully hit the upper boundary of the company's upgraded guidance range. This translated to a resilient EBITDA margin of 24.7%.

Net Income, Free Cash Flow, and Balance Sheet Leverage

ENAV achieved a consolidated Net Income of €93.1 million in 2025. This result vastly outperformed the initial 2025-2029 Strategic Plan target of €64 million, underscoring the company's ability to drive profitability even amid regulatory resets.

The company's cash conversion profile remained exceptional, producing €263.6 million in Free Cash Flow for the year. This robust liquidity generation was €64.5 million higher than the previous year, fueled by strong core business collections and the receipt of €11.8 million in EU-backed PNRR (National Recovery and Resilience Plan) funds.

This superior cash generation drove a rapid deleveraging of the balance sheet. Net financial debt was slashed to €137.4 million by year-end, down drastically from €258.3 million at the close of 2024. As a result, ENAV's Net Debt-to-EBITDA leverage ratio fell to an ultra-conservative 0.5x. The company maintains massive liquidity headroom, supported by €451.9 million in cash equivalents, €203 million in undrawn credit lines, and an €80 million commitment from the European Investment Bank (EIB).

d Shareholder Remuneration (DPS Curve)

Capitalizing on its strong cash flow visibility and low leverage, ENAV management structurally upgraded its shareholder remuneration profile. The Board of Directors proposed a dividend of €0.29 per share for the 2025 financial year. Furthermore, the company upgraded its forward-looking Dividend Per Share (DPS) curve, raising the 2026 payout commitment to €0.30 per share, an increase over the initial strategic plan projections. This revised payout trajectory directly reflects the company's operational outperformance and high confidence in sustainable cash distributions through the RP4 cycle.

Strategic Initiatives and Non-Regulated Business Expansion

The 2025-2029 Strategic Roadmap and Commercial Diversification

The 2025-2029 Business Plan establishes a dual-track strategy focusing on continuous regulatory excellence and accelerated expansion into the non-regulated market. ENAV aims to rapidly scale its commercial footprint, targeting a doubling of non-regulated revenues to €106 million by 2029. To support this aggressive growth trajectory, the company has designated up to €350 million in balance sheet headroom specifically for selective M&A activities.

Through its subsidiary Techno Sky, ENAV monetizes its operational maintenance and engineering expertise both domestically and abroad. A flagship achievement in this segment is the contract to implement an advanced Remote Digital and Virtual Tower (RDVT) at Senai Airport in Johor Bahru, Malaysia. This project replaces a traditional control tower with high-definition digital operations, successfully showcasing ENAV's proprietary digital tower technology in the Far East. Domestically, Techno Sky also secured a highly lucrative €36.6 million contract with Teledife to modernize radar surveillance systems across six Italian Air Force bases through 2032.

Software sales and international consultancy represent another critical growth vector. IDS AirNav drives the commercialization of aeronautical software, particularly its Aeronautical Information Management (AIM) suites and the CRONOS platform. Recent contract wins include extensive system modernizations for air navigation service providers in Austria, Bulgaria, Liberia, and Egypt. ENAV is also aggressively expanding its high-margin aeronautical consultancy business, notably securing strategic contracts to redesign and optimize national airspace in Saudi Arabia and Brunei.

In the rapidly growing Unmanned Traffic Management (UTM) sector, ENAV's subsidiary D-Flight holds dual certification from the Italian aviation authority (ENAC) as both a Common Information Service Provider (CISP) and a U-Space Service Provider (USSP). To consolidate its drone ecosystem capabilities, ENAV recently acquired an 85% stake in the AIView Group for an enterprise value of approximately €10.5 million. This acquisition directly integrates advanced artificial intelligence and data analytics into ENAV's drone-based infrastructure monitoring services.

The strategic plan also introduces two entirely new, non-regulated business lines: an Energy Service Company (ESCO) offering and the Digital Academy. The ESCO division will transform ENAV's internal energy optimization practices into turnkey advisory services for third-party airports, while the Digital Academy will provide proprietary e-learning and advanced aviation management training to external commercial customers.

Internal Technological Upgrades and Airspace Modernization

Internally, ENAV has committed roughly €570 million in capital expenditures through 2029 to fundamentally modernize its air traffic control infrastructure. The centerpiece of this operational transformation is the 4-Flight system, a next-generation Air Traffic Management platform based on SESAR concepts and the Coflight engine. Full deployment of the 4-Flight system is scheduled for the Rome Area Control Center (ACC) by 2028 and the Milan ACC by 2030. Concurrent with this software upgrade, ENAV is structurally consolidating its airspace management from four ACCs down to two, while systematically transitioning 26 traditional airport control towers to remote digital operations.

On the airspace efficiency front, ENAV continues to lead Europe in the deployment of Free Route Airspace (FRA-IT). In March 2024, the company successfully lowered the FRA-IT minimum altitude from 9,000 meters to 6,500 meters, allowing domestic and medium-range flights to plan direct, unconstrained trajectories. This initiative is augmented by the integration of cross-border operations (SECSI-FRA) with Balkan providers, further removing operational bottlenecks across European borders.

ESG Milestones and Climate Leadership

These route optimizations directly support ENAV's aggressive climate targets and ESG commitments. The Free Route and Arrival Manager (AMAN) systems are highly effective environmental tools, enabling a reduction of approximately 331,700 tons of CO2 from managed airline traffic in 2025 alone. Operationally, ENAV has successfully maintained Carbon Neutrality for its direct footprint since 2022 and has formally committed to a 92% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2029 versus its 2019 baseline. Reflecting this sustained leadership in sustainable aviation and technological modernization, the company continues to be recognized on the prestigious CDP "A List" for climate change disclosure.

Industry Positioning and Peer Comparison

Traffic Volume Recovery and Growth Trajectories

ENAV SpA continues to establish itself as a clear leader in traffic volume recovery and growth among major European Air Navigation Service Providers (ANSPs). In 2025, ENAV achieved a 5.9% year-over-year increase in en-route Service Units (SUs), managing a record 2.4 million flights across Italian airspace. This performance materially outpaced peer averages, including the United Kingdom's NATS (+4.2%), Germany's DFS (+4.0%), and France (+3.6%). By comparison, DFS Deutsche Flugsicherung handles approximately 3 million flights annually during peak years, representing one of the busiest and most complex airspaces in Europe. Meanwhile, Switzerland's Skyguide safely guided over 868,000 flights by the late summer of 2025, successfully returning its traffic to pre-crisis levels.

Delay, Punctuality, and Operational Reliability

When evaluating operational efficiency and punctuality, ENAV operates at a distinct advantage compared to its northern European counterparts. For 2025, ENAV recorded an average en-route delay of just 0.010 minutes per flight, dramatically outperforming the stringent European Commission target of 0.14 minutes. Skyguide reported a commendable punctuality rate of approximately 96% during the busy 2025 summer season, though it continues to grapple with localized delays driven by weather events and air traffic controller (ATCO) staff shortages. In the United Kingdom, NATS has actively deployed technological solutions like "Pairwise separation" at Heathrow to reduce average arrival delays by nearly 20%. However, NATS also experienced significant operational disruptions in recent years, including a major technical glitch in August 2023 that led to widespread flight cancellations and heavy delays across the UK. DFS has faced unique operational hurdles in Germany, including a sharp rise in hybrid attacks and drone incursions that forced temporary operational halts at major hubs like Munich and Berlin Brandenburg in late 2024.

Technological Deployment and Airspace Attractiveness

ENAV leverages technological leadership to make Italian airspace highly attractive to airlines, directly supporting its outsized traffic growth. ENAV is the only European service provider to lower the Free Route Airspace minimum altitude across its entire national territory from 9,000 meters to 6,500 meters, completing this nearly two years ahead of the EU's December 2025 deadline. This provides immense flexibility for airspace users, cutting fuel burn and reducing CO2 emissions by approximately 331,700 tons in 2025 alone. NATS has also made strides in this area, recently expanding its own free route airspace across Scottish upper airspace to save an estimated 17,000 tonnes of CO2 annually.

Skyguide is heavily investing in modernizing its infrastructure through the Virtual Centre Programme, aiming to transition to location-independent ATM operations between Zurich and Geneva by the end of 2027. To support this, Skyguide partnered with Thales to implement the OpenSky platform, migrating mission-critical systems to an open, modular architecture. Similarly, DFS is focusing on system resilience by deploying the PHOENIX nG cloud-native platform at the Munich Area Control Center in collaboration with Frequentis, marking the first operational deployment of Europe's new ATM Master Plan service delivery model.

Success in Non-Regulated Revenue Diversification

While the core regulated business dominates all ANSP financial profiles, ENAV and DFS are actively expanding their non-regulated commercial footprints. ENAV successfully grew its non-regulated revenues to €52.1 million in 2025, driven by software sales, remote tower deployments in Malaysia, and extensive consulting contracts in Saudi Arabia and the broader Middle East. DFS pursues a parallel strategy through its subsidiary DFS Aviation Services (DAS), which markets ATC technology globally. DAS recently secured contracts to supply advanced air traffic control systems to Dubai Air Navigation Services and is consulting on the redesign of terminal maneuvering areas at Athens International Airport. While NATS also operates a commercial arm pursuing international growth, ENAV's integrated approach—combining ATM software (IDS AirNav), maintenance (Techno Sky), and advanced drone/UTM capabilities (D-Flight)—positions it uniquely to capture a diverse array of global aviation modernization budgets.
