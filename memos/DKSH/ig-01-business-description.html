Company Overview and Services

The Market Expansion Services (MES) Model

DKSH Holding AG is a leading provider of Market Expansion Services (MES), primarily focused on facilitating business growth across the Asia-Pacific region, Europe, and North America. With a corporate history spanning over 160 years, the company operates in 35 markets and employs approximately 26,840 specialists. DKSH generated net sales of CHF 11.1 billion in 2025 by acting as a strategic link between global brand owners and local customers. The company's core purpose is to enrich people's lives through the reliable supply of essential products, healthcare solutions, and technological innovations.

The Market Expansion Services model offers integrated, one-stop-shop solutions for companies seeking to enter new markets, scale existing operations, or optimize their supply chains. Rather than merely acting as a traditional distributor, DKSH manages highly complex value chains by offering customized combinations of specialized services. Clients can choose to outsource specific segments of their operations or hand over their entire route-to-market strategy. This structure allows global manufacturers to focus on their core competencies, such as research and development, while DKSH navigates local market complexities. The company delivers these tailored solutions across four highly specialized Business Units: Healthcare, Consumer Goods, Performance Materials, and Technology.

Business Model and Value Proposition

Revenue Generation and Fee Structures

DKSH operates a highly scalable business model that generates revenue through a tiered structure of specialized services, ranging from basic logistics to comprehensive commercial outsourcing. The company’s fee structure and margin profile are directly correlated with the complexity and depth of the services provided to its partners. At the foundational level, pure distribution and third-party logistics (3PL) or fourth-party logistics (4PL) services typically yield a Core EBIT margin of 1.0% to 2.0%. When clients opt for more integrated solutions, such as Commercial Outsourcing—which encompasses full agency representation, contract sales organizations (CSO), and contract sales and marketing organizations (CSMO)—the margin profile expands significantly to between 5.0% and 7.0%.

Value-Added Services Versus Pure Distribution

While traditional distribution focuses strictly on the physical movement and warehousing of goods, DKSH differentiates itself by embedding high-margin, value-added services throughout the commercial lifecycle. Pure distribution tasks such as transportation, order fulfillment, and basic inventory management serve as the operational backbone. However, the company's true value proposition lies in services that actively drive top-line growth and market share expansion for its clients.

A prime example is DKSH's regulatory consultancy, where specialized affairs experts manage complex product dossiers, secure marketing authorizations, and maintain critical liaisons with local health and trade authorities. This service drastically reduces the time-to-market for international brands entering stringent Asian regulatory environments. Additionally, the company provides robust field activation and trade marketing services, deploying thousands of representatives to optimize retail execution, improve shelf visibility, and conduct in-store promotions.

DKSH has also heavily invested in digital market expansion as a core value-added service. The proprietary DKSH Discover platform functions as a cloud-based, AI-supported ecosystem that connects clients and customers, generating thousands of qualified leads and facilitating seamless omni-channel sales. In the industrial and technical sectors, DKSH further separates itself from pure distributors through extensive application engineering and after-sales support. The company operates numerous innovation centers and laboratories across Asia, allowing it to develop customized product formulations, conduct validation testing, and provide ongoing maintenance and training for capital equipment.

The Asset-Light Operating Model

A defining characteristic of DKSH's financial resilience and agility is its strict adherence to a low-risk, asset-light operating model. Rather than committing capital to heavy infrastructure, the company primarily utilizes leased office spaces, leased distribution centers, and leased transportation fleets. When transportation is not leased directly, DKSH frequently outsources logistics to specialized third-party providers, further reducing fixed asset burdens. This philosophy extends to the company's technology infrastructure, where IT investments have transitioned almost entirely to a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) operational expenditure model rather than capital-intensive hardware deployments.

Because of this structural design, DKSH maintains exceptionally low capital expenditure requirements. Over the past five years, the company's CapEx has consistently ranged between just 0.3% and 0.5% of total net sales. This lean capital requirement is complemented by highly disciplined working capital management, characterized by efficient cash collection processes and just-in-time inventory optimization. As a result, working capital has historically been maintained below 9.0% of annualized net sales.

The combination of low CapEx and optimized working capital directly translates into superior cash generation. DKSH reliably achieves a cash conversion rate—defined as free cash flow relative to profit after tax—well above its stated target of 90.0%. In recent periods, this figure has frequently exceeded 100.0%, such as the 113.6% conversion achieved in 2024. This immense cash-generative capacity inherently funds the company's capital allocation strategy, allowing DKSH to execute numerous bolt-on acquisitions and continuously grow its progressive ordinary dividend entirely from internally generated cash flow. Ultimately, this asset-light framework yields an exceptionally strong Return on Net Operating Capital (Core RONOC), which consistently tracks near 20.0%, underscoring the high-value nature of the Market Expansion Services platform.

Business Units Deep-Dive

DKSH organizes its global operations into four highly specialized business units: Healthcare, Consumer Goods, Performance Materials, and Technology. This structure mirrors the group's core fields of expertise and allows the company to tailor its Market Expansion Services strictly to the distinct regulatory, operational, and commercial requirements of each industry. While Healthcare and Consumer Goods represent the vast majority of the company's top-line revenue, Performance Materials acts as a critical profit engine due to its structurally higher margin profile.

Breakdown of 2025 Net Sales by Business Unit

Healthcare

The Healthcare business unit is DKSH’s largest and most significant division, accounting for 52.6% of the group's net sales and 42.8% of its Core EBIT in 2025. The division provides a comprehensive suite of services for ethical pharmaceuticals, consumer health items, over-the-counter (OTC) products, and medical devices. Its custom-made offerings span the entire commercial life cycle, including product registration, regulatory consultancy, market entry studies, physical distribution, and cash collection.

Financially, the division has consistently delivered above-GDP growth, with net sales reaching CHF 5.8 billion in 2025, representing an organic growth rate of 4.6% at constant exchange rates (CER). Core EBIT outpaced top-line expansion, growing 7.5% at CER to reach CHF 174.2 million. This robust bottom-line performance drove a 10 basis point improvement in the Core EBIT margin to 3.0%. The margin expansion marks the division's fourth consecutive year of profitability improvement, driven by an intentional shift toward higher-value commercial outsourcing services and a strong focus on DKSH's Own Brands portfolio, which generates margins exceeding 20.0%. Looking forward, under the leadership of newly appointed Head of Healthcare Patrik Grande, the segment is aggressively pursuing new business development, having recently secured major partnerships with global leaders such as Bayer, Eli Lilly, and Reckitt.

Consumer Goods

Operating as Asia’s leading Market Expansion Services provider for fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG), food services, and luxury and lifestyle products, the Consumer Goods division constitutes 30.5% of DKSH's total net sales. The division operates 42 dedicated distribution centers across the Asia-Pacific region, bringing thousands of global and local brands to retail shelves, hospitality venues, and boutiques.

In 2025, Consumer Goods generated net sales of CHF 3.4 billion, equating to a 1.2% growth rate at CER. While top-line growth was modest for the full year, the division experienced a marked acceleration in the second half of 2025, reaching 2.8% growth at CER fueled by strong consumption in Malaysia, Vietnam, and Singapore. Core EBIT for the division reached CHF 89.7 million, increasing by 5.4% at CER. This earnings growth resulted in a roughly 10 basis point margin expansion to 2.7% for the full year, successfully exceeding management's strategic target of 2.5% established in recent years. Margin improvements were heavily supported by the accretive 2025 acquisition of Zircon-Swis Fine Foods, which bolstered the higher-margin food service segment, alongside new client wins including Nestlé and Del Monte.

Performance Materials

The Performance Materials segment functions as a global distributor and developer of specialty chemicals and ingredients tailored for the food and beverage, pharmaceutical, personal care, and industrial sectors. Distinct from the Asia-centric focus of the other divisions, Performance Materials maintains a deep geographic footprint across Europe and North America alongside its Asian operations. A defining feature of this segment is its network of 55 state-of-the-art innovation centers, where DKSH application engineers develop cutting-edge, customized formulations that help clients reduce time-to-market.

Despite accounting for only 12.3% of total group sales, Performance Materials is DKSH's most profitable division by margin, contributing a massive 27.3% of the group's total Core EBIT. In 2025, the division reported net sales of CHF 1.4 billion, demonstrating highly resilient growth of 1.4% at CER amidst a broadly declining global chemical market. The Asia-Pacific region, which generates 60.0% of the unit's sales, significantly outperformed with 5.5% growth at CER. Core EBIT advanced 1.9% at CER to CHF 111.0 million, resulting in an exceptional Core EBIT margin of 8.2%. Strong pricing discipline and a robust M&A strategy—including the 2025 acquisitions of AIC Ingredients in Malaysia and Aston Chemicals in Europe—solidified the division's premium margin profile.

Technology

The Technology business unit is the most specialized and smallest of DKSH's divisions, representing 4.6% of group net sales and 7.9% of Core EBIT. It provides total solutions for capital investment goods, scientific instruments, and precision machinery. Services range from initial market strategy to heavy after-sales support, encompassing installation, commissioning, operator training, and the ongoing supply of critical spare parts and consumables.

Operating against a challenging macroeconomic backdrop characterized by delayed corporate capital expenditures and short-term uncertainty, the Technology division delivered resilient results in 2025. Net sales contracted slightly by 1.4% at CER to CHF 513.5 million, while Core EBIT stood at CHF 32.0 million, representing a 3.1% decline at CER. Consequently, the division operated with a healthy Core EBIT margin of 6.2%. Throughout 2025, DKSH executed a deliberate portfolio transformation within this unit, divesting lower-margin cable businesses in Australia and Taiwan to focus capital on higher-value segments. This included completing five strategic acquisitions in the Scientific Solutions space, adding roughly CHF 50.0 million in annualized net sales and positioning the unit to capture future market consolidation opportunities across the Asia-Pacific region.

Geographic Footprint

DKSH Holding AG operates a highly expansive and deeply entrenched geographic network, maintaining a physical presence across 35 markets globally. While the company selectively serves Europe and North America—primarily through its Performance Materials division—its core identity and operational gravity are overwhelmingly concentrated in the Asia-Pacific region. By leveraging a vast infrastructure and deep local market knowledge, DKSH provides global brand owners with minimized risk and rapid scale across some of the world's most complex commercial environments. In 2025, the group generated total net sales of CHF 11.1 billion, with the vast majority originating from a diversified mix of established and high-growth Asian economies.

Customer Base and Network Stickiness

Client Profile and Scale

DKSH functions as a critical strategic partner for a vast and highly diversified customer base, ranging from local small-to-medium enterprises to major multinational and Fortune 500 companies. The company’s client portfolio is extensive across all operating divisions, heavily weighted toward global brand owners and manufacturers seeking seamless market entry and expansion in Asia. Within the Consumer Goods business unit alone, DKSH represents over 850 global, regional, and local clients. Recent business development in this segment has secured partnerships with prominent fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) leaders such as Nestlé and Del Monte.

The Healthcare division similarly operates at massive scale, representing 650 clients and acting as the commercialization partner of choice for some of the world's most significant pharmaceutical and medical device manufacturers. Key global healthcare partners tightly integrated into DKSH's distribution and marketing infrastructure include Sanofi, Bayer, Pfizer, Eli Lilly, and Reckitt. The Technology business unit supports an additional 800 clients and suppliers, while the Performance Materials division maintains deep relationships with specialty chemical leaders like Synthomer and Kronos.

Customer Concentration and Switching Costs

While explicit quantitative metrics regarding corporate-level customer concentration (such as the exact percentage of revenue derived from the top ten clients) and specific financial switching cost penalties are not publicly disclosed in the available research, regional dynamics indicate that individual partnerships can scale significantly; for instance, Sheng Siong has grown to become DKSH's second-largest customer in Singapore following highly integrated omnichannel initiatives. The lack of extreme top-level concentration risk is mitigated by the sheer volume of brand partners—totaling well over 2,000 across the enterprise—and a broad geographic dispersion.

Although specific contractual penalties for terminating services are not detailed, the operational and strategic switching costs for DKSH's clients are structurally profound. Moving away from DKSH's platform forces global manufacturers to either build their own complex, capital-intensive local infrastructure or piece together a fragmented network of smaller, less capable local distributors. Furthermore, DKSH embeds itself deeply into client operations through regulatory consultancy, actively managing product dossiers and holding marketing authorizations on behalf of clients in strictly regulated markets. Severing this relationship poses immense risk to a brand's regulatory compliance and speed-to-market.

Technological integration acts as another significant barrier to exit. DKSH operates one of the largest SAP platforms in Asia, utilizing tailor-made in-house IT solutions to directly connect its business partners' systems with its own. This digital tethering, combined with access to proprietary market insight platforms and thousands of embedded field marketing representatives, makes the unwinding process operationally disruptive and financially prohibitive for global brands.

Network Stickiness and Contract Lengths

The culmination of these high operational switching costs and DKSH's ability to consistently deliver above-market growth results in exceptionally strong network stickiness. On average, clients maintain their relationship with DKSH for more than ten years. Over this extended lifecycle, brand owners typically expand their engagements beyond initial market entry, utilizing DKSH's services across an average of four different Asian markets. This long-term retention is fundamentally driven by the company's service-oriented business model, which allows global partners to outsource end-to-end complexity while minimizing their own operational risk and fixed capital investments in foreign jurisdictions.

Competitive Position and Moat

Structural Barriers to Entry

DKSH's competitive position is fundamentally anchored by its 160-year corporate history and its deep integration into the commercial ecosystem of the Asia-Pacific region. As a dominant Market Expansion Services (MES) provider, the company leverages massive economies of scale, cross-regional synergies, and significant bargaining power with trade channels to create a highly defensible platform. The MES industry inherently presents formidable barriers to entry, which DKSH actively reinforces through its expansive physical footprint and operational excellence.

Management explicitly identifies the company's extensive capillary distribution network, massive field sales force, and highly efficient cash collection processes as the primary structural moats protecting the enterprise. Replicating this localized physical infrastructure across 35 distinct markets would require prohibitive capital investments and decades of relationship-building. Furthermore, DKSH operates specialized facilities that comply with the most stringent international quality and regulatory standards, including Good Distribution Practice (GDP), Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP), PIC/S, and multiple ISO certifications. Navigating these complex, highly localized regulatory environments acts as a severe deterrent to new market entrants and cements DKSH's role as an indispensable strategic partner.

Network Effects and Technological Defensibility

Beyond physical logistics, DKSH commands an unparalleled direct presence across a vast array of customer touchpoints, spanning both modern and traditional trade channels. This capability ensures deep penetration into hypermarkets, convenience chains, fragmented mom-and-pop shops, and specialized medical facilities. Because the business of Asian market expansion relies heavily on trust and enduring localized partnerships, companies that have spent decades cultivating these relationships possess a distinct and enduring competitive advantage. DKSH further capitalizes on its scale by cross-selling tailored solutions across its divisions, such as leveraging its Food and Beverage Ingredients portfolio to create entirely new commercial pathways for clients in the bakery and plant-based sectors.

Technology serves as another massive differentiator that compounds DKSH's structural moat. The company operates one of the largest SAP platforms in Asia, utilizing tailor-made in-house IT solutions to seamlessly connect its operations with the enterprise systems of its partners. DKSH is aggressively widening this gap by deploying advanced AI initiatives across its business units to optimize pricing strategies, forecast demand, and execute sophisticated inventory management. The integration of AI into fulfillment routing and credit limit advisory further drives operational efficiencies and risk mitigation. Ultimately, these heavy technological investments elevate the barriers to entry, granting large-scale distributors a permanent and widening competitive edge over smaller, less capitalized regional players.

Competitive Landscape and Peer Differentiation

The competitive landscape varies significantly depending on the specific industry and business unit in question. Within the Consumer Goods and Healthcare divisions, the primary alternatives to DKSH are typically either highly fragmented, single-market local distributors or a brand's internal decision to construct its own captive distribution infrastructure. By offering an integrated, pan-Asian solution, DKSH allows global brands to bypass these disjointed local networks and scale rapidly without assuming fixed capital risks in foreign jurisdictions.

In the Performance Materials segment, the competitive landscape features established global specialty chemical distributors, notably including IMCD and Azelis. However, DKSH differentiates itself fundamentally from these global peers through its extreme geographic concentration in Eastern markets. While competitors are often more heavily weighted toward North America and Europe, roughly two-thirds of DKSH's Performance Materials business is generated entirely within Asia. This distinct Asian dominance is fortified by deep historical connections with major Chinese and Indian suppliers. Consequently, DKSH is uniquely positioned to navigate supply chain shifts and capitalize on the surge of Asian chemical exports with far greater agility than Western-centric distributors who may be locked into rigid, geography-specific supply constraints.

Financial Profile

Revenue Scale and Growth Trajectory

DKSH Holding AG operates at a massive financial scale, generating consistent top-line expansion despite recurring macroeconomic and geopolitical headwinds. For the full year 2025, the company reported total net sales of CHF 11.1 billion, representing a robust 2.9% growth rate at constant exchange rates (CER). This top-line performance was primarily driven by organic growth, which contributed 2.5% to the total, while strategic acquisitions added a further 0.4%. Although currency translation effects—specifically the appreciation of the Swiss franc—exerted a negative 3.1% impact on reported net sales, the underlying operational momentum remained highly resilient.

Taking a longer-term perspective, DKSH has successfully established a multi-year trajectory of outperforming the underlying economic growth of its core markets. Over the trailing five-year period from 2021 to 2025, the company delivered a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.2% at constant exchange rates. This sustained top-line acceleration is fundamentally supported by the company's diversified presence across multiple high-growth Asian economies, a resilient portfolio weighted heavily toward daily consumption items, and highly sticky commercial partnerships. Furthermore, management successfully drove a marked acceleration in the second half of 2025, where net sales growth stepped up to 3.6% at CER, firmly outpacing the weighted average GDP growth of the region.

Group Profitability and Divisional Margins

Profitability expansion remains a cornerstone of DKSH's financial profile, driven by strict cost discipline, economies of scale, and an intentional portfolio shift toward higher-margin, value-added services. In 2025, the group's Core EBIT reached a record CHF 349.0 million, representing a 6.7% increase at CER. Because operating profit grew at more than twice the rate of net sales, the consolidated Core EBIT margin expanded by roughly 10 basis points to 3.2%. This milestone marked the fifth consecutive year of sequential Core EBIT margin improvement for the enterprise.

The Capital-Light Operating Model

The financial agility of DKSH is rooted in its strict adherence to a low-risk, asset-light operating model. Rather than tying up capital in heavy infrastructure, the company primarily operates through leased office spaces, leased distribution centers, and leased transportation fleets. As a direct result of this structural design, the company's capital expenditure (CapEx) requirements are exceptionally low. In 2025, total CapEx amounted to just CHF 38.2 million, representing a mere 0.3% of total net sales. This extremely lean ratio of 0.3% to 0.5% of net sales has been consistently maintained over the past five comparative periods.

This low capital intensity is paired with highly sophisticated working capital management. By leveraging advanced digital tools and localized field teams, the company enforces timely cash collection from its fragmented customer base while maintaining highly optimized, just-in-time inventory levels. These operational efficiencies translated into a remarkably high Return on Net Operating Capital (Core RONOC) of 19.7% and a Core Return on Equity (ROE) of 12.4% for the 2025 fiscal year.

Exceptional Cash Generation and Capital Allocation

The ultimate byproduct of expanding margins, low CapEx, and optimized working capital is highly predictable and immense free cash flow generation. In 2025, DKSH generated CHF 215.5 million in free cash flow. This equated to a cash conversion rate (free cash flow as a percentage of core profit after tax) of 95.2%. This stellar result marked the sixth consecutive year that DKSH successfully exceeded its stated corporate target of achieving at least a 90.0% cash conversion rate.

This self-funding mechanism provides management with vast optionality for capital allocation, allowing the firm to execute its strategic objectives entirely from internally generated liquidity. During 2025, the robust cash flow funded nine accretive bolt-on acquisitions and allowed the company to further optimize its leverage profile. Consequently, DKSH concluded the year with a highly conservative balance sheet, characterized by an equity ratio of 33.1% and a net cash position of CHF 25.2 million. Furthermore, this cash generation engine underpins a highly reliable and progressive dividend policy. For the 2025 period, the Board of Directors proposed a 6.4% increase in the ordinary dividend to CHF 2.50 per share, marking DKSH's 13th consecutive year of uninterrupted dividend growth.

Strategic Direction and Margin Expansion

Under the current leadership team, DKSH has pursued a disciplined, multi-year strategy focused on profitable growth, margin expansion, and cash conversion. Management's core objective is to steadily pivot the business mix away from pure, lower-margin distribution services toward higher-value commercial outsourcing, regulatory consultancy, and the company's highly profitable Own Brands portfolio. This intentional mix shift is a primary driver behind the company's mid-term goal to expand its Core EBIT margin by an average of at least 10 basis points annually. The successful execution of this objective was evident in 2025, marking the fifth consecutive year of Core EBIT margin increases for the group.

To further support margin expansion and sales force productivity, management is actively integrating artificial intelligence and advanced digital tools across the enterprise. The company's AI initiatives are broadly focused on demand forecasting, pricing optimization, dynamic credit limit advisory, and enhanced supply chain routing. These digital investments are designed to permanently elevate the structural barriers to entry within the Asian distribution landscape, granting large-scale operators like DKSH a distinct competitive edge over smaller regional peers in optimizing the cost-to-serve.

Accelerated Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)

A central pillar of DKSH's corporate strategy is its role as a primary consolidator within the highly fragmented Market Expansion Services industry. Over the past six years, the company has dramatically accelerated its M&A tempo, completing 35 acquisitions between 2019 and 2025 compared to just 16 in the prior seven-year period. Management deliberately targets bolt-on acquisitions that immediately build scale in higher-margin segments, with over 80.0% of recent M&A spending allocated to structurally more profitable business areas.

In 2025 alone, DKSH announced nine strategic transactions funded entirely by internal free cash flow, preserving the company's robust balance sheet flexibility. Notably, five of these acquisitions were concentrated in the Scientific Solutions segment of the Technology division—including the purchases of CLMO, Taqkey Science, Quantum Biotech, MDxK, and Biomedic—which collectively added approximately CHF 50.0 million in annualized net sales. In the Performance Materials division, DKSH executed the strategic acquisition of Malaysia-based AIC Ingredients to materially expand its food formulation and blending capabilities in the region. Looking ahead, management retains significant leverage headroom, targeting up to roughly 2.0x Net Debt-to-EBITDA, providing ample capacity to pursue a robust pipeline of consolidation opportunities across Asia-Pacific and beyond.

Key Investment Considerations

DKSH Holding AG presents a highly compelling investment profile for capturing the structural expansion of Asian economies without assuming the localized operational risks typically associated with direct emerging market exposure. The core bull case for owning the stock centers on its position as an indispensable proxy for Asian consumption and the continent's rapidly modernizing commercial infrastructure. By operating as the vital link between global manufacturers and fragmented local markets, the company leverages powerful macroeconomic tailwinds, high barriers to entry, and a heavily cash-generative financial model.

The Premier Proxy for Asian Consumption and Demographics

Investing in DKSH offers uniquely diversified exposure to the secular growth of the Asian middle class and rising regional consumption. Macroeconomic forecasts indicate that by 2030, approximately two-thirds of the world's middle class will reside in Asia, driving immense demand for daily consumer goods, specialty chemicals, and advanced technology. Furthermore, the region's resilient economic baseline is highlighted by an expected gross domestic product (GDP) growth rate of 4.6% in 2026, outpacing most Western markets.

Beyond general consumption, DKSH is heavily levered to the structural megatrend of Asia's aging population. This demographic shift is severely increasing the regional burden of chronic diseases and driving an unprecedented surge in healthcare expenditure. Because DKSH's Healthcare division generates over half of the group's revenue, the company acts as a direct beneficiary of this rising demand for essential medicines, medical devices, and modernized clinical infrastructure. By providing localized market access to global pharmaceutical leaders, DKSH captures this healthcare spending while insulating investors from the binary risks of traditional drug development.

Indispensable Infrastructure and High Barriers to Entry

The physical and regulatory distribution infrastructure that DKSH has constructed across 35 markets forms an exceptionally deep economic moat. Establishing a pan-Asian commercial footprint requires decades of relationship-building, massive upfront capital, and the navigation of highly complex local regulatory frameworks. Rather than undertaking this prohibitive and risky endeavor, global brand owners actively choose to outsource their route-to-market strategies to DKSH's existing capillary network.

An Asset-Light, Cash-Generative Compounding Engine

The financial architecture of DKSH is designed to maximize capital efficiency and consistently generate immense shareholder value. Because the company primarily utilizes leased distribution centers and outsourced logistics, it maintains an exceptionally low capital intensity. Capital expenditures routinely consume a mere 0.3% to 0.5% of total net sales. When combined with highly disciplined working capital management and robust cash collection processes, this asset-light framework results in massive free cash flow generation.

The company reliably exceeds its stated mid-term target of converting at least 90.0% of its profit after tax into free cash flow, frequently surpassing the 100.0% mark in recent operational periods. This self-funding mechanism allows management to execute strategic growth initiatives entirely from internally generated liquidity. Furthermore, this immense cash flow directly supports a highly attractive and progressive dividend policy. DKSH has cultivated a multi-year track record of returning capital to shareholders, recently proposing its 13th consecutive ordinary dividend increase to CHF 2.50 per share for the 2025 fiscal year.

A Primary Consolidator in a Fragmented Market

Investors also benefit from DKSH's role as a proactive consolidator within the highly fragmented Market Expansion Services industry. The company utilizes its pristine balance sheet, characterized by significant leverage headroom of up to 2.0x Net Debt-to-EBITDA, to execute an accelerating pace of accretive bolt-on acquisitions. Demonstrating this acceleration, DKSH completed 35 acquisitions between 2019 and 2025, more than doubling the 16 transactions executed in the preceding seven-year period.

Crucially, this inorganic growth strategy is not merely designed to buy top-line revenue, but to structurally enhance the group's profitability. Management deliberately targets acquisitions that operate in specialized, high-margin segments, successfully directing more than 80.0% of recent M&A spending into these elevated profit pools. By constantly folding smaller, specialized distributors into its massive pan-Asian platform, DKSH realizes immediate cost synergies and cross-selling opportunities. This disciplined acquisition engine, paired with an intentional organic shift toward higher-value commercial outsourcing services, strictly underpins management's commitment to expanding the consolidated Core EBIT margin by an average of at least 10 basis points annually.
