2. Guidance Credibility -- FY23 to FY25

Theon listed on Euronext Amsterdam in February 2024. The guidance track record since listing is short but unblemished:

| Period | Initial Guidance | Mid-Year Update | Final Actual | Outcome |

|--------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|---------|

| FY26 | EUR 570-600m (Feb 2026) | Not yet updated | -- | Pending |

Key observations:

1. Guidance has only been revised upward. No downgrades. No "kitchen sink" quarters. The pattern is conservative initial guidance followed by a mid-year upgrade, followed by delivery at or above the upgraded range.

2. Coverage visibility supports the pattern. At the time of initial FY25 guidance, ~80% of the lower end was already covered by soft backlog. By March 2025, committed orders and options covered ~90% of full-year guidance.

3. FY26 guidance of EUR 570-600m implies ~30% growth (20%+ organic, remainder from Kappa Optronics consolidation). Given the EUR 2.3-2.4bn backlog and the OCCAR delivery schedule, this looks achievable on the same conservative-then-upgrade pattern.

Credibility verdict: High. Two-for-two on beats since IPO. Pattern of under-promise/over-deliver is established.

The backlog-to-revenue ratio of ~5.2x is extraordinary and provides multi-year visibility. Management has stated that the backlog "provides visibility for the first time beyond the typical 18-month horizon, spanning 2029." This is a structural shift from the pre-IPO model of shorter-duration orders.

Conversion Mechanics

Several factors affect how quickly backlog converts to revenue:

1. OCCAR contract phasing: The EUR ~1bn Bundeswehr NVG contract (100,000 additional units, plus 4,000 for Belgium) is a multi-year framework agreement. Deliveries of the Mikron binocular (powered by Exosens 16mm tubes) will ramp over 2026-2029. This is not a single-year bolus.

2. Image Intensifier Tube (IIT) supply: Theon has secured >400,000 IITs through its Long-Term Commercial Agreement with Exosens (extended to end of 2030) and is vertically integrating through Harder Digital. Tube availability has historically been the gating factor for NVG delivery.

3. Manufacturing capacity: Part of the OCCAR assembly will take place at the Hensoldt Theon NightVision (HTN) JV facility in Wetzlar, Germany. Capex is ramping (EUR 18.6m in FY25 vs EUR 10.7m in FY24) to support throughput expansion.

4. Framework agreement structure: Many new contracts are framework agreements with options, meaning not all of the EUR 2.4bn backlog is firm commitment. The "contractual options" component (EUR 378m disclosed at H1-25, likely higher at year-end) adds optionality but also uncertainty.

Conversion Concerns

The primary risk is execution on the OCCAR contract -- ramping from ~74,000 previously ordered NVGs (largely delivered) to a cumulative ~200,000 units requires significant production scaling. If Harder Digital's capacity ramp underdelivers or Exosens tube supply tightens, revenue recognition could slip right. However, management's proactive securing of the Exosens LTCA and the 9.8% equity stake (EUR 268.7m) signals they view supply chain as the key constraint and are actively de-risking it.

Conversion verdict: Strong visibility, manageable execution risk. The 5.2x backlog-to-revenue ratio provides exceptional forward coverage, but the market should expect a multi-year conversion profile, not a single-year revenue spike.

4. Management Tone & Commentary

FY25 Preliminary Results (16 Feb 2026) -- Key Messages

Management described FY25 as a "transformative" year. The CEO press release carried several distinctive messages:

1. Strategic positioning validated. Management emphasised that the OCCAR contract represents "the largest single procurement of NVGs in the history of a European NATO member" and positions Theon as the "recognised" partner for European defence optronics.

2. Margin discipline intact. The explicit call-out that "profitability margins exceeded expectations" signals management awareness that investors track margin quality alongside revenue growth.

3. Vertical integration working. Harder Digital, acquired in late 2024 at EUR 34m for a 60% stake, was cited as a contributor to improved margins. The subsidiary manufactures Gen I-III Image Intensifier Tubes and is ramping production in Germany and Latvia.

4. Guidance confidence on FY26. The EUR 570-600m FY26 revenue guidance was issued simultaneously with FY25 prelims, projecting ~30% growth. This is unusually bold for a company that typically guides conservatively.

5. Shift to longer-duration contracts. Management highlighted the transition from 18-month order horizons to multi-year framework agreements as a structural improvement to the business model. This reduces volatility and improves planning.

6. Supply chain proactively secured. The Exosens 9.8% stake (EUR 268.7m) and the 400,000+ IIT LTCA extension to 2030 were presented as strategic moves to secure the critical supply chain chokepoint.

Confidence Level Assessment

Management tone is highly confident, bordering on bullish. The combination of record results, a transformational order book, a new FY26 guidance range 30% above FY25, and proactive supply chain investments projects conviction. There is no hedging language, no flagging of risks beyond standard disclaimers. The rights issue (EUR 150m, Dec 2025) was fully supported by major shareholders (Venetus 61.4%, CHRE 9.6%), which adds credibility.

6. Specific Contract Wins

The OCCAR/Bundeswehr Mega-Contract (Dec 2025)

  • Value: ~EUR 1bn (contract revision)
  • Scope: 100,000 Night Vision Goggles for the German Bundeswehr + 4,000 for Belgian Armed Forces
  • Product: Mikron binocular NVG, powered by Exosens 16mm tubes
  • Builds on: Previous OCCAR orders of 66,000 NVGs (Germany) + 8,000 (Belgium), majority delivered
  • Cumulative programme: ~200,000 NVGs including options
  • Manufacturing: Part assembly at Hensoldt Theon NightVision (HTN) JV in Wetzlar, Germany
  • Significance: Largest single NVG procurement by a European NATO member. Cements Theon as the European champion for man-portable night vision equipment.

Other Notable Contract Activity (FY25)

1. Thermal clip-on sights (Germany): Contract for up to 25,000 sets, valued at >EUR 100m. This is the IRIS-C Thermal Clip-on product line.

2. Multi-year framework agreement (European NATO member): For NVGs and IRIS-C Thermal Clip-ons. Specific customer not disclosed, but the shift to multi-year frameworks is a recurring theme.

3. EUR 50m new orders (Jun 2025): Announced separately, directing revenue guidance to the upper end of the EUR 410-430m range.

4. EUR 47m new orders (Dec 2024, pre-FY25): Announced alongside direction of FY24 guidance toward upper range.

Acquisition Activity (FY25)

1. Kappa Optronics (Aug 2025): 100% acquisition for EUR 75m EV. Germany-based aviation and land optronics specialist. ~EUR 37m revenue, ~EUR 8m EBITDA. 60 engineers, asset-light R&D model. Fourth consecutive German industrial investment.

2. Exosens 9.8% stake (Oct 2025, closed Jan 2026): EUR 268.7m for second-largest shareholder position. Strategic supply chain play. Exosens is the key supplier of Image Intensifier Tubes. Funded partly by EUR 150m rights issue (Dec 2025).
