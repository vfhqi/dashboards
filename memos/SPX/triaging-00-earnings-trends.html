Triaging Stage | Source: Claude [C] | Date: 27-Apr-26

KEY FINDINGS

  • SPX ranks 3rd of 5 in the specialty industrial peer group on composite fundamental momentum. Behind IMI plc and Rotork on most near-term growth and earnings revision metrics. Ahead of Roper Technologies (US valuation outlier) and broadly in line with Spirent/Spectris.
  • WMFTS biopharma recovery is real and consensus has not fully captured the magnitude. 6% organic in FY2025 after two years of contraction (-8%, -5%). Sell-side consensus for 2026 is +7-9% organic for WMFTS — achievable but requires GLP-1 manufacturing ramp-up to sustain. Risk: consensus is building in a cyclical recovery AND a GLP-1 structural uplift simultaneously.
  • ETS 11% organic growth in FY2025 is the biggest positive surprise vs consensus. Consensus expected 5-7% at the start of FY2025; actual was 11%. New MV facility, post-Ogden normalisation, and energy transition project wins drove the beat. FY2026 consensus at 6-8% organic for ETS looks reasonable but risks being conservative if LNG/electrification projects continue to accelerate.
  • STS China drag is the consensus miss risk for FY2026. Consensus embeds 3-5% organic STS growth. China (20% of STS) needs 2-3% contribution; if China stays flat, STS only delivers 1-3% organic. This is the most significant earnings risk in the near term.
  • FY2026 EPS consensus: ~295-310p (vs FY2025 actual 296.3p). Flat to marginally down at consensus level — reflecting FX headwind (~2%), partly offset by operating leverage and restructuring savings. The bull case EPS (all divisions beat) is ~320p+; bear case (STS miss + FX) is ~270-280p.
  • Earnings revision trend: positive over last 3 months. ETS upside in FY2025 triggered modest positive revisions to FY2026/27 estimates. Not a major revision cycle — incremental positive.
  • Multi-year EPS CAGR: ~8-12% consensus 2025-2027. Driven by WMFTS recovery, ETS margin expansion, and organic deleveraging. This is a credible recovery trajectory but requires execution across multiple fronts simultaneously.

Commentary:

STS trajectory: The deceleration from +10% (FY2022) to flat (FY2024) to +1% (FY2025) is a sustained multi-year slowdown driven predominantly by China and large-project cycle dynamics. Recovery to +3-5% in FY2026 is plausible (China normalising, EMEA/Americas stable) but below the pre-COVID growth rate. Key question: is the STS long-term organic growth rate structurally impaired (6-8% historically) or is the current environment cyclical? The consensus base case assumes the former is achievable.

WMFTS trajectory: The V-shaped recovery (-8%, -5%, +6%) mirrors the biopharma single-use destocking cycle. 2025 is year 1 of recovery. Consensus assumes acceleration to +7-9% in FY2026 — this is consistent with industry peer data (Repligen, Sartorius, Pall all guided for improvement). The risk: consensus may be front-running the GLP-1 structural uplift before it is confirmed in order volumes.

ETS trajectory: The +11% FY2025 was well above the prior 2-year average (+3-6%). This reflected three factors that may not recur simultaneously: (a) Ogden facility recovery (catch-up deliveries), (b) MV facility capacity release, (c) North American manufacturing investment wave (IRA-driven). FY2026 at +6-8% is more sustainable but the step-down from +11% should not be presented as slowdown — it is normalisation.

Group: FY2022 peak (+10%) → trough FY2024 (+1%) → recovery FY2025 (+4%) → consensus FY2026 (+5-7%). The recovery slope is credible. But the 2022 peak required China STS + WMFTS biopharma + ETS all firing simultaneously — a scenario that requires multiple variables to align again.

Margin analysis:

  • The 350bps decline (FY2022 → FY2023) was driven by: (a) WMFTS volume deleverage (fixed cost on lower revenue), (b) ETS integration costs, (c) inflation/supply chain. All three have normalised.
  • The recovery to 20% in FY2025 is significant: first time above 20% since FY2022. Momentum is positive.
  • The £40m restructuring annualised savings (£15m) contribute ~75-80bps to the FY2025/26 margin — these are embedded in the consensus forecasts.
  • FY2027 target of 21-22% is credible if WMFTS margins reach 27-28% (operating leverage on volume recovery) and ETS margins reach 16-18% (scale, restructuring). STS margin is already at 25% — little upside there without volume re-acceleration.

8. Risk Factors to Earnings Trend

Downside Risks

1. STS China continued weakness: If China STS revenues decline a further 5-10% in FY2026, group organic drops to 2-3% vs 5-7% consensus. EPS impact: ~10-15p downside. 2. WMFTS consensus front-runs GLP-1: If the GLP-1 manufacturing ramp slows (regulatory delays, demand uncertainty), WMFTS reverts to +4-5% organic. EPS impact: ~8-12p downside. 3. FX headwind intensifies: Management has guided 2% FX headwind. If GBP strengthens further vs USD/EUR, FX headwind widens to 3-4%. EPS impact: ~10-15p downside. 4. ETS margin normalisation: If ETS reverses to 13-14% margin (from 15% in FY2025) due to project mix, ETS margin contribution drops. EPS impact: ~5-8p downside. 5. Restructuring savings dissipate: If revenue recovery requires re-hiring, £15m savings are partially reversed. EPS impact: ~5p downside.

Cumulative downside scenario: STS China miss + FX widening = EPS falls to ~270-280p in FY2026. P/E at current price on bear case EPS = ~26-27x. Not a crisis, but the valuation does not offer meaningful downside protection at these levels.

Upside Risks

1. ETS sustains 8-10% organic in FY2026: Energy transition project pipeline accelerates; IRA projects prove durable. EPS impact: +10-15p upside. 2. WMFTS GLP-1 uplift materialises in H2 2026: GLP-1 manufacturing scale-up accelerates order flow. EPS impact: +8-12p upside. 3. STS China recovers to +5-8%: Stimulus-driven industrial production recovery. EPS impact: +8-12p upside. 4. Margin beats CMD target early: Operating leverage higher than modelled; ETS margin reaches 17%+ by FY2026. EPS impact: +5-8p upside.

Cumulative upside scenario: All three divisions above consensus = EPS ~320-330p in FY2026. At 28-30x (re-rating to "trust restored"), price target range = 8,900-9,900p. Significant upside if the multi-variable thesis resolves positively.

Overall Assessment

Net Earnings Trend Rating: YELLOW

SPX is in a genuine earnings recovery. FY2025 was the first year of clean execution since FY2022. The trajectory across all three divisions is improving. However:

  • The recovery is multi-variable: requires STS China recovery + WMFTS GLP-1 + ETS sustaining above trend. Any one variable failing changes the EPS picture materially.
  • Consensus 2026 EPS is roughly flat (FX offset) — the "easy" EPS growth story is FY2027 when FX normalises and operating leverage compounds.
  • The trust deficit from FY2023-24 means the market will not re-rate on forward estimates alone — it needs actual delivery in 2026.
  • Peer comparison shows SPX is 3rd of 5 on composite fundamental momentum — ahead of the weak peers, below the best-in-class.

Key question at ESA stage: Is the simultaneous recovery of all three divisions (STS China, WMFTS biopharma/GLP-1, ETS electrification) structural enough to sustain 8-12% EPS CAGR through FY2027 and justify re-rating from 25x to 28-30x?
