Spirax Group (SPX) — Earnings Delivery Assessment [C]

C Triaging @ View in Notion

Triaging Stage | Source: Claude [C] | Date: 27-Apr-26

KEY FINDINGS

  • FY2025 results: adjusted operating profit £339.9m, beat consensus ~2.4%. Adjusted EPS 296.3p (+3% YoY). First clean beat since FY2022. The beat was driven by ETS, not broad-based.
  • Watson-Marlow 6% organic, 160bps margin expansion to 26.2%. WMFTS is the highest-quality delivery in FY2025 — volume recovery, operating leverage, and margin demonstrably improving. This is the most credible evidence of structural recovery.
  • ETS 11% organic growth — the material upside surprise. New MV facility fully operational, post-Ogden delivery catch-up, and energy transition project wins. Margin improvement to ~15% from ~13% in FY2024. Consensus expected 5-7% organic.
  • STS only 1% organic — the disappointing division. China + large-project deferrals combined to suppress STS below consensus (+3-4% expected). EMEA and Americas STS were stable to positive; China was the drag.
  • £40m restructuring completed. FCF £199m, net debt/EBITDA 1.5x. Balance sheet substantially repaired. Cash generation strong. Dividend maintained and covered 2.3x.
  • FY2026 guidance: mid-single-digit organic, ~2% FX headwind. Implies group organic of 5-6%. No specific divisional guidance. This is management's "prove it" year — the first year post-restructuring with a new CEO.
  • Overall FY2025 delivery verdict: YELLOW. Beat on an absolute basis; beat was concentrated in ETS (one division); STS disappointed; track record still in repair. Positive step, not validation.

Divisional Profitability

STS:

  • Adj. operating margin: ~25% (stable vs FY2024 ~25%). Pricing power sustaining margin through volume softness. The STS model proves its resilience here — even with +1% organic, margin holds.
  • The 25% margin stability is the most underappreciated quality signal in the FY2025 results. STS's competitive moat (direct sales, installed base, application engineering) allows it to protect margins even in subdued demand environments.

WMFTS:

  • Adj. operating margin: ~26.2% (vs ~24.6% in FY2024) = +160bps expansion.
  • Volume recovery flowing directly to the bottom line: WMFTS has high fixed cost base (R&D, sales engineers, premium manufacturing). +6% organic volume at stable pricing = significant operating leverage.
  • 26.2% margin is the highest in 3 years for WMFTS. Not yet at the FY2022 peak (~28-29%), but trajectory is clear.

ETS:

  • Adj. operating margin: ~15% (vs ~13% in FY2024) = +200bps expansion.
  • ETS is still the lowest-margin division and the most margin-expandable. The +200bps in FY2025 reflects: (a) operating leverage on +11% organic volume, (b) MV facility cost efficiencies, (c) restructuring savings disproportionately benefitting ETS.
  • CMD target of 16-18% by FY2027 is now looking achievable (would require +100-300bps further expansion from the 15% FY2025 base).

2. Quality of Delivery

What Went Right

1. ETS operational turnaround confirmed. The Ogden facility incident (H2 2023) was the most significant operational disruption. New MV facility fully operational by FY2025. +11% organic confirms this is resolved. Not a flash in the pan — energy transition project pipeline is real and multi-year.

2. WMFTS margin recovery validates biopharma destocking thesis. The 160bps margin improvement is hard evidence that the WMFTS business model is intact. Margin would not have recovered this quickly if the business had structural problems.

3. Restructuring delivery and FCF. £40m restructuring completed on time and within budget (unusual for Spirax — this is a management execution positive). FCF conversion at 82% ahead of target — signals discipline in working capital and capex management under Patel.

4. Balance sheet progress. Net debt/EBITDA at 1.5x (below the 2.5x ceiling the market was worried about in FY2022-23). M&A capacity restored in principle.

What Disappointed

1. STS China + large projects. The +1% organic for STS conceals a better underlying picture ex-China: EMEA and Americas STS were estimated at +4-6% organic. China (20% of STS) was likely -8 to -12% — consistent with broad China industrial production weakness and large project deferrals.

2. Group organic of +4% — below the pre-announcement expectation of +5-6%. The headline beat on profit was achieved via margins (ETS, WMFTS), not via revenue. Revenue missed vs the optimistic consensus expectation. This distinction matters: margin beats are more credible if accompanied by revenue beats.

3. Guidance tone: cautious. "Mid-single-digit organic with ~2% FX headwind" translates to ~5-6% organic and flat-to-slightly-down EPS in FY2026. Management is not providing divisional guidance — conservative signalling from a new CEO who does not want to over-promise.

3. Last 3 Years Delivery Track Record

FY2023 Delivery

  • Guidance: Mid-single-digit organic growth, "modest margin improvement"
  • Delivered: +4% organic (below the optimistic read of guidance), margin fell to 18.5%
  • Key miss: WMFTS biopharma destocking emerged in H2 2023 and was not in guidance. Margin miss reflected WMFTS deleverage + ETS integration cost overrun.
  • Assessment: MISS. Credibility dented. First year of post-Chromalox delivery failure.

FY2024 Delivery

  • Guidance: "Modest organic growth" (effectively 2-4% after the FY2023 miss)
  • Delivered: +1% organic, margin 19.2% (marginal improvement vs 18.5%)
  • Key misses: STS China worse than expected (-high single digit vs flat assumed); WMFTS declined a further -5% (destocking extended longer than consensus expected); ETS Ogden facility disruption not guided.
  • Assessment: NEAR-MISS / DISAPPOINTING. Three divisions all delivered at or below the low end of expectations. Second consecutive year of guidance disappointment.

FY2025 Delivery

  • Guidance (CMD 2024 reset): Conservative "mid-single-digit organic"
  • Delivered: +4% organic, margin ~20%, ETS +11% surprise, EPS 296.3p
  • Assessment: IN LINE TO BEAT. First clean delivery year. Beat achieved through profit/margin, not revenue. ETS was the material upside.

4. Management Communication Quality

CEO Patel's Commentary Style (FY2025)

  • Language: measured, quantified, conservative. No hyperbole. No commitment to recovery trajectories beyond "mid-single-digit organic."
  • Positive: Patel named specific ETS operational improvements (MV facility, project pipeline); WMFTS divisional margin progression given with specificity; restructuring completion given with cost and savings quantum.
  • Negative: No China recovery timeline given. No specific FY2026 divisional guidance. "Cautious optimism" language used — which the market correctly interprets as limited near-term upside catalysts.

Q&A Handling

  • Analyst questions on China: deflected to "gradual normalisation assumed." No specific data on China order intake, pipeline, or customer activity.
  • Analyst questions on GLP-1: acknowledged as an emerging opportunity for WMFTS; declined to quantify. Appropriate caution (too early to claim the uplift).
  • Analyst questions on M&A: confirmed "M&A capacity returning but no specific targets." WMFTS bolt-on M&A remains the preferred use of excess balance sheet.

Guidance Specificity Rating: LOW

Management is deliberately conservative on forward guidance — appropriate for a new CEO with a delivery track record to rebuild. But it limits the short-term re-rating catalyst.

FY2026 EPS flat vs FY2025 (~296-305p range):

  • Operating leverage on volume recovery: ~+8-12p
  • FX headwind (2% implied by guidance): ~-8-12p
  • Restructuring savings retention: ~+5p
  • Net: flat to marginally positive. Consistent with consensus ~295-310p.

The 2027 case is stronger: By FY2027, FX headwinds normalise, WMFTS GLP-1 becomes quantifiable in order intake, and ETS margin expansion compounds. FY2027 EPS consensus ~330-350p implies ~+12-18% growth from FY2025 base.

6. What Needs to Be True for ESA

At Triaging stage, the question is: should SPX progress to ESA? The delivery assessment informs this.

Arguments to progress to ESA:

  • FY2025 is the first clean delivery year — sufficient to warrant deeper investigation
  • WMFTS recovery is genuine (margin evidence, not just revenue)
  • ETS operational turnaround proven
  • Valuation at ~25x offers a reasonable margin of safety vs historical 30-35x
  • New CEO has a clear mandate and the financial profile to execute it

Arguments to park:

  • Trust deficit: two years of misses still in market memory; 1 year of beats insufficient for re-rating
  • Multi-variable dependency: needs STS China + WMFTS GLP-1 + ETS sustaining simultaneously
  • Guidance is flat-to-slightly-down EPS in FY2026; EPS catalyst is 12+ months away
  • RS at 60th percentile and below-leadership technical setup suggests institutional conviction is limited

ESA recommendation: PROGRESS to ESA, but with targeted scope. The key ESA question is the Watson-Marlow structural growth case (biopharma recovery + GLP-1 opportunity) and the Patel CEO capital allocation track record. If WMFTS is genuinely inflecting structurally and ETS continues to prove itself, the multi-variable risk diminishes to a STS China watch item.

Overall FY2025 delivery rating: YELLOW
