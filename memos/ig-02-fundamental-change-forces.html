BRAV — Fundamental Change Forces

  • Verdict: "Margin over volume" strategy is genuinely delivering: group EBITA 4.5%→5.3%, EPS +17% despite flat sales #J
  • Crown jewel: Denmark EBITA trough-to-peak 0-1% → 6.6% (560bp recovery) is execution proof, not accounting-driven #J
  • Management signal: New CFO Petra Vranjes (ex-Ericsson/Telia), capital-discipline background + early share purchase = alignment signal #J
  • Recovery timing: H2 2026 base case for Nordic construction recovery: order intake +11% Q4 2025 is the leading signal #A
  • Bear risk: Revenue -2.2% organic FY2025, not a growth story yet; structural shrinkage if H2 2026 recovery fails to materialise #J
  • Competitive threat: Assemblin Caverion now 1.5x larger by revenue; procurement scale advantage Bravida cannot easily match #A

Operational Turnaround

The "Bravida Way" is delivering visible margin expansion through genuine management choice, with Denmark the standout evidence and new CFO appointment reinforcing capital discipline.

  • Strategy execution: CEO Mattias Johansson's margin-over-volume model is now visibly delivering: -2.2% organic growth was deliberate, not market weakness #J
    • Evidence: Group EBITA margin 4.5% (2024) → 5.3% (FY2025); EPS +17% YoY against flat sales
    • Mechanism: Deliberately rejected low-margin work, focused on service mix (now 50% of revenues)
  • Denmark turnaround: EBITA 0-1% trough (2022-23) → 6.6% in Q4 2025 = 560bp+ recovery: reflects genuine operational fix, not accounting #J
    • Drivers: Resolution of legacy loss-making projects, cost discipline, service revenue up to 59% of Danish revenue (from 50%)
    • Execution engine: New Danish CEO Christian Alsø (ex-ISS, appointed May 2024)
  • CFO signal: Petra Vranjes (ex-Ericsson/Telia) appointment signals board prioritising financial discipline and capital allocation #J
    • Alignment: Vranjes made early share purchase post-appointment
    • Background: Both prior employers are capital-intensive, IR-sophisticated businesses
  • Balance sheet: Net debt/EBITDA 1.1x (down from 1.4x mid-year), Q4 operating cash flow SEK 1.2bn vs SEK 756m prior year #I
    • Context: Well below 2.5x leverage target; M&A capacity intact (4 deals in 2025, SEK 454m annual sales added)

Market Recovery Thesis

H2 2026 is the base case for Nordic construction recovery, with early signals now visible in order intake, macro easing, and regulatory demand tailwinds.

  • Recovery signal: Order intake +11% in Q4 2025: the leading indicator the inflection is approaching #A
    • Macro backdrop: Sweden LTV rule easing (Apr 2026: 85%→90%), Riksbank rate 1.75%
    • External forecast: Veidekke forecasts 7% Nordic construction growth 2026 (vs 1% in 2025)
  • Sustainability tailwind: Denmark CO2 limits (12 kg CO2e/m²) structurally driving retrofit, HVAC, and automation demand #A
  • Competitive position: ~300 Nordic branches provide distributed moat; ~11% market share (vs Assemblin Caverion ~11%, Instalco ~4%) #I

Bear Case

Three material risks: the growth story is absent if the cycle doesn't recover, ASCA's scale advantage is structural, and headline EPS growth masks more modest EBITDA progress.

  • Growth risk: Revenue -2.2% organic FY2025; margin gains earned by shrinking, not growing; structural shrinkage if H2 2026 recovery fails #J
  • Competitive threat: Assemblin Caverion (ASCA) now 1.5x larger by revenue: procurement advantages, scale, and geographic breadth Bravida cannot easily match #A
    • Timing: Pressure will intensify as ASCA completes integration
  • EPS quality: EPS +17% YoY against EBITDA +8-10% implies growth front-loaded by deleveraging and WC improvement, not structural EBITDA gains #A
