9. Risk Assessment

_Reconstructed bear case centres on five interconnected risks. Any two occurring simultaneously could trigger further material downside._

9.1 Customer Concentration (Structural)

  • Judgement: This is HFG's single biggest structural vulnerability. #J
  • Top 5 customers = ~85% of revenue. Tesco is the largest, estimated at 40-50%+ of UK revenue. #I
  • Cost-plus contracts provide stability but zero pricing power. HFG is a cost centre for its customers, not a strategic partner. #A
  • Contract loss or material volume reduction from any top-3 customer would be an existential event for the relevant facility. HFG builds dedicated plants for specific retailers. #A
  • Bear scenario: Tesco in-sources protein processing or switches to a competitor (Cranswick, 2 Sisters). HFG has no diversified fallback. #A

9.2 Seafood / Foppen Operational Failure (Acute)

  • Judgement: Foppen is a live operational crisis, not a legacy problem. #J
  • US FDA export ban from Greek facility persists into H1 2026 minimum. Production shifted to Netherlands at higher cost. #I
  • Wider UK seafood division hit by soft white-fish demand as consumers trade down on high shelf prices. Classic margin vice: input costs rising, consumer willingness to pay falling. #A
  • Bear scenario: FDA clearance takes 12+ months. Foppen becomes a permanent drag or requires write-down. #A

9.3 Expansion Execution Risk (Medium-term)

  • Judgement: Canada, Saudi, and Poland builds running simultaneously create concentration of execution risk. #J
  • Canada (Walmart): ~£15m capex in H1 2025 alone. Build-phase inflation above plan. Launch early 2027. #I
  • Saudi (NADEC JV): Launch H2 2026. JV structure adds complexity. #I
  • Poland expansion: adding to capex pile. #I
  • Core capex guided £50-55m for FY26 on top of project capex. Net debt expected to rise. #A
  • Bear scenario: one or more builds delayed, over-budget, or volume ramp slower than planned. Free cash flow stays negative through FY27. #A

  • Bear scenario: FY26 PBT comes in at the low end (£60m) or below. A third profit warning is possible if Foppen remediation stalls or beef inflation worsens. #A

  • Customer concentration is real and structural. HFG has no answer for this other than geographic diversification (Canada, Saudi), which takes years to move the needle. #A
  • Foppen is a genuine operational failure. Not a one-off — the FDA issue has persisted for 6+ months with no clear resolution date. #A
  • Earnings direction is negative. FY26 will be worse than FY25. The stock is not cheap on forward earnings if those earnings disappoint again. #A
  • Capital allocation track record is mixed. Foods Connected and Fairfax Meadow were both disposed of — tacit admission of failed diversification. #A

What the bears get wrong or overstate:

  • Cost-plus model is misunderstood. Bears frame it as "no upside." But it also means "limited downside." In a recession, HFG's earnings are more resilient than margin-exposed peers like Cranswick or Bakkavor. #A
  • Balance sheet is adequate. 0.9x net debt/EBITDA. New £450m RCF secured. Covenants at 3.0x. There's headroom. #A

  • Valuation discount is meaningful. If earnings trough in FY26 and recover from FY27 as Canada/Saudi ramp, the current price is a genuine buying opportunity. #A
