GYM Q7 KEY DRIVERS ASSESSMENT [C]

The Gym Group plc (GYM-GB) | Key Drivers Assessment | April 2026

KEY FINDINGS

1. Site expansion to 335 sites by end-2028 is the primary share price driver. If GYM delivers 75 new sites at 27-30% ROIC, the EBITDA LNR run rate reaches £70-75m+ by 2028-2029. At 8-10x EV/EBITDA, this implies material share price upside from current levels.

2. ARPMM growth is the most financially sensitive single variable. A 1% improvement in ARPMM (approximately £0.22/month on £21.60) translates to approximately £2.5m additional annualised revenue at current member volumes. Cumulative ARPMM trajectory over 24 months is the keystone metric.

3. Multiple re-rating from turnaround discount to compounder premium has already begun but has further to run. GYM currently trades at approximately 7.6x EV/EBITDA — a material discount to a proven compound growth gym business, justified by the recency of the turnaround. As evidence of sustained delivery accumulates, this discount will close.

4. Capital allocation signal quality is high. The first-ever buyback, combined with FCF-funded expansion and a declining leverage trajectory, signals management confidence in the durability of cash generation. Further capital returns (special dividend, buyback expansion) are plausible as EBITDA compounds.

5. Hamish Latchem's property execution capability is the single most important operational variable. The site expansion programme lives and dies on pipeline quality and opening execution. Latchem's Aldi background provides the most credible evidence that this capability exists.

6. Technology differentiation is the secondary operational driver. GYM is investing in digital member experience (app, onboarding, AI coaching features). If this investment produces measurably superior retention vs PureGym, it widens the competitive moat and justifies ARPMM premium.

7. The driver interdependencies are tight: site expansion generates revenue that funds the buyback; ARPMM growth funds new site investment; technology improvement drives retention which sustains ARPMM. All three primary drivers reinforce each other in a virtuous cycle when working. They undermine each other when any fails.

8. GYM screens as the highest-quality fundamental opportunity in the trio at current prices. BFIT trades at higher leverage with more complex multi-country execution risk. SATS trades on higher margins but lower growth. GYM offers the best combination of growth rate, leverage comfort, and execution track record.

9. Bull case 24-month price target: 250-300p. Bear case: 130-150p. Base case: 220-240p. Current price: 182p. Implied upside in base case: approximately 25-30%.

10. Thesis fragility hinges primarily on ARPMM trajectory. This is the one variable that management has least control over — it is set by market pricing dynamics and competitive behaviour. All other drivers are in management's control.

SECTION 2 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Five primary share price drivers have been identified for the next 24 months. They are listed in order of financial materiality:

1. Site expansion programme (75 new sites, 2026-2028)

2. ARPMM growth (price/mix improvement, retention)

3. Margin expansion via operating leverage as new sites mature

4. Capital allocation signal quality (buyback, potential dividend)

5. Multiple re-rating from turnaround discount to quality compounder

Two secondary drivers are also assessed:

6. Technology differentiation (app, digital experience, retention)

7. Competitive landscape evolution (PureGym, JD Gyms)

The overall assessment is GREEN — the drivers are real, the evidence of delivery is strong, and the financial model supports material upside from current valuation.

Binary assessment: the investment case is not binary (the company does not rely on a single event). It is a compounding growth story with multiple positive catalysts. The risk is gradual deterioration rather than a step-change failure.

Overall rating: GREEN

DRIVER 1: SITE EXPANSION — 75 NEW SITES IN 3 YEARS

Definition and Guidance

GYM has committed to opening approximately 75 new sites between 2026 and 2028. In FY2026, 20 new sites are targeted. The remaining 55 are planned for FY2027-2028. All sites funded from FCF. Capex guidance for FY2026: £60-65m.

The financial model: at an average cost of approximately £3.0m per site (FY2026 implied) and 27-30% ROIC, each new site generates £810k-£900k EBITDA at maturity (year 2-3 post-opening). Over 75 sites, the incremental EBITDA contribution at full maturity is approximately £60-67m. This effectively doubles the current EBITDA LNR run rate.

Plausibility Assessment: MEDIUM-HIGH

Latchem's appointment is the most significant risk mitigant. His Aldi experience building 700 new UK stores over 13 years provides precisely the institutional capability required. The pipeline of 20 sites targeted for 2026 (vs 16 achieved in FY2025) is a manageable step-up.

The risk is the outer years (2027-2028) where 55 sites need to be identified, secured, and built. The UK retail property market's structural vacancy — created by the collapse of mid-market retail post-pandemic — creates a genuine opportunity for gym operators to access large-format space. Savills data confirms out-of-town retail parks are the fastest-growing gym location format.

Bear Case: Pipeline quality deteriorates after the first 20 high-quality urban sites. GYM is forced into lower-density suburban or secondary-town locations. Average ROIC falls to 22-24%. FCF self-funding thesis strains.

Driver Rating: GREEN — High plausibility, strong execution capability, supportive property market, and FCF-funded structure limits financial risk.

DRIVER 2: ARPMM GROWTH

Definition and Guidance

ARPMM is average revenue per member per month. In FY2025 it was £21.60, up 4% from £20.81 in FY2024. The Standard membership headline price is £25.64/month. The gap between headline and blended ARPMM reflects Off-Peak memberships, legacy member pricing, and promotions.

Management assumes approximately 3% LFL revenue growth in FY2026, which implicitly includes 2-3% ARPMM growth and modest member volume growth.

Plausibility Assessment: MEDIUM

The deceleration from 7% (FY2024) to 4% (FY2025) is real and must be explained. The most likely explanation is a combination of: (a) the end of the post-COVID pricing catch-up; (b) increased proportion of new members joining at current rates but forming a smaller share of the long-tenure base; and (c) deliberate management restraint to protect retention.

The management lever is tenure extension. If average member tenure increases from 18 months toward 24 months, blended ARPMM improves mechanically because longer-tenure members are more likely to be paying higher prices (either through repricing or through being retained from higher-price joining cohorts). The 45-day intervention protocol and the improved app experience are driving this improvement.

Bear Case: ARPMM growth decelerates to 1-2% by FY2027 as the price ceiling for budget gym positioning is reached. Standard membership approaching £28-30/month triggers material churn increase. Revenue growth becomes entirely dependent on new site volumes.

Driver Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — Direction positive, deceleration trend warrants monitoring. 3% LFL assumption is achievable but not a high bar.

DRIVER 3: MARGIN EXPANSION VIA OPERATING LEVERAGE

Definition

As GYM's estate scales from 260 to 335 sites, the fixed cost base (central overhead, technology platform, corporate functions) is spread across a larger revenue base. Simultaneously, new sites opened in FY2023-2025 are maturing and crossing the profitability threshold. Both effects produce margin expansion.

Financial Model

Current EBITDA LNR margin: 23%.

Target run rate (management implied): EBITDA LNR of approximately £65m on an estate of approximately 300+ sites.

Revenue at 300 sites (assuming stable ARPMM and member count per site): approximately £270-280m.

Implied margin: approximately 23-24%. Modest expansion.

The more material driver is the increase in EBITDA LNR per site from new site maturation. Each new site opened in FY2025 generates sub-£250k EBITDA in year 1 but approximately £630k+ at maturity. Over a 2-3 year maturity period, the contribution per site rises approximately 2.5x. This creates a significant lag benefit in aggregate EBITDA as the FY2024-2026 cohorts reach maturity in FY2026-2028.

Plausibility Assessment: HIGH

This is a mechanical, time-driven benefit. The only scenario in which it fails to materialise is if new sites close (minimal risk given 10-15 year lease commitments) or if site-level revenue is materially below expectations.

Bear Case: Margin expansion is offset by cost inflation (energy, labour) running ahead of revenue growth. UK national living wage increases and energy cost volatility are the primary cost risks.

Driver Rating: GREEN — Mechanical and time-driven. High plausibility. The only risk is cost inflation overwhelming the maturation benefit.

DRIVER 4: CAPITAL ALLOCATION — BUYBACK AND DIVIDEND POTENTIAL

Definition

The £10m buyback announced in March 2026 is GYM's first-ever capital return programme. It runs to December 2026 and buys approximately 5.5m shares at 182p.

Signal Quality

A first-ever buyback from a company that was posting statutory losses in FY2022 is a high-quality capital allocation signal. Management is communicating: the business generates durable excess cash; the balance sheet has capacity beyond operational needs; and the company is being run for shareholder value, not purely for growth investment.

The EPS accretion from cancelling 5.5m shares (approximately 4.5% of shares outstanding) is meaningful at approximately 5% uplift to EPS.

Future Capital Returns

The question for 24-month horizon is: will GYM reinstate a dividend? The company has not paid a dividend since COVID-related suspensions. Dividend reinstatement would attract income-oriented institutional investors who currently cannot hold GYM and would be a further re-rating catalyst.

Pre-condition: sustained profitability and FCF generation for 2-3 years. By FY2027, if the company has delivered 2 more years of the current trajectory, the case for a regular dividend is strong.

Plausibility Assessment: HIGH for sustained buyback; MEDIUM for near-term dividend

Driver Rating: GREEN — Capital return signal is positive and credible. Dividend reinstatement within 24 months is plausible but not certain.

DRIVER 5: MULTIPLE RE-RATING

Definition

GYM currently trades at approximately 7.6x EV/EBITDA (LTM). A business of GYM's quality — growing EBITDA at 15-20% per year, FCF positive, low leverage, improving returns — should, in a mature phase, trade at 10-12x EV/EBITDA.

The current discount to this long-run target reflects: (a) the recency of the turnaround (only 2 years of delivery); (b) the ongoing execution risk of the 75-site expansion; (c) the small-cap liquidity discount (GYM is a micro-cap by global standards).

Multiple Re-rating Pathway

Each successful half-year reporting period under the Next Chapter strategy reduces the market's execution risk discount. By mid-2026, Orr will have been in post for nearly three years — approaching the timeframe at which a "turnaround premium" becomes a "proven track record premium."

If EBITDA LNR reaches £65m in FY2027 and the multiple re-rates from 7.6x to 9.0x, the implied EV increases from approximately £430m to approximately £585m — a 36% increase in enterprise value. After netting debt, the equity value uplift is proportionately larger.

Plausibility Assessment: MEDIUM-HIGH

Multiple re-rating is probabilistic and depends on market conditions as well as company delivery. A UK small-cap market re-rating (which is possible if sterling strengthens or UK macro improves) would accelerate the re-rating beyond what company-specific factors alone can achieve.

Bear Case: Macro environment deteriorates. UK equities suffer de-rating. GYM's multiple stays at 7-8x despite strong delivery. This is a market risk rather than a company risk.

Driver Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — Strong logic, correct direction, but timing is uncertain and market-dependent.

DRIVER 6: TECHNOLOGY DIFFERENTIATION

Definition

GYM's rebuilt member app (launched June 2024) includes 300+ exercise guidance videos, personalised workout plans, early-life onboarding checklists, and workout tracking. The Ensono technology partnership provides scalable platform infrastructure.

The financial impact is indirect but measurable: 100-150bps conversion improvement from the new app. Retention improvement via the 45-day engagement protocol is driving the extension of average member tenure to 18 months.

AI Coaching Opportunity

The next frontier is AI-powered personalisation. An AI coaching layer in the member app — adapting workout plans in real-time based on visit patterns and logged performance — would differentiate GYM from PureGym's unmanned model. PureGym cannot easily replicate in-app AI coaching because its members expect a purely self-service experience.

Plausibility Assessment: MEDIUM

Technology investment is necessary for competitive positioning but is not yet a differentiating competitive moat. PureGym, BFIT, and SATS are all investing in digital. The race is for incremental differentiation rather than a step-change advantage.

Driver Rating: YELLOW-GREEN — Supportive secondary driver. Not the primary value driver but essential to defend the ARPMM premium vs PureGym's unmanned model.

SECTION 10 — DRIVER INTERDEPENDENCIES

The drivers are interconnected in a reinforcing loop when positive:

1. Site expansion → more members → more revenue → more FCF to fund further sites and buybacks

2. ARPMM growth → higher lifetime value per member → better site economics → higher site ROIC → justifies more site expansion

3. Technology improvement → better retention → higher average tenure → ARPMM improvement → more FCF

4. Multiple re-rating → higher share price → lower cost of equity → potentially enables M&A or accelerated growth

5. Capital returns → improved investor confidence → more institutional sponsorship → higher share price → further re-rating

The loop fails if ARPMM decelerates sharply, because that reduces the revenue per member that underpins the site economics, reduces FCF, and undermines the self-funding narrative.

SECTION 13 — MANAGEMENT TRACK RECORD

Will Orr (CEO, appointed September 2023)

Track record at GYM: Two full years of consistent delivery against guidance, with multiple beats. EBITDA LNR has grown from approximately £35m (run rate at appointment) to £56.7m — approximately 62% improvement in 24 months. Statutory loss of £8.4m in FY2023 converted to £7.4m profit in FY2025.

Orr's communication style is direct and evidence-based. He uses the "virtuous circle" language to describe the compounding nature of the Next Chapter strategy: better retention → higher ARPMM → more FCF → more sites → more members → better retention. This is not a novel concept, but it is one he appears to be executing against.

Hamish Latchem (CPO, appointed December 2024)

Latchem is the newest and most strategically important addition to the senior team. His 13-year Aldi career as National Store Development Director provides the exact institutional knowledge required for the 75-site expansion. The first full year of his tenure (FY2026) is the test. 20 site openings would represent the highest annual total in GYM's history.

CFO and finance function

The consistent delivery on financial guidance suggests a strong financial control function. The proactive communication of the January 2026 pre-close update (which explicitly signalled a beat of consensus) reflects good investor communication discipline.

Overall track record quality: HIGH — Two years of clean delivery, appropriate conservatism in guidance, strategic hires made for the right reasons.

SECTION 15 — THESIS FRAGILITY

The investment thesis rests on three assumptions that must hold:

1. ARPMM grows at 2-4% annually for 24 months. This is the most fragile assumption because it depends on consumer willingness to pay modestly higher prices and GYM's ability to retain members long enough to capture the pricing benefit. One price increase cycle that produces higher-than-expected churn would break this assumption.

2. New site ROIC holds at 25-30%. This depends on Latchem's pipeline quality and the property market availability of good-quality sites. If the best locations have been pre-empted by PureGym, ROIC falls. This is observable through site-by-site tracking.

3. The management team remains stable. Will Orr is the architect of the recovery. His departure — which would be a significant risk signal — is the key man risk. There is no disclosed succession planning for the CEO role.

SECTION 17 — SCEPTICAL / BEAR THESIS

The complete bear thesis for GYM at 182p:

GYM is a business that has recovered from a COVID-induced impairment and is now being priced as though the recovery will compound indefinitely. The valuation assumes: continued ARPMM growth, successful delivery of 75 new sites at 27-30% ROIC, and a multiple expansion from 7.6x to 9-10x.

Each assumption is plausible in isolation. Together, they require consistent execution in a market where the primary competitor (PureGym) is significantly larger, growing faster globally, and is backed by stronger financial sponsors.

The ARPMM deceleration from 7% to 4% over two years may be the first sign that the pricing power story is reaching its limit. If ARPMM continues to decelerate to 2% in FY2026, the EPS growth rate drops sharply (from 83% in FY2025 to perhaps 15-20%), the multiple re-rating narrative stalls, and the stock trades sideways or lower despite continued absolute progress.

The site expansion plan is ambitious. 75 new sites in 3 years requires flawless execution from a new CPO who has been in post for less than 18 months. Aldi is not the same as a gym. The conversion rates, lead times, and operational ramp-up of a gym site are meaningfully different from a food retailer. Latchem may be excellent but the assumption that his Aldi skills transfer perfectly to GYM is an untested hypothesis.

At 182p, the stock is pricing in execution of the bull case. The bear case — ARPMM at 2%, sites at 15/year, multiple at 7x — still implies limited downside from current prices. The genuine risk is not capital loss but opportunity cost: GYM plateaus at 180-200p while better-execution stories compound elsewhere.

Downside Risk Rating: YELLOW — Downside is limited by valuation support, but the upside story depends on sustained execution. The bear case is primarily a "disappointing outcome" rather than a "capital destruction" thesis.
