SECTION 1 — STAGE HISTORY: GYM 5-YEAR

GYM's five-year stage history is a classic recovery from Stage 4 destruction to Stage 2 advance. Understanding the stage progression frames the current setup in its proper context.

2020 (Pre-COVID): Stage 2 Uptrend

GYM listed in 2015 at 195p and reached approximately 350p by early 2020, driven by rapid site expansion. The business was in a strong Stage 2 uptrend through most of 2019.

2020 (COVID): Stage 4 Decline Begins

Government-mandated gym closures from March 2020 triggered a catastrophic Stage 4 decline. Revenue collapsed. The stock fell from approximately 280p in February 2020 to below 100p by May 2020 — a 65% decline in three months.

2020-2021 (Recovery and False Dawn): Stage 1 / Stage 2 Attempt

Reopening in 2020 and 2021 produced a partial recovery to approximately 220-250p by late 2021. This was a Stage 1 base-building and partial Stage 2 attempt. The recovery was fragile — dependent on the removal of COVID restrictions rather than genuine operational improvement.

2021-2023 (Deep Stage 4): The True Decline

Rising inflation, energy costs, and the residue of COVID-related debt produced a sustained Stage 4 decline. The stock fell from the 220p range in late 2021 to a low of approximately 85-95p by early-mid 2023. This was the structural low — the point at which the business was priced for distress.

Mid-2023 (Stage 1 Base Formation)

From the low of approximately 90p in spring 2023, the stock entered Stage 1 base formation. Will Orr's appointment in September 2023 and the first evidence of operational improvement under the Next Chapter strategy attracted early buyers. Volume picked up on the right side of the base.

Late 2023 to Present (Stage 2 Advance)

Stage 2 began in earnest in late 2023 / early 2024 as fundamental results confirmed the strategic change was working. The stock has advanced from approximately 95-100p at the Stage 1/2 transition to 182p at present — an advance of approximately 85-90% from the Stage 2 start.

The 52-week low of 131.2p establishes the most recent lower end of the Stage 2 range. The distance from 131.2p to the current 182p is +38.7% — confirming sustained upward progress within the Stage 2 structure.
