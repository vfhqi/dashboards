[W] XVIVO Perfusion -- Earnings & Fundamental Delivery [C]

Query #5-C | Triaging Level | 19-Apr-2026

Key Findings

  • 2025 was a tale of two halves: Q1 strong (+14% organic), Q2 a sharp miss (-11% organic), Q3-Q4 recovery (+6% and +10% organic ex-heart respectively). Full year: SEK 812m revenue, -1% in SEK, +4% in local currency.
  • Full-year revenue declined vs FY2024 (SEK 822m) due to SEK headwinds and the absence of large heart trial revenue that inflated 2024 comps (Q4 2024 included SEK 60m heart sales).
  • Abdominal segment was the standout performer all year: liver +31% FY, kidney +79% in Q3, record Q4 quarter. This is the emerging growth engine.
  • US service business was a persistent drag: -21% organic in Q4. Management acknowledged it "needs drastic improvement" and initiated restructuring in H2.
  • Profitability inflected sharply in Q4: 25% EBITA margin (record), up from low-teens in Q2-Q3. Driven by operating leverage on recovering revenue plus cost discipline.
  • Heart technology is the medium-term catalyst: US PRESERVE trial enrollment completed 5 months early (141 patients, 14 sites). PMA submission to FDA targeted by Q2 2026. European 12-month follow-up data (published Apr 2026 in European Heart Journal) showed 92% vs 86% survival, 38% risk reduction in severe complications.
  • Q1 2026 report delayed to 24-Apr-2026 to coincide with ISHLT PRESERVE data presentation -- management signalling confidence in heart data.
  • Analyst consensus is constructive: "Outperform" / "Strong Buy" ratings from 4-5 covering analysts. Price targets range SEK 460-567.
  • Key risk is US lung market cyclicality: Q2 2025 exposed dependence on US transplant volumes and showed how quickly organic growth can turn negative.
  • FY2025 EBITDA baseline set at 20%. Management guided 2026 as a "growth investment year" -- top-line growth prioritised over margin expansion.

1. LTM Beat/Miss Record

XVIVO's last four quarters (Q1-Q4 2025) tell a clear V-shaped delivery story:

Q1 2025 (reported Apr 2025): Beat. Net sales SEK 219m, +18% reported, +14% organic. Thoracic SEK 142m. Abdominal SEK 57m. US lung sales +26%. Stock surged ~9% pre-market on the result. This was a clean beat that reinforced the growth narrative from the strong FY2024.

Q2 2025 (reported Jul 2025): Significant miss. Net sales SEK 178m, -15% reported, -11% organic. The worst quarter in recent history. US lung market destocking, weak heart sales (trial comps rolling off), and a -6% FX headwind combined to produce a sharp disappointment. The stock fell ~2% on results day, but the real damage was to narrative credibility. Analysts had to reset expectations. Management initiated cost restructuring.

Q3 2025 (reported Oct 2025): Partial recovery, roughly in line. Net sales SEK 189m, -5% in SEK, +1% local currency, +6% organic ex-heart. Abdominal carried the quarter (liver +34%, kidney +79%). Thoracic still soft. Adjusted EBITDA margin only 9%, reflecting restructuring costs and investment spending. Stock edged up ~1.3% on relief that the trough was behind them.

Q4 2025 (reported Jan 2026): Beat. Net sales SEK 226m, +10% organic ex-heart, +12% organic ex-heart in local currency. Record EBITA margin of 25%. Record operating cash flow of SEK 87m in the quarter. Abdominal had a record quarter (liver +36%). Lung showed recovery. The result validated the restructuring and signalled the business was back on track.

Summary verdict: 2 beats, 1 miss, 1 in-line. The miss (Q2) was severe enough to reset expectations, making the subsequent "beats" partly a function of lowered consensus rather than pure outperformance. Net-net, delivery has been uneven but the trajectory since Q2 has been upward.

2. Guidance Track Record

XVIVO does not issue formal quantitative revenue or earnings guidance. Instead, management communicates through:

  • Long-term ESG/volume target: Machine perfusion volume growth of at least 12% per year. This is more of a structural aspiration than near-term guidance.
  • Qualitative outlook statements: On each earnings call, management provides directional commentary on segment trends, investment priorities, and profitability trajectory.
  • FY2025 EBITDA baseline: After Q4, management explicitly set 20% EBITDA as the 2025 baseline and stated that 2026 would prioritise top-line growth over maintaining the 25% Q4 margin level. This is an important signal -- they are pre-emptively managing down margin expectations for 2026 to fund commercial investment (field force, US service rebuild, heart launch preparation).

Credibility assessment: Management's qualitative guidance has been directionally accurate but they did not adequately flag the Q2 2025 risk. The US lung destocking was partially foreseeable given transplant volume data, yet the earnings call commentary before Q2 did not sufficiently prepare the market. Post-Q2, management's tone became more measured and realistic. The Q4 recovery suggests the restructuring actions taken in H2 were appropriate. On balance, guidance credibility is moderate -- they are transparent on strategy but not always ahead of near-term operational headwinds.

3. US vs Europe Growth Decomposition

The geographic decomposition reveals two distinct growth profiles:

United States:

  • US lung (XPS/EVLP) remains the largest single revenue driver. Q1 2025 saw +26% US lung growth, but Q2 collapsed due to destocking and reduced transplant waiting lists at some centres. Recovery was visible by Q4.
  • US kidney disposables grew +47% in Q2 and maintained strong momentum through H2. This is an underappreciated growth vector.
  • US service business was the major drag: -21% organic in Q4 2025. Management described this as needing "drastic improvement" and restructured the commercial team in the US, adding surgical capacity and reorganising field force to be closer to customers.
  • 4 new EVLP accounts added in the US during Q2 despite the weak quarter, suggesting underlying adoption is continuing.
  • Net: US growth was volatile in 2025. The lung franchise is cyclical around transplant volumes, kidney is emerging, and service needs fixing. Heart (post-PMA) will be the US game-changer.

Europe:

  • Liver was the star: +32% in Q2, +34% in Q3, +36% in Q4, +31% full year. XVIVO has reached ~25% penetration in core EU liver markets, suggesting significant runway remains.
  • Kidney also contributed meaningfully in Europe, though exact European kidney numbers are less disaggregated in public disclosures.
  • Thoracic/lung in Europe appears more stable than the US -- the European lung transplant market does not exhibit the same destocking volatility.
  • Heart: European multicenter trial (NIHP2019) completed with strong 12-month data. CE Mark submission is pending. Revenue contribution from heart in Europe currently limited to trial-related sales and the Australian market (~SEK 10m/quarter in Q3).

Geographic mix conclusion: Europe is the steady growth base (driven by abdominal expansion), while the US is higher-variance (lung cyclicality, service drag, but kidney and heart upside). For 2026-2027, the US will determine the growth trajectory -- heart PMA approval could step-change the US revenue profile.

4. Product-Level Performance

XPS (Lung Perfusion System):

  • XPS remains the core franchise. US FDA-approved since 2019. Adoption continues via new EVLP accounts (4 added in Q2 2025 alone), but revenue is volume-dependent on transplant activity.
  • Q1 2025: strong, +26% US lung. Q2: sharp decline as US transplant volumes softened. Q3-Q4: gradual recovery. Organic growth ex-heart was +12% in Q4, suggesting lung volumes normalised.
  • Only ~23% of donor lungs offered in the US are currently transplanted, creating a large addressable market opportunity for EVLP technology.

Abdominal Products (Liver & Kidney):

  • Liver machine perfusion: the breakout product line. +31% FY2025 growth. Record Q4. 25% penetration in core EU markets with significant headroom.
  • Kidney: US kidney disposables +47% (Q2), +79% (Q3). Smaller base but growing rapidly.
  • Kidney Assist Transport is being prepared for a 2027 full-scale launch alongside Heart Assist Transport.

Heart Assist Transport:

  • Not yet a revenue contributor at scale. Heart sales in Q4 2024 were SEK 60m (largely trial-related), creating a tough comp in 2025. By Q3 2025, heart sales were ~SEK 10m (mostly Australia).
  • Clinical progress is the story: US PRESERVE trial enrolled 141 patients at 14 sites, completing 5 months ahead of schedule. One-year follow-up data being presented at ISHLT April 2026.
  • European trial (NIHP2019) 12-month data published in European Heart Journal (Apr 2026): 92% survival in XVIVO group vs 86% in control (ice); 33% severe complication rate vs 47% (38% risk reduction). Compelling data.
  • PMA submission to FDA targeted by Q2 2026. CE Mark in Europe also pending. Full commercial launch targeted for 2027.
  • This is the long-dated catalyst. If approved, heart could eventually rival or exceed the lung franchise in revenue potential.

Service Business:

  • US service (on-site perfusion services provided to transplant centres): SEK 26m in Q4 2024, declining -21% organic by Q4 2025.
  • Management restructured the US commercial team in H2 2025, adding field force closer to customers. The fix is underway but not yet visible in numbers.

6. Management Commentary Themes

Key themes from recent earnings calls (Q2-Q4 2025 and year-end):

1. "Valley of Disappointment" narrative: After Q2 2025, management and commentators explicitly framed XVIVO as in a "valley of disappointment" -- the gap between the end of heart trial revenue and the eventual commercial heart launch. CEO Christopher Rosenblatt acknowledged the challenging period but maintained conviction in the long-term strategy.

2. Restructuring and cost discipline: From Q2 onwards, management emphasised cost restructuring -- reducing overhead and reinvesting in customer-facing field force. The mantra was "invest close to the customer." By Q4, the benefits were visible in the 25% EBITA margin.

3. Abdominal as the near-term growth engine: Consistent messaging that liver and kidney machine perfusion is the secular growth story for the next 2-3 years. 25% penetration in core EU liver markets with significant headroom. Kidney launch in 2027.

4. Heart technology confidence: Strong emphasis on heart as the transformational product. Enrollment completing 5 months early was framed as a confidence signal. The delay of Q1 2026 reporting to coincide with ISHLT PRESERVE data presentation signals management wants the market to focus on heart progress.

5. US service business honesty: Unusually candid commentary that the US service business "needs drastic improvement." This transparency is a positive signal -- management is not hiding the problem.

6. Currency headwinds: SEK weakness against USD has created a persistent FX drag on reported numbers. Management has guided investors to focus on local currency and organic growth metrics.

7. 2026 as an investment year: The clearest forward-looking message from Q4 was that 2026 will prioritise growth over profitability. The 20% EBITDA baseline (not the 25% Q4 peak) is the starting point. This is managing expectations for margin investment.

Tone assessment: Measured and honest since Q2. Management did not oversell during the weak period and has been appropriately cautious about near-term profitability while maintaining conviction on the strategic direction. The overall tone has shifted from defensive (Q2) to cautiously optimistic (Q4 and into 2026).

7. Analyst Reactions & Consensus Changes

Coverage universe: XVIVO is covered by approximately 5-7 analysts, including Stifel, DNB Carnegie, Danske Bank, Pareto Securities, Redeye, SEB, and Handelsbanken.

Consensus rating: "Outperform" / "Strong Buy." 4-5 analysts rate the stock a Buy, with no Sells currently visible in aggregated data.

Price targets: Range of SEK 460-567 (average ~SEK 519) based on MarketScreener data. A separate source cites an average of SEK 320 (range SEK 225-408), which may reflect an older or differently constituted panel.

Post-Q2 2025 reaction: The sharp miss triggered estimate cuts across the board. Analysts reduced FY2025 revenue and earnings expectations. Some commentary framed the Q2 result as a "one-off" driven by US market dynamics, while others flagged concern about the structural dependence on US lung volumes.

Post-Q4 2025 reaction: The strong Q4 result and record margin restored confidence. The "valley of disappointment" narrative was updated with the view that the trough was behind the company. Heart data progress (PRESERVE enrollment completion) was cited as a de-risking catalyst.

Key analyst themes:

  • Heart PMA approval is the dominant valuation driver in most models.
  • The abdominal segment is increasingly recognised as a standalone growth story.
  • US service business fix is a "show me" story -- analysts want to see numbers improve before giving credit.
  • Most analysts model a return to double-digit organic growth in 2026-2027 on the back of heart commercialisation and continued abdominal expansion.

Consensus estimates for 2026 (indicative): Revenue consensus appears to be in the SEK 900-950m range (implying ~10-17% growth), with EBITDA margins modestly above 20%. These estimates will be sensitive to the ISHLT heart data presentation and Q1 2026 results (24-Apr-2026).

8. Upcoming Catalysts & Outlook

Immediate (next 30 days):

  • ISHLT Annual Meeting, Toronto (April 2026): Presentation of preliminary one-year clinical data from the US PRESERVE trial. This is the single most important near-term catalyst. Positive data would de-risk the PMA submission pathway and could trigger analyst upgrades.
  • Q1 2026 interim report (24-Apr-2026): Delayed from 22-Apr specifically to align with ISHLT. Market will watch for: (a) continuation of organic growth recovery, (b) US service business improvement, (c) any quantitative guidance for FY2026.
  • European Heart Journal publication (Apr 2026): 12-month follow-up from European NIHP2019 trial already published -- 92% survival vs 86% control. This publication strengthens the regulatory and scientific evidence base.

Medium-term (2026):

  • PMA submission to FDA: Targeted by Q2 2026 (i.e., imminently). A successful submission initiates the FDA review process, which typically takes 6-12 months.
  • European CE Mark for heart: Pending. Timeline not precisely disclosed but management has framed it as a 2026 priority.
  • US service business turnaround: Must show improvement in Q1-Q2 2026 numbers to validate the restructuring.
  • Continued abdominal segment execution: Liver and kidney momentum needs to be maintained. Any deceleration here would be a negative signal.

Long-term (2027+):

  • Full-scale commercial launch of Heart Assist Transport and Kidney Assist Transport (2027): This is the transformational event. Management is building the organisation for this launch through 2026.
  • US heart market: If PMA is approved, XVIVO enters the US heart preservation market. TransMedics is the incumbent competitor. The PRESERVE data (and European data) suggest XVIVO's HOPE-based approach may have clinical superiority.

Key risks to monitor:

1. PRESERVE data disappoints at ISHLT -- would delay PMA submission and damage the investment case.

2. US lung transplant volumes remain soft, dragging organic growth.

3. US service business fails to recover, suggesting structural rather than cyclical issues.

4. FX headwinds persist (SEK weakness vs USD/EUR).

5. Competition from TransMedics in heart and lung markets.
