Key Findings

1. Revenue tripled in three years (SEK 258m in 2021 to SEK 822m in 2024, 47% CAGR), but 2025 saw a sharp deceleration to SEK 812m (-1% in SEK, +4% local currency), driven by US heart trial revenue roll-off and FX headwinds.

2. Underlying organic growth ex-heart trials was +8% in 2025, anchored by Abdominal (+30% local currency) — liver and kidney are now the structural growth engine, partly offsetting a -2% decline in Thoracic.

3. Margin trajectory is positive but non-linear. Gross margin stabilised at 73-77% (2024: 75%, 2025: ~74%). Adjusted EBITDA margin expanded from ~17% (2023) to 22% (2024) then retreated to 20% (2025) on lower volumes and elevated R&D spend.

4. EPS collapsed from SEK 5.47 (2024) to SEK 0.80 (2025), reflecting one-off intangible write-downs, heavy clinical trial investment (SEK 153m R&D capex), and currency drag. The 2024 EPS was itself boosted by non-recurring items (net income SEK 172m included revaluation gains).

5. Balance sheet is solid but depleting. Cash fell from SEK ~450m (end-2024) to ~SEK 280m (Q3 2025), with FY2025 total cash flow of SEK -85m. The EUR 20m revolving credit facility provides a backstop.

6. TransMedics (TMDX) is 7x XVIVO's revenue and pulling away — $606m in 2025 (+37% YoY), 60% gross margin, 18% operating margin, first full year of positive free cash flow ($152m). TMDX trades at ~6.7x EV/Sales vs XVIVO at ~11-12x.

7. Consensus expects recovery in 2026: analysts project ~SEK 1.0bn+ revenue and meaningful EPS recovery (consensus ~SEK 1.07), implying 25-30% revenue growth and margin expansion as heart trial costs moderate and kidney/liver scale.

8. XVIVO trades at a premium to both TMDX and medtech sector averages, reflecting its earlier-stage growth profile and European niche positioning. At SEK 301 / SEK 9.45bn market cap, the stock prices in substantial execution on the abdominal expansion.

9. Q4 2025 showed inflection: SEK 226m revenue (+10% organic local currency), 25% EBITDA margin, record SEK 87m operating cash flow — directionally encouraging ahead of Q1 2026 results (24 April 2026).

10. Consensus revisions have been net negative over the past 12 months (average target cut ~16%), but the most recent quarter beat (Q4 2025: SEK 226m vs SEK 214m consensus) may stabilise sentiment.

XVIVO experienced extraordinary revenue acceleration from 2021 to 2024, driven by the US FDA approval of its XPS lung perfusion system and expanding adoption of STEEN Solution. The 2022 spike (+61% in SEK) benefited from a weak krona amplifying USD-denominated US revenues. Growth decelerated sharply in 2025 for three identifiable reasons: (i) the wind-down of US heart trial revenue (a high-value but non-recurring revenue stream), (ii) significant SEK appreciation against USD, and (iii) temporary softness in US lung transplant volumes.

2. Margin Evolution

#### Gross Margin

| Year | Gross Margin | Notes |

|------|-------------|-------|

| 2022 | ~72% (est.) | Product mix shifting toward disposables |

| 2023 | 74% | Improving product mix |

| 2024 | 75% | Thoracic disposables GM ~85%, Abdominal ~65% |

| 2025 | ~74% (est.) | FX drag and mix shift toward lower-margin abdominal |

Gross margins are structurally high due to the single-use consumable model (disposable perfusion sets). Thoracic disposables carry ~85% gross margins — among the highest in medtech. As the Abdominal segment grows faster (at ~65% GM), blended gross margins may face modest dilution, though volume leverage should partially offset this.

#### EBITDA and Operating Margins

| Year | Adj. EBITDA (SEKm) | Adj. EBITDA Margin | EBIT (SEKm) | EBIT Margin |

|------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------|-------------|

| 2022 | ~55 (est.) | ~13% | 2.3 | 0.6% |

| 2023 | ~103 | ~17% | 5.4 | 0.9% |

| 2024 | 183 | 22% | 88.4 | 10.7% |

| 2025 | 159 | 20% | n/a | n/a |

The margin story from 2022-2024 was one of accelerating operating leverage: revenue growth of 38-44% dropped through to EBITDA at 22% (from 13%). However, 2025 broke the trend — EBITDA margin contracted by 2pp despite the company not reducing gross margins materially. The culprit: elevated R&D spending (SEK 153m in investment cash flow, including US clinical trials for the heart preservation device) and lower revenue absorption of fixed costs.

The gap between EBITDA and EBIT is wide, reflecting heavy amortisation of capitalised development costs and the SEK 20m intangible write-down in 2024. On a reported EBIT basis, XVIVO has been barely profitable in recent years.

Path to sustainable profitability requires: (i) heart trial costs to moderate post-approval, (ii) abdominal revenue to scale beyond SEK 250-300m to absorb fixed manufacturing/commercial costs, and (iii) FX stability or hedging.

#### Strategic Differences

TransMedics operates a vertically integrated model: it builds the OCS device, runs a national logistics network (NOP), and collects per-transplant service fees. This creates high switching costs and recurring revenue. TMDX has FDA PMA approval for lung, heart, and liver — the only company with all three. Revenue is almost entirely US-based.

XVIVO operates a consumables/disposable model: single-use perfusion sets and preservation solutions sold to transplant centres. It has broader geographic diversification (US + Europe) and is the European market leader in liver perfusion. XVIVO's heart preservation device (hypothermic approach) is in US clinical trials but not yet approved.

#### TMDX Risks Worth Noting

TransMedics has faced regulatory and governance scrutiny:

  • DOJ investigation into billing practices (2024-ongoing)
  • Questions about NOP logistics pricing
  • High customer concentration risk
  • Share price declined significantly from 2024 peaks

These issues create potential opportunity for XVIVO if TMDX faces operational disruption, though XVIVO's technology is complementary (hypothermic vs normothermic) rather than directly substitutive.

The valuation premium (11-12x EV/Sales vs 6-8x for growth medtech) reflects the market's expectation of a long runway of high-teens to mid-twenties revenue growth as the organ perfusion market expands.

European medtech comparables trading at similar revenue multiples include companies like Surgical Science (robotic surgery simulation, ~10-12x), Vitrolife (IVF, ~8-10x), and Chemometec (cell counting, ~12-15x). XVIVO sits comfortably within this Scandinavian growth medtech cohort, though its current growth rate (4% LCY) is below the cohort average, which creates valuation vulnerability if the recovery thesis does not play out.

The single strongest predictor of medtech multiples is organic revenue growth rate. For every percentage point of organic growth above the 5-6% industry average, companies typically earn 1-2 additional turns of EV/EBITDA. XVIVO's re-rating therefore depends directly on returning to double-digit organic growth.

8. Fundamental Conclusion

The investment thesis for XVIVO rests on three pillars:

Pillar 1 — Abdominal Growth Engine (High Confidence). Liver (+31%) and kidney (+47% US) are growing rapidly and have significant TAM ahead. XVIVO is the European market leader in liver perfusion and is building a credible US kidney business. This segment should compound at 25-35% for several years.

Pillar 2 — Heart Device FDA Approval (Medium Confidence, High Impact). The hypothermic heart preservation device in US clinical trials represents a potential step-change in the revenue trajectory. FDA approval would open a large addressable market where TransMedics currently has the only approved product. Timeline: 2027-2028 at earliest.

Pillar 3 — Operating Leverage at Scale (Medium Confidence). At SEK 1bn+ revenue (expected 2026), XVIVO should begin to demonstrate sustainable EBITDA margins of 25%+ and generate meaningful free cash flow. The fixed cost base (R&D, commercial infrastructure, manufacturing) was built for a larger revenue base.

Key risks:

  • Heart trial failure or delay (removes Pillar 2 and a portion of the valuation premium)
  • TransMedics competitive aggression in liver/kidney
  • FX exposure (57% of revenue in USD, costs partly in SEK/EUR)
  • Valuation is pricing in recovery — limited room for further disappointment

Bottom line: XVIVO is a high-quality, high-margin consumables business in an expanding structural market. 2025 was a transition year, not a structural break. The Q4 2025 inflection and 2026 consensus estimates suggest a return to growth. However, at SEK 301 and 11x trailing EV/Sales, much of the recovery is already priced in. The stock is interesting but not cheap. The fulcrum question is whether abdominal growth and heart device progress can sustain 25%+ organic revenue growth to justify the current multiple.
