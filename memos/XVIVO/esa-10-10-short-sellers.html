C. Reimbursement Shortfall (MEDIUM IMPACT, MEDIUM PROBABILITY)

CMS reimbursement codes effective July 2025 are positive. But actual payment adequacy and hospital willingness to adopt at scale remain unproven. Reimbursement disappointment would slow the US adoption curve.

TransMedics, despite its Scorpion trouble, remains the dominant US player in normothermic perfusion with a larger installed base and existing PMA approvals. If TMDX stabilises and defends aggressively, XVIVO's US heart market penetration could be slower than expected.

10. SCEPTICAL REBUTTAL

If a short-seller were to build a bear case on XVIVO today, it would likely centre on:

1. "The US lung franchise is ex-growth." H1 2025 showed organic decline. The EVLP market in the US may be approaching penetration limits at major centres. New customer additions (4 in H1 2025) are slowing. Without continuous new centre adoption, the core revenue engine stalls.

2. "The heart opportunity is priced in but not assured." The market is pricing a successful PRESERVE PMA and rapid US rollout. But FDA timelines slip. Reimbursement may be inadequate. TransMedics already has an installed base. The option value embedded in the stock price may never materialise.

3. "Profitability is a mirage." Yes, EBITDA margin hit 25% in Q4 2025. But total cash flow was negative. R&D spend of SEK 153M is necessary to maintain the pipeline. Strip out heart trial revenue recognition and the underlying business isn't generating free cash flow yet.

4. "Management is navigating its first real crisis." CEO Rosenblad has been in role since 2022. The CFO just moved roles. The US commercial organisation is still being built. This team has never dealt with a major FDA setback or a competitive price war.

5. "The TMDX comparison is actually negative for XVIVO." TMDX's collapse lowered the valuation ceiling for the entire organ preservation sector. Institutional investors burned by TMDX are less willing to pay premium multiples for XVIVO, regardless of fundamental differences.

Honest assessment: These are legitimate concerns, not fabricated bearish narratives. A thoughtful short thesis could be constructed. The reason no one has published one is structural (Nordic small-cap, limited liquidity, no options market) rather than fundamental.
