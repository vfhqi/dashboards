Estimate Revisions & Consensus Calibration

Rating: Red

The Prolonged Earnings Downgrade Cycle

Nestle's earnings trajectory over the last twelve months has been defined by a severe and extended downgrade cycle. Since the onset of the pandemic, consensus EPS estimates for the 2024-2026 period have collapsed by 19% to 22%. Analysts maintain a highly sceptical posture, warning that the company has not yet reached the bottom of this structural downgrade cycle. Negative estimate momentum continues to plague the stock, driven by gross margin uncertainty stemming from severe commodity inflation.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Recent L3M and L1M revisions further reflect a rapidly deteriorating near-term outlook for the core business. Analysts forecast that Nestle's EPS will decline by 9% in 2025. This sharp downward revision is driven by a confluence of worsening currency headwinds, impending US tariffs, and steep cost inflation for essential inputs like cocoa and green coffee. For 2026, forecasts have been aggressively tempered to only low-single-digit EPS growth, as improving underlying operations are expected to be offset by further currency drags and specific execution failures, such as an infant formula product recall.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Feb

Historical Misses vs. Future Guidance

A core component of the prevailing bear narrative is management's repeated inability to meet its own forward guidance. There have been several high-profile operational missteps over the past 18 months, leaving the company consistently unable to provide organic sales growth targets that it can reliably meet, let alone exceed. In a glaring example of this trend, Nestle missed consensus sales expectations by 1% at its 3Q24 results, delivering weaker organic sales growth of just 1.9% against a consensus expectation of 3.3%.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Consequently, management was forced to aggressively cut its 2024 outlook during the third quarter. The company downgraded its organic sales growth expectation to approximately 2%, abandoning its previous floor of "at least 3%". Furthermore, the operating margin outlook was reduced to a 30-basis-point year-over-year decline, directly contradicting earlier guidance of a moderate improvement. This fundamental reset triggered mid-single-digit consensus EPS downgrades for the 2024 fiscal year.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Consensus Calibration: What the Market is Getting Wrong

The street consensus remains structurally flawed in its assessment of currency impacts over the long term. Consensus models typically account for foreign exchange headwinds only two years into the future before assuming a zero-impact terminal state. Sceptical analysts argue this is a material miscalibration, modelling an ongoing currency headwind of approximately 2% per annum indefinitely. Because consensus models effectively ignore this perpetual drag, they derive an inflated functional currency sales compound annual growth rate (CAGR), which artificially artificially props up discounted cash flow valuations.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Additionally, the broader market appears to be underestimating the margin destruction associated with the current commodity cycle. Nestle faces staggering cocoa cost inflation of approximately 117% into 2025 as elevated spot prices filter through the income statement. The market consensus assumes Nestle will be able to seamlessly push these costs onto consumers. However, cautious analysts point out that Europe is Nestle's largest market for confectionery, a region where it is notoriously difficult to negotiate price increases with consolidated retailers. The assumption that Nestle will be unable to fully recover this cocoa inflation leads bearish analysts to position their gross margin and EPS forecasts materially below the street consensus for 2025 and 2026.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Subsidiary Divergence: Emerging Markets vs. Global Core

While the consolidated parent company faces a severe downgrade cycle, estimate revisions for certain emerging market subsidiaries highlight a stark geographic divergence in operational momentum.

In India, sell-side analysts have been actively upgrading top-line and bottom-line estimates following sequential earnings beats. Analysts upgraded EPS estimates for Nestle India by 7% to 10% for the FY27-28 period, driven by a moderation in localized commodity baskets and aggressive rural distribution expansion. This localized margin expansion and volume acceleration stand in direct contrast to the margin compression and volume stagnation experienced by the global parent.

Conversely, Southeast Asian operations are mirroring the parent company's struggles with margin realization. Analysts covering Nestle Malaysia were forced to lower their FY26 and FY27 earnings estimates by 2% to 8%. This localized downgrade cycle was driven by higher-than-expected operating expenses and prolonged consumer hesitancy, tracking the broader global narrative of a consumer base increasingly unwilling to absorb relentless price hikes.

The Dominant Bull Narrative

Rating: Green

The dominant bull narrative for Nestle centers on a comprehensive strategic reset designed to structurally accelerate organic sales growth. Analysts anchoring the bullish perspective argue that the company is taking painful but necessary steps to shed underperforming assets and aggressively reinvest in core, high-margin categories. This optimistic thesis is built upon three foundational pillars: the strategic turnaround under new CEO Laurent Freixe, the structural growth engine of the Purina PetCare division, and a renewed commitment to aggressive portfolio simplification.

The Freixe Turnaround: Resetting to Grow

The appointment of company veteran Laurent Freixe as CEO in late 2024 marked a decisive pivot away from the margin-first strategy of his predecessor, Mark Schneider. Upon taking the helm, Freixe immediately moved to reset market expectations by lowering the 2024 organic sales growth target to approximately 2%, the lowest annual rate seen in decades. Crucially, the medium-term trading operating margin target was lowered to 17%, down from the previous goal of 17.5% to 18.5%. This deliberate sacrifice of near-term profitability is designed to free up capital for a significant ramp-up in advertising and marketing expenditures, which are deemed essential to win back market share lost to cheaper alternatives during the recent inflationary cycle. To help offset these aggressive marketing investments, management has also announced a sweeping cost-cutting initiative aimed at securing 2.5 billion Swiss francs in savings by 2027.

Analysts at Rothschild & Co Redburn note that the November 2024 Capital Markets Day provided the market with crucial confidence that the company's recent operational missteps are isolated rather than systemic. The firm highlights that Nestle's newly optimized portfolio is well-positioned to deliver sustainable organic sales growth of 4% or more annually. This growth is expected to be healthily balanced between volume/mix improvements and pricing power.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Mar

Purina PetCare: The Structural Growth Engine

A central component of the bullish thesis is the continued dominance and expansion of the Purina PetCare division, which stands out as a high-margin, fast-growing category.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Mar

To capitalize on the structural tailwinds of global pet adoption and product premiumization, Nestle is currently executing a massive capital expenditure program. The company has committed to a CHF 3 billion global investment plan spanning 2022 to 2025 to expand its pet care manufacturing footprint. This includes dedicating $2 billion specifically to the highly lucrative US market, alongside targeted capacity expansions in emerging regions. With an estimated 20% share of the global pet food market, Purina is viewed as a highly defensible asset that will drive long-term value creation.

The momentum of the pet care portfolio is particularly evident in key emerging markets like India, where the division recently reported its highest-ever turnover since integration.

Analysts at Nuvama Institutional Equities emphasize that Purina's Indian operations are consistently delivering high double-digit growth. This hyper-growth is being driven by strong consumer reception for premium cat food brands like Purina Felix and Friskies, as well as the Pro Plan dog food range.

Huatai Research points out that by the end of 2024, the Pet Care division had grown to account for an impressive 20.6% of Nestle's total consolidated revenue.

Portfolio Simplification & Capital Allocation

The third pillar of the bull case relies on management's willingness to aggressively simplify the corporate structure and divest dilutive businesses. In a major strategic move, Nestle announced that its struggling mineral water operations, which include brands like Maison Perrier and Acqua Panna, will be separated into a standalone business effective January 1, 2025. This water segment, which represents less than 4% of total revenue, has recently been plagued by supply constraints and contamination issues, and its separation will allow management to evaluate strategic partnership opportunities without distracting from the core food and beverage portfolio.

Rothschild & Co Redburn analysts highlight further simplification efforts, noting the planned sale of the company's remaining ice cream business to joint venture partner Froneri, a transaction expected to close in the second quarter of 2026. Furthermore, bullish investors frequently point to the potential divestment of Nestle's US frozen food business, which includes brands like DiGiorno and Stouffers. This specific division has been a persistent drag on the company's top-line performance, delivering only about 1% organic sales growth per annum over the past decade.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Mar

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Finally, the bull narrative is sweetened by the optionality of Nestle's 20% strategic stake in French cosmetics giant L'Oreal, an investment the company has held since 1974. A partial or complete sale of this highly lucrative asset could provide incoming CEO Laurent Freixe with massive cash proceeds to fund aggressive share buyback programs, offering a significant near-term catalyst for the stock while the operational turnaround takes hold.

The Dominant Bear Narrative

Rating: Green

The dominant bear narrative surrounding Nestle is anchored in the premise that the company is enduring a painful, protracted operational reset rather than a temporary cyclical dip. Analysts driving the sceptical view characterize 2025 as a fundamental "transition year" hampered by a weak consumer backdrop, severe margin constraints, and the stark reality that historical pricing power has evaporated.

Rothschild & Co Redburn cautions that over the longer term, a combination of structural currency headwinds, a stretched balance sheet, and a multitude of execution missteps will fundamentally limit Nestle's earnings per share growth potential.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Mar

IIFL Research shares a similarly cautious view, attributing the company's recent subdued performance to fundamentally weak urban demand and the difficult anniversarisation of past price hikes.

Volume Recovery Risk and Geographic Drags

The bedrock of the bear case rests on Nestle's persistent inability to sustainably drive Real Internal Growth (RIG), the company's metric for volume and product mix.

Rothschild & Co Redburn highlights that a surprise strategic transformation in China, aimed at aggressively reducing excess inventory levels at local distributors, will heavily weigh on the company's overall RIG for at least a twelve-month period.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 1 Aug 25

Maybank Investment Banking Group adds another layer of volume risk, noting that consumer boycotts driven by geopolitical tensions have historically driven down sales volumes for Nestle's core brands in key emerging markets.

Furthermore, Rothschild & Co Redburn points out that execution failures, such as a recent infant formula product recall linked to contamination, are acting as a direct drag on organic sales growth, further complicating the volume recovery narrative.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Feb

Systematix Institutional Research channel checks corroborate this localized weakness, revealing that the core nutrition and baby food segments are actively declining in certain regions, with flagship brands like Cerelac dropping by 10% to 15% year-over-year.

Private Label Competition and Widening Price Gaps

As consumer elasticity normalizes, Nestle is acutely suffering from aggressive market share incursions by private label brands.

Rothschild & Co Redburn details that Nestle implemented several rounds of high-single-digit to low-double-digit price increases across its portfolio to combat inflation. This divergent pricing strategy significantly widened the absolute price gap between Nestle's premium products and private-label alternatives, directly resulting in higher consumer elasticity and subsequent volume share losses.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 25 Mar

Reinvestment Drag on Margins

A central tenet of the bearish thesis is that Nestle's profitability will suffer materially as the company attempts to buy back the market share it lost during its aggressive pricing phase.

Sunidhi Securities notes that incoming CEO Laurent Freixe explicitly lowered the company's medium-term trading operating margin target to 17%, a stark downgrade from the previous 17.5% to 18.5% goal. This margin reset is required because the company plans to dramatically increase its spending on advertising and marketing to desperately bolster its waning brand equity.

Rothschild & Co Redburn argues that 2025 will be a highly challenging year for profitability due to an inescapable timing lag between the realization of internal cost savings and this heavily upweighted marketing spend. The broker also warns that this reinvestment drag is severely compounded by steep commodity inflation in key inputs like robusta coffee beans and cocoa, alongside intensifying pushback on pricing from consolidated retail partners.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 22 Oct 24

Nuvama Institutional Equities echoes these commodity concerns, noting that milk prices—which account for approximately 20% of Nestle's raw material basket—remain firmly elevated, acting as an ongoing headwind to gross margin expansion.

GLP-1 Headwinds

Finally, sceptical analysts cite the rapid proliferation of GLP-1 weight-loss medications as a looming structural threat to Nestle's long-term terminal growth rate.

Rothschild & Co Redburn models a distinct downside risk to the company's equity if GLP-1 agonists gain broader consumer adoption, leading to persistent volume declines in the highly profitable US food category. The broker specifically estimates that approximately 14% of Nestle's total consolidated net sales are directly at risk from these medications, primarily within the US food, confectionery, and beverage portfolios.

Rothschild & Co Redburn • 4 Feb

However, Rothschild & Co Redburn maintains that despite proactive steps, further aggressive portfolio rationalization may be required to permanently insulate the business from the structural demand destruction caused by GLP-1 adoption.
