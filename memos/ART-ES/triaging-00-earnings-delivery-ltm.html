FY2025 Earnings Delivery — Beat Analysis

EBITDA beat of EUR3.5-8.0m vs guidance is the headline, but the margin beat of 80-130bps (15.8% vs 14.5-15.0% guided) is the more important signal — it means margin expansion accelerated in H2 beyond what management projected at interim results. Something structurally positive happened.

  • Judgement: FY25 was a comprehensive operational beat. Revenue in-range (EUR508m vs EUR500-515m), EBITDA significantly above range (EUR80.5m vs EUR72.5-77.0m), net profit +93.4%. The beat was margin-driven, not volume-driven — the highest-quality type of beat. [C+AS]
  • Judgement: The EBITDA margin of 15.8% blew through the strategic plan terminal target of 12.5-13.0% set for year-end 2026. A three-year plan was made obsolete in year one. [C+AS]
  • Analysis: H2 2025 margin of ~16.4% vs H1 ~15.2% (+120bps sequential) is the critical data point. Three forces drove the acceleration: [C]
    • Favourable product mix — more HV deliveries in H2, higher-margin GIS products from Hitachi JV
    • Capacity utilisation improvement — Mexican HV lab and Turkish plant at fuller capacity
    • Continued pricing discipline in supply-constrained markets (12-24 month lead times for HV ITs)
  • Analysis: Direct margin expanded from 35.0% (FY24) to 37.6% (FY25) — a 260bps improvement. Direct margin grew 18.6% on 15.3% revenue growth, confirming pricing and mix are both positive. [C+AS]
    • AS attributes this to "appropriate pricing strategy, positive volume effects, and structural equipment redesigns" — implying structural improvements, not just cyclical tailwinds. [AS]
  • Analysis: Below-the-line improvement amplified the EBITDA beat into a near-doubling of net profit. Lower financial expenses (debt at 0.3x vs 0.5x prior year) and potentially favourable tax effects drove the delta between +43.6% EBITDA growth and +93.4% NI growth. [C]
  • Revenue of EUR508m landed in the EUR500-515m range, slightly above midpoint — revenue was not the story. [C+AS]
  • Orders of EUR590.1m (+16.0%) were not explicitly guided but significantly outpaced revenue, pushing cumulative firm order portfolio to ATH of EUR355m. [C+AS]
  • Board approved 50% dividend payout (EUR0.3969/share, +140% YoY) — a positive surprise signalling confidence in forward cash generation. [AS]

Guidance Track Record — L3Y

The escalating beat pattern is the clearest signal of management quality: FY23 "on track," FY24 "ahead of plan," FY25 "plan rendered obsolete." Each year the beat has been larger. This is either deep operational conservatism (bullish) or a market environment so strong that even conservative management cannot help but beat (neutral until the cycle turns).

  • Judgement: Management has never missed guidance in three years as a public company. Every reporting period delivered at or above guidance. This is an exceptionally rare track record in European industrials and justifies a credibility premium. [C+AS]
  • Judgement: The strategic plan (2024-2026 "Energizing Futures Together") was exceeded on every metric a full year early — revenue 94-97% of terminal target, EBITDA margin 280-330bps above terminal target, EBITDA 15-24% above terminal target. Management is already designing the 2027-2029 cycle. [C+AS]

Regional & Segment Delivery

EMEA was the standout geography (+25.7%), but North America remains the forward growth engine. Network Reliability's order acceleration (26.2%) is the most bullish leading indicator — faster growth in the higher-margin diversification segment.

  • Judgement: Geographic diversification is both a strength (stability, risk mitigation) and a constraint (dilutes exposure to the hottest regional growth vectors, particularly US hyperscaler buildout). [C+AS]
  • Analysis: EMEA led FY25 with 25.7% revenue growth — likely driven by EU Green Deal infrastructure, post-Ukraine energy security investment, and RTR Energia consolidation. [C]
  • Analysis: North America accounted for ~30% of H1 orders with the company targeting 39% of revenue strategically. Industry experts note Arteche has captured approximately 20% US market share in specialised instrument transformers. [C+AS]
  • Analysis: Network Reliability order growth of 26.2% significantly exceeding revenue growth of 16.5% implies a rapidly building NR backlog. This is the diversification bet working — and if NR carries higher margins (likely given auxiliary relay leadership and power quality solutions), it is a margin-positive leading indicator. [C]
  • APAC order intake surged 25.5% to EUR88.8m, driven by Southeast Asia and Australia. [AS]
  • Segment revenue: M&M EUR368.7m (72%), Automation EUR86.1m (17%, +8.1% orders), Network Reliability EUR53.4m (11%, +26.2% orders). [AS]

Management Credibility Assessment

Credibility is HIGH — but untested in adversity. Every beat has occurred in a favourable market environment. The true measure of management quality comes when the cycle moderates.

  • Judgement: Management credibility is among the highest in European small/mid-cap industrials. Evidence: (1) never missed guidance, (2) systematic beats every period, (3) guidance raised at interims, (4) strategic plan exceeded a year early, (5) conservative communication style — factual, not promotional. [C+AS]
  • Judgement: The primary risk to credibility is that conservatism has been subsidised by an exceptionally strong market. The margin expansion from 11.5% to 15.8% in a single year is too large to attribute solely to management execution — the supply-constrained environment (12-24 month lead times, pricing power, capacity scarcity) created a margin tailwind that would have lifted any well-run manufacturer. [C]
    • Management's contribution was being positioned to exploit it — through prior investment in Turkey/Mexico capacity, the Hitachi JV, and product diversification. Credit to both execution and environment. [C]
  • Analysis: The uplisting from BME Growth to the main Spanish market (Feb 2026) is a corporate maturity milestone. Management views it as "absolute confirmation of the group's sustained growth, mature corporate governance, and adherence to top-tier market ESG standards." [AS]
    • However: no concurrent equity raise or public offering means free-float expansion relies entirely on legacy shareholder turnover. The promised liquidity improvement remains theoretical until trading volumes demonstrably decouple from historical patterns. [AS]
  • Analysis: Management tone from the FY25 results call was "overwhelmingly positive, confident, and resolute" — executives believe "the business profile is more consolidated than ever." R&D maintained above 3% of turnover, with ECO-X optical transformer redesign and SF6-free MV products as development priorities. [AS]

Cash Flow Delivery — The Hidden Bear Flag

This section surfaces the most important divergence between headline profitability and underlying cash generation. It is the single highest-priority item for further diligence.

  • Judgement: FY25 free cash flow of EUR3.4m on EUR80.5m EBITDA represents a catastrophic ~4% cash conversion ratio. This is a severe deterioration from EUR16.9m in FY24 and demands immediate investigation. Record profitability masking negative cash dynamics is an early warning signal. [AS]
  • Analysis: The FCF collapse is driven by three factors compounding simultaneously: [AS]
    • EUR25.3m capacity expansion capex (part of EUR60m 3-year programme)
    • EUR10.6m spent on RTR Energia acquisition
    • Elevated working capital absorption — inventory and receivables expanding to support EUR590m backlog
  • Analysis: H1 2025 cash flow confirms the strain: operating cash flow of EUR21.1m was entirely consumed by EUR30.1m investment outflows (EUR22.2m investing + EUR12.9m financing including EUR9.4m dividends), resulting in EUR13.9m net cash decline. Cash position dropped to EUR74.3m. [AS]
  • Analysis: Net debt rose from EUR25.6m (FY24-end) to EUR37.3m (H1 2025) — a 45.6% increase in six months, pushing leverage from 0.50x to 0.57x EBITDA. While still safe in absolute terms, the velocity of re-leveraging is concerning. [AS]
  • Analysis: The EUR0.6m loss from discontinued operations related to legacy legal contingencies in the Turnkey Solutions segment highlights lingering trailing risks from past structural realignments. [AS]
  • Action: Obtain full FY25 cash flow statement. Reconcile C's estimated EUR35-45m FCF with AS's reported EUR3.4m. Determine whether H2 2025 cash conversion improved from H1's negative trajectory. If FY25-end net debt was EUR22.7m (per company reported) vs H1's EUR37.3m, this implies a EUR14.6m H2 cash recovery — partial good news. [W]
  • Action: Monitor working capital as % of revenue — if NWC/revenue stabilises or improves through FY26, the growth-phase cash absorption is temporary. If it continues deteriorating, the aggressive dividend policy becomes unsustainable. [W]

Risks to Continued Delivery

Four structural risks that could break the delivery record: law of large numbers, margin ceiling, cycle dependency, and base effect comparison pressure. Additionally, AS surfaces two execution-level risks that C underweights: cash conversion sustainability and competitive intensity from Chinese manufacturers.

  • Judgement: The law of large numbers is real — revenue has nearly doubled from EUR282m to EUR508m. Getting from EUR508m to EUR700m+ requires the same absolute addition from a higher base. FY26 guidance of 9-15% growth already signals deceleration. [C]
  • Judgement: At 15.8% EBITDA margin, Arteche is approaching the upper end of what pure equipment manufacturers achieve. Pfisterer at 15.5% validates a ~15% structural ceiling for niche electrical suppliers. Room to 17-18% exists but requires sustained mix improvement, not just cyclical pricing. [C+AS]
  • Analysis: Base effect comparison risk is the near-term stock price threat. After +93.4% NI growth, even a "good" FY26 of 25-35% growth is a sharp deceleration. Markets re-rate growth stocks when the second derivative turns negative — growth still positive but decelerating. This is the classic "growth trap" afflicting companies transitioning from hyper-growth to merely strong growth. [C]
  • Analysis: Cycle dependency is the medium-term risk. Competitors are adding capacity: Hitachi Energy USD1bn US commitment, Chinese manufacturers expanding, new greenfield projects 2-3 years from operation. Supply-demand balance will normalise in 2027-2028. Pricing power will erode. [C+AS]
    • Ambit Capital warns demand-supply gap could narrow significantly in next 2 years as announced capacities come online, leading to rapid deflation of current pricing premiums. [AS]
    • Emkay Research cautions prolonged economic slowdowns and geopolitical uncertainties could severely temper electrical ordering momentum. [AS]
    • Jefferies highlights that if Western grid operators rapidly digest current backlogs by 2028, Arteche's newly expanded production capacities could face a sudden demand vacuum — stranded-asset risk. [AS]
  • Analysis: Raw material inflation risk — copper, aluminium, steel are key inputs. Pass-through clauses have 1-3 month implementation lags. A sudden commodity spike could compress near-term margins before pricing catches up. Morgan Stanley notes base metals can account for 20-40% of COGS for comparable manufacturers. A 10% spike in copper and aluminium could compress near-term EPS by up to 6% for exposed manufacturers. [AS]
  • Analysis: Chinese competitive threat to the lower-voltage segments is intensifying. Chinese OEMs offer pricing up to 40% below Western equivalents, leveraging vast domestic overcapacity. While Arteche's HV niche is protected by qualification barriers and customer approvals, MV and lower-voltage products face direct price competition. The company must grow high-margin segments faster than low-margin segments erode. [AS]
  • Analysis: Elevated interest rates and macroeconomic uncertainty continue to suppress broader business confidence across Europe. If capital costs remain "higher for longer," financing of secondary utility upgrades may be deferred, threatening the rapid backlog conversion that Arteche's current valuation demands. [AS]

L3Y Trend Context — The Compounding Story

Revenue growth averaging ~14.2% over 3 years. Margin expansion accelerating (140bps, 140bps, 430bps). Net profit 2Y CAGR of ~94%. Balance sheet improving throughout. This is the "golden triangle" of fundamental improvement — revenue growth, margin expansion, and deleveraging compounding simultaneously.

  • Judgement: The L3Y pattern reveals a business that crossed a critical operating leverage threshold in FY25. The nonlinear margin jump (430bps vs prior 140bps) typically occurs when incremental revenue drops through to EBITDA at very high incremental margins because fixed costs are already covered. [C]
  • Analysis: Three inflection points defined the L3Y period: [C]
    • FY2023: Order book crossed EUR470m — first clear signal of super-cycle acceleration. Margins expanded for the first time after FY22 trough
    • FY2024: Margin proof point (10.1% to 11.5%) confirmed operating leverage thesis. Stock began to re-rate
    • FY2025: Everything compounded — revenue crossed EUR500m, EBITDA doubled in 2Y, net profit went 5x in 4Y. Stock surged to EUR30 ATH. Main market listing validated institutional readiness
  • Analysis: Revenue-per-employee improved meaningfully — headcount grew from ~2,500 to ~2,600 (+4%) while revenue grew 15.3%. This is genuine productivity improvement. [C]
  • Stock price trajectory mirrors earnings with a lag. Re-rating from ~EUR5 (early 2024) to EUR30 (March 2026) is a 6x move driven by three forces: earnings growth (genuine), multiple expansion (10x PE to 38x PE), and improved market access (BME Growth to main market). [C]

Market Reaction & Stock Price Context

The ~18% pullback from ATH should be contextualised: the stock is still up ~400% from 12 months ago. At EUR24.6, valuation is more reasonable but not cheap — the base case is priced in; upside requires the bull case.

  • Judgement: The pullback from EUR30 to EUR24.6 brings valuations from stretched (38x trailing PE) to in-range (31x trailing, ~22-26x forward). The correction was driven by profit-taking and broader European market weakness, not fundamental deterioration. [C]
  • Analysis: Forward PE range of 22-26x (FY26E EUR55-65m NI) is broadly in line with ABB (22-24x), Schneider (25-27x), and moderately below Siemens Energy (30-35x turnaround premium). This is fair for Arteche's growth rate but constrained by liquidity and coverage limitations. [C]
  • Analysis: Share price appreciation of 467% during BME Growth tenure and subsequent 88% revaluation during FY24 alone reflect genuine earnings re-rating, but the "tourist" investor base (attracted by the run-up) may exit quickly in any correction, amplifying downside volatility. [AS]
