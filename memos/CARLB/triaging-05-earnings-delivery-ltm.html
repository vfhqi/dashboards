KEY FINDINGS

  • - Q1 2026 organic volume growth of +2.8% beat the soft Q1 2025 base (organic volumes were roughly flat in Q1 2025 ex-San Miguel), but comp quality is mixed: the headline beat is partly driven by the Pepsi Kazakhstan/Kyrgyzstan distribution take-on in CEEI (estimated ~70% volume lift in those sub-markets) and weather tailwinds in the Nordics. Underlying organic beer volume growth is materially lower than the headline.
  • - Management has lifted the 2026 full-year organic operating profit guidance range from 3-5% (the FY 2025 target) to 2-6%, which is technically an upgrade in floor but a widening of the range — it signals increased uncertainty rather than conviction in a higher outcome. The upper end at 6% is unchanged.
  • - The war-driven macro environment is the dominant forward risk: CEO Jacob Aarup-Andersen explicitly stated the company is "planning for a continued crisis for the rest of the year" and that ripple effects on supply chains and commodities will persist through 2026. Hedging covers 2026 input costs, but 2027 exposure is openly disclosed as unhedged risk.
  • - Britvic integration is delivering ahead of plan: approximately 30% of the GBP 110m synergy target was captured in FY 2025 alone, and the target has been raised from GBP 100m to GBP 110m. This is a structural positive and the most reliable earnings driver in the near term.
  • - Premium beer is holding: Carlsberg brand +10%, Tuborg +4%, 1664 Blanc +2% in Q1 2026. However, CEO commentary on consumer polarisation ("premium or economy; the core middle gets squeezed") indicates the mass-market and mid-tier beer segment is under pressure — a risk for competitors but also a signal that Carlsberg's own mainstream brands in Western Europe remain soft.
  • - Revenue per hectoliter (price/mix) grew only +1% organically in Q1 2026, following +1% in Q1 2025. This is a deceleration from the 2022-2024 period when pricing was the primary earnings growth driver. With volumes now the growth engine and pricing contribution minimal, gross margin sustainability into H2 2026 depends heavily on commodity cost behaviour.
  • - Net debt / EBITDA stood at 3.28x at end-FY 2025, down from the 3.4x post-acquisition pro forma but still well above the 2.5x target (end-2027 at latest). India IPO ($700m target) is the key near-term capital release event, still at preparatory stage as of Q1 2026.
  • - Heineken Q1 2026 (reported 23 April) showed total volume growth of +1.2% with consolidated volume down -0.2%, versus Carlsberg's organic +2.8%. At headline, Carlsberg outperformed on volume — but the key difference is Carlsberg's CEEI Pepsi distortion; on a like-for-like beer volume basis the outperformance margin is narrower.

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW — Q1 beat is real but partially distorted; guidance maintained with wider range; macro risk elevated**
  • - One-line delivery: Q1 2026 organic volumes +2.8%, organic revenue/hl +1%, total reported revenue +3.0% to DKK 20.7bn. Investing.com and Yahoo Finance describe the result as a beat vs sell-side expectations, with 5% organic operating profit growth referenced.
  • - Key positive surprise: Carlsberg organic volumes in Q1 2026 reversed the Q1 2025 organic stagnation (Q1 2025 organic revenue growth was roughly flat excluding San Miguel). The Asia region, specifically Vietnam's Tet/festive season tailwind and Huda brand resurgence, drove APAC's +3.4% organic volume.
  • - Key negative nuance: The CEEI region's apparent volume surge is significantly inflated by the take-on of Pepsi distribution rights in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan from Q1 2026 (the previous incumbent was Royal Unibrew). Stripping out this structural addition, underlying organic beer volume in CEEI would be materially lower than the headline +4.6% regional figure.
  • - Forward guidance: Full-year 2026 organic operating profit guidance lifted to 2-6% from the prior 3-5% range (FY 2025). The midpoint (4%) is unchanged. Bloomberg headlined this as "keeps 2026 profit guidance" while Yahoo Finance headlined "Carlsberg Lifts 2026 Outlook". Both characterisations are technically correct. The key fact is the lower bound was cut to 2%, widening downside scenario protection.
  • - FX: The original FY 2026 guidance embedded a c. DKK 100-200m FX headwind. No material change to FX guidance was disclosed at Q1 2026, but the broader macro backdrop (Iran conflict, dollar/CNY movements) warrants monitoring.
  • - The Q1 beat relative to expectations — organic volumes in line, profit metrics ahead — did not prompt a meaningful guidance upgrade. This is consistent with management's cautious tone on H2 2026.
  • - Overall investment implication: Q1 2026 strengthens the thesis marginally — the beat is real, Britvic is delivering, but management itself is not raising the midpoint of guidance and is explicitly preparing for continued disruption. Net/net, no re-rating catalyst emerges from Q1 alone.
  • - Earnings quality: YELLOW. Volume growth partly structural/comp-driven; price/mix contribution thin; macro uncertainty high; but Britvic synergies are a genuine, ahead-of-schedule positive.

2. COMPANY DESCRIPTION AND CONTEXT

  • **Overall rating: GREEN — Carlsberg is a high-quality franchise facing a cyclical headwind, not a structural impairment**
  • - Carlsberg Group is the world's third-largest brewer by volume, headquartered in Copenhagen. Core brands: Carlsberg, Tuborg, 1664 Blanc, Somersby, Birra Moretti (licensed), and since January 2025, Britvic's UK and Ireland soft drinks portfolio (Robinsons, J2O, Fruit Shoot, Tango, R. White's). Soft drinks now represent approximately 30% of total company volume.
  • - The Britvic acquisition (completed January 2025 for approximately GBP 3.3bn) transformed Carlsberg's profile from a pure beer company to a beverages company with genuine scale in UK/Ireland soft drinks. Leverage increased to 3.4x NIBD/EBITDA at completion, now at 3.28x end-FY 2025.
  • - Financial scale: FY 2025 reported revenue DKK 89.1bn (+18.8% YoY, majority from Britvic consolidation). Organic operating profit growth was 5% in FY 2025, landing at the top of the 3-5% guidance range. FY 2025 MPM operating profit: DKK 13,996m. EBITDA: DKK 18,784m.
  • - L3Y revenue and profit trajectory:
  • | Year | Revenue (DKK bn) | Op. Profit MPM (DKK bn) | Op. Margin (approx.) | |------|-----------------|------------------------|----------------------| | 2022 | 70.3 | ~11.5 | 16.3% | | 2023 | 73.6 | ~11.1 | 15.1% | | 2024 | 75.0 | ~11.4 | 15.2% | | 2025 | 89.1 | 14.0 | 15.7% |
  • - The margin trajectory tells an important story: the 2022 peak margin at 16.3% was eroded in 2023-2024 by input cost inflation, volume weakness (post-Russia exit) and portfolio restructuring. The 2025 recovery to 15.7% was Britvic-assisted. Underlying beer business margins remain below the 2022 peak.
  • - Key strategic context — three pillars under CEO Jacob Aarup-Andersen (JAA): (1) Grow premiums and alcohol-free; (2) Expand soft drinks through composite selling; (3) Deliver Britvic synergies and deleverage. The India IPO is a capital liberation lever nested under pillar 3.
  • - Share price context: CARL B has been a significant underperformer vs European consumer staples in 2023-2025, weighed by Asia volume weakness, Russia exit value destruction, and leverage concerns post-Britvic. The stock has re-rated only modestly as Britvic synergies began to materialise.
  • - Structural market position: Carlsberg holds the number two position in Indian beer (c.20% share behind United Breweries) and is the dominant player in Vietnam and Laos. China is a significant position via Chongqing Brewery, but that exposure is mid-tier and faces intense domestic competition.
  • - Peer context: Heineken is the closest European comparable. AB InBev remains the global dominant player. Carlsberg trades at a discount to both on forward P/E multiples (approximately 15x forward vs. Heineken c.17-18x), which partly reflects the leverage discount and partly reflects lower Asia profit quality.

3. EARNINGS DELIVERY AND BEAT/MISS ANALYSIS

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW-GREEN — beat vs subdued expectations; organic volume outperformed; caution warranted on quality of beat**
  • - Headline delivery: Organic volume +2.8% (Q1 2026) vs organic volume effectively flat in Q1 2025 (ex-San Miguel). Reported revenue +3.0% to DKK 20.7bn. Organic profit delivered 5% growth, described as "topping expectations."
  • - Beat vs expectations: Multiple sources confirm volumes were in line to slightly ahead of consensus forecasts. The 5% organic profit growth was above the sell-side estimate range of approximately 3-4%, making this a modest but clear profit beat.
  • - Q1 2025 comp: Q1 2025 was characterised by CEO JAA as "a soft start to the year, impacted by loss of the San Miguel brand and continued subdued consumer spending." This establishes Q1 2025 as a genuinely weak comp across most geographies, making the Q1 2026 comparison more favourable than it appears at face value.
  • - Regional volume delivery:
  • | Region | Organic Volume Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 base | Notes | |--------|----------------------|-------------|-------| | Asia | +3.4% | Weak (Vietnam, China subdued) | Vietnam Tet tailwind + easy comp | | CEEI | +4.6% | Moderate | Pepsi Kazakhstan/Kyrgyzstan take-on inflating headline | | Western Europe | +1.2% (ex-San Miguel: +2.4%) | Flat to slightly negative | Weather tailwind in Nordics |
  • - Q4 2025 / Q3 2025 delivery pattern: Q3 2025 organic volumes fell 3% and organic sales declined 1.4% vs consensus estimate of -1.6% decline — a slight beat on the headline but an underlying miss on volumes. Q4 2025 (part of FY 2025 release) saw the full-year land at the top of guidance (5% organic OP growth). Pattern: Carlsberg has tended to meet or narrowly beat on profit metrics while volumes have been consistent disappointments through most of 2025.
  • - Sceptical interpretation: The Q1 2026 volume inflection is partly mechanical. CEEI's Pepsi take-on is purely structural (distribution rights switch, not a demand signal). Western Europe's weather tailwind is non-recurring. Vietnam comp normalisation is genuine improvement but cycling an easy base. The "quality" of the organic volume beat is therefore below face value.
  • - Price/mix: Organic revenue per hl grew just +1% across the group, the same as Q1 2025. This is anaemic. Premium beer mix should theoretically be driving +2-3% revenue/hl. The modest +1% suggests mix improvements are being partially offset by geographic mix headwinds (Asian markets delivering volume at lower ASP than Western Europe).
  • - Earnings delivery rating: YELLOW-GREEN. Profit beat is real and encouraging. Volume beat is real but partially non-recurrent. Revenue/hl growth is thin.

4. REVENUE AND ORGANIC GROWTH ANALYSIS — KQ1 DEEP DIVE

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW — volume returning but comp quality and sustainability are the correct sceptical questions**

KQ1: Is volume momentum real and sustainable?

  • - Total organic volume: +2.8% in Q1 2026, versus effectively flat in Q1 2025 (excluding the San Miguel drag which is now lapped). This is the first genuinely positive organic volume quarter in several periods. However, disaggregation reveals the three key drivers all carry caveats.
  • - Vietnam: Strong growth supported by easy comparatives and good festive season momentum. Huda (local mainstream) showed strong growth; 1664 Blanc performed well in premium. Vietnam in Q1 2025 was depressed by a combination of an anti-corruption-driven consumer spending pullback, a post-COVID normalization drag, and competitive pressure. The Q1 2026 rebound is partly comp normalisation rather than a structural demand inflection. The sustainability test will be H2 2026, when Q2-Q3 2025 comps are no longer easy.
  • - China: Growth for premium products (Carlsberg, Tuborg, WFSM brands) but soft mainstream volumes driven by domestic economic weakness and intense domestic competition. China/Hong Kong SAR represented 69% of Asia's total 2025 volume. Premium-only growth in China with mainstream softness means net organic volume growth in China itself is likely low single digits or flat. The premium enclave model is strategically sensible but does not move the needle on total volume.
  • - India: Continued positive momentum. Carlsberg holds approximately 20% share of the Indian beer market. Volume growth in India is genuine demand-driven with distribution expansion overlay. However, India was not cited as a material volume driver specifically for Q1 2026 in management commentary; rather, the India story is a medium-term IPO-related narrative.
  • - CEEI: Organic volume +4.6%, but approximately 70% of volume in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan was attributable to the Pepsi distribution take-on (previously held by Royal Unibrew). The actual organic beer volume growth in CEEI ex-Pepsi redistribution was likely low single digits. This is a critical disclosure buried in the trading statement but not prominently featured in mainstream news coverage.
  • - Western Europe: Organic volume +1.2% reported, or +2.4% excluding the San Miguel drag effect. Soft drinks (Britvic: Robinsons, J2O, Tango) contributed meaningfully to volume growth in the UK and Ireland, with mid-single-digit percentage growth. Weather tailwinds in Denmark, Norway and Sweden (easy comparables from poor Q1 2025 weather) boosted beer volumes in the Nordics. This tailwind will not recur in Q2.
  • - H2 2026 sustainability (KQ1 core question): The easy comp tailwind dissipates from Q2 onwards — Vietnam comps normalise, CEEI's Pepsi structural inflow is a one-off calendar event (it happened in Q1 2026), and Nordic weather normalises. Genuine drivers that do persist: India structural growth, Carlsberg/Tuborg premium brand momentum in Asia, Britvic composite selling if economics materialise. The 2-6% full-year guidance implies management believes H2 volumes will hold: the midpoint volume scenario likely assumes low single-digit H2 organic growth sustained by pricing and premiumisation.
  • - Market share signals: Heineken Q1 2026 reported total volume growth of +1.2% vs Carlsberg's +2.8%. Both companies cite Vietnam as a driver. Carlsberg's outperformance is partly the CEEI Pepsi distortion; underlying beer share gap is narrower.
  • - Revenue momentum rating: YELLOW. Headline improvement is welcome but partially inflated by non-recurrent factors. H2 2026 volume sustainment at +2%+ requires premium mix and Britvic cross-selling to offset the comp tailwind fading.

5. MARGIN PROFILE AND QUALITY — KQ2 DEEP DIVE

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW — 2026 hedged but 2027 openly exposed; consumer polarisation is real but beer holding up**

KQ2a: Carlsberg's own COGS and commodity hedge position

  • - Gross margin context: Approximately 45% of Carlsberg's COGS is packaging materials — predominantly aluminium (cans, approximately 45% of packaging costs), glass, PET, paper/cardboard, plus barley (key agricultural input), hops, energy and logistics.
  • - 2026 commodity hedging: CEO JAA stated at Q1 2026 that Carlsberg is "hedged against cost increases this year" — i.e., the 2026 COGS profile is largely locked in. This is consistent with Carlsberg's standard practice of 12-18 month rolling hedging of major commodity inputs. With 2026 hedges in place, the oil-driven aluminium price surge (LME aluminium hit $3,269/tonne in March 2026) and barley volatility will not materially impair FY 2026 gross margins.
  • - 2027 commodity exposure: No early 2027 hedging was disclosed in the Q1 2026 trading statement. This is the forward risk. If oil and aluminium prices remain at elevated levels through H2 2026 (when companies typically begin building 2027 hedges), the cost of locking in 2027 protection will be materially higher than comparable 2025 hedge levels.
  • - Oil-COGS transmission mechanism for Carlsberg: - Aluminium: oil prices and LME aluminium are correlated via energy-intensive smelting. Aluminium has risen approximately 15% YoY in 2025-2026. Carlsberg's can-heavy packaging mix (cans represent c.45% of packaging costs) creates direct exposure. - Transport/logistics: diesel-linked freight costs affect distribution in all markets, most acutely in emerging Asia. - Glass: less oil-correlated but energy-intensive manufacture. - Barley: commodity grain prices affected indirectly by energy cost of production and fertiliser (gas-linked).
  • - EBIT margin trend:
  • | Period | Revenue (DKK bn) | Op. Margin | Comments | |--------|-----------------|-----------|---------| | FY 2022 | 70.3 | ~16.3% | Peak margin pre-input cost inflation | | FY 2023 | 73.6 | ~15.1% | Input cost inflation peaked | | FY 2024 | 75.0 | ~15.2% | Stabilisation | | FY 2025 | 89.1 | ~15.7% | Britvic inclusion lifts mix |
  • - Operating leverage in Q1 2026: Organic profit growth of 5% on organic revenue growth of approximately 3-4% (volume +2.8% + price/mix +1%) implies positive operating leverage. Britvic synergies (30% of GBP 110m delivered in FY 2025) are continuing to contribute cost saves into the P&L.
  • - Britvic margin contribution: GBP 110m synergy target (raised from GBP 100m) covers manufacturing consolidation, procurement savings, and overhead rationalisation. If 30% was delivered in 2025 (c.GBP 33m), the remaining GBP 77m is spread over 2026-2028, providing continuing tailwind.

KQ2b: Consumer demand channel — oil/macro impact on premium beer demand

  • - CEO JAA's Q1 2026 commentary: "People look either for the premium brand or the economy brand. So what will get squeezed is the core brands in the middle." This is the K-shaped consumer demand narrative — consistent with Coca-Cola's simultaneous April 2026 commentary.
  • - Premium beer holding: Carlsberg brand +10%, 1664 Blanc +2%, Tuborg +4% in Q1 2026 globally. Alcohol-free +7%. This suggests the premium cohort has not yet traded down meaningfully.
  • - Trade-down risk: Carlsberg explicitly flagged that impact on consumer sentiment from the geopolitical/macro environment has "so far been limited." This is a one-quarter observation. If oil prices remain elevated or rise, and consumers face extended cost-of-living pressure through H2 2026, premium beer is more vulnerable than premium spirits (experiential, infrequent) or mainstream beer (inexpensive). The on-trade is the channel most exposed.
  • - Beer's defensive characteristics: Beer has historically demonstrated relatively low demand elasticity during recessions, supported by affordable indulgence psychology. The net observation is that premium beer is resilient but not immune.
  • - Margin quality rating: YELLOW. 2026 is hedged and operating leverage is real. 2027 commodity re-hedging is the primary forward risk. Consumer demand for premium is holding in Q1 but macro pressure is real.

Overall rating: YELLOW — deleveraging path credible but dependent on India IPO timing

  • - Net debt position: NIBD/EBITDA of 3.28x at end-FY 2025. Target: maximum 2.5x by end-2027. The path requires approximately 0.78x of de-leveraging over two years, which implies either EBITDA growth of approximately 14-16% from current levels (without debt reduction) or a combination of free cash generation and debt repayment supplemented by India IPO proceeds.
  • - Free operating cash flow: DKK 7,011m in FY 2025, an increase vs FY 2024, supported by Britvic operating cash generation. This is the structural engine of deleveraging. At current FCF levels, organic de-leveraging alone can close the 0.78x gap by end-2027 — achievable without the India IPO but tight.
  • - India IPO: Carlsberg has appointed three banks and is preparing a potential listing of Carlsberg India that could raise approximately $700m (approximately DKK 5bn). This represents approximately 25% of the Indian business. The India IPO would be a material one-off accelerant to the deleveraging path, potentially reducing NIBD/EBITDA by 0.25-0.30x at a stroke. As of Q1 2026, the process is at the preparatory stage — no binding commitments and no timeline announced. India equity markets have been volatile, which may affect timing.
  • - Capital returns: Carlsberg has maintained its dividend policy but share buyback activity is constrained by the leverage position. The company has stated that buybacks will resume in a meaningful way only once the 2.5x leverage target is approached.
  • - PepsiCo Nordics/Baltics expansion: The deal to take over PepsiCo bottling in Denmark, Finland, Latvia, Estonia and Lithuania from 1 January 2029 is a long-lead strategic commitment. Near-term capital allocation impact is minimal, but it signals Carlsberg's commitment to the composite beer/soft drinks model.
  • - Capital allocation rating: YELLOW. FCF is strong and Britvic is delivering. Leverage reduction is on track. India IPO is the swing factor.

7. MANAGEMENT GUIDANCE AND FORWARD OUTLOOK

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW — guidance maintained but range widened; tone is cautious; H2 2026 execution risk is real**
  • - Full-year 2026 guidance: Organic operating profit growth of 2-6%, based on the FY 2025 MPM operating profit of DKK 13,996m. This guidance was introduced at the FY 2025 release in February 2026 with the wider range. At Q1 2026, management maintained this range without raising it despite a profit beat.
  • - What the guidance implies: At 2-6% organic, the FY 2026 operating profit range is approximately DKK 14,276m to DKK 14,836m. The midpoint at 4% implies DKK 14,556m.
  • - FX guidance: The original FY 2026 guidance included an estimated FX translation headwind of approximately DKK 100-200m. No update to FX guidance was provided at Q1 2026.
  • - Management tone on consumer environment: CEO JAA was explicitly cautious in public post-earnings appearances (CNBC interview, 29 April 2026): "Any cost increases will be covered by price" — a strong pricing commitment signal. He also acknowledged that demand for beer in western markets remains subdued.
  • - War/Iran macro commentary: JAA stated Carlsberg is planning for "a continued crisis for the rest of the year," referencing the Iran conflict context and its knock-on effects on supply chains and commodities. The company is not projecting a sharp demand recovery predicated on conflict resolution.
  • - KQ2 consumer demand channel: Management explicitly adopted the K-shaped consumer framing (premium or economy; middle squeezed). The 10% Carlsberg brand volume growth in Q1 2026 is cited as evidence the premium end is holding.
  • - Key forward catalysts: (1) India IPO — H2 2026 target, $700m; (2) Britvic synergy run-rate — trajectory toward full GBP 110m by 2028; (3) Vietnam volume sustainability through Q2-Q3 2026; (4) PepsiCo Nordics/Baltics deal (2029 effective).
  • - Forward outlook credibility rating: YELLOW. Management guidance is credible and conservatively set — the 2-6% range deliberately embeds war/macro optionality. The concern is that the floor (2%) is genuinely plausible if oil stays elevated, Vietnam comps turn, and premium beer demand softens.

8. STRATEGIC EXECUTION

  • **Overall rating: GREEN — Britvic ahead of plan is the headline; composite selling early stage but logical**
  • - Britvic integration progress: Ahead of plan. Approximately 30% of the GBP 110m synergy target captured in FY 2025 (c.GBP 33m). The synergy target itself was raised from GBP 100m to GBP 110m in October 2025. At the current pace, Carlsberg could reach GBP 110m full synergy delivery by 2027 rather than the stated 2028 target.
  • - Composite selling (beer + soft drinks): No explicit Q1 2026 disclosure quantifying composite selling wins or revenue/margin contribution. The PepsiCo Nordics/Baltics announcement (January 2029 effective) is the most concrete evidence of the composite selling model's strategic value.
  • - Asia strategy: Vietnam recovery path is the key near-term proof point. Q1 2026 data confirms a rebound, but H2 sustainability question (KQ1) is unresolved. China strategy (premium focus via Carlsberg/Tuborg/WFSM) is sound but volume outcomes will remain modest. India pre-IPO momentum provides a clean, high-growth narrative.
  • - CEO JAA strategic agenda: JAA has three levers — premiumisation, Britvic/composite, and deleveraging. All three are directionally on track as of Q1 2026.
  • - Alcohol-free (AF) as a growth vector: AF brews +7% in Q1 2026. Growing but category contribution to total volume remains sub-5%.
  • - Strategic execution rating: GREEN. The most important strategic bet (Britvic integration) is ahead of plan and the target has been raised.

10. SCEPTICAL AND BEARISH INTERPRETATION

  • **Overall rating: YELLOW — the bear case is moderate not severe, but multiple headwinds converging**
  • - Volume quality concern: The Q1 2026 organic volume beat of +2.8% overstates underlying demand. Remove the CEEI Pepsi distribution take-on effect (estimated at 50-100bps of total group volume) and the Nordic weather comp tailwind (non-recurrent, another 20-30bps), and the underlying organic beer volume growth is closer to 1.5-2.0%.
  • - Comp calendar risk: From Q2 2026 onwards, the Vietnam easy comp begins to normalise. If Vietnam growth moderates to low single digits in Q2-Q3, the Asia regional contribution to group volume falls sharply. This creates a realistic scenario where group organic volume growth dips back toward 1-1.5% in Q2.
  • - Revenue per hectoliter stagnation: +1% organic revenue/hl in Q1 2026 is the same as Q1 2025. If pricing power has peaked (as macro pressure intensifies), and volumes moderate in H2, the revenue growth engine weakens.
  • - Margin sustainability concern: 2026 gross margins are hedged. But the 2027 re-hedging cycle is approaching during a period of elevated commodity prices. COGS inflation of 5-8% into 2027 is a plausible scenario if commodity prices do not normalise.
  • - Consumer demand tail risk: The K-shaped consumer narrative works in Carlsberg's favour (premium holding) in normal cost-of-living cycles. But a deeper consumer retrenchment driven by sustained oil/energy price shock could compress even premium beer demand. This is a H2 2026/2027 tail risk, not a Q1 2026 reality.
  • - Britvic execution risk: The GBP 110m synergy target is ahead of schedule, but approximately 70% of the synergies remain to be delivered (GBP 77m). Integration milestones that slip, or UK consumer softness affecting Britvic revenue, could slow the synergy cadence.
  • - Valuation discount: CARL B trades at approximately 15x forward P/E vs Heineken at 17-18x. The discount reflects: (a) 3.28x leverage; (b) earnings quality concerns around Asia volumes; (c) higher emerging market exposure. The discount is rational but also means any positive re-rating requires either de-leveraging or a sustained volume/margin improvement cycle.
  • - Bear case scenario (probability 20-25%): Vietnam comps normalise in Q2-Q3, group organic volume returns to 0-1%. Commodity re-hedging in H2 2026 locks in higher 2027 COGS. India IPO delayed to 2027. Full-year 2026 lands near the 2% floor of guidance. This scenario requires multiple things to go wrong simultaneously but the ingredients are all present.
  • - Overall risk profile rating: YELLOW. The business is in better shape than FY 2025, and management is executing. But the macro environment is genuinely hostile, the Q1 beat contains meaningful quality caveats, and the 2027 COGS/consumer risk is real.
