approximately £1,786 million in revenue, representing a 2% year-over-year increase. This stable top-line

trajectory was accompanied by the successful delivery of strategic cost-saving initiatives that structurally improved the company's profitability. Consequently, FY25 Adjusted EBITDA is projected to land in the range of

£355 million to £360 million, marking a 14% to 15% increase over the prior year. This performance equates to

an Adjusted EBITDA margin of approximately 20%, aligning with management's prior guidance and broader market expectations. Momentum has successfully continued into the new fiscal year, with Q1 2026 starting

Regulatory Headwinds & Mitigation Strategies

The UK Autumn Budget: Unprecedented Tax Hikes

The regulatory landscape for Evoke plc shifted dramatically following the UK Autumn Budget announced in November 2025. Chancellor Rachel Reeves introduced unprecedented tax hikes targeting the online gambling sector, fundamentally altering the economics of the UK market. Beginning in April 2026, the Remote Gaming

Duty (RGD) will nearly double, rising from 21% to 40%. Furthermore, a new online sports betting duty of 25%

will be implemented in April 2027 to cover all sports except horse racing. This new sports betting duty is

designed to replace the existing 15% General Betting Duty. 28 30 32 • CIRC 29 31 • News

Prior to any mitigating actions, Evoke estimates that these combined tax changes will increase annual duty costs by approximately £125 million to £135 million once fully implemented in 2027. Given the phased

implementation timeline, the pre-mitigation financial impact for the 2026 fiscal year alone is projected to be

around £80 million. The severity of this blow is magnified by the fact that in 2024, Evoke already paid £329 million in taxes and duties to the UK Exchequer, which represented over 60% of its total UK profits. 33

Comprehensive Mitigation Plans

To navigate this severe financial headwind, Evoke's management has moved quickly to execute a comprehensive

mitigation plan aimed at offsetting approximately 50% of the duty increases over the medium term. A primary lever in this strategy is the aggressive rationalization of the company's physical retail estate. The company

has confirmed plans to close up to 200 William Hill betting shops that have been rendered commercially

unsustainable under the new tax regime, a move that could affect up to 1,500 jobs. 38 39 • CIRC

Alongside these physical closures, the company is pursuing broader operating cost reductions and supplier

savings. Management also plans to fundamentally alter the core customer proposition to defend margins. This will be achieved by implementing a significant reduction in overall marketing spend and scaling back

promotional generosity and bonus frameworks for players. 40 41 42 • CIRC

Market Consolidation and Black Market Risks

While the tax hikes present a severe short-term challenge, Evoke anticipates longer-term strategic benefits

through forced market consolidation. The rising cost of compliance and taxation is expected to force smaller,

unlisted operators to exit the UK market entirely. 43 44 • CIRC

Jefferies analysts echo this view, highlighting that smaller sub-scale operators will likely face effective tax rates

exceeding 50% on their net gaming revenue if they attempt to maintain their historical promotional spending levels. The broker points out that these sub-scale operators lack the bargaining power to renegotiate supplier

agreements and will struggle to absorb the margin compression, thereby allowing scaled operators like Evoke to

capture their displaced market share. 45 46 • Jefferies Research

Beyond the immediate taxation threat, the Group continues to navigate ongoing safer gambling regulations stemming from the UK Gambling Act Review White Paper. This includes the implementation of a new

statutory levy on operators to fund research, education, and treatment, as well as strict online slot staking limits

capped at £5 for adults and £2 for customers under 25. Management has warned that the combination of

these stringent affordability checks and the recent tax hikes will inevitably make regulated betting products much

more expensive for consumers. Consequently, Evoke's Board has expressed deep concern that these

mounting regulatory pressures will ultimately jeopardize customer protection by driving recreational players away

from licensed platforms and toward the untaxed, unregulated black market. 47 48 • ARS 49

Launch of the Strategic Review

In December 2025, Evoke's board of directors formally initiated a comprehensive review of the company's strategic options. This strategic pivot was heavily influenced by the UK Autumn Budget, which introduced

severe gambling tax hikes that exacerbated the company's existing financial pressures. The primary objective

of the strategic review is to explore a broad range of alternatives designed to maximize shareholder value. Management explicitly noted that these alternatives could include the potential sale of the entire Group, or the

targeted divestment of specific assets and business units. To assist with evaluating these structural options,

the board appointed Morgan Stanley and Rothschild & Co as its joint financial advisers. 51 55 • CIRC

The Bally's Intralot Takeover Proposal

Following the initiation of the strategic review, Evoke confirmed in April 2026 that it was in active discussions

regarding a formal takeover approach from Bally's Intralot. Bally's Intralot, which was formed through a

strategic tie-up between Greek lottery firm Intralot and US casino operator Bally's Corporation, proposed an

acquisition price of 50 pence per share. This 50p-per-share proposal values the UK betting group's equity at

approximately £225.3 million. The proposed transaction structure is expected to be delivered as an all-share

combination that also includes a partial cash alternative. From a valuation perspective, the 50p offer price

announcement. 56 59 • IRPRESS 57 • News 58 • News 60 • News

Balance Sheet and High Debt Load

position of approximately £1,787.7 million. This debt structure includes a mix of GBP sterling, Euro, and US

Dollar-denominated liabilities. To proactively manage this burden and ensure sufficient liquidity for working

capital volatility, the company maintains a £150 million Revolving Credit Facility (RCF). By the end of 2024, the

Group had drawn down £85 million from this facility, highlighting the cash pressures associated with exceptional

transformation costs and weaker first-half trading. 69 • Investor Presentation 70 72 • ARS
