Strategic Turnaround and Value Creation Plan

The strategic overhaul of evoke plc was initiated as a complete operational reset under the leadership of CEO Per

Widerström, who joined the business in late 2023. To signify this deep commitment to uniting as one company

with a shared vision, the Group officially rebranded from 888 Holdings to evoke plc in early 2024. Central to this

transformation is the Value Creation Plan (VCP), a comprehensive blueprint designed to drive sustainable growth

and deliver high returns on equity. The VCP is operationalized by an almost entirely new executive leadership

team that brings fresh expertise from both inside and outside the gaming sector. By shifting to a more agile,

leaner operating model, management aims to correct legacy operational inefficiencies that previously hindered

the Group's performance. 1 2 4 • ARS 3 • Transcript - Earnings Calls

The Value Creation Plan (VCP) Framework The Value Creation Plan is built around three core financial and strategic principles. First, the company aims to

drive profitable and sustainable revenue growth by increasing its player base and growing the share of wallet

through improved customer lifecycle management. Second, evoke focuses on improving profitability and

efficiency through operating leverage, heavily supported by capability building in data insights and intelligent

automation. Third, the business is committed to rapid deleveraging through highly disciplined capital allocation

and resource management. 5 8 • ARS 6 7 • ARS

Management has tied these strategic pillars to clear, medium-term financial targets that signal confidence in the turnaround. 9 • ARS

Financial Performance and Outlook

Group Earnings Trajectory

Evoke plc has demonstrated a marked financial recovery, successfully arresting historical revenue declines and

driving significant profitability improvements across recent reporting periods. In FY24, the Group returned to

growth with total revenue reaching £1,754.5 million, representing a 3% year-over-year increase. This top-line

recovery was accompanied by an Adjusted EBITDA of £312.5 million, reflecting a 4% increase and establishing a

baseline margin of approximately 18%. The momentum accelerated into H1 2025, where the Group reported

£887.8 million in revenue alongside a massive 44% year-over-year surge in Adjusted EBITDA to £165.9 million. By Q3 2025, Evoke recorded its fifth consecutive quarter of top-line expansion, with Group revenue growing 5%

year-over-year to £435 million. Full-year 2025 trading updates project total revenue of approximately £1,786 million, with Adjusted EBITDA expected to land between £355 million and £360 million. This full-year

expectation represents a 14% to 15% year-over-year earnings increase and secures the Group's targeted 20%

Deleveraging Strategy and Debt Refinancing

Rapid deleveraging is a critical financial priority for the Group, as historical debt loads have suppressed equity

valuations. Leverage peaked at 6.7x before the current management team drove it down to 5.7x at the

conclusion of FY24. Through disciplined capital allocation and the expansion of trailing twelve-month

Adjusted EBITDA to £363 million, the Group further reduced its net leverage ratio to 5.0x by the end of H1 2025. Management has firmly committed to reducing this ratio to below 3.5x by the end of 2027. To optimize its capital structure in the interim, Evoke executed a highly successful debt refinancing in Q3 2025. The Group refinanced its 2027 EUR fixed-rate notes, replacing them with new 8.0% EUR fixed-rate notes due in 2031. This

transaction clears any major debt maturities until 2028 and, combined with updated hedging arrangements, secures approximately £5 million in annualized cash interest cost savings. 66

M&A Activity, Divestitures, and Regulatory Headwinds

Regulatory Shockwaves: The UK Budget Impact

The November 2025 UK Budget dealt a severe blow to Evoke plc and the broader domestic gambling sector. The Chancellor announced that the Remote Gaming Duty (RGD) will increase substantially from 21% to 40%, effective April 2026. Additionally, a new 25% online sports betting duty, which excludes horse racing, will be

implemented from April 2027. Prior to any mitigating actions, the Board expects these aggressive tax changes

Brand Portfolio and Product Innovation

Multi-Brand Strategy and Marketing Transformation

Evoke plc operates a diversified multi-brand strategy anchored by William Hill, 888, and Mr Green, tailored to

optimize market share across the UK and key international jurisdictions. A central pillar of the Group's recent

transformation is a decisive shift in marketing strategy, pivoting away from volume-chasing promotional activity

toward a strict focus on customer value and return on investment. Management has implemented advanced

data-driven Customer Lifecycle Management (CLCM) systems to enhance customer segmentation and deliver

personalized marketing. This disciplined approach allowed the company to reduce marketing spend by £12

million in the first half of 2025 while still generating top-line growth, significantly boosting contribution margins. The Group is increasingly transitioning its marketing efforts from being promotions-led to product-led,

leveraging targeted product improvements to drive organic customer engagement and brand differentiation. 103 • PROXY 104 105 106 • INTRM 107 • INTRM
