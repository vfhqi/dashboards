Company Overview and Strategic Transformation

The Value Creation Plan and Leadership Reset

Under the leadership of CEO Per Widerström and CFO Sean Wilkins, Evoke plc has embarked on a comprehensive strategic and operational reset. The corporate identity was officially rebranded from 888 to evoke in May 2024

to foster a unified "One Company" ethos and align the organizational culture. Central to this transformation is

the Value Creation Plan (VCP), which serves as the overarching blueprint for the group's future. The VCP is built

upon three core pillars: driving profitable and sustainable revenue growth, improving profitability and efficiency

through operating leverage, and deleveraging via disciplined capital allocation. Evoke established clear

medium-term financial targets to anchor this plan, aiming for 5-9% annual revenue growth, approximately 100

basis points of Adjusted EBITDA margin expansion per year, and a leverage reduction to below 3.5x by the end of

2027. To execute this vision effectively, the company completely reshaped its executive team and flattened

management layers to drive sharper, faster decision-making. 1 2 3 4 5 • ARS

Leverage Trajectory and Capital Structure

A central pillar of Evoke's financial strategy is rapid deleveraging through disciplined capital allocation. The

company made significant progress in 2025, reducing its net debt to EBITDA ratio to 5.0x by June 2025, a

substantial 1.7x reduction compared to the 6.7x leverage multiple recorded just 12 months prior. While total

net debt remained broadly stable at £1,818.2 million, the rapid expansion in LTM Adjusted EBITDA was the primary driver of this improved leverage profile. To optimize its capital structure, Evoke successfully

refinanced its 2027 EUR fixed-rate notes in the third quarter of 2025, issuing new 8.0% notes due in 2031 to extend maturities and secure annualized cash interest savings of approximately £5 million.
