Full-Year and Q4 2025 Results

Evoke plc demonstrated underlying momentum in its full-year 2025 results, with total annual revenue growing by

2% year-over-year to approximately £1,786 million. The company expects its full-year Adjusted EBITDA to align

with market expectations following disciplined cost management. During the fourth quarter of 2025, revenue

increased by 7% on a quarter-over-quarter basis. However, Q4 revenue declined by 3% year-over-year, or 4% in

constant currency, due to exceptionally strong comparatives from operator-friendly sporting results in the prior

year. Despite the headline decline, the underlying Q4 performance revealed robust growth across several divisions, with total gaming revenue up 9% year-over-year. This was supported by a 10% year-over-year increase in Retail revenue and a 14% year-over-year increase in International revenue. These gains were ultimately offset

by betting revenue, which fell 22% year-over-year as the company lapped the difficult comparatives from late

2024. 1 3 4 5 6 7 • Press Release 2 • INTRM

Q3 2025 Performance

The third quarter of 2025 marked Evoke's fifth consecutive quarter of year-over-year revenue growth. Group

revenue reached £435 million, representing a 5% increase over Q3 2024, or 4% in constant currency. The UK &

Ireland Online division posted 1% revenue growth, reflecting an 8% increase in sports betting that was partially

offset by a 2% decline in gaming. This gaming decline was largely attributed to a deliberate reduction in

marketing for the 888 brand as the company targeted higher overall marketing returns. The Retail division

recorded a 6% revenue increase during Q3, driven equally by a 6% rise in sports betting and a 6% increase in

gaming following the successful rollout of new gaming cabinets. 8 9 10 11 12 • INTRM

Strategic Review and Potential Takeover Proposals

UK Budget Catalyst and Strategic Review Initiation

The catalyst for Evoke plc's current strategic review was the UK government's autumn budget in November 2025,

which introduced severe tax increases for the regulated gambling sector. Chancellor Rachel Reeves raised the

remote gaming duty from 21% to 40%, effective from April 2026. Furthermore, the budget outlined a new

online sports betting duty of 25%, applicable to all sports excluding horse racing, scheduled for implementation in

2027. Prior to any mitigation actions, Evoke's management estimated that these aggressive tax changes

would generate an annualized £125 million to £135 million increase in duty costs. 41 • Press Release

Debt Context and Capital Structure

Evoke's vulnerability to the UK tax hikes has been heavily compounded by its substantial internal debt burden. The company's net debt currently stands at approximately £1.8 billion. A significant portion of this leverage

was accumulated during the company's £2 billion debt-funded acquisition of William Hill's non-US operations in

2021. As of mid-2025, Evoke's net debt to EBITDA ratio stood at a highly elevated 5.0x, though this was a

sequential reduction from 5.7x at the end of 2024. The combination of mounting debt obligations and soaring

operational tax costs effectively forced the company to put itself up for sale under what media outlets described

M&A Activities and Market Strategy

Broader Market Footprint and the Core Markets Strategy

Evoke plc has fundamentally restructured its geographic footprint to prioritize risk-adjusted returns and operational scale. The company has divided its global footprint into two distinct classifications: "Core Markets" and "Optimise Markets." The strategy designates the UK, Italy, Spain, Romania, and Denmark as the

five Core Markets, which collectively account for approximately 90% of total group revenue. For its Optimise

Markets, the company is deliberately stepping away from growth-at-all-costs to focus strictly on maximizing cash

flow, streamlining operations, and executing targeted market exits. 66 67 68 69 • ARS

US B2C Exit and Authentic Brands Partnership Termination

A primary example of the Optimise Markets strategy in action is the complete exit from the highly competitive

United States B2C market. Management determined that the intense competition and massive scale

requirements in the US would necessitate unjustifiable levels of investment to reach profitability. As part of

this strategic withdrawal, Evoke terminated its brand license agreement with Authentic Brands Group, which had

previously granted the company exclusive rights to use the Sports Illustrated brand for betting and gaming. This termination required a $50 million fee, structured as a $25 million upfront cash payment and the remaining

$25 million payable between 2027 and 2029. 70 71 • ARS 72 73 • INTRM

Corporate Governance and ESG Initiatives

Board Diversity Policy and Governance Framework

Evoke plc maintains a structured framework for evaluating and promoting diversity at the highest levels of the

organization. The Board recognizes that a diverse and inclusive leadership team is essential for maintaining a

competitive advantage and fostering independent, critical perspectives. As part of its gender diversity objectives, the company actively seeks to align with the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) UK Listing Rules. Specifically, the policy mandates a target of having at least 40% female representation on the Board. Furthermore, Evoke requires that at least one senior leadership position—such as the Chair, Chief Executive

Officer, Senior Independent Director, or Chief Financial Officer—is held by a woman. 99 100 101
