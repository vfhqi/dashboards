Strategy Architecture and Objectives

Evoke's strategic framework is built upon a fundamental bifurcation of its geographic footprint into "Core" and

"Optimise" markets. The five Core Markets—the UK, Italy, Spain, Romania, and Denmark—represent

approximately 90% of total group revenue. Within these specific regions, the company targets podium positions

and sustainable profitable growth by leveraging local expertise, high barriers to entry, and established regulatory

frameworks. Conversely, the Optimise markets are managed strictly to maximize cash flow and value

generation. This disciplined resource allocation strategy includes exiting unprofitable regions, such as the US

B2C market, to prioritize risk-adjusted returns in core jurisdictions. 1 5 • ARS

The company operates a diverse portfolio of internationally renowned brands, primarily led by William Hill, 888,

the new CVP focuses on betting and gaming done properly, supported by a refreshed brand identity and enhanced user experience. UK Budget Disruption and Mitigation Levers

The regulatory landscape in the United Kingdom experienced a seismic shift following the November 2025 UK Budget, which fundamentally altered the economic framework for betting and gaming operators. The

government announced that Remote Gaming Duty (RGD) will increase from 21% to 40% starting in April 2026. Furthermore, a new online sports betting duty of 25%, replacing the existing 15% General Betting Duty, will be

implemented for all sports excluding horse racing from April 2027. Prior to any mitigating actions, Evoke's

management estimated that these aggressive tax hikes would increase duty costs by approximately £125 million

to £135 million on an annualized basis once fully implemented. Approximately £80 million of this pre-

mitigation impact is expected to materialize in the 2026 financial year due to the phased implementation timeline. 57 58 59 60 • CIRC

Evoke's Board has strongly cautioned that the drastic duty increases will jeopardize the progress made by the

licensed betting industry regarding safer gambling. The company argues that absorbing these taxes will force

regulated operators to make their products more expensive for consumers, which will inevitably drive players

toward the unregulated black market. In the black market, there is no accountability for operators, no
