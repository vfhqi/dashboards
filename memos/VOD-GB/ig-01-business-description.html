Company Overview and Business Segments

Vodafone Group plc operates as a leading European and African integrated telecommunications company, providing connectivity and digital services at a massive global scale. The company serves over 360 million mobile and fixed broadband customers across its global footprint. Vodafone's operational network spans 15 countries, with strategic investments in five additional markets and partner network agreements in over 40 more. While historically reliant on traditional mobile connectivity, Vodafone has steadily transformed its business model to encompass a broader digital services platform, including global Internet of Things (IoT) solutions, cloud infrastructure, and African financial services.

Geographic Footprint and Core Operations

Vodafone's revenue generation is structurally divided across five primary geographic segments: Germany, the United Kingdom, Other Europe, Türkiye, and Africa. Germany remains the company's largest single market, where it offers a comprehensive portfolio of mobile, fixed, and TV services. In the German market, Vodafone boasts the largest gigabit broadband footprint in the country, reaching 25 million households through its cable network and an additional 5 million through fiber. In the UK, the recent merger with Three UK has created the country's largest mobile network operator, serving over 28 million customers. The UK business utilizes a multi-brand strategy for the consumer segment, encompassing the primary Vodafone brand alongside Three, VOXI, SMARTY, and Talkmobile.

Strategic Infrastructure: Subsea Cables and Satellite Networks

Vodafone is a primary investor in the global subsea cable infrastructure that forms the backbone of the international internet. The company maintains capacity on 80 subsea cable systems spanning more than one million kilometers and reaching 100 countries. Recent investments include the 2Africa project, which is the world's largest subsea cable system designed to connect 33 countries across Africa, Europe, and Asia. Vodafone is also constructing the Thetis Express, a high-capacity optical fiber system linking Crete with mainland Greece to position the region as a major digital corridor.

Company Publication • 14 Feb 25

Press Release • 30 Jan

To complement its terrestrial and subsea networks, Vodafone is aggressively expanding into space-based connectivity. The company formed a joint venture called Satellite Connect Europe with AST SpaceMobile to deliver direct-to-device (D2D) low Earth orbit (LEO) satellite connectivity. This technology allows standard 4G and 5G smartphones to connect to satellites without specialized hardware, effectively eliminating dead zones across the continent. Additionally, Vodafone has partnered with Amazon's Project Kuiper (Amazon Leo) to provide high-speed satellite backhaul for remote mobile masts. This initiative allows Vodafone to expand 4G and 5G coverage into rural areas of Europe and Africa without the prohibitive cost of laying fixed fiber links.

Financial Performance and Corporate Strategy

Vodafone Group has recently undergone a massive operational and geographic transformation, repositioning the business to achieve sustainable, medium-term cash flow growth. The company has aggressively streamlined its portfolio to exit highly competitive, low-growth environments while doubling down on its strongest assets in Europe and Africa.

Financial Trajectory and Near-Term Guidance

Following several years of structural headwinds, Vodafone has established a trajectory of sustainable top-line growth across its reshaped geographic footprint. In the third quarter of FY26, the company reported organic group service revenue growth of 5.4%. This performance was fueled by strong double-digit growth in Africa and Türkiye, alongside continued stabilization in Germany, which delivered 0.7% service revenue growth in Q3 as the company laps the negative impacts of multi-dwelling unit (MDU) TV law changes.

Competitive Landscape and Peer Comparison

European Telecommunications Industry Dynamics

The European telecommunications sector is characterized by high fragmentation, intensive capital expenditure requirements for next-generation networks, and an ongoing wave of strategic market consolidation. Operators must balance the heavy investment burdens of 5G Standalone (SA) and fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) deployments against historically constrained returns on capital employed. To navigate these pressures, major players are increasingly pivoting toward structural de-layering, infrastructure monetization, and the expansion of high-margin digital and business-to-business (B2B) services.

Climate Transition and Net-Zero Targets

Vodafone's ESG strategy is heavily anchored in its "Protecting the Planet" pillar, which dictates aggressive timelines for decarbonization. Regulatory Environment and Strategic Outlook

Management remains engaged with European regulators to shape an environment conducive to ongoing infrastructure investment.
