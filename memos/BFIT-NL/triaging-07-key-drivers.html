BFIT Q7 KEY DRIVERS ASSESSMENT [C]

KEY FINDINGS

1. BFIT has 7 identifiable share price drivers for the next 24 months. Four are operational execution (FCF inflection, Clever Fit synergies, France 24/7 expansion, ARPM growth). Two are financial structure (convertible bond resolution, multiple re-rating). One is strategic optionality (franchise model scale).

2. FCF inflection is the primary fulcrum driver. The market is currently valuing BFIT on a backward-looking EBITDA basis. A credible FCF yield of 5–7% by end-2026 would re-rate the stock materially.

3. The convertible bond put (June 2026) is the single most important near-term binary event. Resolution removes the largest overhang. Failure to navigate cleanly creates a credit event risk that would compress the multiple toward 10–12x EBITDA.

4. France 24/7 is the highest-quality short-duration catalyst — the €10m saving is confirmed, the regulatory amendment is done, and implementation is already underway. Further amendments have option value.

5. Clever Fit integration is a 12–24 month execution story. It adds revenue, margin, and strategic optionality (franchise model) — but management is navigating territory new to the business.

6. ARPM growth is a slow-burning but durable driver. Even 3% annual ARPM growth on 6m members delivers ~€50m in incremental annualised revenue at minimal marginal cost.

7. The trio comparison reveals BFIT as the highest-upside but highest-execution-risk opportunity versus GYM-GB (highest quality) and SATS-NO (most consistent delivery). Watson ranks BFIT as the best setup for a 12–24 month investment horizon IF bond risk is successfully managed.

8. Management has a consistent track record of meeting guidance (FY2023, FY2024, FY2025 all within guidance range) and demonstrated willingness to raise guidance when performance warrants (Q1 2026).

9. The valuation case is compelling at current price (~€30). EV/EBITDA of ~5.3x forward is near trough levels despite the material improvement in business quality. The 12-month analyst consensus of €37–38 implies ~24% upside.

10. The thesis requires 3 of 7 drivers to materialise — it is not dependent on a single event. The interdependencies are manageable. The critical single point of failure is the convertible bond.

DRIVER #1: FCF INFLECTION AND DELEVERAGING

Definition: The transition from negative/breakeven FCF to materially positive FCF generation, accompanied by a reduction in net leverage from ~3.4x to below 2.0x EBITDA.

Management guidance: FCF positive in 2025 (achieved, ~€26m). Target net leverage below 2.0x EBITDA by 2026. The FY2026 trajectory — with capex reduced to ~50 net new owned clubs versus 125 in 2025 — implies capex reduction of ~€70–90m YoY. Combined with EBITDA growth to €415–455m, FCF could reach €100–130m in FY2026.

Public consensus: FCF estimates for BFIT are a key debate point. Most analyst models show FCF inflecting sharply in 2026 — from below €100m (including acquisition impacts in 2025) to €100–130m in 2026 and €150–180m in 2027. This trajectory would place BFIT on a 5–6% FCF yield by 2026–2027 at current price levels — a compelling value proposition for a growing business.

Recent performance: FY2025 FCF ~€26m (including ~€160m Clever Fit acquisition cash). Adjusting for the acquisition, run-rate FCF was ~€180–200m — confirming the operating model generates strong cash. The FY2025 number is misleading; investors focusing on the adjusted figure will correctly identify the FCF inflection as already underway.

Plausibility: HIGH. The reduction in capex is mechanically certain — management has guided explicitly to fewer club openings. EBITDA growth is directionally confirmed. The main risk is that EBITDA undershoots guidance.

Bear case: EBITDA delivers at low end (€415m) + higher interest costs from bond refinancing (~€15m incremental) + integration costs for Clever Fit (~€10m) = FCF lands at €60–70m rather than €100m+. Still positive and improving, but the market re-rating would be more modest.

Plausibility rating: HIGH. Execution risk: MEDIUM.

DRIVER #2: CLEVER FIT INTEGRATION SYNERGIES AND GERMANY SCALE

Definition: Realisation of €10–20m in cost synergies from the Clever Fit acquisition, plus revenue upside from the Clever Fit franchise network. Germany becoming a top-3 revenue market for BFIT by 2027.

Management guidance: No formal synergy quantification. Management has stated that profitability of the Clever Fit estate improved "in the first months." The acquisition price of €175m total (€160m + €15m earn-out) implies a pre-synergy EV/EBITDA of approximately 8–12x — relatively standard for a franchise platform acquisition.

Strategic logic: Clever Fit's 383 franchise clubs provide a franchise management infrastructure, brand, and franchisee base. The BFIT brand extension to franchise (targeting France, Germany, Spain) uses this infrastructure as the platform. Procurement synergies (Clever Fit gyms using BFIT-negotiated equipment and supply rates) add margin immediately. IT rationalisation and HQ cost savings are achievable within 12–18 months.

Recent performance: "Improved profitability in first months" (management, Q1 2026 call). This is a positive early signal. The quality of franchise clubs is variable — standard for franchise networks — and BFIT will need to invest in franchisee upskilling and brand standard enforcement.

Germany scale: 450 combined clubs, market leader. Germany gym penetration is ~15% of population (versus ~20% in Netherlands). The addressable opportunity is meaningful. However, BFIT's owned clubs in Germany are only 67 — franchise clubs (383) are the majority. The owned clubs will have higher revenue recognition on BFIT's P&L; franchise clubs contribute a fee only.

Plausibility: MEDIUM-HIGH. Synergies are credible and partially confirmed. The risk is that franchise integration is slower than expected — Clever Fit's franchisees are independent operators who may resist BFIT's standardisation agenda.

Bear case: franchisees resist integration, Clever Fit EBITDA contribution disappoints, BFIT writes down the earn-out, and the acquisition adds €15–20m in ongoing integration costs without the revenue ramp. This scenario is unlikely but possible.

Plausibility: MEDIUM-HIGH. Execution risk: MEDIUM-HIGH.

DRIVER #3: ARPM GROWTH THROUGH UPSELL / FRANCHISE FEES

Definition: Average revenue per member per month grows from €24.91 (FY2025) to €26–28 by 2027 through Premium tier adoption, franchise fee inclusion, and incremental ancillary revenue.

Management guidance: ARPM growth has been guided implicitly through the launch and promotion of Premium memberships and ancillary services. No specific ARPM target. The franchise fee revenue from Clever Fit will inflate reported group ARPM without corresponding membership growth.

Recent performance: FY2025 ARPM grew €0.67 (+2.8%) from €24.24 to €24.91. This is modest but consistent. Q1 2026 ARPM data not specifically disclosed in the update — the member/club density metric (2,981 members/club) is the proxy.

Mechanism: Two independent levers. First, mix-shift: as more members choose Premium (multi-club access), reported ARPM increases by €5–10. Over 50% of new joiners choosing Premium per industry commentary. Second, franchise fee: Clever Fit franchise fees are reported as revenue. The franchise fee per member is lower than direct membership revenue — but it is incremental and capital-light.

Plausibility: HIGH. ARPM growth of 3–5%/year is structurally supported by Premium adoption. The franchise revenue contribution is mechanically certain from the Clever Fit consolidation.

Bear case: ARPM growth stalls at <2% if Premium adoption plateaus and price competition intensifies. McFit's refresh strategy (adding women's zones, social spaces) targets the same Premium-adjacent customer. Competitive pressure on ARPM is a watch.

Plausibility: HIGH. Execution risk: LOW.

DRIVER #4: FRANCE/SPAIN CLUB ROLLOUT — 24/7 STAFFLESS MODEL

Definition: The combination of continued club openings in France (highest-density market, ~900 clubs) and Spain (second-fastest growth market, 230 clubs) with the progressive rollout of the 24/7 staffless model in France. Structural cost reduction of €10m+ in FY2026, with potential for €40–60m over 3–5 years.

Management guidance: France 24/7 pilot begins May 2026 — 50 clubs initially, expanding to ~200 qualifying clubs during 2026. Management expects further regulatory amendments to expand the eligible pool. Spain is described as a high-growth market with attractive club economics (lower rents and labour costs versus France).

Recent performance: The regulatory approval itself (April 2026 announcement) is the key delivery point. The €10m EBITDA raise directly reflects this. Implementation is the next step — the execution risk now shifts from regulatory to operational.

Operating model significance: France's 894 clubs contribute a disproportionate share of BFIT's revenue (~60%) but have historically carried higher costs than Netherlands/Belgium clubs due to mandatory staffing. The staffless model, once rolled out at scale, normalises French club unit economics. A French club converted to staffless will see €30–50k per year in cost reduction per club — material at 200+ clubs.

Spain growth optionality: Spain is following the France 2018–2022 growth curve — rapid network expansion, low market saturation, favourable demographics. A 230-club estate in Spain could grow to 350–400 clubs by 2028 at the current pace, adding €80–120m in incremental revenue.

Plausibility: HIGH for the confirmed €10m France saving. MEDIUM for the further regulatory expansion pathway.

Bear case: French regulatory environment reverses (political change, labour union pressure). The current amendment is based on a change in a specific article of French labour code — reversals are possible but historically unusual once the precedent is set. Spain competitive environment intensifies (Planet Fitness, Vivagym, domestic operators).

Plausibility: HIGH (confirmed €10m) + MEDIUM (further expansion). Execution risk: LOW (confirmed pilot).

DRIVER #5: CONVERTIBLE BOND REFINANCING RESOLUTION — JUNE 2026

Definition: The €303.7m convertible bond put option (exercisable by bondholders 17 June 2026) is resolved without credit stress, using the €290m committed bank facilities plus operating cash.

Financial mechanics: Management has assessed that bondholders are likely to exercise the put (H1 2025 assessment). €290m in committed facilities from ABN AMRO, ING, Rabobank are in place. The company also has operating cash generation (~€30–40m per quarter). Net of these resources, the balance sheet can absorb a full put exercise.

Market perception: The stock's discount to analyst targets (~€30 vs ~€38 consensus) reflects partly this risk overhang. Resolution of the put removes a specific financial risk and de-risks the investment case, potentially causing immediate re-rating.

Refinancing cost impact: The convertible bonds carry a relatively low coupon (terms not separately disclosed in available data, but 2021-vintage convertibles were typically issued at 0.5–2.0% coupon). The replacement facilities from ABN AMRO/ING/Rabobank will carry a higher commercial rate — likely EURIBOR + 200–300bps, implying ~4–6% all-in cost. On €300m, this adds €12–18m/year in incremental interest costs versus the convertible bond coupon.

Plausibility: HIGH that the put is managed without crisis. The facilities are committed. MEDIUM risk of incremental interest cost headwind on earnings.

Bear case: A concurrent market stress event (liquidity crunch, banking sector disruption, BFIT-specific bad news) in May/June 2026 makes the facilities unavailable or significantly more expensive to draw. This is a tail risk, not a base case.

Plausibility: HIGH (resolution). Execution risk: MEDIUM (refinancing cost).

DRIVER #6: MULTIPLE RE-RATING ON FCF PROOF POINT

Definition: As FCF inflects to €100–130m in FY2026 and management demonstrates deleveraging commitment, the market re-rates BFIT from ~5.3x forward EBITDA toward the 8–10x range typical for European gym and leisure operators with positive FCF and growing members.

Current valuation: EV/EBITDA of approximately 5.3x forward — near the historical trough. Forward PE ~20x. The disconnect: the EV/EBITDA is exceptionally low (reflecting leverage and FCF history), while the PE is moderate (reflecting the earnings base). As FCF improves, the leverage argument weakens and EV/EBITDA should normalise upward.

Peer context:

  • GYM-GB trades at a premium to BFIT on a quality-adjusted basis.
  • SATS-NO trades at a premium on operating efficiency metrics.

- Planet Fitness (US comparator) trades at 20–25x EV/EBITDA — a significant premium reflecting the pure-play franchise model quality.

Analyst consensus target: ~€37–38 (vs current €30.14) implies ~26% upside at consensus. The range of €23.50–€46.00 reflects the wide uncertainty spectrum. The bull case (€46) requires a combination of strong FCF delivery, leverage reduction, and franchise scale optionality.

Plausibility: MEDIUM. Re-rating is not mechanical — it requires market recognition of the FCF story over 2–4 quarters of delivered proof points. The market may remain sceptical of reported FCF until the convertible bond is fully resolved.

Bear case: FCF delivers but is accompanied by higher interest costs (from bond refinancing) and Clever Fit integration costs — the headline FCF is below the market's expectations, and re-rating is delayed 12–18 months.

Plausibility: MEDIUM. Execution risk: LOW (this is a market perception shift, not an operational execution risk).

DRIVER #7: FRANCHISE MODEL SCALE — BFIT-BRANDED FRANCHISE ROLLOUT

Definition: BFIT extends its own brand into the franchise model (using Clever Fit infrastructure) across France, Germany, and Spain. A franchise rollout could add 200–400 incremental clubs by 2028 at near-zero capex.

Strategic rationale: The owned-club model requires €1.0–1.5m capex per club plus lease commitments. A BFIT-branded franchise requires near-zero capex from BFIT's perspective — the franchisee funds the fit-out, BFIT earns a fee. If BFIT can charge 6–8% of franchise club revenue as a fee, and if franchise clubs generate €400–600k in revenue each, the fee is €25–50k per club. At 200 franchise clubs, this is €5–10m in pure fee income.

Timeline: Management is "exploring the potential" of franchise rollout under the BFIT brand. This language is exploratory — no formal commitment. Timeline is likely 2027–2028 for first BFIT-branded franchises, if pursued.

Plausibility: MEDIUM. This is an option, not a confirmed driver. The infrastructure is being built. The first proof points will come from Clever Fit integration performance — if that franchise model is managed effectively, the confidence to extend the BFIT brand increases.

Bear case: BFIT brand franchise economics are poor (price point too low for franchisees to earn adequate returns), or regulatory complexity in France makes the franchise model unworkable at low prices. This is a real risk — the low-cost gym franchise model is proven in Germany/Central Europe but untested in France.

Plausibility: MEDIUM. Execution risk: HIGH.

SECTION 10: DRIVER INTERDEPENDENCIES

The drivers are interconnected. The chain of dependencies flows:

1. Bond resolution (Driver 5) enables FCF re-deployment toward deleveraging (Driver 1)

2. FCF inflection (Driver 1) enables multiple re-rating (Driver 6)

3. France 24/7 (Driver 4) directly feeds EBITDA improvement that supports Driver 1

4. Clever Fit integration (Driver 2) provides both ARPM expansion (Driver 3, via franchise fees) and franchise model infrastructure (Driver 7)

Critical interdependency: if the bond put resolution (Driver 5) is messy — i.e., drawn on unfavourable terms or with covenant constraints that restrict new investment — it could impair Clever Fit integration (Driver 2) and franchise rollout (Driver 7) by constraining balance sheet flexibility.

Single point of failure: The June 2026 bond put event. It is the only driver where a negative outcome (credit stress, inability to meet put at face value) would cascade across multiple other drivers simultaneously. All other drivers are operationally independent of each other.

Market re-rates to 9x forward EBITDA on €500m 2027 EBITDA. Implied EV = €4.5bn. Less net debt of ~€1.0bn = equity value €3.5bn = €53/share. Base case (€37–38): EBITDA at mid-point €435m. FCF €90–100m. Bond resolved with modest cost increase. Re-rating to 7.5x forward EBITDA on 2026 EBITDA. EV = €3.26bn. Less net debt ~€1.2bn. Equity = €2.06bn = ~€31–38/share depending on timing and leverage. Consistent with analyst consensus €37.13.

Bear case (€20–22): EBITDA at low end €415m. Bond put triggers higher costs (€20m incremental interest). Clever Fit underperforms. Multiple stays at 5x. EV = €2.08bn. Less net debt €1.4bn. Current P/E: Forward PE of ~20x (on consensus) is moderate. Historical PE range for BFIT has been 15–30x underlying earnings.

SECTION 15: THESIS FRAGILITY

The thesis requires 3–4 of 7 drivers to materialise. Specifically:

Minimum viable combination:

  • Bond put resolved cleanly (Driver 5) — required
  • FCF inflects to €80m+ in FY2026 (Driver 1) — required

- At least one of: France 24/7 expansion, Clever Fit synergies, ARPM growth — any one of three delivers the incremental margin story

The thesis does not require franchise model scale (Driver 7) or immediate multiple re-rating (Driver 6). Those are upside, not base case.

Single point of failure: Driver 5. If the bond put is mismanaged — a low-probability but finite-risk scenario — the thesis collapses into a credit story, not a growth story.

SECTION 17: SCEPTICAL / BEAR THESIS

The most coherent bear case for BFIT is not a gradual deterioration story — it is a sequence of negative surprises that compound.

Sequence: (1) Bond put is exercised by a majority of bondholders, exhausting the committed facilities; (2) in H2 2026, a European macro deterioration (recession, energy shock) reduces new joiner acquisition; (3) France 24/7 pilot progresses slowly due to safety concerns or regulator pushback post-pilot; (4) Clever Fit franchise revenue disappoints because franchisees, facing their own cost pressures, fail to grow membership; (5) EBITDA lands at €400–415m vs the low-end guide of €415m; (6) Management must either cut guidance or issue equity to repair the balance sheet — both outcomes compress the multiple.

In this scenario, BFIT trades back to €20–22, erasing much of the re-rating from the 52-week low.

The probability of this sequence is low (~15–20%) but the severity is material. It is not a scenario where each event is independently low-probability — they are positively correlated through the macro channel (recession drives bond stress, lower membership growth, and energy cost spikes simultaneously).

What makes the bear case wrong: The France 24/7 regulatory change is done and irreversible. The Clever Fit deal is closed — the asset is acquired. The operational cost reductions from 24/7 will materialise regardless of the macro environment. These are structural improvements that underpin a floor for the investment case even in the bear scenario.

Rate downside risk: YELLOW (Y) — meaningful but not base case. The structural cost improvements act as a floor.
