1. Published Short-Seller Reports

No formal activist short-seller report has been published against Basic-Fit by any major firm (Muddy Waters, Citron, Gotham City, Hindenburg, Viceroy, ShadowFall, QualityValue, or equivalents). A thorough search returns no such document.

The absence is meaningful. Basic-Fit is not a fraud candidate. Its revenue is verifiable, membership data auditable, and its business model operationally transparent. The bear case is entirely investment-fundamental: leverage, cohort underperformance, French market saturation, and capital allocation. None of these constitute actionable short-seller material in the Hindenburg/Muddy Waters sense.

Days-to-cover data is not available from search results. No named major hedge fund short position has been publicly disclosed via Dutch AFM filings at the >0.5% threshold that would attract attention.

The short interest trend likely softened from late 2025 into 2026 as: (i) the convertible refinancing was executed; (ii) the FY2025 H1 results showed strong EBITDA growth; and (iii) the sale-process speculation created takeover premium optionality.

6. Counter-Arguments to Bull Case

Bull thesis: Proven low-cost gym model with long European runway.

Bear rebuttal: The European runway was clearest pre-2020. Post-COVID, the competitive landscape has filled in significantly. PureGym, Anytime Fitness, and local low-cost operators have entered or expanded in Basic-Fit's core markets. The moat on first-mover advantage is thinner than the bull case assumes.

Bull thesis: COVID cohort underperformance is temporary; clubs will mature normally.

Bear rebuttal: The underperformance of 2020-2022 clubs in France is now three to five years old. Gyms typically reach maturity within two years. The extended lag suggests structural issues — location selection errors, competitive response, or French consumer preferences — not merely timing. Management has not provided a granular club-by-club analysis that allows external validation.

Bull thesis: Deleveraging trajectory is clear; leverage hits <2.0x by 2026.

Bear rebuttal: The leverage target uses EBITDA adjusted for IFRS 16 in the denominator. On a truly pre-IFRS 16 basis, leverage is running higher. The convertible refinancing (April 2026) added new debt at market rates, not at 1.5% coupon — the cost-of-debt burden rises.

Bull thesis: Sale to private equity would crystallise value; Buckley Capital validated this.

Bear rebuttal: Management explicitly rejected a strategic review in 2024. CEO René Moos owns ~14% and is unlikely to facilitate a sale at current prices. Private equity would need to run a contested process against an unwilling management team — unusual in European mid-caps and typically value-destructive for short-term holders.

7. Historical Pattern

No prior formal short campaigns against Basic-Fit.

Key historical events:

  • COVID closure (2020-2021): All gyms closed. Basic-Fit raised equity capital and maintained its network. Share price recovered to all-time highs (~€50) by 2022. This demonstrated network resilience.
  • 2022-2024 de-rating: Share price fell ~50% from peak. Driven by rising rates, leverage concerns, French cohort underperformance, and multiple guidance misses. Management maintained expansion despite investor pressure — this was the key point of dispute.
  • 2024 Buckley Capital activism: Public letter from top-20 investor urging a sale. Management declined. Stock remained depressed.
  • 2025-2026 recovery: Convertible refinancing executed. FY results showed strong EBITDA growth. Share buyback programme announced (€40m). Stock recovered from trough.

Management response pattern: defend the long-term strategy, resist external pressure, provide data on new cohort performance. CEO Moos has been consistent and tenacious but has occasionally over-guided.

9. Risk Assessment — Bear Scenario

Assumptions in bear scenario:

  • French revenue growth stalls at 0-2% (vs bull case 8-10%)
  • EBITDA margin remains flat or contracts 100bps from cost inflation and competition
  • New club roll-out slows to 50-60 clubs/year (vs 100+ in boom years)
  • Market re-rates EV/EBITDA from 8x to 6x

Bear scenario outputs:

  • EBITDA (pre IFRS 16) stalls at ~€350m
  • At 6x EV/EBITDA and ~€900m net debt (pre-IFRS 16): equity value ~€1.2bn, implying share price €20-22 vs ~€30 (early 2026 estimate)
  • Downside: ~30-35% from current levels

Bear probability estimate: 20-25%. The core franchise is operationally sound; new cohorts are performing. The most acute risk (June 2026 convertible) has been refinanced. The residual risk is France and the pace of leverage reduction.

10. Sceptical Rebuttal to Bear Case

Bear: IFRS 16 inflates leverage.

Bull rebuttal: Every gym operator globally uses the same accounting. Comparing Basic-Fit on a pre-IFRS 16 basis to other leisure businesses is an apples-to-apples problem. The relevant comparison is within the gym sector — on that basis, Basic-Fit's leverage is not an outlier. Lease covenants are tested pre-IFRS 16 and are met comfortably.

Bear: France is saturated.

Bull rebuttal: The 2023 and 2024 club cohorts are performing to plan. If saturation were structural, new clubs would underperform immediately. The underperformance appears specific to the COVID-vintage cohort. France remains the largest single low-cost gym market in Europe, with sub-20% penetration of the addressable fitness market.

Bear: Management won't sell; undervaluation persists.

Bull rebuttal: The share buyback programme at €40m is accretive at current prices. If management is right about the earnings trajectory (EBITDA-less-rent to €317m+ by 2024, growing), the market will reprice without a sale. The buy-back signals management conviction.

Catalysts that prove bears wrong:

1. French cohort EBITDA trajectory improves (quantitative quarterly evidence)

2. Net leverage drops below 2.0x (pre-IFRS 16) on schedule by 2026

3. Club openings in Spain and new markets outperform, diversifying France concentration

4. Private equity bid emerges, validating the Buckley Capital thesis
